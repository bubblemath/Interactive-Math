One-sample z-test · Lesson 20

Assume nothing happened. Then measure your surprise.

A hypothesis test runs on one idea. Adopt the null — the drink does nothing, the mean hasn't moved — and ask: if that were true, how surprising is the data we actually got? That surprise, measured as a p-value, is the whole verdict. Small enough, and we reject the null; otherwise we simply fail to reject it. This ladder drills every part of that logic except the arithmetic, which the calculator handles.

The decision rule

Compare the p-value to α: p < α → reject H₀; p ≥ α → fail to reject. And the wording matters — never "accept" or "prove."

The rules that trip people up
Hypotheses are about μ
H₀ and Hₐ describe the population parameter, never the sample: H₀: μ = μ₀, never x̄.
A p-value is conditional
It's the chance of data this extreme if H₀ were true — not the chance that H₀ is true.
You never accept H₀
Failing to reject isn't proof. You "fail to reject" — the evidence just wasn't strong enough.
Conclusions live in context
Reject → "enough evidence to support the claim that…"; fail → "not enough evidence…," always naming the real claim.
⚖️

It's a jury trial. You start by assuming innocence — the null — and keep it until the evidence is unlikely beyond a reasonable doubt. A low p-value is that string of coincidences too unlikely to swallow. And notice: acquittal never means "proven innocent." Failing to reject H₀ is not proof that H₀ is true.

Why it matters

The grammar of scientific evidence

It's how claims get tested

Drug trials, product claims, quality control — all run this exact loop: assume no effect, measure surprise, decide. The p-value is the number editors, regulators, and juries argue over.

The misreadings are everywhere

"The p-value is the chance the drug doesn't work." "We proved it's safe." Both are wrong, and both change decisions. Getting the logic right is what separates evidence from spin.

Words carry the meaning

"Fail to reject" versus "accept," "support the claim" versus "prove" — the vocabulary isn't fussiness. Each wrong word claims certainty the test never delivered.

Strip away the arithmetic and a hypothesis test is a single honest sentence: if nothing were going on, our data would be this surprising — surprising enough to doubt "nothing," or not. Hypotheses frame the question, the p-value measures the surprise, α draws the line, and the conclusion says exactly what the evidence supports — and, just as carefully, what it doesn't.
