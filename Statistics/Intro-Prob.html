Lesson 7: Probability and Sample Spaces

Key Terms and Definitions

Experiment
Any occurrence where the outcome is uncertain.
Example: Tossing a coin, rolling a die, drawing a card, selecting a family and recording genders.
Sample Space (S)
Set of all possible outcomes of the experiment.
Coin toss: S = {Heads, Tails}
Die: S = {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6}
3 children: S = {MMM, MMF, MFM, MFF, FMM, FMF, FFM, FFF}
Event (E)
Any subset of the sample space.
Rolling an even number: E = {2, 4, 6}
Drawing a heart: E = {A, 2, 3, ..., K of hearts}
Theoretical Probability
If event E has n(E) equally likely outcomes and sample space S has n(S) equally likely outcomes:
P(E) = n(E) / n(S)
Example: P(rolling a 3) = 1/6 = 0.167
Empirical Probability
Based on observed frequency of an event in repeated trials.
P(E) = (observed number of times E occurs) / (total number of trials)
Probability Range
Probability is always between 0 (impossible) and 1 (certain).
P = 0.5 means a 50% chance.

Quick Facts

  • There are 6 sides to a standard die (numbered 1-6).
  • There are 52 cards in a standard deck: 4 suits x 13 cards each.
  • Red cards: 26 (hearts and diamonds), Black cards: 26 (spades and clubs).
  • Face cards: 12 total (4 Jacks, 4 Queens, 4 Kings).
  • Empirical probability is based on data; theoretical is based on equally likely outcomes.
