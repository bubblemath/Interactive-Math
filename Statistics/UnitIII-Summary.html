📊 Central Limit Theorem (CLT)

📊

CLT for Sample Means

Discover how the sampling distribution of x̄ becomes approximately normal as sample size increases.

  • Sampling Distribution – Distribution of x̄ values
  • Mean of x̄: μ_x̄ = μ
  • Standard Error: SE = σ / √n
  • CLT Condition: n ≥ 30 (or population normal)
  • Z = (x̄ − μ) / (σ/√n)

CLT for Proportions

Understand the sampling distribution of sample proportions (p̂) for categorical data.

  • Sample Proportion: p̂ = x/n
  • Mean of p̂: μ_p̂ = p
  • Standard Error: SE = √(pq/n)
  • Conditions: np ≥ 10 and nq ≥ 10
  • Z = (p̂ − p) / √(pq/n)

📐 Confidence Intervals

📐

Confidence Intervals

Learn how to estimate population parameters using point estimates and margins of error.

  • Point Estimate – Single best guess (x̄ or p̂)
  • Margin of Error: E = z* × SE
  • CI Formula: estimate ± margin of error
  • Confidence Level – 90%, 95%, 99%
  • Interpreting confidence intervals correctly
