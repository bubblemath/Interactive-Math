📈 Unit I: Descriptive Statistics

This unit introduces the foundations of statistics—from understanding how to collect and organize data to calculating measures that describe its center and spread. Learn to transform raw numbers into meaningful stories that inform decisions and reveal patterns.

Key Topics: Population vs. Sample • Sampling Methods • Levels of Measurement • Histograms & Box Plots • Mean, Median, Mode • Standard Deviation & Variance
Explore Unit I →

🎲 Unit II: Probability & Distributions

Explore the mathematics of uncertainty. Learn to quantify chance using probability rules, count possible outcomes systematically, and model random phenomena with discrete distributions—forming the foundation for statistical inference.

Key Topics: Sample Spaces & Events • Probability Rules • Conditional Probability • Permutations & Combinations • Binomial Distribution • Expected Value
Explore Unit II →

📐 Unit III: Normal Models & Regression

Transition from descriptive to inferential statistics. Explore the power of the Normal Distribution, understand how the Central Limit Theorem enables predictions about populations, construct confidence intervals, and model relationships using correlation and regression.

Key Topics: Uniform Distribution • Normal Distribution & Z-Scores • Central Limit Theorem • Confidence Intervals • Correlation • Linear Regression
Explore Unit III →

🔬 Unit IV: Hypothesis Testing

Master the formal framework for testing claims about populations using sample data. Learn to set up hypotheses, calculate test statistics, find p-values, and draw conclusions—from single-sample tests to Chi-Square analysis.

Key Topics: Null & Alternative Hypotheses • Z-Tests & T-Tests • Proportion Tests • Two-Sample Comparisons • Chi-Square Tests • Type I & II Errors
Explore Unit IV →
