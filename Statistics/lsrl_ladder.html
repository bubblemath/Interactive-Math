LSRL & residuals · Lesson 19

ŷ is a guess. y − ŷ is how wrong it was.

A regression line is a prediction machine: feed it an x, it returns a predicted ŷ. A residual is a single miss — the gap between what actually happened and what the line guessed. The least-squares line is just the one line that makes those misses, squared, as small as possible. Master those two objects and the whole lesson — predictions, interpretations, residual plots, extrapolation — falls out of them.

Four things the line and its residuals tell you
Least squares, exactly
The LSRL minimizes the sum of squared residuals. The plain sum of residuals is always 0 — positive and negative misses cancel.
Residual = miss
y − ŷ. Positive means the point sits above the line (the line under-predicted); negative means below.
Residual plots diagnose shape
Random scatter around 0 → a line fits. A curve or U-shape in the residuals → the relationship isn't linear.
Don't predict past the edges
Extrapolating beyond the data's x-range assumes the pattern continues — which you have no evidence for.
✋

Residual is observed minus predicted — in that order. residual = y − ŷ. A point above the line has a positive residual because the real value beat the prediction. Flip the subtraction and every sign is backwards, which quietly wrecks the residual plot and every interpretation built on it.

Why it matters

A line is a promise about the future

Prediction is the payoff

Correlation says two things move together; regression turns that into a number you can act on — a forecast, a dose, a price. The residuals are the honesty check on that forecast.

The residual plot is the truth-teller

A high r can still hide a curved relationship. The residual plot is where that shows up — a pattern there means the straight line was the wrong promise, no matter how good r looked.

Extrapolation is where lines lie

Every famous bad forecast is an extrapolation: a line that fit beautifully in range, pushed somewhere it was never tested. Knowing the edge of your data is knowing the edge of your trust.

Every rung is the same two objects in a new costume: the prediction ŷ, and the miss y − ŷ. Slope and intercept describe the line; residuals audit it; the residual plot judges its shape; extrapolation marks where it stops being evidence. Learn to move fluently between the line and its misses and regression stops being formulas — it becomes a forecast you know exactly how far to trust.
