📊 Unit 1: Descriptive Statistics

Data Storytelling & Foundational Concepts

🎯 Unit Overview

This unit introduces the foundations of statistics—from understanding how to collect and organize data to calculating measures that describe its center and spread. You'll learn to transform raw numbers into meaningful stories that inform decisions and reveal patterns in the world around us.

📖

Stat Concepts – Lesson 1

Master the fundamental vocabulary of statistics and learn to classify different types of data.

  • Population vs. Sample – Entire group vs. subset studied
  • Parameter vs. Statistic – Population measure vs. sample measure
  • Levels of Measurement: Nominal, Ordinal, Interval, Ratio
  • Qualitative vs. Quantitative variables

Sampling Methods – Lesson 2

Explore how to properly collect data using various sampling techniques and understand experimental design.

  • Simple Random Sample (SRS) – Equal chance for all
  • Stratified – Divide into groups, sample from each
  • Systematic – Every kth individual
  • Cluster – Random locations, sample all there
  • Observational studies vs. Experiments

Frequency Chart Practice

Organize raw data into structured tables that reveal patterns and proportions.

  • Frequency – Count of each value
  • Relative Frequency – Proportion (frequency ÷ n)
  • Cumulative Frequency – Running total
  • Cumulative Relative Frequency – Running proportion
  • Converting between fractions, decimals, and percents

🎯

Measures of Central Tendency

Calculate and interpret values that describe the "center" of a dataset.

  • Mean (x̄ or μ) – Sum of values ÷ count
  • Median – Middle value when ordered
  • Mode – Most frequent value
  • Grouped Data – Using midpoints and frequencies
  • Five-Number Summary – Min, Q1, Median, Q3, Max
Launch Applet →
📉

Variation

Understand how spread out data is—crucial for distinguishing usual from unusual values.

  • Range – Max − Min
  • IQR – Q3 − Q1 (middle 50%)
  • Variance (σ² or s²) – Average squared deviation
  • Standard Deviation (σ or s) – Square root of variance
  • Population (÷N) vs. Sample (÷n−1) formulas
