📊 Statistics Unit Review

Lessons 13–19: Distributions, Sampling, Correlation & Regression

📈 Continuous & Normal Distributions

  • Density Curves: On/above x-axis; total area = 1.
  • Uniform: Rectangle-shaped; probability = area.
  • Normal: Symmetric, unimodal, bell-shaped; inflection at μ ± σ.
  • Empirical Rule: 68%-95%-99.7% within 1–2–3 σ.
  • Z-score: z = (x − μ) / σ.

🎲 Sampling Distributions

  • Definition: Distribution of a statistic across all samples of size n from a population.
  • Means: Use for quantitative data; Proportions: for categorical.
  • Normal approx (p̂): np ≥ 10 and n(1−p) ≥ 10.

⚖️ Confidence Intervals

  • Purpose: Estimate population parameter with statistic ± margin of error.
  • Types: Z-interval, T-interval, 1-prop Z-interval.
  • Decision: If hypothesis value outside CI, data don't support it.

📊 Scatterplots & Correlation

  • x (explanatory) predicts y (response).
  • r: strength and direction of linear association; unitless; sensitive to outliers.

📏 Regression & Residuals

  • LSRL: ŷ = a + bx minimizes squared residuals.
  • Slope b: Change in y per unit x; Intercept a: ŷ when x = 0.
  • Residual: y − ŷ; sums to 0; avoid extrapolation.
