Conditional fluency · given F, the world gets smaller

Conditioning doesn't change the event. It changes the universe.

"Given F" is an instruction: cross out every outcome that isn't F. What's left becomes the new sample space, and the denominator drops from n(S) to n(F). The formula P(E|F) = P(E ∩ F) ÷ P(F) is nothing but that sentence written in symbols — and once you see the picture, you never have to memorize it.

The one question

Before every second draw, ask: did the first one change anything? Put it back and nothing changed — independent. Keep it and the jar is different — dependent.

Three formulas, one idea
P(E | F) = P(E ∩ F) ÷ P(F)

Multiply both sides by P(F) and you get the dependent rule: P(A ∩ B) = P(A) · P(B | A). And when B doesn't care about A — when P(B | A) = P(B) — that collapses into the independent rule: P(A ∩ B) = P(A) · P(B). You are not learning three formulas. You are learning one, twice rearranged.

✋

Mutually exclusive is not independent. They are closer to opposites. If A and B cannot both happen, then learning that A occurred tells you B definitely did not — and "telling you something" is exactly what dependence means. Two mutually exclusive events with nonzero probability are always dependent. Almost everybody gets this backwards.

Why this matters

Most bad reasoning is a swapped conditional

The direction is everything

P(positive | disease) is about 99%. P(disease | positive) can be under 10%. Same test, same day — utterly different numbers, and swapping them has ruined careers and convicted innocent people.

Replacement is a real decision

Put the marble back and the jar forgets. Keep it and the jar remembers. That single choice decides whether you multiply by P(B) or by P(B|A).

Trees make it visible

Multiply along a path, add across paths. Every conditional problem in this lesson is one of those two moves — and a tree shows you which.

Conditional probability is where probability stops being a game about dice and starts being a tool for thinking. Every time new information arrives — a test comes back positive, a marble is drawn and kept, a witness says the car was blue — the world of possibilities shrinks, and the odds of everything left inside it change. Learning to shrink the sample space correctly, and to keep straight which thing you are conditioning on, is the difference between reasoning from evidence and being fooled by it.
