📖 What is Statistics?

Statistics is the science of collecting, analyzing, and drawing conclusions from data.

🎯 Population vs. Sample

Population: The entire collection of individuals or objects about which information is desired.

Sample: A subset of the population, selected for study in some prescribed manner.

📈 Parameter vs. Statistic

Parameter: A numerical summary of the population.

Statistic: A numerical summary of the sample.

📊 Types of Statistics

Descriptive Statistics: Methods of organizing and summarizing data (like creating graphs, calculating averages).

Inferential Statistics: Involves generalizing from a sample to a population (making predictions based on sample data).

🔄 Variables and Data

Variable: Any characteristic whose value may change from one individual to another.

Data: Observations on a single variable or simultaneously on two or more variables.

📖 Types of Variables

Qualitative (Categorical): Identifies basic differentiating characteristics of the population. Examples: colors, names, categories.

Quantitative Discrete: Numerical observations with a listable set of values, usually counts of items.

Quantitative Continuous: Numerical data that can take on any values in the domain, usually measurements of something.

📊 The Four Scales of Measurement

  • Nominal: Categories, colors, names, labels. Data are not ordered. Example: smartphone brands, favorite foods
  • Ordinal: Categorical data that can be ordered, but differences can't be measured. Example: rankings (good, satisfactory, unsatisfactory)
  • Interval: Numerical data that can be ordered, differences can be measured, but no true zero. Example: temperature in Celsius/Fahrenheit
  • Ratio: Numerical data with a true zero where ratios are meaningful. Example: height, weight, age, SAT scores
