Hypothesis Testing Interactive Worksheet

One-sample z-test practice with the four-part framework

Overview

We begin with a null hypothesis (H₀) and consider an alternative (Hₐ). We compute a p-value and compare it to the chosen significance level (α).

  • p-value: Probability of a result as far out as the result we got, assuming H₀ is true.
  • Decision rules: If p-value < α, reject H₀. If p-value ≥ α, fail to reject H₀.
  • Hypotheses are statements about population parameters (μ, σ), not sample statistics (x̄, s).

Do Say / Don't Say

✓ Do say: "Fail to reject H₀" (not "accept H₀").

✓ Do say: "There is enough evidence to support the claim that..." (when rejecting H₀)

✓ Do say: "There is not enough evidence to support the claim that..." (when failing to reject H₀)

✗ Don't say: "Accept H₀" or "We proved the null hypothesis"

✗ Don't say: Hypotheses in terms of x̄ or s; always write them in terms of μ (population mean)

Guided Workflow: The Four Parts

  1. Hypotheses: State H₀ and Hₐ in terms of μ.
  2. Model: Check assumptions, then choose test type (one-sample z-test with σ known).
  3. Mechanics: Compute z-statistic and p-value.
  4. Conclusion: Compare p-value to α and write the decision in context.
