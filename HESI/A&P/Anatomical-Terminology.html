📐 Body Planes & Sections

Sagittal Plane

Midsagittal (Median): Divides body into equal left and right halves

Parasagittal: Divides body into unequal left and right portions

Frontal (Coronal) Plane

Divides the body into anterior (front) and posterior (back) portions

Think: Like a crown sitting on your head

Transverse (Horizontal) Plane

Divides the body into superior (upper) and inferior (lower) portions

Also called: Cross-sectional or axial plane

🔬 Clinical Application

Understanding body planes is essential for interpreting medical imaging:

  • CT Scans: Typically show transverse (axial) sections
  • MRI: Can show all three planes
  • X-rays: Usually show frontal (AP) or lateral views

💡 Memory Tricks

  • Superior/Inferior: "Super" heroes fly UP, "inferior" things are DOWN
  • Anterior/Posterior: Your "anterior" is what you present to the world (front)
  • Proximal/Distal: "Proximal" = proximity = close; "Distal" = distant = far
  • Medial/Lateral: "Medial" = middle; "Lateral" = to the side (like lateral pass in football)
  • Sagittal: Think of an arrow (sagitta = arrow in Latin) going front to back
  • Coronal: Think of a crown (corona) sitting on your head

⚠️ Common HESI Traps

  • Ventral vs. Anterior: In humans, these are the same. In quadrupeds, ventral = belly side
  • Proximal/Distal: Only used for limbs, not trunk organs
  • Popliteal vs. Patellar: Popliteal is BACK of knee, Patellar is FRONT
  • Plantar vs. Palmar: Plantar = sole of FOOT, Palmar = palm of HAND
  • Transverse vs. Cross-section: These mean the same thing - don't be confused by alternate terms
