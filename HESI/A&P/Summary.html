💡 HESI A&P Success Tips

⏰ Time Management

You have about 60 seconds per question. Don't spend more than 90 seconds on any single question. Mark difficult ones and return if time permits.

🎯 Focus on Function

The HESI emphasizes FUNCTION over memorization. Know what each organ/system DOES, not just what it's called.

📍 Know Locations

Be able to identify WHERE structures are located. Many questions ask about the position of organs relative to each other.

🔗 Connect Systems

Understand how body systems work TOGETHER. For example, how the cardiovascular and respiratory systems cooperate for gas exchange.

❌ Eliminate Wrong Answers

If unsure, eliminate obviously wrong choices first. This increases your odds even when guessing.

📚 Use All Resources

Complete all study modules before taking practice tests. Review weak areas identified by the diagnostic dashboard.

📋 HESI A&P Content Overview

~50% Body Systems

Cardiovascular, respiratory, digestive, nervous, musculoskeletal, urinary, reproductive, endocrine, integumentary, lymphatic/immune systems.

~20% Anatomical Terminology

Directional terms, body planes, body regions, anatomical position, and movement terminology.

~15% Histology

Four tissue types (epithelial, connective, muscle, nervous), their subtypes, locations, and functions.

~15% General Concepts

Homeostasis, cell structure, levels of organization, and how structure relates to function.
