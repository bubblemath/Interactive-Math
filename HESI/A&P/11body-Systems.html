📊 HESI A&P Section Overview

The HESI A2 Anatomy & Physiology section tests your understanding of the human body's structure and function. You'll need to know the 11 major body systems, their organs, and how they work together. Focus on function and location—these are the most commonly tested concepts!

🧠 Visual Mnemonics

🦴 Cranial Bones: "Old People From Texas Eat Spiders"

Occipital Parietal (2) Frontal Temporal (2) Ethmoid Sphenoid

❤️ Heart Blood Flow: "Tri Before You Bi"

Tricuspid valve comes before Bicuspid (Mitral) valve in blood flow through the heart.

RA → Tricuspid → RV → Lungs → LA → Bicuspid → LV → Body

🫁 Lung Lobes: "3 on Right, 2 on Left (heart takes space)"

Right lung has 3 lobes (Superior, Middle, Inferior). Left lung has 2 lobes (Superior, Inferior) because the heart occupies space on the left.

🧠 Spinal Regions: "Breakfast, Lunch, and Dinner"

Cervical = 7 (Breakfast at 7) Thoracic = 12 (Lunch at 12) Lumbar = 5 (Dinner at 5)

🍽️ Digestive Enzymes: "Amylase Attacks stArch, Lipase Loves Lipids, Protease Processes Proteins"

Match the first letter of the enzyme to what it breaks down!

⚗️ Adrenal Hormones: "The deeper you go, the sweeter it gets"

Adrenal cortex layers from outside to inside:

Glomerulosa → Salt (Aldosterone) Fasciculata → Sugar (Cortisol) Reticularis → Sex (Androgens)
