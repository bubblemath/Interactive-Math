🌐 The 3 Domains

  • Archaea (extremophiles)
  • Bacteria (true bacteria)
  • Eukarya (eukaryotes)
  • Domain is the BROADEST level

👑 The 6 Kingdoms

  • Archaebacteria & Eubacteria
  • Protista (protists)
  • Fungi (decomposers)
  • Plantae (plants)
  • Animalia (animals)

📝 Binomial Nomenclature

  • Two-part scientific naming
  • Genus + species (italicized)
  • Genus capitalized, species lowercase
  • Example: Homo sapiens

📊 Taxonomic Hierarchy

  • Domain → Kingdom → Phylum
  • Class → Order → Family
  • Genus → Species
  • Species is MOST specific

🧠 Visual Mnemonic: "Dear King Philip Came Over For Good Soup"

Domain → Kingdom → Phylum → Class → Order → Family → Genus → Species

Remember: Domain is the BROADEST (most inclusive), Species is the MOST SPECIFIC (least inclusive).

⚠️ Common Pitfalls to Avoid

  • Confusing Archaebacteria and Eubacteria - Both are prokaryotes, but Archaea live in extreme environments and have different cell wall composition
  • Forgetting Domain is broader than Kingdom - Domain is the HIGHEST level of classification
  • Mixing up scientific name format - Genus species (italicized), Genus capitalized, species lowercase
  • Confusing Fungi with Plants - Fungi are heterotrophs (decomposers), plants are autotrophs (photosynthesis)

🏥 Why It Matters in Nursing

Understanding classification helps nurses identify pathogens and select appropriate treatments. Bacterial infections require antibiotics, while fungal infections need antifungals. Knowing whether an organism is prokaryotic or eukaryotic affects drug selection and infection control protocols.

Domain Archaea

The Extremophiles

Cell Type: Prokaryotic (no nucleus)

Cell Wall: NO peptidoglycan (unique lipids)

Habitat: Extreme environments (hot springs, salt lakes, deep ocean vents)

Examples: Methanogens, halophiles, thermophiles

Domain Bacteria

True Bacteria

Cell Type: Prokaryotic (no nucleus)

Cell Wall: Contains peptidoglycan

Habitat: Everywhere! Soil, water, inside organisms

Examples: E. coli, Streptococcus, Salmonella

🧬

Domain Eukarya

The Eukaryotes

Cell Type: Eukaryotic (has nucleus)

Cell Wall: Varies by kingdom

Includes: All organisms with membrane-bound organelles

Complexity: Most diverse domain

👑 The Six Kingdoms

🌋

Archaebacteria

Domain: Archaea
Cell Type Prokaryotic
Cell Wall No Peptidoglycan
Nutrition Autotroph/Heterotroph
Organization Unicellular
Examples:

Methanogens, Halophiles (salt-lovers), Thermophiles (heat-lovers)

🦠

Eubacteria

Domain: Bacteria
Cell Type Prokaryotic
Cell Wall Peptidoglycan
Nutrition Autotroph/Heterotroph
Organization Unicellular
Examples:

E. coli, Streptococcus, Cyanobacteria, Salmonella

🔬

Protista

Domain: Eukarya
Cell Type Eukaryotic
Cell Wall Varies
Nutrition Autotroph/Heterotroph
Organization Mostly Unicellular
Examples:

Amoeba, Paramecium, Algae, Plasmodium (malaria)

🍄

Fungi

Domain: Eukarya
Cell Type Eukaryotic
Cell Wall Chitin
Nutrition Heterotroph (Saprobe)
Organization Mostly Multicellular
Examples:

Mushrooms, Yeast, Mold, Candida

🌿

Plantae

Domain: Eukarya
Cell Type Eukaryotic
Cell Wall Cellulose
Nutrition Autotroph (Photosynthesis)
Organization Multicellular
Examples:

Trees, Flowers, Ferns, Mosses, Grasses

🐾

Animalia

Domain: Eukarya
Cell Type Eukaryotic
Cell Wall None
Nutrition Heterotroph
Organization Multicellular
Examples:

Humans, Dogs, Fish, Insects, Birds
