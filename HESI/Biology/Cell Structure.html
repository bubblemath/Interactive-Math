🧱 Cell Membrane & Transport

Phospholipid Bilayer Structure

Transport Mechanisms

Simple Diffusion

Movement of small, nonpolar molecules (O₂, CO₂) from high to low concentration directly through the membrane.

No ATP Required

Osmosis

Diffusion of WATER across a selectively permeable membrane from high to low water concentration.

No ATP Required

Facilitated Diffusion

Movement of large or polar molecules through protein channels or carriers. Still follows concentration gradient.

No ATP Required

Active Transport (Pumps)

Movement AGAINST concentration gradient using carrier proteins. Example: Na⁺/K⁺ pump.

Requires ATP

Endocytosis

Cell membrane engulfs materials to bring them INTO the cell. Types: phagocytosis, pinocytosis, receptor-mediated.

Requires ATP

Exocytosis

Vesicles fuse with membrane to release contents OUT of the cell. Used for secretion.

Requires ATP

🧪 Tonicity & Osmosis

Hypotonic

Less solute outside → Water enters cell → Cell swells (may lyse)

Isotonic

Equal solute → No net water movement → Cell stays same

Hypertonic

More solute outside → Water leaves cell → Cell shrinks (crenation)

🔄 Cell Cycle & Division

👆 Click on a phase to learn more

Interphase (G1, S, G2)

G1 Phase (Gap 1)

Cell grows, performs normal functions, prepares for DNA replication

S Phase (Synthesis)

DNA replication occurs - chromosomes are duplicated

G2 Phase (Gap 2)

Cell continues to grow, prepares for mitosis

Mitosis (PMAT)

Prophase

Chromatin condenses, nuclear envelope breaks down, spindle forms

Metaphase

Chromosomes line up at the MIDDLE (metaphase plate)

Anaphase

Sister chromatids are pulled APART to opposite poles

Telophase

Nuclear envelopes reform, chromosomes decondense, cytokinesis begins

⚖️ Mitosis vs. Meiosis Quick Comparison

Mitosis

  • • 1 division → 2 identical cells
  • • Diploid (2n) → Diploid (2n)
  • • For growth & repair
  • • Somatic (body) cells
  • • No crossing over

Meiosis

  • • 2 divisions → 4 unique cells
  • • Diploid (2n) → Haploid (n)
  • • For reproduction
  • • Gametes (sex cells)
  • • Crossing over occurs

💡 Memory Tricks

  • 🔹 Mitochondria = "Mighty" - Powerhouse makes ATP energy
  • 🔹 Rough ER = "Rough with Ribosomes" - Has ribosomes attached
  • 🔹 Golgi = "Post Office" - Packages and ships proteins
  • 🔹 Lysosomes = "Suicide Sacs" - Digest and destroy
  • 🔹 PMAT - Prophase, Metaphase, Anaphase, Telophase
  • 🔹 "Metaphase = Middle" - Chromosomes line up in the middle
  • 🔹 "Anaphase = Apart" - Chromatids pull apart

⚠️ Common HESI Traps

  • ❌ Ribosomes vs. Rough ER: Ribosomes make proteins; Rough ER modifies them
  • ❌ Mitosis vs. Meiosis: Mitosis = 2 identical cells; Meiosis = 4 unique gametes
  • ❌ Facilitated diffusion: Uses proteins but NO ATP (still passive!)
  • ❌ Hypo/Hyper: Think about where the SOLUTE is, not the water
  • ❌ Centrioles: Found in animal cells, NOT plant cells
