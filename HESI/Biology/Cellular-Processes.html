Interphase

Interphase is the longest phase of the cell cycle where the cell grows, replicates DNA, and prepares for division.

Key Events:

  • Cell grows and performs normal functions (G1)
  • DNA replication occurs (S phase)
  • Organelles duplicate
  • Cell prepares proteins for division (G2)
  • Chromatin is loosely organized

🔄 Cell Division Overview

Cell division is the process by which a parent cell divides into two or more daughter cells. Mitosis produces genetically identical cells for growth and repair, while meiosis produces genetically diverse gametes for reproduction.

Mitosis vs. Meiosis

  • Mitosis: 1 division → 2 identical cells
  • Meiosis: 2 divisions → 4 unique cells
  • Mitosis: diploid (2n) → diploid (2n)
  • Meiosis: diploid (2n) → haploid (n)

Cell Cycle Checkpoints

  • G1 checkpoint: Cell size & DNA damage
  • G2 checkpoint: DNA replication complete
  • M checkpoint: Chromosome alignment
  • Prevents errors and cancer

Cellular Metabolism

🔋 Cellular Respiration Overview

Cellular respiration is the process by which cells break down glucose to produce ATP (energy). The overall equation is: C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + ATP

1. Glycolysis

  • Occurs in cytoplasm
  • Breaks glucose into 2 pyruvate
  • Produces 2 ATP (net)
  • Produces 2 NADH
  • Anaerobic process

2. Krebs Cycle

  • Occurs in mitochondrial matrix
  • Also called citric acid cycle
  • Produces 2 ATP per glucose
  • Produces NADH and FADH₂
  • Releases CO₂

3. Electron Transport Chain

  • Inner mitochondrial membrane
  • Produces most ATP (32-34)
  • Uses oxygen as final acceptor
  • Creates water as byproduct
  • Chemiosmosis drives ATP synthesis

🌱 Photosynthesis Overview

Photosynthesis converts light energy into chemical energy stored in glucose. The overall equation is: 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + light → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂

Light-Dependent Reactions

  • Occur in thylakoid membranes
  • Require sunlight
  • Split water molecules
  • Produce ATP and NADPH
  • Release oxygen

Calvin Cycle (Light-Independent)

  • Occurs in stroma
  • Uses ATP and NADPH
  • Fixes CO₂ into glucose
  • Also called dark reactions
  • Regenerates RuBP

Cell Transport

🌊 Passive Transport

Movement of molecules across the cell membrane WITHOUT energy (ATP). Molecules move from HIGH to LOW concentration (down the concentration gradient).

Simple Diffusion

  • Small, nonpolar molecules
  • Directly through membrane
  • Examples: O₂, CO₂
  • No proteins needed

Facilitated Diffusion

  • Larger or polar molecules
  • Uses channel or carrier proteins
  • Examples: glucose, ions
  • Still no ATP required

Osmosis

  • Diffusion of WATER
  • Through semipermeable membrane
  • Toward higher solute concentration
  • Critical for cell homeostasis

⚡ Active Transport

Movement of molecules across the cell membrane USING energy (ATP). Molecules move from LOW to HIGH concentration (against the concentration gradient).

Primary Active Transport

  • Directly uses ATP
  • Sodium-potassium pump
  • Moves 3 Na⁺ out, 2 K⁺ in
  • Maintains cell potential

Endocytosis

  • Cell engulfs materials
  • Phagocytosis (solids)
  • Pinocytosis (liquids)
  • Receptor-mediated

Exocytosis

  • Cell releases materials
  • Vesicles fuse with membrane
  • Secretion of proteins
  • Waste removal
