🏥 Why This Matters in Nursing

Every calculation you make as a nurse directly impacts patient safety. A misplaced decimal point in a medication dosage can mean giving a patient 10 times too much or too little medication. Mastering these basic operations builds the foundation for safe clinical practice.

📋 Quick Reference Guide

Use this section as a reference while practicing. Click any topic to expand.

🔢 Order of Operations (PEMDAS)

Parentheses → Exponents → Multiplication/Division (left to right) → Addition/Subtraction (left to right)

Example: 3 + 4 × 2 = 3 + 8 = 11 (NOT 14)
Example: (3 + 4) × 2 = 7 × 2 = 14
⚠️ Common Mistake

Don't just work left to right! Always check for multiplication/division before addition/subtraction.

➕ Adding & Subtracting Decimals

Key Rule: Line up the decimal points vertically, then add/subtract as usual.

  12.50
+  3.75
-------
  16.25
🏥 Clinical Application

When calculating total fluid intake: 250.5 mL + 175.25 mL = 425.75 mL

✖️ Multiplying Decimals

Key Rule: Multiply as whole numbers, then count total decimal places in both factors and place the decimal point that many places from the right.

2.5 × 1.2 = ?
25 × 12 = 300
Total decimal places: 1 + 1 = 2
Answer: 3.00 = 3
⚠️ Common Mistake

Forgetting to count decimal places from BOTH numbers being multiplied.

➗ Dividing Decimals

Key Rule: Move the decimal point in the divisor to make it a whole number. Move the decimal point in the dividend the same number of places.

4.5 ÷ 0.5 = ?
Move decimal 1 place: 45 ÷ 5 = 9

➕ Adding & Subtracting Fractions

Same Denominator: Add/subtract numerators, keep denominator

Different Denominators: Find LCD, convert, then add/subtract

1/4 + 2/4 = 3/4

1/3 + 1/4 = ?
LCD = 12
4/12 + 3/12 = 7/12

✖️ Multiplying Fractions

Key Rule: Multiply numerators together, multiply denominators together, then simplify.

2/3 × 3/4 = 6/12 = 1/2

➗ Dividing Fractions

Key Rule: Keep the first fraction, Change to multiplication, Flip the second fraction (KCF)

2/3 ÷ 1/4 = 2/3 × 4/1 = 8/3 = 2 2/3
⚠️ Common Mistake

Flipping the wrong fraction! Only flip the second fraction (the divisor).

🔢 Rounding Rules

Look at the digit to the RIGHT of where you're rounding:

  • If it's 5 or greater → Round UP
  • If it's 4 or less → Round DOWN (keep the same)
Round 3.847 to nearest tenth:
Look at hundredths place (4) → Round down
Answer: 3.8

Round 3.857 to nearest tenth:
Look at hundredths place (5) → Round up
Answer: 3.9
