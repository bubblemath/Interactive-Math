📊 HESI A2 Math Section Overview

The HESI A2 Mathematics section tests your ability to perform calculations essential for safe nursing practice. Understanding dosages, fluid measurements, and patient data all require solid math foundations. Algebra is a critical component—solving for unknown values appears throughout clinical calculations.

🏥 Why Algebra Matters in Nursing

Every calculation you make as a nurse directly impacts patient safety. Algebraic thinking helps you:

  • Calculate medication dosages when you know the desired dose and concentration
  • Determine IV flow rates based on total volume and time requirements
  • Solve for unknown quantities in clinical formulas and ratios
  • Interpret lab value thresholds and safe dosage ranges
  • Translate word problems into mathematical equations for real-world scenarios

📋 Algebra Rules Reference Vault

⚖️ The Golden Rule of Equations

Whatever you do to one side, you MUST do to the other!

An equation is like a balanced scale. To keep it balanced:

  • Add the same number to both sides
  • Subtract the same number from both sides
  • Multiply both sides by the same number
  • Divide both sides by the same number

🔄 Order of Operations (PEMDAS)

When solving, work in reverse PEMDAS!

Parentheses → Exponents → Multiplication/Division → Addition/Subtraction

To isolate x, undo operations in reverse order: Start with addition/subtraction, then multiplication/division.

📦 Distribution Property

a(b + c) = ab + ac

Multiply the outside term by EACH term inside the parentheses.

Example: 3(x + 4) = 3x + 12

🔗 Combining Like Terms

Only combine terms with the same variable (or no variable)

Like terms: 3x + 5x = 8x ✅

Unlike terms: 3x + 5 cannot be combined ❌

Example: 2x + 3 + 4x - 1 = 6x + 2

⚠️ Inequality Rules

Flip the inequality sign when multiplying or dividing by a negative!

Example: -2x > 6 → Divide by -2 → x < -3

The sign flips from > to < because we divided by a negative number.
