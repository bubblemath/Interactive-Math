💡 Strategies & Common Pitfalls

🧮 Calculator Strategies

✅ Convert Fractions to Decimals

The calculator doesn't handle fractions directly. Convert first: 3/4 = 3 ÷ 4 = 0.75

✅ Estimate Before Calculating

Round numbers mentally first to check if your answer is reasonable. If you calculate 250 × 4 and get 10, you know something's wrong!

✅ Double-Check Decimal Placement

After every calculation, verify your decimal point. A misplaced decimal can mean giving a patient 10× too much medication!

✅ Use Memory Functions

If available, use M+, M-, and MR to store intermediate results in multi-step problems.

⚠️ Common Pitfalls to Avoid

🚨 Proportion Setup Errors

Always put matching units in matching positions! mg/mL = mg/mL, not mg/mL = mL/mg. A setup error can result in a 10× or 100× dosage error.

🚨 Decimal Point Disasters

When multiplying decimals, count total decimal places. 0.5 × 0.2 = 0.10 (not 1.0). In nursing, this could mean giving 0.1 mg instead of 1.0 mg!

⚠️ Forgetting to Flip Inequality Signs

When multiplying or dividing an inequality by a negative number, flip the sign! -2x > 6 becomes x < -3

⚠️ Military Time Confusion

Remember: Add 12 to PM times (except 12 PM = 1200). Subtract 12 from times ≥1300 to get PM. 1500 = 3:00 PM

⚠️ Percent vs. Decimal

25% = 0.25, not 25 or 0.025. To convert: divide by 100 (or move decimal 2 places left).

⚠️ Unit Conversion Direction

Larger to smaller = multiply. Smaller to larger = divide. 2 kg = 2000 g (multiply by 1000)

🎯 Test-Taking Strategies

🚩 Flag and Move On

If a question takes more than 90 seconds, flag it and move on. Return to flagged questions after completing easier ones.

📋 Eliminate Wrong Answers

Even if you're unsure, eliminate obviously wrong answers. This improves your odds from 25% to 33% or 50%!

🔍 Check Units in Your Answer

If the question asks for mL and your answer is in L, you need to convert! Always verify your answer has the requested units.
