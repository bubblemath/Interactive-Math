💡 General Tips for Success

1
Read the Questions First
Previewing the questions helps you read the passage with a specific purpose, allowing you to "hunt" for the necessary information more efficiently.
2
Identify the Passage Type
Determine if the text is Informational, Persuasive, or Narrative to better understand the author's intent and approach your analysis accordingly.
3
The "Evidence Test"
For every answer you choose, ensure you can point to a specific sentence or phrase in the text that supports it. If you can't find evidence, reconsider your choice.
4
Eliminate Extremes
Be wary of answer choices containing absolute words like "always," "never," or "only"—these are rarely the correct choice in reading comprehension.

Main Ideas

Learn to distinguish between the broad topic of a passage and the specific central point the author is making. This module covers both explicit main ideas and those that are implied. Master the skill of identifying what the author is truly saying about their subject.

Supporting Details

Master the ability to identify the "legs of the table"—the facts, reasons, and examples that hold up the main idea. Think of a paragraph like a table: the main idea is the tabletop, and supporting details are the legs. Without strong supporting details, the main idea "falls down."

Start Learning
💡
Inference

Practice "reading between the lines." This tutorial teaches you how to draw logical conclusions based on the information provided, even when the author doesn't state them directly. Look for clues, use the evidence test, and watch for signal phrases like "It can be inferred" or "The author suggests."

Start Learning
🔍
Context Clues

Build your vocabulary using the IDEAS acronym (Inference, Definition, Example, Antonym, Synonym) to decode the meaning of unfamiliar words based on the surrounding text. Remember: the HESI isn't measuring your vocabulary—it's measuring your ability to figure out meaning from context!
