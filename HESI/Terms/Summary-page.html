💡 Success Strategies

1
The Building Block Strategy
Break down unfamiliar medical terms into prefixes, roots, and suffixes. Even if you've never seen the word, you can decode its meaning from its component parts.
2
Context Detective
Use surrounding words and sentences to determine meaning. Look for definition clues, synonyms, antonyms, and examples within the passage that reveal the word's meaning.
3
The Substitution Method
Replace the unknown word with each answer choice. The correct answer should make the sentence logical and maintain its original meaning without changing the context.
4
Watch for Traps
Be careful with commonly confused words (affect/effect, discrete/discreet) and words with different medical vs. general meanings (acute, void, dilated, precipitous).
