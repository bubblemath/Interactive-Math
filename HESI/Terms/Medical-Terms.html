🧱 The Building Block Strategy

Medical terminology follows a predictable pattern. By mastering prefixes, roots, and suffixes, you can decode virtually ANY medical term—even ones you've never seen before!

🔤 Prefixes

Come at the beginning and modify the meaning. Examples: hyper- (excessive), hypo- (under), pre- (before)

🫀 Roots

The core meaning, usually referring to a body part or system. Examples: cardi- (heart), hepat- (liver), neur- (nerve)

🔚 Suffixes

Come at the end and indicate condition, procedure, or specialty. Examples: -itis (inflammation), -ectomy (removal), -ology (study of)

🔗 Combining Vowels

Usually "o" or "i" used to connect word parts for easier pronunciation. Example: cardi/o/logy

🩺 Nursing Context

Understanding medical terminology isn't just about passing the HESI—it's essential for reading patient charts, understanding physician orders, communicating with the healthcare team, and providing safe patient care. Every term you learn is a tool for your nursing practice!

📝 Combining Vowel Rules

Use a combining vowel: When connecting a root to a suffix that begins with a consonant.
Don't use: When the suffix begins with a vowel (usually).
Always use: When connecting two roots together.
