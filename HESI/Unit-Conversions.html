🏥 Why Conversions Matter in Nursing

Medication errors due to unit conversion mistakes are among the most common—and preventable—errors in healthcare. A misplaced decimal or wrong unit can result in a 10x or 100x dosing error. Mastering these conversions is literally a matter of patient safety.

📏 Metric System - The Staircase Method

Moving DOWN the stairs (larger → smaller): Move decimal RIGHT (multiply)

Moving UP the stairs (smaller → larger): Move decimal LEFT (divide)

Unit Abbreviation Relationship
Kilogramkg1 kg = 1,000 g
Gramg1 g = 1,000 mg
Milligrammg1 mg = 1,000 mcg
MicrogrammcgBase unit
LiterL1 L = 1,000 mL
MillilitermLBase unit
2.5 g → mg: Move decimal 3 places RIGHT = 2500 mg
500 mcg → mg: Move decimal 3 places LEFT = 0.5 mg

⚖️ Weight Conversions

Key Formula: 1 kg = 2.2 lbs

Pounds to Kilograms: Divide by 2.2
154 lbs ÷ 2.2 = 70 kg

Kilograms to Pounds: Multiply by 2.2
70 kg × 2.2 = 154 lbs
🏥 Clinical Tip

Weight-based dosing (mg/kg) requires accurate kg conversion. Always double-check your conversion before calculating doses!
