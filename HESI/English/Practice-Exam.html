💡 Success Strategies

⏱️ Time Management

  • Use the 60-second rule — spend no more than 1 minute per question
  • Flag difficult questions and return to them
  • Don't leave any questions blank

📖 Reading Strategy

  • Read aloud mentally — errors often "sound wrong"
  • Identify the core subject and verb first
  • Watch for shifts in tense or number

🎯 Elimination Technique

  • Eliminate obviously wrong answers first
  • Look for common grammar traps
  • When stuck, choose the simplest option

🏥 Clinical Focus

  • Think about how nurses document
  • Clarity and precision matter in healthcare
  • Ambiguous pronouns can cause medical errors

⚠️ Common Pitfalls to Avoid

Affect vs. Effect

The medication had an affect

The medication had an effect

Affect = verb, Effect = noun (usually)

Who vs. Whom

The patient who I assessed

The patient whom I assessed

Whom = object of verb/preposition

Its vs. It's

The hospital changed it's policy

The hospital changed its policy

Its = possessive, It's = it is

Comma Splices

The patient arrived, he was stable

The patient arrived; he was stable

Use semicolon or conjunction
