🎯 The 8 Parts of Speech on the HESI

You'll be tested on your ability to:

  • Identify each part of speech in context
  • Understand the function of each word in a sentence
  • Choose the correct word form (e.g., adjective vs. adverb)
  • Recognize errors in part of speech usage

📖 Grammar Reference Vault

Master these eight parts of speech—the building blocks of every sentence.

📦 Nouns

A person, place, thing, or idea

Examples: nurse, hospital, medication, compassion
HESI Tip: Look for words that can follow "the" or "a/an" — they're usually nouns.

👤 Pronouns

Words that replace nouns

Examples: he, she, it, they, who, which, this
HESI Tip: Watch for pronoun-antecedent agreement (the pronoun must match what it replaces).

🏃 Verbs

Action words or state of being

Examples: administer, monitor, is, were, has been
HESI Tip: Verbs change form based on tense. Know regular (-ed) vs. irregular forms.

🎨 Adjectives

Words that describe nouns

Examples: careful, sterile, acute, chronic
HESI Tip: Adjectives answer: Which one? What kind? How many?

⚡ Adverbs

Words that modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs

Examples: carefully, quickly, very, extremely
HESI Tip: Many (not all!) adverbs end in -ly. They answer: How? When? Where? To what extent?

🔗 Prepositions

Words showing relationships between nouns

Examples: in, on, at, by, with, under, between
HESI Tip: Prepositions start phrases: "in the room," "by the bed," "with the patient"

🤝 Conjunctions

Words that connect words, phrases, or clauses

Examples: and, but, or, because, although, however
HESI Tip: FANBOYS = For, And, Nor, But, Or, Yet, So (coordinating conjunctions)

😮 Interjections

Words expressing emotion

Examples: Oh! Wow! Ouch! Well, Alas
HESI Tip: Interjections are grammatically independent—they don't connect to other words.

⚠️ Commonly Confused Words

Affect vs. Effect

affect verb (to influence)
effect noun (a result)

"The medication will affect the patient. The effect was immediate."

Good vs. Well

good adjective
well adverb

"She is a good nurse. She performs well under pressure."

Their / There / They're

their possessive pronoun
there adverb (location)
they're contraction (they are)

Its vs. It's

its possessive pronoun
it's contraction (it is)

"The hospital updated its policy. It's effective immediately."

🏥 Nursing Context Spotlight

See why grammar matters in professional healthcare communication.

📋 Documentation Matters

In nursing, precise grammar isn't just about passing tests—it's about patient safety. Ambiguous documentation can lead to medication errors, miscommunication between providers, and even legal issues. Compare these examples:

❌ Poor Grammar

"Patient seen by doctor they said medication working good."

Problems: Unclear pronoun reference ("they"), incorrect adverb ("good" should be "well"), missing punctuation.

✓ Correct Grammar

"Patient was seen by Dr. Smith, who reported that the medication is working well."

Clear: Specific noun, correct pronoun, proper adverb, complete sentence.

💊 Medical Terminology & Parts of Speech

Medical suffixes often indicate the part of speech:

-itis, -osis, -emia

Usually NOUNS

arthritis, diagnosis, anemia

-ic, -al, -ous

Usually ADJECTIVES

cardiac, renal, infectious

-ly

Usually ADVERBS

orally, intravenously, rapidly

-ate, -ize

Usually VERBS

medicate, stabilize, irrigate
