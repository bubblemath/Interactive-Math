🔤 FANBOYS — Coordinating Conjunctions

Use these with a comma to join two independent clauses:

For And Nor But Or Yet So

Pattern: [Independent clause], FANBOYS [Independent clause]

🔤 AAAWWUBBIS — Subordinating Conjunctions

These create dependent clauses that cannot stand alone:

After Although As When While Until Because Before If Since

Pattern: AAAWWUBBIS [dependent clause], [independent clause]

📍 Comma Rules (High Frequency!) ▼

1. Introductory Elements

Use a comma after introductory words, phrases, or clauses.

✓ After administering the medication, the nurse documented the response.

✗ After administering the medication the nurse documented the response.

2. Compound Sentences (FANBOYS)

Use a comma before coordinating conjunctions joining independent clauses.

✓ The patient's vitals were stable, but the physician ordered monitoring.

✗ The patient's vitals were stable but the physician ordered monitoring.

3. Serial (Oxford) Comma

Use commas to separate three or more items in a series.

✓ The nurse assessed pain, administered medication, and documented the intervention.

4. Nonessential Information

Use commas to set off nonrestrictive clauses and appositives.

✓ Dr. Smith, who specializes in cardiology, reviewed the ECG.

5. ⚠️ Comma Splice (ERROR!)

Do NOT use a comma alone to join two independent clauses.

✗ The patient was discharged, she will follow up in two weeks.

✓ The patient was discharged; she will follow up in two weeks.
✓ The patient was discharged, and she will follow up in two weeks.
📍 Semicolon & Colon Rules ▼

Semicolon: Joining Independent Clauses

Use a semicolon to join two closely related independent clauses without a conjunction.

✓ The patient's fever spiked at 0300; the nurse notified the physician immediately.

Semicolon: With Conjunctive Adverbs

Use semicolon before words like however, therefore, moreover, consequently.

✓ The wound showed infection; therefore, the physician ordered antibiotics.

✗ The wound showed infection, therefore, the physician ordered antibiotics.

Colon: Introducing Lists

Use a colon after a complete sentence to introduce a list.

✓ The patient exhibited three symptoms: fever, chills, and fatigue.

✗ The symptoms include: fever, chills, and fatigue.
📍 Apostrophe Rules ▼

Its vs. It's (HESI Favorite!)

Its = possessive (no apostrophe) | It's = it is / it has

✓ The hospital updated its visitation policy. (possessive)
✓ It's important to verify allergies. (it is)

Singular Possession

Add 's to singular nouns to show possession.

✓ The patient's chart indicated a history of diabetes.

Plural Possession

Add only an apostrophe to plural nouns ending in s.

✓ The nurses' station was recently renovated.
✓ The children's ward has new equipment. (irregular plural)
📍 Sentence Types & Errors ▼

Simple Sentence

One independent clause with a subject and verb.

✓ The nurse administered the medication.

Compound Sentence

Two independent clauses joined by comma + FANBOYS or semicolon.

✓ The patient rested, and the nurse monitored vitals.

Complex Sentence

One independent clause + one or more dependent clauses.

✓ Because the patient was unstable, the nurse called the physician.

⚠️ Fragment (ERROR!)

An incomplete sentence missing a subject, verb, or complete thought.

✗ Because the patient was experiencing shortness of breath.

✓ Because the patient was experiencing shortness of breath, the nurse administered oxygen.

⚠️ Run-on / Fused Sentence (ERROR!)

Two independent clauses with no punctuation between them.

✗ The patient was stable the physician ordered discharge.

✓ The patient was stable; the physician ordered discharge.
