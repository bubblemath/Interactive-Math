📋 Ratio & Proportion Quick Reference

Use this section as a reference while practicing. Master these concepts for HESI success!

📐 What is a Proportion?

A proportion states that two ratios are equal. If a/b = c/d, then a × d = b × c (cross-multiplication).

Example: If 2/3 = x/12

Cross-multiply: 2 × 12 = 3 × x
24 = 3x
x = 8

🔄 Setting Up Proportions - The Golden Rule

UNITS MUST MATCH! Keep the same units on top and the same units on bottom.

Correct Setup:
mg/mL = mg/mL ✅

Incorrect Setup:
mg/mL = mL/mg ❌
⚠️ Common Mistake

Inverting one side of the proportion. Always check that your units align before solving!

✖️ Cross-Multiplication Method

The most reliable method for solving proportions:

Steps:
1. Set up proportion with matching units
2. Cross-multiply (multiply diagonals)
3. Divide to isolate the unknown

Example: 100 mg/5 mL = 250 mg/x mL
100 × x = 5 × 250
100x = 1250
x = 12.5 mL

⚖️ Weight-Based Dosing Formula

Dose = Weight (kg) × Dose per kg

Example:
Order: 15 mg/kg
Patient weight: 70 kg

Dose = 70 kg × 15 mg/kg = 1050 mg
🏥 Clinical Tip

Always convert pounds to kilograms first! 1 kg = 2.2 lbs. Divide weight in lbs by 2.2.

💧 IV Flow Rate Formulas

mL/hour = Total Volume (mL) ÷ Time (hours)

gtt/min = (Volume × Drop Factor) ÷ (Time in minutes)

Example 1: Infuse 1000 mL over 8 hours
mL/hr = 1000 ÷ 8 = 125 mL/hr

Example 2: 125 mL/hr with 15 gtt/mL tubing
gtt/min = (125 × 15) ÷ 60 = 31.25 ≈ 31 gtt/min

⚗️ Solution Dilution Ratios

Common nursing dilution ratios and their meanings:

Ratio Meaning Concentration
1:100 1 part per 100 parts total 1%
1:1000 1 part per 1000 parts total 0.1%
1:10,000 1 part per 10,000 parts total 0.01%

🔢 Nursing Rounding Rules

  • Tablets: Round to nearest half tablet (0.5) if scored; otherwise, whole tablets only
  • Liquid medications: Round to nearest tenth (0.1 mL)
  • IV rates: Round to nearest whole number for gtt/min
  • When in doubt: Round DOWN for safety (less medication is safer than more)
