🏥 Why Percentages Matter in Nursing

Every IV solution you hang has a percentage concentration. D5W means 5% dextrose—that's 5 grams per 100 mL. Understanding percentages helps you verify orders, calculate solute amounts, and ensure patient safety.

📋 Percentage Rules Quick Reference

Use this section as a reference while practicing. Master these concepts for HESI success!

🔄 The "Is/Of" Proportion Method

The foundation for ALL percentage problems:

Part/Whole = Percent/100

Or written as: IS/OF = %/100

Example: What is 25% of 80?
x/80 = 25/100
100x = 80 × 25 = 2000
x = 20

🔢 Converting Percentages

Percent → Decimal: Divide by 100 (move decimal 2 places LEFT)

Decimal → Percent: Multiply by 100 (move decimal 2 places RIGHT)

Fraction → Percent: Divide numerator by denominator, then × 100

45% → 0.45 (move decimal left)
0.125 → 12.5% (move decimal right)
3/8 → 0.375 → 37.5%
⚠️ Common Mistake: Decimal Placement

Moving the decimal the wrong direction! Remember: Percent to decimal = SMALLER number (divide). Decimal to percent = BIGGER number (multiply).

📈 Percent Change Formula

% Change = [(New - Original) / Original] × 100

Patient weight: 180 lbs → 171 lbs
Change = 171 - 180 = -9
% Change = (-9 / 180) × 100 = -5%
5% decrease
🏥 Clinical Application

A weight loss of more than 5% in a week may indicate fluid imbalance or nutritional concerns requiring intervention.

⚗️ Solution Concentration Guide

Percent concentration = (grams of solute / 100 mL solution) × 100

Or simply: X% = X grams per 100 mL

Solution Concentration Meaning
D5W5%5 g dextrose per 100 mL
D10W10%10 g dextrose per 100 mL
NS (Normal Saline)0.9%0.9 g NaCl per 100 mL
Half NS0.45%0.45 g NaCl per 100 mL
D5NS5% + 0.9%5 g dextrose + 0.9 g NaCl per 100 mL

🎯 Three Types of Percent Problems

  1. Find the Part: What is 15% of 200? → 0.15 × 200 = 30
  2. Find the Percent: 45 is what percent of 180? → (45/180) × 100 = 25%
  3. Find the Whole: 36 is 20% of what? → 36 ÷ 0.20 = 180
