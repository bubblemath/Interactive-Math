  • Sterilization: How autoclaves use pressure and temperature
  • IV Fluids: Understanding solutions and concentrations

📊 States of Matter Topics on HESI

  • Properties of States: Solid, liquid, gas characteristics

  • Oxygen Tanks: Pressure increases when heated (Gay-Lussac's Law)
  • Anesthesia: Gas delivery depends on pressure-volume relationships
  • Hyperbaric Chambers: Increased pressure helps dissolve more O₂ in blood
  • Autoclaves: High pressure raises boiling point for sterilization

🔥 Endothermic vs. Exothermic

  • 🧊→💧 Melting: Solid to Liquid
  • 💧→💨 Vaporization: Liquid to Gas
  • 🧊→💨 Sublimation: Solid to Gas
  • 💧→🧊 Freezing: Liquid to Solid
  • 💨→💧 Condensation: Gas to Liquid
  • 💨→🧊 Deposition: Gas to Solid

🔬 Kinetic Molecular Theory (KMT)

The KMT explains how particles behave in different states:

  1. All matter is made of tiny particles (atoms or molecules)
  2. Particles are in constant motion
  3. Higher temperature = faster particle motion
  4. Particles collide elastically (no energy lost)
  5. Gas particles have negligible volume compared to container

Common Pitfall: Evaporation vs. Boiling

Evaporation: Occurs at ANY temperature, only at the surface

Boiling: Occurs at a SPECIFIC temperature (boiling point), throughout the liquid

Both are vaporization, but they happen differently!

Why It Matters

Understanding particle behavior helps explain:

  • Why ice floats (particles spread out when water freezes)
  • Why gases are compressible (lots of space between particles)
  • Why heating increases pressure in a closed container

🎈 Gas Laws Explorer

📊 Boyle's Law

At constant temperature, pressure and volume are inversely proportional

P₁V₁ = P₂V₂
↑ Pressure = ↓ Volume
↓ Pressure = ↑ Volume

Memory Tip: "Boyle's squeezes" — squeeze the volume, pressure goes up!

📊 Charles's Law

At constant pressure, volume and temperature are directly proportional

V₁/T₁ = V₂/T₂
↑ Temperature = ↑ Volume
↓ Temperature = ↓ Volume

Memory Tip: "Charles likes it hot" — heat it up, volume expands!

📊 Gay-Lussac's Law

At constant volume, pressure and temperature are directly proportional

P₁/T₁ = P₂/T₂
↑ Temperature = ↑ Pressure
↓ Temperature = ↓ Pressure

Memory Tip: "Gay-Lussac's pressure cooker" — heat a sealed container, pressure rises!

📊 Combined Gas Law

Combines all three laws into one equation

(P₁V₁)/T₁ = (P₂V₂)/T₂

Use this when multiple variables change between two conditions.

Common Pitfall: Forgetting to Convert to Kelvin!

Gas law calculations MUST use Kelvin for temperature!

K = °C + 273

Using Celsius will give you wrong answers. Always convert first!

Clinical Connection: Oxygen Tank Safety

A nurse moves an oxygen tank from a cold storage room (15°C) to a warm patient room (25°C).

What happens? According to Gay-Lussac's Law, the pressure inside the tank INCREASES because temperature increased while volume stayed constant.

This is why oxygen tanks have pressure relief valves!

STP = Standard Temperature and Pressure

Pressure: 1 atm (760 mmHg)
