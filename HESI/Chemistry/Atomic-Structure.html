⚠️ Common Pitfalls to Avoid

  • Confusing Atomic Number with Mass Number - Atomic # = protons only; Mass # = protons + neutrons
  • Forgetting neutral atoms have equal protons and electrons - Only ions have different numbers
  • Mixing up cations and anions - Cations LOSE electrons (+), Anions GAIN electrons (-)
  • Isotopes change neutrons, NOT protons - Changing protons changes the element!

🏥 Why It Matters in Nursing

Understanding atomic structure helps nurses comprehend electrolyte imbalances (Na⁺, K⁺, Cl⁻, Ca²⁺), medication mechanisms, and diagnostic imaging. Electrolytes are ions, and their charges affect how they move across cell membranes and influence patient health.

🔢 Key Formulas to Remember

Atomic Number = Number of Protons = Number of Electrons (in neutral atom)
Mass Number = Protons + Neutrons
Neutrons = Mass Number - Atomic Number

⚡ Ions vs. Isotopes

🔴 Cations (Positive Ions)

  • Formed by LOSING electrons
  • More protons than electrons
  • Written with + superscript (Na⁺, Ca²⁺)
  • Metals typically form cations
  • Example: Na → Na⁺ + e⁻

🔵 Anions (Negative Ions)

  • Formed by GAINING electrons
  • More electrons than protons
  • Written with - superscript (Cl⁻, O²⁻)
  • Nonmetals typically form anions
  • Example: Cl + e⁻ → Cl⁻

🔄 Isotopes

  • Same element, different neutrons
  • Same atomic number
  • Different mass numbers
  • Example: Carbon-12 vs Carbon-14
  • Written as: ¹²C or C-12

🔵 Electron Shell Rules (Bohr Model)

The 2-8-8 Rule for First 20 Elements

  • Shell 1 (K shell): Maximum 2 electrons
  • Shell 2 (L shell): Maximum 8 electrons
  • Shell 3 (M shell): Maximum 8 electrons (for first 20 elements)
  • Valence electrons: Electrons in the outermost shell
  • Octet Rule: Atoms are most stable with 8 valence electrons

🏥 Clinical Connection: Electrolytes as Ions

Common electrolytes in patient care are all ions:

  • Na⁺ (Sodium): Lost 1 electron, critical for fluid balance
  • K⁺ (Potassium): Lost 1 electron, essential for heart function
  • Cl⁻ (Chloride): Gained 1 electron, works with sodium
  • Ca²⁺ (Calcium): Lost 2 electrons, needed for bones and muscles
