🎯 Tips for HESI Chemistry Success

⏱️
Watch the Clock
With only 60 seconds per question, don't get stuck on complex calculations. Mark difficult questions and return to them if time permits. Trust your first instinct on straightforward questions.
🏥
Think Clinically
Connect chemistry concepts to nursing practice. Understanding why electrolytes (Na⁺, K⁺, Ca²⁺) matter and how blood pH affects patients will help you remember key concepts.
🧠
Use Memory Tricks
Mnemonics like "OIL RIG" (Oxidation Is Loss, Reduction Is Gain) and "B-A-D" (Bases Accept, Acids Donate protons) can help you quickly recall important concepts.
📖
Know Your Formulas
Memorize key formulas: pH = -log[H⁺], the relationship between pH and pOH (pH + pOH = 14), and basic gas law relationships. These appear frequently on the exam.
🔄
Practice Actively
Don't just read—interact with the material. Use the practice problems in each module and take the full practice exam under timed conditions to simulate test day.
✅
Eliminate Wrong Answers
If you're unsure, eliminate obviously incorrect options first. Even narrowing down to two choices significantly improves your odds. Look for extreme words that often signal wrong answers.
⚠️ Common Pitfalls to Avoid
  • Atomic Number vs. Atomic Mass: The atomic number (protons) is always the smaller whole number; atomic mass is the decimal representing the weighted average of isotopes.
  • pH Scale Confusion: Remember the inverse relationship—lower pH means MORE acidic (higher H⁺ concentration), not less.
  • Ion Charges: Don't confuse cations (positive, lost electrons) with anions (negative, gained electrons). Metals form cations; nonmetals form anions.
  • Balancing Equations: Always check that atoms are balanced on both sides. Coefficients change amounts; subscripts change the compound itself.
