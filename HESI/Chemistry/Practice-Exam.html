📖 Chemistry Reference Vault

⚗️ Basic Formulas

Molarity (M) = moles of solute / liters of solution
Dilution: M₁V₁ = M₂V₂
Moles = mass (g) / molar mass (g/mol)
Density = mass / volume

⚖️ Stoichiometry

Percent yield = (actual yield / theoretical yield) × 100
Avogadro's number = 6.022 × 10²³
1 mole = 6.022 × 10²³ particles

🔬 Acids & Bases

pH = -log[H⁺]
pOH = -log[OH⁻]
pH + pOH = 14 (at 25°C)
[H⁺][OH⁻] = 1 × 10⁻¹⁴ (Kw)

🌡️ Gas Laws

PV = nRT (Ideal Gas Law)
P₁V₁/T₁ = P₂V₂/T₂ (Combined Gas Law)
R = 0.0821 L·atm/(mol·K)
STP: 0°C (273 K), 1 atm

⚡ Electrochemistry

Oxidation = loss of electrons (OIL)
Reduction = gain of electrons (RIG)

📊 Concentration Units

% (w/v) = (g solute / mL solution) × 100
% (v/v) = (mL solute / mL solution) × 100
ppm = mg/L (for dilute aqueous solutions)
mEq/L = (mg/L × valence) / atomic weight

🩺 Clinical Reference Values

Blood pH: 7.35 - 7.45
Serum Na⁺: 136-145 mEq/L
Serum K⁺: 3.5-5.0 mEq/L
Serum Ca²⁺: 8.5-10.5 mg/dL
Blood glucose (fasting): 70-100 mg/dL
