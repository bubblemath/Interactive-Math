✖️ The Cross-Multiply & Divide Method

Step-by-Step Process

1
Set up the proportion: Make sure units match on each side
miles hours = miles hours
2
Cross-multiply: Multiply diagonally
a b = c d → a × d = b × c
3
Solve for the unknown: Divide both sides
x = (known product) ÷ (number with x)

📊 Direct vs. Inverse Variation

Feature Direct Variation Inverse Variation
Formula y = kx xy = k (or y = k/x)
Relationship As x increases, y increases As x increases, y decreases
Constant k = y/x (ratio is constant) k = xy (product is constant)
Example More hours worked = more pay More workers = less time per person
Graph Straight line through origin Curved (hyperbola)

⚡ Unit Rate Cheat Sheet

Unit Rate = Total Amount ÷ Number of Units
Price per item:
$12 for 4 items = $3 each
Speed (mph):
150 miles in 3 hours = 50 mph
Rate of work:
20 pages in 4 hours = 5 pages/hr

🗺️ Scale Drawings & Maps

Map Distance Actual Distance = Scale 1

Example: If 1 inch = 50 miles, and two cities are 3 inches apart on the map, the actual distance is 3 × 50 = 150 miles.

⭐ Level 1: Basic Proportions & Unit Rates

Example 1.1: Basic Proportion Setup

▼
📝 Problem

If 3 notebooks cost $7.50, how much would 5 notebooks cost?

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution
1

Set up the proportion: Keep units aligned (notebooks over dollars)

3 notebooks$7.50 = 5 notebooks$x
2

Cross-multiply:

3 × x = 7.50 × 5
3x = 37.50
3

Solve for x:

x = 37.50 ÷ 3 = 12.50
Answer: $12.50

Example 1.2: Finding a Unit Rate

▼
📝 Problem

A car travels 180 miles in 3 hours. What is the car's speed in miles per hour?

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution
1

Identify what we need: Miles PER hour means miles ÷ hours

2

Calculate the unit rate:

Speed = 180 miles ÷ 3 hours = 60 mph
Answer: 60 miles per hour

Example 1.3: Direct Variation (Basic)

▼
📝 Problem Direct Variation

If y varies directly with x, and y = 12 when x = 4, find y when x = 7.

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution
1

Find the constant k: In direct variation, k = y/x

k = 12 ÷ 4 = 3
2

Use the formula y = kx:

y = 3 × 7 = 21
Answer: y = 21
⭐⭐ Level 2: Multi-Step Applications

Example 2.1: Recipe Scaling

▼
📝 Problem

A recipe for 4 servings requires 2½ cups of flour. How much flour is needed for 10 servings?

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution
1

Convert mixed number: 2½ = 2.5 cups

2

Set up the proportion:

4 servings2.5 cups = 10 servingsx cups
3

Cross-multiply and solve:

4x = 2.5 × 10 = 25
x = 25 ÷ 4 = 6.25 cups
Answer: 6.25 cups (or 6¼ cups)

Example 2.2: Scale Drawing

▼
📝 Problem

On a map, 1 inch represents 25 miles. If two cities are 3.5 inches apart on the map, what is the actual distance between them?

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution
1

Set up the proportion:

1 inch25 miles = 3.5 inchesx miles
2

Cross-multiply:

1 × x = 25 × 3.5
x = 87.5 miles
Answer: 87.5 miles

Example 2.3: Inverse Variation (Basic)

▼
📝 Problem Inverse Variation

If y varies inversely with x, and y = 6 when x = 4, find y when x = 8.

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution
1

Find the constant k: In inverse variation, k = x × y

k = 4 × 6 = 24
2

Use the formula xy = k:

8 × y = 24
y = 24 ÷ 8 = 3
3

Verify: Notice that when x doubled (4→8), y halved (6→3). This confirms inverse variation!

Answer: y = 3
⭐⭐⭐ Level 3: Complex Word Problems

Example 3.1: Work Rate Problem (Inverse)

▼
📝 Problem Inverse Variation

If 4 workers can complete a project in 15 days, how many days would it take 6 workers to complete the same project?

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution
1

Identify the relationship: More workers = less time. This is INVERSE variation.

2

Find the constant k:

k = workers × days = 4 × 15 = 60
3

Solve for the new time:

6 × days = 60
days = 60 ÷ 6 = 10
Answer: 10 days

Example 3.2: Currency Exchange

▼
📝 Problem Direct Variation

If $45 USD can be exchanged for €40.50 EUR, how many euros would you receive for $120 USD?

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution
1

Set up the proportion:

$45€40.50 = $120€x
2

Cross-multiply:

45x = 40.50 × 120
45x = 4860
3

Solve:

x = 4860 ÷ 45 = 108
Answer: €108 EUR

Example 3.3: Combined Rate Problem

▼
📝 Problem

A store sells pens in packs. A pack of 12 pens costs $8.40. At this rate, what would be the cost of 30 pens?

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution
1

Method 1 - Unit Rate: Find cost per pen first

$8.40 ÷ 12 = $0.70 per pen
30 × $0.70 = $21.00
2

Method 2 - Proportion:

12$8.40 = 30$x
12x = 8.40 × 30 = 252
x = 252 ÷ 12 = $21.00
Answer: $21.00

📝 Calculator Tips for Proportions

  • Cross-multiply first: Calculate the products before dividing
  • Check your answer: Plug it back into the original proportion
  • Unit rates: Many can be done mentally (divide by small numbers)
  • Inverse variation: Remember to multiply, not divide!
