🎯 What is Quantitative Reasoning?

The Quantitative Reasoning section tests your ability to apply mathematical concepts to real-world situations. It covers:

  • Fractions, Decimals & Percents — Converting, calculating, and applying
  • Ratios & Proportions — Setting up and solving proportional relationships
  • Unit Conversions — Metric, customary, and time conversions
  • Linear Applications — Real-world problems using y = mx + b

🏆 Success Strategies

🧠 Know When to Use Mental Math

Many TSIA2 questions are designed to be solved quickly without a calculator. Look for:

  • Nice numbers (10, 25, 50, 100)
  • Common fractions (1/2, 1/4, 3/4)
  • Percent shortcuts (10%, 25%, 50%)

🧮 Use the Calculator Wisely

A calculator appears only on certain questions. When it does:

  • Double-check your entries
  • Estimate first to verify reasonableness
  • Don't over-rely on it for simple math

📐 Set Up Before Solving

For word problems, always:

  • Identify what you're solving for
  • Write out the equation or proportion
  • Check units match on both sides

⏰ Pace Yourself

Even though the test is untimed:

  • Aim for ~2 minutes per question
  • Don't get stuck — mark and move on
  • Review flagged questions at the end

⚠️ Common Pitfalls to Avoid

  • Misreading the question — Read carefully what they're asking for
  • Unit confusion — Make sure your answer is in the requested units
  • Calculation errors — Double-check arithmetic, especially with decimals
  • Proportion setup — Keep consistent units on each side
