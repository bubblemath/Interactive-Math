🎯 What is Quantitative Reasoning?

Quantitative Reasoning on the TSIA2 includes:

  • Ratios, proportions, and percentages
  • Identifying and interpreting linear equations ← This module!
  • Real-world problem solving with numbers

🔍 The "Linear Lens" Approach

When you see a word problem, put on your "Linear Lens" and ask:

📈
What's changing?
This is your slope (m)
🏁
Where does it start?
This is your y-intercept (b)

📚 Linear Reference Vault

🔑 The Slope-Intercept Form

y = mx + b
m = slope
Rate of change
"How much y changes for each unit of x"
b = y-intercept
Starting value
"The value of y when x = 0"

📐 Slope Formula

m = (y₂ - y₁) / (x₂ - x₁)

Rise over Run = Change in y ÷ Change in x

🗝️ Keyword Translation Matrix

These words in problems translate to parts of your equation:

Word/Phrase Math Meaning Example
"per", "each", "every" Slope (m) - rate $5 per hour → m = 5
"starts at", "initial", "base" Y-intercept (b) Starts at $20 → b = 20
"plus", "additional" Adding to base $20 plus $5/hr
"total", "cost", "amount" y (output) Total cost = y
"hours", "miles", "items" x (input) Number of hours = x
"increases by", "grows" Positive slope (+m) Increases by $3 each week
"decreases by", "loses" Negative slope (-m) Decreases by 2% per year

⚠️ Common Pitfall: Confusing Slope and Intercept

Problem: "A gym charges $50 to join and $30 per month."

Wrong: y = 50x + 30 ❌

Right: y = 30x + 50 ✅

The "per month" rate is the slope, the one-time "join" fee is the intercept!

📝 Worked Examples

📚 College Life Example 1: Textbook Rental

Problem: A campus bookstore rents textbooks for a $10 deposit plus $5 per week. Write an equation for the total cost (C) after w weeks.

1 Identify the parts:
• What's the starting cost? → $10 deposit (this is b)
• What changes per week? → $5 per week (this is m)
2 Set up the equation:
Using y = mx + b format:
C = 5w + 10
3 Verify with a test value:
After 3 weeks: C = 5(3) + 10 = 15 + 10 = $25 ✓
💡 Key Insight: "Per week" signals the slope, "deposit" signals the y-intercept!
💼 Part-Time Job Example 2: Interpreting a Graph

Problem: A graph shows a student's earnings. The line passes through (0, 50) and (8, 170). What does the slope represent, and what is the equation?

1 Find the slope:
m = (y₂ - y₁) / (x₂ - x₁)
m = (170 - 50) / (8 - 0) = 120 / 8 = 15
2 Identify the y-intercept:
The point (0, 50) tells us b = 50
3 Write the equation:
y = 15x + 50
4 Interpret:
• Slope (15) = The student earns $15 per hour
• Y-intercept (50) = The student started with $50 (maybe a signing bonus?)

⚠️ Watch Out!

Don't confuse which point is (x₁, y₁) vs (x₂, y₂). As long as you're consistent, you'll get the same slope!

📱 Phone Plan Example 3: Comparing Two Linear Models

Problem: Plan A costs $30/month plus $0.10 per text. Plan B costs $45/month with unlimited texts. After how many texts is Plan A more expensive than Plan B?

1 Write equations for both plans:
Plan A: y = 0.10x + 30
Plan B: y = 45 (constant, no slope)
2 Find where they're equal:
0.10x + 30 = 45
0.10x = 15
x = 150 texts
3 Interpret:
At 150 texts, both plans cost $45.
After 150 texts, Plan A becomes more expensive.
4 Verify:
At 200 texts:
• Plan A: 0.10(200) + 30 = $50
• Plan B: $45
Plan A is indeed more expensive! ✓
💡 TSIA2 Strategy: When comparing plans, set the equations equal to find the "break-even" point!
