Find common denominator first!

Multiplying

Straight across!

Dividing

Keep-Change-Flip!

⭐ Level 1: Basic Computation

Example 1.1: Fraction to Decimal Conversion

▼
📝 Problem

Convert 34 to a decimal.

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution
1

Identify the operation: To convert a fraction to a decimal, divide the numerator by the denominator.

34 means 3 ÷ 4
2

Perform the division:

3 ÷ 4 = 0.75
3

Verify (optional): Check by converting back: 0.75 × 4 = 3 ✓

Answer: 0.75

Example 1.2: Decimal to Percent Conversion

▼
📝 Problem

Convert 0.85 to a percent.

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution
1

Remember the rule: To convert a decimal to a percent, multiply by 100 (or move the decimal point 2 places to the right).

2

Apply the conversion:

0.85 × 100 = 85
3

Add the percent symbol:

85%
Answer: 85%

Example 1.3: Adding Fractions (Same Denominator)

▼
📝 Problem

Calculate: 14 + 14

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution
1

Check denominators: Both fractions have the same denominator (4), so we can add directly.

2

Add the numerators, keep the denominator:

1 + 14 = 24
3

Simplify: Find the GCF of 2 and 4, which is 2. Divide both by 2.

2 ÷ 24 ÷ 2 = 12
Answer: 12
⭐⭐ Level 2: Intermediate Application

Example 2.1: Adding Fractions (Different Denominators)

▼
📝 Problem

Calculate: 23 + 12

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution
1

Find the LCD: The denominators are 3 and 2. The Least Common Denominator is 6.

2

Convert each fraction:

23 = 2 × 23 × 2 = 46

12 = 1 × 32 × 3 = 36
3

Add the fractions:

46 + 36 = 76
4

Convert to mixed number (optional):

76 = 1 16
Answer: 76 or 1 16

Example 2.2: Calculating a Tip (Percent of a Number)

▼
📝 Problem

Your meal costs $12.50 and you want to leave an 18% tip. How much should you tip?

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution
1

Set up the problem: We need to find 18% of $12.50

Tip = Meal Cost × (Percent ÷ 100)
2

Convert percent to decimal:

18% = 18 ÷ 100 = 0.18
3

Multiply:

$12.50 × 0.18 = $2.25
4

Quick check: 10% of $12.50 = $1.25, so 20% ≈ $2.50. Our answer of $2.25 (18%) makes sense! ✓

Answer: $2.25

Example 2.3: Percent Increase

▼
📝 Problem

Your grade went from 72 to 81 after extra credit. What was the percent increase?

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution
1

Find the change:

Change = New − Original = 81 − 72 = 9
2

Apply the percent change formula:

Percent Change = ChangeOriginal × 100
3

Calculate:

972 × 100 = 0.125 × 100 = 12.5%
Answer: 12.5% increase
⭐⭐⭐ Level 3: Complex Word Problems

Example 3.1: Finding Original Price from Sale Price

▼
📝 Problem

A laptop is on sale for $680 after a 15% discount. What was the original price?

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution
1

Understand the relationship: If there's a 15% discount, the sale price is 85% of the original (100% − 15% = 85%).

2

Set up the equation:

Sale Price = Original × 0.85
$680 = Original × 0.85
3

Solve for Original:

Original = $680 ÷ 0.85
Original = $800
4

Verify: $800 × 0.15 = $120 discount. $800 − $120 = $680 ✓

Answer: $800

Example 3.2: Weighted Average (Grade Calculation)

▼
📝 Problem

Your final grade is weighted: 40% exams, 35% homework, 25% participation. If you have 85% on exams, 92% on homework, and 78% on participation, what is your final grade?

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution
1

Set up the weighted average formula:

Final = (Score₁ × Weight₁) + (Score₂ × Weight₂) + (Score₃ × Weight₃)
2

Calculate each weighted component:

Exams: 85 × 0.40 = 34
Homework: 92 × 0.35 = 32.2
Participation: 78 × 0.25 = 19.5
3

Add the components:

34 + 32.2 + 19.5 = 85.7
Answer: 85.7% (approximately 86%)

Example 3.3: Mixed Number Addition

▼
📝 Problem

You ran 3 14 miles on Monday and 2 23 miles on Tuesday. How many total miles did you run?

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution
1

Convert to improper fractions:

3 14 = (3×4)+14 = 134

2 23 = (2×3)+23 = 83
2

Find LCD: LCD of 4 and 3 is 12

134 = 3912

83 = 3212
3

Add:

3912 + 3212 = 7112
4

Convert back to mixed number:

71 ÷ 12 = 5 remainder 11
7112 = 5 1112
Answer: 5 1112 miles
