🕐 Time Conversions: 12-Hour ↔ 24-Hour

The Rule:

  • AM hours (12:00 AM - 11:59 AM): Same as 24-hour (except 12:00 AM = 0000)
  • PM hours (12:00 PM - 11:59 PM): Add 12 to the hour (except 12:00 PM stays 1200)
  • 24-hour to 12-hour: If hour ≥ 13, subtract 12 and add PM

📏 Metric System: "King Henry" Staircase

Mnemonic: King Henry Died By Drinking Chocolate Milk

Kilo (k) = 1,000
← Move decimal LEFT going up
Hecto (h) = 100
Deca (da) = 10
BASE (m, L, g) = 1
Deci (d) = 0.1
Centi (c) = 0.01
Milli (m) = 0.001
→ Move decimal RIGHT going down

Each step = move decimal 1 place

🔗 Dimensional Analysis (Unit Cancellation)

The Method: Multiply by conversion fractions so unwanted units cancel.

5 feet × (12 inches / 1 foot) = 60 inches
feet cancels with feet

Key: Put the unit you want to cancel in the denominator!

⭐ Level 1: Basic Conversions

Example 1.1: 12-Hour to 24-Hour Time

▼
📝 Problem

Convert 3:45 PM to 24-hour (military) time.

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution
1

Identify AM or PM: This is PM (afternoon)

2

Apply the rule: For PM times (except 12 PM), add 12 to the hour

3 + 12 = 15
3

Write in 24-hour format: Keep the minutes the same

1545
Answer: 1545

Example 1.2: Metric Conversion (One Step)

▼
📝 Problem

Convert 2.5 kilometers to meters.

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution
1

Identify the conversion: Kilo to base unit (3 steps down the staircase)

2

Apply the rule: Moving DOWN = multiply (or move decimal RIGHT)

2.5 × 1,000 = 2,500
Answer: 2,500 meters

Example 1.3: Customary Conversion

▼
📝 Problem

Convert 36 inches to feet.

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution
1

Recall the conversion: 12 inches = 1 foot

2

Divide: Going from smaller to larger units

36 ÷ 12 = 3
Answer: 3 feet
⭐⭐ Level 2: Elapsed Time & Multi-Step

Example 2.1: Elapsed Time Calculation

▼
📝 Problem

A flight departs at 2145 and the flight duration is 4 hours 30 minutes. What time does it land?

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution
1

Add the hours:

21 + 4 = 25
2

Add the minutes:

45 + 30 = 75 minutes = 1 hour 15 minutes
3

Combine and adjust:

25 + 1 = 26 hours, 15 minutes
26 - 24 = 2 (next day)
Answer: 0215 (2:15 AM the next day)

Example 2.2: Time Duration Between Two Times

▼
📝 Problem

Your shift starts at 0730 and ends at 1615. How long is your shift?

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution
1

Subtract the hours:

16 - 7 = 9 hours
2

Subtract the minutes:

15 - 30 = -15 (need to borrow)
3

Borrow 1 hour (60 min):

8 hours and (60 + 15 - 30) = 8 hours 45 minutes
Answer: 8 hours 45 minutes

Example 2.3: Multi-Step Customary Conversion

▼
📝 Problem

Convert 2.5 gallons to cups.

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution
1

Gallons to quarts: 1 gallon = 4 quarts

2.5 × 4 = 10 quarts
2

Quarts to pints: 1 quart = 2 pints

10 × 2 = 20 pints
3

Pints to cups: 1 pint = 2 cups

20 × 2 = 40 cups
Answer: 40 cups
⭐⭐⭐ Level 3: Complex Applications

Example 3.1: Elapsed Time Across Midnight

▼
📝 Problem

A night shift worker starts at 10:30 PM and works for 9 hours 15 minutes. What time does the shift end?

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution
1

Convert to 24-hour: 10:30 PM = 2230

2

Add hours and minutes:

22:30 + 9:15 = 31:45
3

Subtract 24 for next day:

31 - 24 = 7, so 0745
4

Convert back to 12-hour: 0745 = 7:45 AM

Answer: 7:45 AM (the next morning)

Example 3.2: Dimensional Analysis Chain

▼
📝 Problem

A car travels at 60 miles per hour. How many feet does it travel in 1 minute?

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution
1

Set up the chain:

60 miles/hour × (5,280 feet/1 mile) × (1 hour/60 minutes)
2

Cancel units: miles cancels, hours cancels

3

Calculate:

60 × 5,280 ÷ 60 = 5,280 feet/minute
Answer: 5,280 feet per minute

Example 3.3: Real-World Application

▼
📝 Problem

A recipe calls for 750 mL of water. You only have a measuring cup marked in cups. How many cups do you need? (1 cup ≈ 240 mL)

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution
1

Set up the conversion:

750 mL × (1 cup / 240 mL)
2

Calculate:

750 ÷ 240 = 3.125 cups
3

Interpret: About 3⅛ cups or just over 3 cups

Answer: Approximately 3⅛ cups (3.125 cups)
