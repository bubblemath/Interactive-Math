📖 Lesson 19: Translations of Sine and Cosine

🌟 Why Does This Matter?

These functions appear throughout science and engineering:

  • 🔦 Optics: Snell's Law uses tangent for light refraction calculations
  • 🏗️ Architecture: Tangent calculates roof pitches, ramp angles, and shadow lengths
  • ✈️ Navigation: Bearing and heading calculations use tangent
  • 📡 Engineering: Secant appears in antenna design and signal processing
  • 🎸 Acoustics: Cotangent models standing wave patterns in musical instruments

🔄 Reciprocal Relationship Recall

csc θ

= 1/sin θ

sec θ

= 1/cos θ

cot θ

= 1/tan θ = cos θ/sin θ

tan θ = sin θ / cos θ

⚠️ Why Asymptotes Exist

Key Insight: When the denominator of a fraction equals zero, the function is undefined—this creates a vertical asymptote!

Tangent: tan θ = sin θ / cos θ

Undefined when cos θ = 0

Asymptotes at: θ = ±π/2, ±3π/2, ...

Cosecant: csc θ = 1 / sin θ

Undefined when sin θ = 0

Asymptotes at: θ = 0, ±π, ±2π, ...

📐 Tangent & Cotangent Lab

🌟 The Slope Connection

Key Insight: Tangent represents the slope of the terminal side of an angle on the unit circle!

This explains why:

  • tan(0) = 0 (horizontal line has slope 0)
  • tan(π/4) = 1 (45° line has slope 1)
  • tan(π/2) is undefined (vertical line has undefined slope)

Property Tangent Cotangent

💡 Memory Trick: The zeros of tangent become the asymptotes of cotangent, and vice versa! They're "opposites" in this sense.

👻 Secant & Cosecant Workshop

🌟 The Reciprocal Connection

Secant and cosecant are the reciprocals of cosine and sine:

  • sec θ = 1/cos θ — "flips" the cosine wave
  • csc θ = 1/sin θ — "flips" the sine wave

The "ghost" sine/cosine wave helps you see where asymptotes and U-shapes come from!

👻 "Ghost Wave" Reciprocal Builder

Why U-Shapes?

When sin θ or cos θ is small (near zero), its reciprocal is large!

1 ÷ 0.1 = 10  |  1 ÷ 0.01 = 100  |  1 ÷ 0 = UNDEFINED (asymptote!)
