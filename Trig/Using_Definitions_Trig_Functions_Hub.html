📋 Directions

The Pythagorean identities are derived from x² + y² = r². These powerful identities allow you to find one trig value from another. Study the algebraic proofs, then practice applying the identities.

📊 The Three Pythagorean Identities

Primary Identity
sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
Tangent Form
tan²θ + 1 = sec²θ
Cotangent Form
1 + cot²θ = csc²θ

📜 Algebraic Proof: Deriving the Pythagorean Identities

Proof of sin²θ + cos²θ = 1

Step 1: Start with the Pythagorean Theorem: x² + y² = r²
This relates the coordinates of point P(x, y) to its distance r from the origin.
Step 2: Divide both sides by r²: (x²/r²) + (y²/r²) = (r²/r²)
We divide every term by r² to create ratios.
Step 3: (x/r)² + (y/r)² = 1
Simplify each fraction.
Step 4: cos²θ + sin²θ = 1 ✓
Since cos θ = x/r and sin θ = y/r, we substitute to get the identity.

Proof of tan²θ + 1 = sec²θ

Step 1: Start with: x² + y² = r²
Step 2: Divide both sides by x²: (x²/x²) + (y²/x²) = (r²/x²)
Step 3: 1 + (y/x)² = (r/x)²
Step 4: 1 + tan²θ = sec²θ ✓
Since tan θ = y/x and sec θ = r/x.

Proof of 1 + cot²θ = csc²θ

Step 1: Start with: x² + y² = r²
Step 2: Divide both sides by y²: (x²/y²) + (y²/y²) = (r²/y²)
Step 3: (x/y)² + 1 = (r/y)²
Step 4: cot²θ + 1 = csc²θ ✓
Since cot θ = x/y and csc θ = r/y.

📋 Directions

The quotient identities express tangent and cotangent as ratios of sine and cosine. These identities are useful when you know sin θ and cos θ and need to find tan θ or cot θ.

➗ The Quotient Identities

Tangent
tan θ = sin θ / cos θ

= (y/r) ÷ (x/r) = y/x

Cotangent
cot θ = cos θ / sin θ

= (x/r) ÷ (y/r) = x/y

📜 Proof of tan θ = sin θ / cos θ

Step 1: sin θ / cos θ = (y/r) / (x/r)
Substitute the definitions of sin and cos.
Step 2: = (y/r) × (r/x)
Dividing by a fraction = multiplying by its reciprocal.
Step 3: = y/x = tan θ ✓
The r's cancel, leaving y/x which is the definition of tan θ.
