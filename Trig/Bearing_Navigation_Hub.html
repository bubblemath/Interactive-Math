📐 Key Prerequisite Skills

🔑 Right Triangle Solving

Given an angle and a side, use SOH CAH TOA to find missing sides:

  • sin θ = opposite / hypotenuse
  • cos θ = adjacent / hypotenuse
  • tan θ = opposite / adjacent

📏 Pythagorean Theorem

For navigation problems where two legs are perpendicular:

d = √(a² + b²)

This finds the straight-line distance when you know the two perpendicular distances traveled.

📍 Coordinate Bearing Lab

📐 Strategy for Finding Bearing from Coordinates

  1. Identify the quadrant based on signs of (x, y)
  2. Find the reference angle: θ = tan⁻¹(|x| / |y|) — note: we use |x|/|y| because bearing is measured from the y-axis (North/South)
  3. Convert to bearing based on quadrant:
    • Q1 (+x, +y): Method 1 = θ, Method 2 = N θ E
    • Q2 (−x, +y): Method 1 = 360° − θ, Method 2 = N θ W
    • Q3 (−x, −y): Method 1 = 180° + θ, Method 2 = S θ W
    • Q4 (+x, −y): Method 1 = 180° − θ, Method 2 = S θ E

🔑 Key Insight: Perpendicular Bearings

If two bearings differ by exactly 90°, the paths are perpendicular and form a right triangle. This allows you to use:

  • Pythagorean theorem: d = √(leg₁² + leg₂²) for the straight-line distance
  • Inverse tangent: θ = tan⁻¹(opposite/adjacent) for the bearing back to start
