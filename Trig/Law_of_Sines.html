🚧 The Right Triangle Wall

Why Can't We Use SOH CAH TOA?

SOH CAH TOA defines trig ratios using the right angle:

  • sin θ = opposite / hypotenuse
  • cos θ = adjacent / hypotenuse
  • tan θ = opposite / adjacent

Problem: An oblique triangle has NO right angle, so there's no hypotenuse! We need a different approach.

Solution: The Law of Sines relates sides to their opposite angles—no right angle required!

The Law of Sines states that in any triangle, the ratio of a side to the sine of its opposite angle is constant. Explore the derivation to understand why this works!

📐 The Law of Sines

For any triangle ABC with sides a, b, c opposite angles A, B, C:

a/sin A = b/sin B = c/sin C

Each side divided by the sine of its opposite angle gives the same value.

📝 Derivation of the Law of Sines

Step 1: Draw an Altitude
Start with any triangle ABC. Draw altitude h from vertex C perpendicular to side c (or its extension).
Step 2: Create Two Right Triangles
The altitude h creates two right triangles. In the left triangle, h is opposite angle A. In the right triangle, h is opposite angle B.
Step 3: Apply SOH CAH TOA to Each Right Triangle
Using sin = opposite/hypotenuse in each right triangle:
sin A = h/b → h = b·sin A
sin B = h/a → h = a·sin B
Step 4: Set the Two Expressions for h Equal
Since both expressions equal h:
b·sin A = a·sin B
Step 5: Divide Both Sides
Divide both sides by (sin A · sin B):
b/sin B = a/sin A
Step 6: Repeat for Side c
Drawing an altitude from A or B and repeating the process gives us the complete Law of Sines:
a/sin A = b/sin B = c/sin C

📋 When to Use the Law of Sines

SAA (Side-Angle-Angle)

Given: One side and two angles (the side is opposite one of the known angles)

Example: a = 10, A = 40°, B = 60°

ASA (Angle-Side-Angle)

Given: Two angles and the side between them

Example: A = 40°, c = 15, B = 60°

⚠️ Key Insight

The Law of Sines requires an angle-side pair (an angle and the side opposite to it). If you know A and a, you can find any other side if you know its opposite angle.

When you know two sides and the included angle (SAS), you can find the area using the formula: Area = ½ · b · c · sin A. This works for any triangle—no need for a right angle!

📏 Area of a Triangle (SAS Formula)

For triangle ABC with sides b and c and included angle A:

Area = ½ · b · c · sin A

The area is half the product of two sides times the sine of the included angle.

📐 Why Does This Work?

The standard area formula is Area = ½ · base · height.

If we use side c as the base, then height h = b · sin A (from the right triangle formed by the altitude).

Substituting: Area = ½ · c · (b · sin A) = ½ · b · c · sin A

Special case: If A = 90°, then sin 90° = 1, and the formula becomes Area = ½ · b · c (the standard right triangle formula).
