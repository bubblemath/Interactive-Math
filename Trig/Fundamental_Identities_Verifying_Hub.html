📚 Prerequisite Bridge

🔗 Review Previous Lesson

This lesson introduces verifying trigonometric identities—proving that two expressions are equivalent by transforming one side to match the other. This is fundamentally different from solving equations!

📖 Lesson 21: Inverse Trig Functions

🌟 Why Does This Matter?

Verifying identities is essential for:

  • 📐 Calculus: Simplifying integrals and derivatives of trig functions
  • 🌊 Physics: Simplifying wave equations and oscillation formulas
  • 📡 Engineering: Reducing complex expressions in signal processing
  • 🔧 Problem Solving: Converting expressions to more useful forms

⚠️ CRITICAL RULE: One Side Only!

When verifying an identity, you must NEVER perform operations on both sides simultaneously.

Transform ONE SIDE until it matches the other. This is NOT equation solving!

Reciprocal Identities

Quotient Identities

Pythagorean Identities

sin²θ + cos²θ = 1

1 + tan²θ = sec²θ

1 + cot²θ = csc²θ

🔄 Pythagorean Identity Transformer

See how sin²θ + cos²θ = 1 can be rearranged:

Original:

sin²θ + cos²θ = 1

Solve for sin²θ:

sin²θ = 1 − cos²θ

Solve for cos²θ:

cos²θ = 1 − sin²θ

💡 Pro Tip: These rearrangements are incredibly useful! When you see (1 − sin²θ), think cos²θ. When you see (1 − cos²θ), think sin²θ.

🧰 Strategy Workshop

🌟 Key Verification Strategies

These strategies will help you transform expressions efficiently:

1️⃣ Convert to Sine & Cosine

When stuck, rewrite everything in terms of sin and cos. This often reveals simplifications.

2️⃣ Factor When Possible

Look for common factors, difference of squares, or other factorable patterns.

3️⃣ Multiply by Conjugate

For (1 ± sin θ) or (1 ± cos θ), multiply by the conjugate to create Pythagorean forms.

4️⃣ Work the More Complex Side

Usually start with the side that has more terms or operations.

✖️ Conjugate Helper

The conjugate technique creates Pythagorean identities:

Expression with (1 − sin θ)

Multiply by: (1 + sin θ)/(1 + sin θ)

Result: (1 − sin²θ) = cos²θ

Expression with (1 + cos θ)

Multiply by: (1 − cos θ)/(1 − cos θ)

Result: (1 − cos²θ) = sin²θ

🎯 "Which Side to Work?" Advisor

For each identity, which side should you transform?

Identity: (sec²θ − 1) / sec²θ = sin²θ

Advice: Work the LEFT side — it has more operations (subtraction, division, and sec²θ can be expanded).

Identity: cos θ = sin θ · cot θ

Advice: Work the RIGHT side — it has a product that can be expanded using cot θ = cos θ/sin θ.

🎓 Learn by Example

Work through these examples step by step. Try to predict each step before revealing it!

Example 1: Using Pythagorean Identity

Verify: (sec²θ − 1) / sec²θ = sin²θ

1 (sec²θ − 1) / sec²θ Start with left side
2 tan²θ / sec²θ sec²θ − 1 = tan²θ (Pythagorean)
3 (sin²θ/cos²θ) / (1/cos²θ) Convert to sin/cos
4 sin²θ ✓ Simplify (cos²θ cancels)

Example 2: Converting to Sine/Cosine

Verify: sin θ · cot θ = cos θ

1 sin θ · cot θ Start with left side
2 sin θ · (cos θ / sin θ) cot θ = cos θ/sin θ
3 cos θ ✓ sin θ cancels

Example 3: More Complex Identity

Verify: tan θ + cot θ = sec θ · csc θ

1 tan θ + cot θ Start with left side
2 (sin θ/cos θ) + (cos θ/sin θ) Convert to sin/cos
3 (sin²θ + cos²θ) / (sin θ · cos θ) Common denominator
4 1 / (sin θ · cos θ) sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
5 sec θ · csc θ ✓ 1/cos θ = sec θ, 1/sin θ = csc θ
