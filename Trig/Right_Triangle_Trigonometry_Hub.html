🔗 Connecting the Two Definitions

Definition I (Lesson 3): sin θ = y/r, cos θ = x/r, tan θ = y/x

Definition II (This Lesson): sin A = Opposite/Hypotenuse, cos A = Adjacent/Hypotenuse, tan A = Opposite/Adjacent

In a right triangle placed in standard position, the "y" coordinate IS the opposite side, the "x" coordinate IS the adjacent side, and "r" IS the hypotenuse. Both definitions give identical values!

🔑 Key Insight: Similar Triangles

Try doubling both sides (6 and 8 instead of 3 and 4). Notice that the ratios stay the same! This is because all right triangles with the same acute angle are similar—their sides are proportional. This is why trig functions depend only on the angle, not the triangle's size.

Cofunctions are pairs of trig functions whose values are equal when their angles are complementary (sum to 90°). Explore why this works visually, then practice converting between cofunctions and solving cofunction equations algebraically.

🔄 The Cofunction Identities

🔑 Why Cofunctions Work

In a right triangle, angles A and B are complementary (A + B = 90°). The side opposite angle A is the same side that is adjacent to angle B. Therefore:

sin A = cos B   and   cos A = sin B

Solve for θ (assume acute angles):

sin(θ + 15°) = cos(2θ)

Step 1: Since sin and cos are cofunctions, set arguments to sum to 90°
Step 2: (θ + 15°) + (2θ) = 90°
Step 3: 3θ + 15° = 90°
Step 4: 3θ = 75°
Step 5: θ = 25°
Check: sin(25° + 15°) = sin 40° and cos(2·25°) = cos 50°. Since 40° + 50° = 90°, sin 40° = cos 50° ✓
