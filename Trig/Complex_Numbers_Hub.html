📖 Lesson 13: Applications of Vectors

🌟 Why Does This Matter?

Complex numbers aren't just abstract math—they're essential tools in science and engineering:

  • ⚡ Electrical Engineering: AC circuits are analyzed using complex numbers. The "imaginary" part represents the phase shift between voltage and current.
  • 🎵 Signal Processing: Audio engineers use complex numbers to analyze and manipulate sound waves, enabling noise cancellation and audio effects.
  • ⚛️ Quantum Mechanics: The fundamental equations of quantum physics require complex numbers—they're not optional!
  • 🌀 Fractal Geometry: The famous Mandelbrot set is generated entirely from complex number operations, creating infinitely detailed patterns.
  • ✈️ Control Systems: Autopilots and stability systems in aircraft use complex numbers to analyze system behavior.

💡 The Imaginary Unit Discovery Lab

🌟 Why Do We Need i?

Consider the equation x² = −1. Try to find a real number solution:

  • 1² = 1 ✗
  • (−1)² = 1 ✗
  • 0² = 0 ✗

No real number squared gives −1! Just as we invented negative numbers to solve x + 5 = 3, and fractions to solve 2x = 1, we invent i to solve x² = −1.

Definition of the Imaginary Unit

i = √(−1)

Therefore: i² = −1

Note: −i is also a square root of −1, since (−i)² = i² = −1

√ Simplifying Radicals Workshop

🌟 Why Does This Matter?

When solving quadratic equations, you'll often encounter square roots of negative numbers. Knowing how to simplify these is essential for:

  • Finding complex solutions to equations like x² + 4 = 0
  • Analyzing circuits where impedance involves √(−1)
  • Working with the quadratic formula when the discriminant is negative

Key Rule for Negative Radicands

√(−n) = √(−1) · √(n) = i√n

Always extract the i first, then simplify the remaining radical!

📐 Step-by-Step Simplifier

Enter a negative number to see how to simplify its square root:

Simplify √(−48):

Step 1: Separate the negative

√(−48) = √(−1) · √(48) = i√48

Step 2: Factor out perfect squares from 48

48 = 16 · 3 = 4² · 3

Step 3: Simplify

i√48 = i√(16·3) = i · 4√3 = 4i√3

√(−48) = 4i√3

⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ WRONG

√(−4) = −2

This confuses √(−4) with −√4

✓ CORRECT

√(−4) = √(−1)·√4 = i·2 = 2i

Always extract i first!

❌ WRONG

√(−2) · √(−8) = √16 = 4

Can't multiply negative radicands directly!

✓ CORRECT

√(−2)·√(−8) = (i√2)(i√8) = i²√16 = −4

Convert to i form first, then multiply!

✖️ Multiplication (FOIL)

Multiply Complex Numbers

Use FOIL, then replace i² with −1.

+ i
+ i

(2 + 3i)(4 − i)

FOIL:

F: 2 · 4 = 8

O: 2 · (−i) = −2i

I: 3i · 4 = 12i

L: 3i · (−i) = −3i² = −3(−1) = 3

= 8 − 2i + 12i + 3

= 11 + 10i

➗ Division (Conjugates)

Divide Complex Numbers

Multiply numerator and denominator by the conjugate of the denominator.

Complex Conjugate: The conjugate of a + bi is a − bi

Key property: (a + bi)(a − bi) = a² + b² (always real!)

+ i
+ i

(3 + 2i) ÷ (1 − 4i)

Step 1: Conjugate of (1 − 4i) is (1 + 4i)

Step 2: Multiply top and bottom by conjugate

= (3 + 2i)(1 + 4i) / (1 − 4i)(1 + 4i)

Step 3: Expand

Numerator: 3 + 12i + 2i + 8i² = 3 + 14i − 8 = −5 + 14i

Denominator: 1 + 16 = 17

= −5/17 + (14/17)i ≈ −0.29 + 0.82i
