🃏 Key Terminology Flashcards

Terminal Side
The ending ray of an angle in standard position, after rotation from the initial side (positive x-axis).
Click to flip
Reference Angle
The acute angle formed between the terminal side and the x-axis. Always positive and ≤ 90°.
Click to flip
r (radius)
The distance from the origin to a point P(x, y). Calculated as r = √(x² + y²). Always positive.
Click to flip
Quadrantal Angle
An angle whose terminal side lies on an axis (0°, 90°, 180°, 270°, 360°). Some trig functions are undefined at these angles.

Quadrantal angles have their terminal side on an axis. Explore why some trig functions are undefined at these angles (division by zero!) and practice identifying values quickly.

👁️ Quadrantal Angle Visualizer

Click an angle to see its terminal side and point

📍 Angle: 0° (0 radians)

Terminal Side Point: (1, 0)

x = 1, y = 0, r = 1

Since y = 0, any function with y in the denominator (cot θ, csc θ) is undefined.

📊 Quadrantal Angles Reference Table

🔑 Key Rules for Undefined Values

Terminal side on y-axis (90° or 270°):

• x = 0, so tan θ and sec θ are undefined (x in denominator)

Terminal side on x-axis (0° or 180°):

• y = 0, so cot θ and csc θ are undefined (y in denominator)
