🌟 Why Does This Matter?

Double-angle and half-angle identities are essential for:

  • 📊 Calculus: Integrating sin²x and cos²x using power-reducing formulas
  • 🌊 Physics: Wave interference, harmonic motion, and resonance
  • 📡 Engineering: Signal processing and frequency analysis
  • 🎵 Music: Overtones and harmonic frequencies
  • 📐 Geometry: Finding exact values for angles like 22.5° or 15°

📐 The Double-Angle Identities

sin(2θ) = 2 sin θ cos θ

tan(2θ) = 2 tan θ / (1 − tan²θ)

🎯 "Three-Way Cosine" Tool

cos(2θ) has THREE equivalent forms! Each is useful in different situations:

Form 1: Mixed

cos(2θ) = cos²θ − sin²θ

Use when: You have both sin θ and cos θ available

Form 2: Cosine Only

cos(2θ) = 2cos²θ − 1

Use when: You only know cos θ, or need to solve for cos θ

Form 3: Sine Only

cos(2θ) = 1 − 2sin²θ

Use when: You only know sin θ, or need to solve for sin θ

Quick Check: If you only know that sin θ = 3/5, which form of cos(2θ) should you use?

➗ Half-Angle "Sign Detective"

⚠️ The ± Challenge

Half-angle formulas have a ± sign. The formula gives the magnitude, but YOU must determine the correct sign based on which quadrant θ/2 is in!

📐 The Half-Angle Identities

sin(θ/2) = ±√[(1 − cos θ)/2]

cos(θ/2) = ±√[(1 + cos θ)/2]

tan(θ/2) = (1 − cos θ)/sin θ = sin θ/(1 + cos θ)

(Note: tan(θ/2) has alternate forms without ±)

🧮 Nested Radical Assistant

Half-angle problems often create "square roots within square roots." Let's simplify:

Example: Find cos(22.5°) = cos(45°/2)

1 Use cos(θ/2) = ±√[(1 + cos θ)/2] with θ = 45°
2 cos(22.5°) = +√[(1 + cos 45°)/2] = +√[(1 + √2/2)/2]

(+ because 22.5° is in Q1 where cosine is positive)

3 = √[(2 + √2)/4] = √(2 + √2)/2

cos(22.5°) = √(2 + √2)/2 ≈ 0.924

📊 Power-Reducing Formula Explorer

Double-angle identities can be rearranged to express sin²θ and cos²θ:

From cos(2θ) = 1 − 2sin²θ, solve for sin²θ:

sin²θ = (1 − cos 2θ)/2

From cos(2θ) = 2cos²θ − 1, solve for cos²θ:

cos²θ = (1 + cos 2θ)/2

💡 Why "Power-Reducing"? These formulas reduce the power from 2 to 1, which is essential in calculus for integrating sin²x and cos²x!

📋 Complete Identity Reference

Type Identity
Double-Angle (sin) sin(2θ) = 2 sin θ cos θ
Double-Angle (cos) cos(2θ) = cos²θ − sin²θ = 2cos²θ − 1 = 1 − 2sin²θ
Double-Angle (tan) tan(2θ) = 2tan θ / (1 − tan²θ)
Half-Angle (sin) sin(θ/2) = ±√[(1 − cos θ)/2]
Half-Angle (cos) cos(θ/2) = ±√[(1 + cos θ)/2]
Half-Angle (tan) tan(θ/2) = (1 − cos θ)/sin θ = sin θ/(1 + cos θ)
Power-Reducing (sin²) sin²θ = (1 − cos 2θ)/2
Power-Reducing (cos²) cos²θ = (1 + cos 2θ)/2
