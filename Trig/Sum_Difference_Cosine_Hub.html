⚠️ CRITICAL WARNING

cos(A + B) ≠ cos A + cos B

The cosine of a sum is NOT the sum of the cosines!

📐 The Correct Identities

Sum Identity:

cos(A + B) = cos A · cos B − sin A · sin B

Difference Identity:

cos(A − B) = cos A · cos B + sin A · sin B

🔄 Sign Swap Memory Trick

SUM → SUBTRACT: cos(A + B) uses −

DIFFERENCE → ADD: cos(A − B) uses +

The sign in the identity is OPPOSITE to the sign in the angle!

📝 Step-by-Step Exact Value Calculator

Work through finding cos(75°) step by step:

1 Decompose the angle:

75° = 45° + 30°

2 Apply the sum identity:

cos(45° + 30°) = cos 45° · cos 30° − sin 45° · sin 30°

3 Substitute exact values:

= (√2/2)(√3/2) − (√2/2)(1/2)

4 Simplify:

= √6/4 − √2/4 = (√6 − √2)/4

cos(75°) = (√6 − √2)/4 ≈ 0.259
