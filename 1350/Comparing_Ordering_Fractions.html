📚 Comparing Fractions Overview

📝 From Your Course Materials

To compare fractions, we have several methods. Your course shows three methods for comparing 12/42 and 10/35:

  • Method 1: Simplify both fractions
  • Method 2: Rewrite with LCM as denominator
  • Method 3: Rewrite with any common denominator
Five Methods for Comparing Fractions
Method When to Use Example
Same Denominator Denominators are already equal 3/8 vs 5/8 → Compare 3 and 5
Same Numerator Numerators are already equal 3/4 vs 3/5 → Larger denom = smaller fraction
Cross Multiplication Quick comparison of any two fractions a/b vs c/d → Compare ad and bc
Benchmark Fractions Compare to 0, 1/2, or 1 3/8 < 1/2 and 5/8 > 1/2
Common Denominator Rewrite with LCM, then compare 2/3 = 8/12, 3/4 = 9/12 → 8/12 < 9/12
Key Principle

If two fractions have the same positive denominator, compare the numerators directly.

3 5
<
4 5

Because 3 < 4, we know 3/5 < 4/5

⚠️ Important Note

If the two fractions don't have the same positive denominator, then use the Fundamental Law of Fractions to get a common denominator:

a/b = (an)/(bn) for any integer n ≠ 0

🔧 Comparison Methods

Method 1: Simplify Both Fractions

From your course materials: Compare 12/42 and 10/35

1

Simplify 12/42

GCF(12, 42) = 6

12/42 = (12÷6)/(42÷6) = 2/7

2

Simplify 10/35

GCF(10, 35) = 5

10/35 = (10÷5)/(35÷5) = 2/7

3

Compare

2/7 = 2/7, so 12/42 = 10/35

Method 2: Rewrite with LCM as Denominator

From your course materials: Compare 12/42 and 10/35

1

Find LCM(42, 35)

42 = 2 × 3 × 7

35 = 5 × 7

LCM = 2 × 3 × 5 × 7 = 210

2

Rewrite 12/42 with denominator 210

12/42 = (12×5)/(42×5) = 60/210

3

Rewrite 10/35 with denominator 210

10/35 = (10×6)/(35×6) = 60/210

4

Compare

60/210 = 60/210, so 12/42 = 10/35

Method 3: Cross Multiplication

Compare a/b and c/d by comparing a×d and b×c

1

Set up the comparison: 12/42 vs 10/35

Cross multiply: 12 × 35 and 42 × 10

2

Calculate

12 × 35 = 420

42 × 10 = 420

3

Compare

420 = 420, so 12/42 = 10/35

Rule: For a/b and c/d (with positive denominators):

  • If ad > bc, then a/b > c/d
  • If ad < bc, then a/b < c/d
  • If ad = bc, then a/b = c/d

Method 4: Same Numerator

When numerators are equal, larger denominator = smaller fraction

1

Example: Compare 3/4 and 3/5

Both have numerator 3

2

Compare denominators

4 < 5

3

Apply the rule

Larger denominator = smaller fraction

So 3/4 > 3/5

Think about it: If you share 3 pizzas among 4 people vs 5 people, who gets more?

Fewer people = bigger slices!

📝 Using Benchmarks

Compare fractions to common benchmarks: 0, 1/2, and 1

  • If numerator < half of denominator → fraction < 1/2
  • If numerator > half of denominator → fraction > 1/2
  • If numerator = half of denominator → fraction = 1/2
