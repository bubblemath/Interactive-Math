📋 What is a Survey?

A survey is a structured inquiry to collect data. Surveys may be conducted in person, over the phone, online, or data collected from a database.

📊 The Three-Step Strategy

  1. Define sets and draw a Venn diagram
  2. Fill in regions starting from the CENTER and working outward
  3. Use the completed diagram to answer questions

⚠️ Common Mistake: Forgetting to Subtract!

The #1 error students make: Using the given total for a set directly without subtracting the intersection.

Example: If 75 people like Classical and 20 like both Classical and Jazz:

  • WRONG: Put 75 in the "Classical only" region
  • RIGHT: Put 75 - 20 = 55 in the "Classical only" region

The 20 people who like both are ALREADY counted in the 75!

🎯 Center-Out Strategy for Three Sets

  1. Region V (center): Fill in the triple intersection first
  2. Regions II, IV, VI: Calculate two-set overlaps minus the center
  3. Regions I, III, VII: Calculate single-set regions minus all overlaps
  4. Region VIII: Calculate total minus the union

📐 Essential Formulas

Two-Set Formulas

Union: n(A ∪ B) = n(A) + n(B) − n(A ∩ B)
Only A: n(A ∩ B') = n(A) − n(A ∩ B)
Only B: n(A' ∩ B) = n(B) − n(A ∩ B)
Neither: n(A ∪ B)' = n(U) − n(A ∪ B)
Exactly One: n(A ∩ B') + n(A' ∩ B)

Three-Set Formula (Inclusion-Exclusion)

n(A ∪ B ∪ C) = n(A) + n(B) + n(C) − n(A ∩ B) − n(A ∩ C) − n(B ∩ C) + n(A ∩ B ∩ C)

🎯 Center-Out Strategy

⚠️ Remember: Always Start from the CENTER!

The #1 mistake is putting totals directly without subtracting overlaps.

For Two Sets:

  1. Fill the CENTER first — the intersection (A ∩ B)
  2. Calculate "Only A" — subtract: n(A) − n(A ∩ B)
  3. Calculate "Only B" — subtract: n(B) − n(A ∩ B)
  4. Calculate "Neither" — subtract: n(U) − n(A ∪ B)

For Three Sets:

  1. Region V — Fill the triple intersection (A ∩ B ∩ C) first
  2. Regions II, IV, VI — Two-set overlaps minus center
  3. Regions I, III, VII — Single sets minus all overlaps
  4. Region VIII — Total minus the union
