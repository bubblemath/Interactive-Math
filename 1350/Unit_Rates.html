📖 What is a Unit Rate?

📝 Definition

A unit rate is a rate with a denominator of 1.

It tells you the amount of something "per one" of something else.

🔑 Key Idea

"Unit" means "one"

A unit rate answers: "How much for ONE?"

Examples of Unit Rates
Context Rate Unit Rate
🚗 Speed 180 miles in 3 hours 60 miles per hour
💵 Price $15 for 12 tickets $1.25 per ticket
🛒 Shopping $6 for 4 pounds $1.50 per pound
⛽ Gas 300 miles on 10 gallons 30 miles per gallon
💼 Work $120 for 8 hours $15 per hour

💡 Why Unit Rates Matter

Unit rates make it easy to compare different options. When everything is "per one," you can quickly see which option is better!

🧮 Finding Unit Rates

📝 The Method

To find a unit rate, divide to get the "per one" value.

Unit Rate = Total Amount ÷ Number of Units

Worked Example

📝 Find the unit rate: $15 for 12 tickets

Step 1: Set up the division: $15 ÷ 12 tickets

Step 2: Divide: 15 ÷ 12 = 1.25

Step 3: Write the unit rate: $1.25 per ticket
