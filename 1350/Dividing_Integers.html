"If a, b, c ∈ ℤ, and b ≠ 0, then a ÷ b = c if and only if a = b · c."

Division is defined as the inverse of multiplication!

🔑 The Sign Rules (Same as Multiplication!)
(+) ÷ (+)
= Positive (+)
(+) ÷ (−)

(−) ÷ (+)
= Negative (−)
(−) ÷ (−)

Same signs → Positive  |  Different signs → Negative

Key Insight

The sign rules for division are exactly the same as for multiplication!

This makes sense because division is the inverse of multiplication.

📖 Formal Definition

Definition of Division of Integers

If a, b, c ∈ ℤ (integers), and b ≠ 0, then:

a ÷ b = c   if and only if   a = b · c

Division is defined in terms of multiplication!

🔄 Division as Inverse of Multiplication

The Inverse Relationship

Division "undoes" multiplication, just like subtraction "undoes" addition.

  • If 3 × 4 = 12, then 12 ÷ 4 = 3 and 12 ÷ 3 = 4
  • If (−3) × 4 = −12, then (−12) ÷ 4 = −3 and (−12) ÷ (−3) = 4

Fact Families with Integers

Every multiplication fact creates a family of related facts:

Positive × Positive

3 × 4 = 12

12 ÷ 4 = 3

12 ÷ 3 = 4

Positive × Negative

3 × (−4) = −12

(−12) ÷ (−4) = 3

(−12) ÷ 3 = −4

Negative × Positive

(−3) × 4 = −12

(−12) ÷ 4 = −3

(−12) ÷ (−3) = 4

Negative × Negative

(−3) × (−4) = 12

12 ÷ (−4) = −3

12 ÷ (−3) = −4

📚 Teaching Tip

  • Emphasize that students already know the sign rules from multiplication!
  • Use fact families to reinforce the connection
  • Have students verify division answers by multiplying back

⚠️ Special Cases

🚫 Division by Zero is UNDEFINED

a ÷ 0 is undefined for any integer a

There is no number that, when multiplied by 0, gives a non-zero result!

📝 From Your Course Materials

"If a, b, c ∈ ℤ, and b ≠ 0, then a ÷ b = c..."

The condition b ≠ 0 is essential!

Why Can't We Divide by Zero?

Let's try to find 8 ÷ 0 = ?

Using the definition: 8 ÷ 0 = ? means 0 × ? = 8

But 0 × (anything) = 0, never 8!

There is NO number that works → UNDEFINED
