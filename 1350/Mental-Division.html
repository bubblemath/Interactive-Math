🎯 From Your Course Materials

Mental division strategies help students build number sense and computational fluency. These strategies connect to multiplication facts and place value understanding.

Mental Division Strategies

🔟 Dividing by Powers of 10

Remove zeros when dividing by 10, 100, 1000...

3400 ÷ 100 = 34

📦 Breaking Up the Dividend

Split the dividend into parts that divide evenly

84 ÷ 4 = (80÷4) + (4÷4) = 21

✖️ Using Multiplication Facts

Think: "What times the divisor equals the dividend?"

56 ÷ 8 = ? → 8 × 7 = 56

🎯 Compatible Numbers

Adjust to numbers that divide evenly

243 ÷ 6 ≈ 240 ÷ 6 = 40

➗ Halving

Divide by 2 repeatedly for divisors like 4, 8

84 ÷ 4 = (84÷2) ÷ 2 = 21
Estimation Strategies

🎯 Compatible Numbers

Find nearby numbers that divide evenly

1,476 ÷ 7 ≈ 1,400 ÷ 7 = 200

📊 Front-End Estimation

Use the leading digits only

1,476 ÷ 7 ≈ 1,400 ÷ 7 = 200

🔄 Rounding

Round both numbers to easier values

1,476 ÷ 7 ≈ 1,500 ÷ 8 ≈ 200

🤔 For Your Teaching Practice

"Mental math isn't about being fast—it's about being flexible. Students who can approach division from multiple angles have stronger number sense and are better problem solvers."

📦 Breaking Up the Dividend

The "Breaking up the dividend" strategy uses the distributive property: split the dividend into parts that each divide evenly by the divisor, then add the partial quotients.

Common Break-Apart Patterns
Problem Break Apart Solution
84 ÷ 4 (80 + 4) ÷ 4 20 + 1 = 21
96 ÷ 6 (60 + 36) ÷ 6 10 + 6 = 16
72 ÷ 3 (60 + 12) ÷ 3 20 + 4 = 24
135 ÷ 5 (100 + 35) ÷ 5 20 + 7 = 27

📚 Teaching Tip

  • The key is choosing parts that divide evenly by the divisor
  • Multiples of 10 times the divisor are often good choices
  • This strategy connects to the distributive property
  • Students should check: Do both parts divide evenly?
