🔍 Error Analysis

📝 Diagnosing Student Errors

Understanding WHY students make mistakes is crucial for effective teaching. Click "Show Diagnosis" to see the error pattern and intervention strategies.

Addition Errors

Error: Adding Negatives

−5 + (−3) = −2 ❌
🔍 Diagnosis

The Error: Student subtracted instead of adding (treated as −5 − 3 but got wrong answer).

Likely Misconception: Confused about what "adding a negative" means.

Intervention:

  • Use chip model: 5 red chips + 3 more red chips = 8 red chips = −8
  • Use word problem: "You owe $5 and then owe $3 more. How much do you owe?"

Correct Answer: −5 + (−3) = −8

Error: Different Signs

−7 + 4 = −11 ❌
🔍 Diagnosis

The Error: Student added the absolute values instead of finding the difference.

Likely Misconception: "Adding always makes bigger."

Intervention:

  • Use chip model: 7 red + 4 yellow → 4 zero pairs cancel → 3 red remain = −3
  • Use number line: Start at −7, move 4 right, land on −3

Correct Answer: −7 + 4 = −3

Subtraction Errors

Error: Subtracting a Negative

5 − (−3) = 2 ❌
🔍 Diagnosis

The Error: Student treated 5 − (−3) as 5 − 3.

Likely Misconception: Ignoring the double negative.

Intervention:

  • Teach: a − (−b) = a + b (subtracting negative = adding positive)
  • Word problem: "If I take away a $3 debt, am I richer or poorer?"

Correct Answer: 5 − (−3) = 5 + 3 = 8

Multiplication/Division Errors

Error: Sign Rules

(−4) × (−3) = −12 ❌
🔍 Diagnosis

The Error: Student thinks negative × negative = negative.

Likely Misconception: "Two negatives make a negative."

Intervention:

  • Use pattern model to discover the rule
  • Chip model: Remove 4 groups of −3 from zero → 12 positives remain
  • Word problem: "Taking away 4 debts of $3 each makes you $12 richer"

Correct Answer: (−4) × (−3) = 12

Error: Order of Operations

−3² = 9 ❌
🔍 Diagnosis

The Error: Student squared the negative along with the 3.

Likely Misconception: The negative sign is part of the base.

Intervention:

  • Emphasize: −3² means −(3²), not (−3)²
  • The exponent only applies to what it's directly attached to
  • Compare: −3² = −9 vs (−3)² = 9

Correct Answer: −3² = −(3²) = −9
