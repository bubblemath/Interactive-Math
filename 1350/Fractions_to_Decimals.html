📖 Converting Fractions to Decimals

📝 The Big Idea

Every fraction can be written as a decimal! The key insight is that a fraction a/b means a ÷ b.

There are two main methods to convert fractions to decimals:

  • Division Method: Divide the numerator by the denominator
  • Equivalent Fraction Method: Rewrite with a denominator that is a power of 10
Two Methods Comparison
Method Process Best For
Division a/b = a ÷ b Any fraction
Equivalent Fraction Convert denominator to 10, 100, 1000, etc. Denominators with only 2s and 5s as factors

🔢 Equivalent Fraction Method

📝 Converting to Powers of 10

If we can rewrite the denominator as a power of 10 (10, 100, 1000, etc.), converting to a decimal is easy!

Key: This only works when the denominator has only 2s and 5s as prime factors.

Why 2s and 5s?

Because 10 = 2 × 5

Denominator Prime Factors Can Convert to Power of 10?
4 = 2² Only 2s ✓ Yes: 4 × 25 = 100
5 Only 5s ✓ Yes: 5 × 2 = 10
8 = 2³ Only 2s ✓ Yes: 8 × 125 = 1000
3 Has 3 ✗ No (will repeat)
6 = 2 × 3 Has 3 ✗ No (will repeat)
Equivalent Fraction Converter
Step-by-Step Examples

Convert 3/4 to a decimal:

Step 1: Check if denominator has only 2s and 5s
4 = 2² ✓ (only 2s)
Step 2: Find what to multiply by to get a power of 10
4 × 25 = 100, so multiply by 25/25
Step 3: Apply the Fundamental Law of Fractions
3/4 = (3 × 25)/(4 × 25) = 75/100
Step 4: Write as decimal
75/100 = 75 hundredths = 0.75

📚 Teaching Tip

This method connects to the Fundamental Law of Fractions:

a/b = (a × n)/(b × n)

Students should recognize that multiplying by n/n = 1 doesn't change the value!

🔍 Identifying Patterns

📝 Terminating vs. Repeating Decimals

When converting fractions to decimals, the result will either:

  • Terminate: End after a finite number of digits (e.g., 0.75)
  • Repeat: Have a pattern that repeats forever (e.g., 0.333...)
The Rule for Terminating Decimals

🔑 Key Rule

A fraction (in lowest terms) will terminate if and only if the denominator has only 2s and 5s as prime factors.

Fraction Denominator Factors Terminates? Decimal
1/2 2 ✓ Yes 0.5
1/4 2² ✓ Yes 0.25
1/5 5 ✓ Yes 0.2
1/8 2³ ✓ Yes 0.125
1/3 3 ✗ No 0.333...
1/6 2 × 3 ✗ No 0.1666...
