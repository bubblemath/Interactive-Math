Understanding Our Number System

📝 What is a Numeral?

A numeral is a symbol or group of symbols used to represent a number. For example, "5", "V", and "五" are all numerals representing the same quantity.

🔢 Cardinal Numbers

A cardinal number tells us "how many" of something there are. It answers the question of quantity: one, two, three, etc.

📊 Numeration System

A numeration system is a method of writing numbers using a set of symbols (digits) according to specific rules. Different civilizations developed different systems!

🌟 Hindu-Arabic System

Our modern system uses 10 digits (0-9) and is called the decimal or base 10 system. It originated in India and was spread by Arab mathematicians.

🤔 Why Base 10?

🖐️ 🖐️

The most likely reason humans developed a base-10 system is simple: we have 10 fingers! Counting on fingers is natural and universal. Other bases exist too - base 12 (duodecimal) appears in clocks, months, and dozens. Base 5 (quinary) relates to one hand. Base 20 (vigesimal) uses fingers AND toes!
