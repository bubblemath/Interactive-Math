📖 What Are Repeating Decimals?

📝 Definition

A repeating decimal is a non-terminating decimal with the property that a digit or string of adjacent digits repeats forever.

From Your Course Materials

"A repeating decimal is a non-terminating decimal with the property that a digit or string of adjacent digits repeats forever."

Examples of repeating decimals:

  • 0.333... (the digit 3 repeats)
  • 0.272727... (the digits 27 repeat)
  • 0.142857142857... (the digits 142857 repeat)
  • 0.8333... (3 repeats after the 8)

📚 Key Vocabulary

Repetend: The repeating block of digits

Period (or Period Length): The number of digits in the repetend

Examples with Vocabulary
Decimal Repetend Period Length
0.333... 3 1
0.8333... 3 1
0.272727... 27 2
0.142857142857... 142857 6

🔑 Key Insight

Every fraction with a denominator that has prime factors other than 2 and 5 will produce a repeating decimal!

✏️ Bar Notation

📝 How to Write Repeating Decimals

To denote the block of digits that repeat, we use a horizontal bar (vinculum) over the repetend.

Bar Notation Examples
Written Out Bar Notation Repetend Period
0.83333... 0.83 3 1
0.272727... 0.27 27 2
0.142857142857... 0.142857 142857 6
0.16666... 0.16 6 1
From Your Course Materials
Example 1: 0.83333... = 0.83
The repetend is 3 and the period length is 1.
Example 2: 0.272727... = 0.27
The repetend is 27 and the period length is 2.
Example 3: 0.142857142857... = 0.142857
The repetend is 142857 and the period length is 6.
⚠️ Important: Where Does the Bar Go?

The bar goes only over the digits that repeat, not over any non-repeating part.

Correct: 0.16 (the 1 doesn't repeat, only the 6)
Incorrect: 0.16 (this would mean 016016016...)

🔍 Pattern Finder

📝 Finding the Repeating Pattern

When you divide to convert a fraction to a decimal, watch for when a remainder repeats - that's when the pattern starts over!

Fraction to Decimal Pattern Finder
Why Do Patterns Repeat?

When dividing, there are only a limited number of possible remainders (0 to denominator-1). Eventually, a remainder must repeat!

Example: 1 ÷ 7
Possible remainders: 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6
After at most 6 steps, a remainder must repeat!
Result: 1/7 = 0.142857
Period length = 6 (the maximum for denominator 7)
Maximum Period Length

🔑 The Rule

For a fraction 1/n (in lowest terms), the maximum period length is n - 1.

Fraction Max Period Actual Period Decimal
1/3 2 1 0.3
1/6 5 1 0.16
1/7 6 6 0.142857
1/9 8 1 0.1
1/11 10 2 0.09

🔄 Converting Repeating Decimals to Fractions

📝 The Algebraic Method

To convert a repeating decimal to a fraction, we use algebra:

  • Step 1: Let x = the repeating decimal
  • Step 2: Multiply by 10^n (where n = period length) to shift the decimal
  • Step 3: Subtract to eliminate the repeating part
  • Step 4: Solve for x
Example: Convert 0.3 to a Fraction
Step 1: Let x = 0.333...
Step 2: Multiply by 10 (period = 1, so 10¹)
10x = 3.333...
Step 3: Subtract: 10x - x = 3.333... - 0.333...
9x = 3
Step 4: Solve: x = 3/9 = 1/3
Example: Convert 0.27 to a Fraction
Step 1: Let x = 0.272727...
Step 2: Multiply by 100 (period = 2, so 10²)
100x = 27.272727...
Step 3: Subtract: 100x - x = 27.2727... - 0.2727...
99x = 27
Step 4: Solve: x = 27/99 = 3/11
