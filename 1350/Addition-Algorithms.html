🧊 Concrete Model (Base-10 Blocks)

Students use physical objects to add values. This hands-on approach makes regrouping ("carrying") visible and tangible. Watch 10 units become 1 long, or 10 longs become 1 flat!

Enter numbers and click "Build Blocks" to start

🤔 How Would You Explain This?

"A student asks why we can't just leave 15 units as 15 units. Why do we HAVE to exchange for a long? How would you explain the importance of regrouping?"

📚 Teaching Tip

Best for: Kinesthetic learners and students who struggle with the abstract nature of digits.

  • Always start with physical manipulatives before moving to pictures or symbols
  • The "exchange" concept is the foundation for understanding carrying in the standard algorithm
  • Ask students: "Why do we use groups of 10?" (connects to base-10 place value)
  • Common error: Students may count individual units instead of recognizing groups
  • Transition: Once comfortable, move to Partial Sums to bridge to written methods
🔢 Partial Sums Algorithm (Expanded/Instructional)

Break down addition by place value: add hundreds, then tens, then ones separately. This method reduces memory load because students don't need to track carries while computing—they add the partial sums at the end.

Enter numbers and click "Start"

🤔 How Would You Explain This?

"A student writes 376 as 3 + 7 + 6 instead of 300 + 70 + 6. How would you help them understand the difference between a digit and its place value?"

📚 Teaching Tip

Best for: Students with strong place-value understanding but who struggle with tracking carries.

  • Emphasize: "The 3 in 376 isn't just 3—it's 300!" (expanded form)
  • This method makes the actual values explicit, reinforcing place value concepts
  • Students can work left-to-right (more intuitive) instead of right-to-left
  • Common error: Misaligning partial sums when adding them together
  • Connection: This is the "why" behind the standard algorithm's carrying
🧱 Lattice Addition Method

A visual grid-based method where numbers are stacked vertically. Each cell holds the sum of digits in that column, split by a diagonal (tens above, ones below). Add along the diagonals from right to left, carrying as needed.

Enter numbers and click "Build Grid"

🤔 How Would You Explain This?

"A student loves the lattice method but makes errors when adding along diagonals. What visual cue or strategy could help them track which numbers belong to each diagonal?"

📚 Teaching Tip

Best for: Visual-spatial learners and students who find the standard algorithm's notation confusing.

  • The grid structure helps students organize their work spatially
  • Carries are handled naturally by the diagonal addition
  • Great for students with dyslexia or those who reverse digits
  • Have students use colored pencils to trace each diagonal
  • Common error: Adding across rows instead of along diagonals
✍️ Standard Algorithm

The traditional right-to-left method with carrying. This is the most efficient algorithm but also the most abstract. Watch the "carry" digit float up to show exactly where it comes from and where it belongs!

Enter numbers and click "Start"

🤔 How Would You Explain This?

"A student consistently forgets to add the carried digit. They get 376 + 459 = 725 (forgetting both carries). What concrete connection could help them remember?"

📚 Teaching Tip

Best for: Students who have mastered place value and are ready for efficiency.

  • Always connect the "carry" back to the concrete model's "exchange"
  • The small "1" we write is actually "10" (or "100") in disguise!
  • Have students verbalize: "6 + 9 = 15. That's 1 ten and 5 ones."
  • Common error: Forgetting to add the carry, or adding it to the wrong column
  • Sequence: Only introduce after Concrete → Partial Sums progression

🧠 Educator's Comparison Lab

Match algorithms to student needs and identify common pitfalls.

🧊 Concrete Model

Best for: Kinesthetic learners, students new to regrouping, those who need to "see" the exchange
⚠️ Pitfall: Counting units individually instead of recognizing groups

🔢 Partial Sums

Best for: Students with place-value strength, those overwhelmed by tracking carries
⚠️ Pitfall: Misaligning columns when adding partial sums

🧱 Lattice Method

Best for: Visual-spatial learners, students who reverse digits, those confused by carry notation
⚠️ Pitfall: Adding across rows instead of along diagonals

✍️ Standard Algorithm

Best for: Efficiency once concepts are mastered, standardized testing
⚠️ Pitfall: Forgetting to add the carried digit
