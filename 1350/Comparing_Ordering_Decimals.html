🔑 The Golden Rule

When comparing decimals, make sure you're comparing like place values. Align the decimal points and add trailing zeros if needed!

✅ Ordering Terminating Decimals

📝 The Method: Align and Compare

To compare terminating decimals:

  • Step 1: Align the decimal points vertically
  • Step 2: Add trailing zeros to make same length
  • Step 3: Compare digit by digit from left to right
  • Step 4: The first different digit determines the order
Example: Compare 0.35 and 0.4
Step 1: Align decimal points
0.35
0.4
Step 2: Add trailing zeros
0.35
0.40
Step 3: Compare digit by digit
0.35
0.40
Result: 3 < 4, so 0.35 < 0.4

❌ Wrong: "0.35 > 0.4 because 35 > 4"
This ignores place value!

🔁 Ordering Repeating Decimals

📝 Two Methods for Repeating Decimals

When comparing repeating decimals, you can:

  • Method 1: Convert to fractions and compare
  • Method 2: Extend the pattern and compare digit by digit
Method 1: Convert to Fractions

Compare 0.3 and 0.27

Step 1: Convert to fractions
0.3 = 1/3
0.27 = 27/99 = 3/11
Step 2: Find common denominator
1/3 = 11/33
3/11 = 9/33
Step 3: Compare: 11/33 > 9/33
Therefore: 0.3 > 0.27
Method 2: Extend the Pattern

Compare 0.3 and 0.27

Step 1: Write out several digits of each
0.3 = 0.333333...
0.27 = 0.272727...
Step 2: Compare digit by digit
0.333333...
0.272727...
Result: 3 > 2, so 0.3 > 0.27

🔀 Mixed Comparisons

📝 Comparing Terminating and Repeating Decimals

When comparing a terminating decimal with a repeating decimal:

  • Option 1: Convert both to fractions
  • Option 2: Extend the repeating decimal and compare
Example: Compare 0.5 and 0.4

Method: Extend and Compare

Step 1: Write out the decimals
0.5 = 0.5000000...
0.4 = 0.4444444...
Step 2: Compare digit by digit
0.5000000...
0.4444444...
Result: 5 > 4, so 0.5 > 0.4
Example: Compare 0.3 and 0.3

Tricky Case!

Step 1: Write out the decimals
0.3 = 0.3000000...
0.3 = 0.3333333...
Step 2: Compare digit by digit
0.3000000...
0.3333333...
Result: First digits match, but 0 < 3, so 0.3 < 0.3
