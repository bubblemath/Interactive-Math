📚 Fractions & Decimals Overview

📝 The Connection

Fractions and decimals are two different ways to represent the same values. Every fraction can be written as a decimal, and every decimal can be written as a fraction.

a/b = a ÷ b

Key Concepts
Conversion Method Example
Fraction → Decimal Divide numerator by denominator 3/4 = 3 ÷ 4 = 0.75
Decimal → Fraction Use place value, then simplify 0.75 = 75/100 = 3/4
Terminating Decimal Denominator has only 2s and 5s as factors 1/8 = 0.125 (8 = 2³)
Repeating Decimal Denominator has other prime factors 1/3 = 0.333... = 0.3̄

➡️ Fraction to Decimal

📝 The Method

To convert a fraction to a decimal, divide the numerator by the denominator.

a/b = a ÷ b

🔍 Terminating vs. Repeating

📝 The Rule

A fraction in lowest terms will produce a terminating decimal if and only if the denominator has no prime factors other than 2 and 5.

Otherwise, it produces a repeating decimal.

1/7 7 = 7 Repeating 0.142857̄

📚 Teaching Tip

Why only 2s and 5s?

  • Our number system is base 10
  • 10 = 2 × 5
  • Denominators with only 2s and 5s can be converted to powers of 10
  • Example: 1/8 = 1/8 × 125/125 = 125/1000 = 0.125
