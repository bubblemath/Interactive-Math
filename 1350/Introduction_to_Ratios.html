📖 What is a Ratio?

📝 Definition from Your Course Materials

Ratio: If a and b are real numbers with b ≠ 0, then the ratio of a to b is the quotient a/b.

🔑 Key Idea

A ratio compares two quantities by showing the relative size of one quantity to another.

Real-World Examples

🍳 Cooking

A recipe calls for 2 cups of flour for every 1 cup of sugar.

The ratio of flour to sugar is 2:1

🏀 Sports

A basketball team has 3 wins for every 2 losses.

The ratio of wins to losses is 3:2

🎓 Classroom

There are 12 girls and 15 boys in a class.

The ratio of girls to boys is 12:15 (or simplified: 4:5)

🚗 Travel

Austin is approximately 180 miles from Houston. It takes 3 hours to travel.

The ratio of miles to hours is 180:3 (or 60 miles per hour)

⚠️ Important Note

Order matters! The ratio 3:4 is NOT the same as 4:3.

Always pay attention to which quantity comes first.

🔀 Part-to-Part vs Part-to-Whole

📝 Two Types of Ratio Relationships

Ratios can compare different things depending on the context:

Part-to-Part Ratios

Part-to-Part

Compares one part of a group to another part of the same group.

🐕 Example: Animal Shelter

An animal shelter has 8 dogs and 5 cats.

Part-to-Part: The ratio of dogs to cats is 8:5

We're comparing one part (dogs) to another part (cats).

Part-to-Whole Ratios

Part-to-Whole

Compares one part of a group to the entire group.

🐕 Example: Animal Shelter (continued)

An animal shelter has 8 dogs and 5 cats. Total: 13 animals.

Part-to-Whole: The ratio of dogs to all animals is 8:13

We're comparing one part (dogs) to the whole (all animals).

Real-World Context: Mixing Paint

🎨 Paint Mixing

To make green paint, you mix blue and yellow.

A ratio of 2:3 (blue:yellow) makes a yellow-green.

A ratio of 3:2 (blue:yellow) makes a blue-green.

The ratio determines the shade!

⚖️ Equivalent Ratios

📝 What Are Equivalent Ratios?

Equivalent ratios express the same relationship between quantities, just like equivalent fractions represent the same value.

🔑 Key Rule

Multiply or divide both parts of a ratio by the same non-zero number to create an equivalent ratio.

If a:b is a ratio, then (a×n):(b×n) is equivalent for any n ≠ 0

Real-World Context: Scaling Recipes

🍪 Cookie Recipe

Original recipe: 2 cups flour : 1 cup sugar

Double the recipe: 4 cups flour : 2 cups sugar

Triple the recipe: 6 cups flour : 3 cups sugar

The ratio stays the same (2:1), but the amounts change!

✂️ Simplifying Ratios

📝 Using GCF to Simplify

Just like simplifying fractions, we can simplify ratios by dividing both parts by their Greatest Common Factor (GCF).

🔑 Steps to Simplify a Ratio

1. Find the GCF of both numbers

2. Divide both parts by the GCF

3. Write the simplified ratio

Worked Example

📝 Simplify 12:18

Step 1: Find factors of 12: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12

Step 2: Find factors of 18: 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 18

Step 3: GCF(12, 18) = 6

Step 4: 12 ÷ 6 = 2, 18 ÷ 6 = 3

Result: 12:18 = 2:3 in lowest terms
