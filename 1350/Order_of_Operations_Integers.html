⚠️ Critical Point for Integers

When working with integers, pay special attention to:

  • −3² vs (−3)² — These are DIFFERENT!
  • Negative signs in front of parentheses
  • Multiple operations with negative numbers

📋 PEMDAS in Detail

Step 1: Parentheses (and other grouping symbols)

Work from the innermost grouping symbol outward.

Symbol Name Example
( ) Parentheses 3 × (4 + 2)
[ ] Brackets 2 × [3 + (4 − 1)]
{ } Braces {2 × [3 + 4]} − 5
Step 2: Exponents

Evaluate all powers and roots.

🚨 The Most Common Error with Integers!

❌ −3²

−3²

= −(3²)

= −(9)

= −9

The negative is NOT squared!

✓ (−3)²

(−3)²

= (−3) × (−3)

= 9

The negative IS squared!

Steps 3 & 4: Multiplication/Division, then Addition/Subtraction

Important: Within each level, work left to right!

M and D: Same Level

24 ÷ 4 × 2

= 6 × 2 (left to right)

= 12

A and S: Same Level

10 − 4 + 3

= 6 + 3 (left to right)

= 9

⚠️ Common Errors to Watch For

Error 1: −3² vs (−3)²

🔍 Student Work

−3² = 9 ❌

The Error: Student squared the negative along with the 3.

Correct Understanding:

  • −3² means −(3²) = −9
  • (−3)² means (−3)(−3) = 9

Teaching Strategy: The exponent only applies to what it's directly attached to. Without parentheses, it's only attached to the 3.

Error 2: Left-to-Right Confusion

🔍 Student Work

24 ÷ 4 × 2 = 24 ÷ 8 = 3 ❌

The Error: Student multiplied before dividing (did 4 × 2 first).

Correct Solution:

  • 24 ÷ 4 × 2
  • = 6 × 2 (left to right)
  • = 12 ✓

Teaching Strategy: M and D are at the same level — always work left to right!

Error 3: Distributing Incorrectly

🔍 Student Work

−2(3 + 4) = −6 + 4 = −2 ❌

The Error: Student only distributed to the first term.

Correct Solution:

  • −2(3 + 4) = −2(7) = −14 ✓
  • OR: −2(3) + (−2)(4) = −6 + (−8) = −14 ✓

Teaching Strategy: Either simplify inside parentheses first, OR distribute to ALL terms.

Error 4: Sign Errors in Multi-Step Problems

🔍 Student Work

5 − 3 × (−2) = 5 − (−6) = 5 − 6 = −1 ❌

The Error: Student changed 5 − (−6) to 5 − 6 instead of 5 + 6.

Correct Solution:

  • 5 − 3 × (−2)
  • = 5 − (−6)
  • = 5 + 6 (subtracting negative = adding positive)
  • = 11 ✓

Teaching Strategy: Review: a − (−b) = a + b

Error 5: Forgetting Parentheses Change Order

🔍 Student Work

(5 + 3) × 2 − 4 = 5 + 6 − 4 = 7 ❌

The Error: Student only multiplied 3 by 2, not the entire parentheses.

Correct Solution:

  • (5 + 3) × 2 − 4
  • = 8 × 2 − 4
  • = 16 − 4
  • = 12 ✓

Teaching Strategy: Parentheses mean "do this FIRST" — the entire result gets multiplied.
