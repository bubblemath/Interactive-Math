📚 Key Concepts

🧠 Pólya's Four-Step Problem-Solving Process

▼

Step 1: Understand the Problem

  • Can the problem be stated differently?
  • What is to be found or what is needed?
  • What are the unknowns?
  • What information is obtained from the problem?
  • What information, if any, is missing or not needed?

Step 2: Devise a Plan

  • Look for a pattern
  • Examine related problems
  • Examine a simpler case
  • Make a table or list
  • Make a diagram
  • Use guess and check
  • Work backward
  • Write an equation

Step 3: Carry Out the Plan

Execute your chosen strategy carefully, showing all work and checking each step.

Step 4: Look Back

  • Is the solution correct? Does it answer the question?
  • Is there an easier solution?
  • Can the solution be generalized?

📊 Types of Sequences

▼

Arithmetic Sequence

Each term is obtained by adding or subtracting a fixed number (the common difference, d).

Formula: aₙ = a₁ + d(n-1)

Examples:
• 3, 6, 9, 12, 15... (d = +3)
• 4, 9, 14, 19, 24... (d = +5)
• 86, 83, 80, 77... (d = -3)

⚠️ Common Mistake: Confusing common difference with common ratio!

Geometric Sequence

Each term is obtained by multiplying by a fixed non-zero number (the common ratio, r).

Formula: aₙ = a₁ × r^(n-1)

Examples:
• 5, 15, 45, 135... (r = 3)
• 256, 128, 64, 32... (r = ½)
• 2, 10, 50, 250... (r = 5)

⚠️ Common Mistake: Forgetting that r can be a fraction (for decreasing sequences)!

Recursive (Fibonacci-type) Sequence

Each term is derived from one or more previous terms.

Fibonacci Sequence:
1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, 21, 34...
(Each term = sum of two previous terms)

Using Differences Method

For sequences that are neither arithmetic nor geometric, find the differences between consecutive terms, then look for a pattern in those differences.

Example: 1, 4, 9, 16, 25...
Differences: 3, 5, 7, 9... (arithmetic!)
This reveals the sequence is perfect squares: n²

🔍 Inductive vs. Deductive Reasoning

▼

Inductive Reasoning

Making a generalization based on specific observations. Goes from specific → general.

Example:
Observation: 2 is even, 4 is even, 6 is even, 8 is even...
Conjecture: All multiples of 2 are even.

⚠️ Inductive reasoning can lead to false conclusions! A single counterexample disproves a conjecture.

Deductive Reasoning

Drawing a specific conclusion from general principles. Goes from general → specific.

Example:
Premise 1: All squares have 4 equal sides.
Premise 2: Figure ABCD is a square.
Conclusion: Figure ABCD has 4 equal sides.

✓ If premises are true and logic is valid, conclusion is guaranteed true.

Counterexamples

A counterexample is a single example that proves a conjecture false.

Famous Example - Circle Regions:
2 points → 2 regions
3 points → 4 regions
4 points → 8 regions
5 points → 16 regions
Conjecture: n points → 2^(n-1) regions

COUNTEREXAMPLE: 6 points → 31 regions (not 32!)

📝 Pólya Process Simulator

Work through a challenging problem using all four steps. After completing each step, you can compare your answer to a model response!

Challenge Problem:

A farmer has chickens and cows. If there are 30 heads and 74 legs total, how many chickens and how many cows does the farmer have?

Step 1: Understand the Problem

What are you trying to find? What information do you have? What are the unknowns?

✅ Model Answer for Step 1

What we need to find: The number of chickens and the number of cows.

What we know:
• Total animals (heads) = 30
• Total legs = 74
• Chickens have 2 legs each
• Cows have 4 legs each

Unknowns: Let c = number of chickens, let w = number of cows

Step 2: Devise a Plan

What strategy will you use? (Make a table, write equations, guess and check...)

✅ Model Answer for Step 2

Strategy: Write a system of equations.

Equation 1 (heads): c + w = 30
Equation 2 (legs): 2c + 4w = 74

Plan: Use substitution or elimination to solve the system. From Equation 1, we can express c = 30 - w, then substitute into Equation 2.

Step 3: Carry Out the Plan

Show your work! Execute your strategy step by step.

✅ Model Answer for Step 3

Substituting c = 30 - w into 2c + 4w = 74:

2(30 - w) + 4w = 74
60 - 2w + 4w = 74
60 + 2w = 74
2w = 14
w = 7 (cows)

Finding chickens:
c = 30 - w = 30 - 7 = 23 (chickens)

Step 4: Look Back

Check your answer. Does it make sense? Verify it works!

✅ Model Answer for Step 4

Verification:

Check heads: 23 chickens + 7 cows = 30 heads ✓
Check legs: 23(2) + 7(4) = 46 + 28 = 74 legs ✓

Does it make sense? Yes! Both conditions are satisfied.

Final Answer: The farmer has 23 chickens and 7 cows.
