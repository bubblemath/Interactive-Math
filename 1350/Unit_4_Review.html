⚠️ The Percent Addition Trap

You can only add percentages when they refer to the SAME base!

❌ "Leo got a 10% raise, then another 10% raise. His total raise was 20%." — FALSE! (Different bases)

✅ "Emanual spent 25% on food and 40% on housing. He spent 65% of his salary." — TRUE! (Same base)

🍎 Educator's Pedagogy Lab

⚠️ The Percent Addition Trap (From Your Materials)

❌ Leo's Raises — FALSE!

"Leo got a 10% raise at the end of his first year on the job and a 10% raise after another year. His total raise was 20% of his original salary."

Why it's wrong: The second 10% is calculated on the NEW (higher) salary, not the original. If original = $100: Year 1: $100 + $10 = $110. Year 2: $110 + $11 = $121. Total raise = 21%, not 20%!

❌ Juanita's Credit Cards — FALSE!

"Juanita paid 45% of their first credit card bill of $620 and 48% of the second credit card bill of $380. They paid 93% of the total bill."

Why it's wrong: Different bases! 45% of $620 = $279. 48% of $380 = $182.40. Total paid = $461.40. Total bill = $1000. Actual percent = 46.14%, NOT 93%!

✅ Emanual's Budget — TRUE!

"Emanual spent 25% of his salary on food and 40% on housing. Emanual spent 65% of his salary on food and housing."

Why it works: SAME BASE! Both percentages are of his salary. 25% + 40% = 65% ✓

🔢 Common Decimal Misconceptions

❌ "0.45 > 0.5 because 45 > 5"

Teaching Strategy: Use a 10×10 grid! Show that 0.5 = 50/100 (50 squares shaded) while 0.45 = 45/100 (45 squares shaded). Visually, 50 > 45.

❌ "6% = 0.6"

Teaching Strategy: Emphasize: "Percent means per HUNDRED." To convert, divide by 100 (move decimal TWO places left). 6% = 6/100 = 0.06, NOT 0.6!

📚 Word Problems from Your Materials

🎟️ Ticket Comparison (Unit Rate)

"Option 1: 12 tickets for $15. Option 2: 20 tickets for $23. Which is the better deal?"

Solution: Find unit rates! Option 1: $15 ÷ 12 = $1.25/ticket. Option 2: $23 ÷ 20 = $1.15/ticket. Option 2 is better!

📐 Scale Drawing

"A map has a scale of 1 inch : 600 feet. If two buildings are 3.5 inches apart on the map, how far apart are they in real life?"

Solution: Set up proportion: 1/600 = 3.5/x. Cross-multiply: x = 3.5 × 600 = 2,100 feet.

💰 Simple Interest

"A loan of $2040 has been made at 7.5% for four months using simple interest. Find the loan's future value."

Solution: I = Prt = $2040 × 0.075 × (4/12) = $51. Future Value = $2040 + $51 = $2091.
