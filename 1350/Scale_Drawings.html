📖 What is Scale?

📝 Definition from Your Course Materials

Scale: The ratio of the length in a drawing to the length of the real object.

🔑 Key Idea

Scale tells you how the drawing size relates to the real-world size.

Scale = Drawing Length : Actual Length

Common Scale Notations
Notation Meaning Example Use
1 inch : 600 feet 1 inch on map = 600 feet in real life City maps
1 : 100 1 unit on drawing = 100 units actual Blueprints
1 cm = 10 km 1 cm on map = 10 km in real life Country maps
1/4" = 1' 1/4 inch = 1 foot Architectural plans
Real-World Applications

🗺️ Maps

Road maps, city maps, and GPS systems all use scale to represent large distances on small screens or paper.

🏠 Blueprints

Architects use scale drawings to plan buildings. A 1:100 scale means 1 cm on paper = 1 meter in real life.

🚗 Model Cars

A 1:24 scale model car is 24 times smaller than the real car.

🔍 Reading Scales

📝 Interpreting Scale Notation

A scale like 1 inch : 600 feet means:

  • Every 1 inch on the drawing represents 600 feet in real life
  • The drawing is much smaller than the actual object
Scale as a Fraction

A scale can be written as a fraction for use in proportions:

1 inch : 600 feet
=
1 inch
600 feet

🔑 Setting Up Proportions

Drawing Length / Actual Length = Scale Drawing / Scale Actual

⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. Flipping the ratio: Always keep drawing units in the numerator and actual units in the denominator!

2. Ignoring units: Check if the scale uses different units (inches vs. feet) before calculating.

3. Misinterpreting direction: 1:600 means the drawing is 600 times SMALLER, not larger!
