🎯 Key Insight from Your Course Materials

"The division of whole numbers is more difficult than multiplication of whole numbers. Given that a, b ∈ W, note that a · b ∈ W, but that a ÷ b may or may not be in the set of whole numbers."

This means division is NOT closed on whole numbers!

Definition of Division

Division is defined as the inverse of multiplication.

Let a and b be whole numbers, with b ≠ 0.
Then a ÷ b = c if and only if a = b · c

a is the dividend (what we're dividing)

b is the divisor (what we're dividing by)

c is the quotient (the result)

🔄 Two Interpretations of Division

The Same Problem, Two Different Questions

The expression 18 ÷ 3 can be interpreted two completely different ways!

📦 Measurement (Quotitive)

"How many groups?"

"Ms. Sallay's class consists of 18 students and she wants to split the students into cooperative learning groups of 3 students. How many groups are needed?"

We KNOW the group size (3), we're finding the NUMBER of groups.

Answer: 6 groups

🍪 Partitive (Sharing)

"How many in each group?"

"Suppose we have 18 cookies and want to give an equal number of cookies to each of 3 friends. How many should each person receive?"

We KNOW the number of groups (3), we're finding the SIZE of each group.

Answer: 6 cookies each

📚 Teaching Tip

  • Measurement is often easier for students to model with manipulatives (make groups of 3 until you run out)
  • Partitive connects to real-world sharing situations students understand
  • Use word problems to help students identify which interpretation applies
  • Both interpretations give the same numerical answer, but the MEANING is different

⚖️ Properties of Division

⚠️ Important: Division is Different from Multiplication!

Division does NOT have the same properties as multiplication. Understanding these differences is crucial for teaching.

Property Comparison
Property Multiplication Division
Closure ✅ a × b is always a whole number ❌ a ÷ b may NOT be a whole number
(e.g., 7 ÷ 3 ∉ W)
Commutative ✅ a × b = b × a ❌ a ÷ b ≠ b ÷ a
(e.g., 12 ÷ 3 ≠ 3 ÷ 12)
Associative ✅ (a × b) × c = a × (b × c) ❌ (a ÷ b) ÷ c ≠ a ÷ (b ÷ c)
(e.g., (24÷4)÷2 ≠ 24÷(4÷2))
Identity ✅ a × 1 = a ✅ a ÷ 1 = a (only on the right!)
Special Cases

✅ Division by 1

a ÷ 1 = a

Any number divided by 1 equals itself.

Example: 18 ÷ 1 = 18

✅ Zero Divided by a Number

0 ÷ a = 0 (when a ≠ 0)

Zero divided by any non-zero number equals zero.

Example: 0 ÷ 5 = 0

🚫 Division by Zero

a ÷ 0 is UNDEFINED

We CANNOT divide by zero! There is no number c such that 0 × c = a (when a ≠ 0).

Why? If 6 ÷ 0 = c, then 0 × c = 6. But 0 × anything = 0, never 6!
