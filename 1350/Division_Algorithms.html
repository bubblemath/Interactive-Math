The Division Algorithm (Theorem)

For any whole numbers a and b, with b ≠ 0:

a = b · q + r

where 0 ≤ r < b

a = dividend (what we're dividing)

b = divisor (what we're dividing by)

q = quotient (how many times b goes into a)

r = remainder (what's left over)

🤔 For Your Teaching Practice

"Different students learn differently. Some need the concrete model first, others can jump to partial quotients. The goal is understanding, not just procedure. Ask yourself: 'Which algorithm helps THIS student understand division?'"

➖ Repeated Subtraction

The Foundation of Division

Repeated subtraction directly models the question: "How many times can we subtract the divisor from the dividend?"

This connects to the measurement model of division.

📚 Teaching Tip

  • This method is inefficient for large numbers but builds understanding
  • Students can use manipulatives to physically remove groups
  • Ask: "How many times did we subtract?" to find the quotient
  • This naturally leads to partial quotients: "What if we subtract MORE at once?"

📊 Partial Quotients Algorithm

From Your Course Materials

The Partial Quotients Algorithm allows students to use "friendly" numbers they're comfortable with, then add up all the partial quotients at the end.

Partial Quotients Visualizer

Enter values and click "Calculate" or "Animate"

How It Works
1
Choose a "friendly" multiple of the divisor
Pick a number you can easily multiply (10×, 100×, 5×, 2×)
2
Subtract that multiple from the dividend
Record the partial quotient on the side
3
Repeat until remainder < divisor
Keep subtracting friendly multiples
4
Add all partial quotients
The sum is your final quotient

📚 Teaching Tip

  • Students can choose ANY multiple they're comfortable with
  • More efficient choices (100×, 50×) lead to fewer steps
  • This method builds number sense and mental math skills
  • Great for students who struggle with the standard algorithm

✍️ Standard Long Division

From Your Course Materials

The Standard Division Algorithm follows the pattern: Divide, Multiply, Subtract, Bring Down (DMSB).

Standard Long Division Visualizer

Enter values and click "Calculate" or "Animate"

The DMSB Process
D
DIVIDE
How many times does the divisor go into the current digits?
M
MULTIPLY
Multiply the quotient digit by the divisor
S
SUBTRACT
Subtract the product from the current number
B
BRING DOWN
Bring down the next digit and repeat

📚 Teaching Tip

  • Use the mnemonic "Does McDonald's Serve Burgers?" for DMSB
  • Connect each step to the concrete model (base-ten blocks)
  • Common errors: forgetting to bring down, incorrect subtraction
  • Practice estimation first: "About how many times does 7 go into 96?"

⚡ Short Division

From Your Course Materials

"Short Division: A modification of the Standard Division Algorithm" - This condensed method keeps the work mental, writing only the quotient and small remainder notations.

Short Division Visualizer

Enter values and click "Calculate" or "Animate"

How Short Division Works

Short division condenses the standard algorithm by:

  • Writing only the quotient above the dividend
  • Keeping remainders as small superscripts before the next digit
  • Doing multiplication and subtraction mentally

📚 Teaching Tip

  • Short division works best with single-digit divisors
  • Students should master standard long division first
  • The small superscript numbers represent "carrying" the remainder
  • This method is faster once students are fluent with basic facts
