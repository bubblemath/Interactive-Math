📖 What is a Proportion?

📝 Definition from Your Course Materials

Proportion: A statement that two given ratios are equal.

a/b = c/d if and only if ad = bc

🔑 Key Idea

A proportion is an equation that says two ratios are equal.

Think of it as a "balanced scale" where both sides have the same value.

Real-World Examples

👕 Shopping

If 2 shirts cost $20, then 4 shirts cost $40.

Proportion: 2/20 = 4/40

Proportion: 3/8 = x/120

🚗 Travel

If you travel 180 miles in 3 hours, how far in 5 hours?

Proportion: 180/3 = x/5

❌ Cross Products

📝 The Fundamental Property of Proportions

From your course materials:

a/b = c/d if and only if ad = bc

🔑 Cross Products Rule

In a proportion, the product of the extremes equals the product of the means.

a × d = b × c

⚠️ Important Warning!

Cross-multiplication only works when there is an EQUALS sign between two ratios!

You cannot cross-multiply when adding, subtracting, or comparing ratios without an equation.

Example from Your Materials

🖍️ Glue Sticks Problem

Problem: If there are 3 glue sticks for every 8 students in the 3rd grade, how many glue sticks for all 120 3rd grade students?

Set up: 3/8 = x/120

Cross multiply: 8 × x = 3 × 120

Simplify: 8x = 360

Divide: x = 360 ÷ 8 = 45 glue sticks

Worked Example

📝 Is x = 10 correct for 2/5 = x/25?

Substitute: 2/5 = 10/25

Method 1 (Cross Products):

2 × 25 = 50

5 × 10 = 50

50 = 50 ✓

Method 2 (Simplify):

10/25 = 2/5 (divide by 5)

2/5 = 2/5 ✓

Yes, x = 10 is correct!
