📋 Learning Objectives

  • Understand division through various models
  • Explain the inverse relationship between multiplication and division
  • Understand special cases of division by 0 and 1
  • Apply division algorithms including the standard algorithm
  • Develop mental division skills and estimation techniques
  • Understand divisibility, factors, and multiples
  • Apply divisibility tests for 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, and 11

1 Division Models & Concepts

Build conceptual understanding of what division means through multiple models. Explore the Repeated Subtraction Model (Ms. Sallay's cooperative learning groups), Set (Partition) Model (sharing cookies among friends), Missing Factor Model, Number Line Model, and Array Model. Understand the TWO interpretations: Measurement (How many groups?) and Partitive (How many in each group?).

2 Properties & Special Cases

Understand the rules and restrictions of division. Explore the inverse relationship between multiplication and division through fact families. Learn the special cases: Division by 1 (a ÷ 1 = a), Zero divided by a number (0 ÷ a = 0), and why Division by 0 is UNDEFINED. Discover why division is NOT closed on whole numbers.

Fact Families Division by Zero Closure Property Inverse Operations

3 Division Algorithms

Compare multiple division algorithms to match methods to student learning styles. Explore Repeated Subtraction, Partial Quotients Algorithm, Scaffold Algorithm, Standard Long Division (DMSB: Divide, Multiply, Subtract, Bring Down), and Short Division. Step-by-step animations show each algorithm in action with student input for custom problems.

4 Division in Other Bases

Deepen place value understanding by dividing in unfamiliar number systems. Use the Base-2 and Base-5 multiplication tables to find quotients. Practice division in Base-2 (binary), Base-5, and Base-8. Includes a multi-base converter and the "What Base Am I?" game to build number sense across systems.

5 Mental Division & Estimation

Build number sense and computational fluency with mental division strategies: Breaking Up the Dividend, Using Compatible Numbers, Dividing by Powers of 10, and Halving. Master estimation techniques including Front-End Estimation and Compatible Numbers. Includes a Strategy Selector challenge and practice zone.

Master tests for determining if a number is divisible without dividing. Learn divisibility rules for 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, and 11 with step-by-step explanations. Includes an interactive divisibility checker, factor finder, "Which Numbers Divide Evenly?" game, and a preview of GCF/LCM connections.
