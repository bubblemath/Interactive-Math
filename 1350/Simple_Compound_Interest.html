📚 Interest Concepts

📝 From Your Course Materials

Simple Interest: "Interest calculated only on the principal, and it is only calculated once at the end of the time period."

Compound Interest: "Interest computed on the original principal as well as on any accumulated interest."

Key Formulas

Simple Interest

I = Prt
A = P(1 + rt)
Linear growth

Compound Interest

A = P(1 + r/n)nt
Exponential growth

Why the Difference?

🔑 The Key Insight

Simple Interest: You earn interest only on your original principal. The interest is the same every period.

Compound Interest: You earn interest on your principal AND on previously earned interest. This creates exponential growth!

Example with $1000 at 8% for 1 year:

Simple: $1000 × 0.08 × 1 = $80 interest

Compound (quarterly): Each quarter earns interest on the growing balance!

Q1: $1000 × 0.02 = $20 → Balance: $1020

Q2: $1020 × 0.02 = $20.40 → Balance: $1040.40

Q3: $1040.40 × 0.02 = $20.81 → Balance: $1061.21

Q4: $1061.21 × 0.02 = $21.22 → Balance: $1082.43
