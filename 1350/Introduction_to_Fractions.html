📖 What is a Fraction?

📝 From Your Course Materials

"Children are exposed to fraction words before being introduced to the formal definition of a fraction."

  • "Cut my sandwich into fourths."
  • "The tooth fairy left me a quarter (quarter-dollar)"
  • "Give me half the cookie"
  • "I got 9 out of 10 correct on my spelling test."

"So, you can use this to your advantage when introducing fractions to help them understand that fractions are parts of a whole."

Definition of a Fraction

To interpret the meaning of a fraction ab, we must agree on:

# Agreement Example
1 The "whole" that is being considered The whole pizza, a cup of water, an inch
2 The whole is subdivided into "b" equal parts Pizza cut into 8 equal slices
3 There are "a" number of selected parts You ate 3 of the 8 slices
a ← Numerator (parts selected) b ← Denominator (equal parts in whole), b ≠ 0
The Set of Rational Numbers
Set of Numbers Symbol Examples
Natural (Counting) ℕ 1, 2, 3, ...
Whole W 0, 1, 2, 3, ...
Integers ℤ ..., −3, −2, −1, 0, 1, 2, 3, ...
Rational ℚ ℚ = {a/b | a, b ∈ ℤ and b ≠ 0}

ℕ ⊂ W ⊂ ℤ ⊂ ℚ

📚 Teaching Tip

Connect fractions to students' everyday experiences:

  • Sharing pizza or cookies equally
  • Telling time (half past, quarter to)
  • Measuring ingredients in cooking
  • Sports statistics (batting average, free throw percentage)

⚖️ Equivalent Fractions

📝 Fundamental Law of Fractions

Let ab be a fraction. Then:

ab = a × nb × n, for any integer n ≠ 0

"Fractions that express the same quantity are called equivalent or equal fractions."

✂️ Simplifying Fractions

📝 From Your Course Materials

"The Fundamental Law of Fractions is helpful for simplifying fractions as well as finding common denominators."

A fraction is in simplest form (or lowest terms) when the GCF of the numerator and denominator is 1.

Simplify a Fraction
/
Method: Using GCF

To simplify a fraction, divide both numerator and denominator by their GCF:

Step 1: Find the GCF of numerator and denominator
Step 2: Divide both by the GCF
Step 3: The result is in simplest form

Example: Simplify 12/18

GCF(12, 18) = 6

12 18
=
12 ÷ 6 18 ÷ 6
=
2 3
