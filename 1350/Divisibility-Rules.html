🎯 From Your Course Materials

Definition: If a is a non-negative integer and b is a positive integer, we say that a is divisible by b, or that b divides a, if and only if the remainder is 0 when a is divided by b.

Notation: "b divides a" is written as b | a

If b | a, then b is a factor or divisor of a, and a is a multiple of b.

Why Learn Divisibility Rules?
  • Quick mental checks - Determine divisibility without long division
  • Finding factors - Essential for simplifying fractions
  • GCF and LCM - Foundation for future lessons
  • Prime factorization - Identifying prime numbers
  • Number sense - Deeper understanding of number properties

🤔 For Your Teaching Practice

"Introduce divisibility rules in order of ease, not numerical order. Start with 2, 5, and 10 (just look at the last digit!), then move to 4 and 8, then 3 and 9 (digit sums), and finally composite rules like 6."

📋 Complete Divisibility Rules

2 Divisibility by 2

If the last digit is even (0, 2, 4, 6, 8), the number is divisible by 2.

3,458 → Last digit is 8 (even) ✓

3 Divisibility by 3

If the sum of all digits is divisible by 3, the number is divisible by 3.

351 → 3+5+1 = 9, and 9÷3 = 3 ✓

4 Divisibility by 4

If the last two digits form a number divisible by 4, the number is divisible by 4.

97,128 → 28 ÷ 4 = 7 ✓

5 Divisibility by 5

If the last digit is 0 or 5, the number is divisible by 5.

7,815 → Last digit is 5 ✓

6 Divisibility by 6

If the number is divisible by BOTH 2 AND 3, it's divisible by 6.

354 → Even (2✓) AND 3+5+4=12 (3✓) ✓

7 Divisibility by 7

Double the last digit, subtract from remaining digits. If result is divisible by 7, so is the original.

203 → 20 - (3×2) = 20 - 6 = 14 ÷ 7 ✓

8 Divisibility by 8

If the last three digits form a number divisible by 8, the number is divisible by 8.

8,096 → 096 ÷ 8 = 12 ✓

9 Divisibility by 9

If the sum of all digits is divisible by 9, the number is divisible by 9.

351 → 3+5+1 = 9, and 9÷9 = 1 ✓

10 Divisibility by 10

If the last digit is 0, the number is divisible by 10.

97,100 → Last digit is 0 ✓

11 Divisibility by 11

If the alternating sum of digits (subtract, add, subtract...) is divisible by 11.

918,082 → 9-1+8-0+8-2 = 22 ÷ 11 ✓

📚 Teaching Tip

  • Group by type: Last digit (2, 5, 10), Last digits (4, 8), Digit sum (3, 9), Composite (6)
  • Why do these work? Connect to place value and remainders
  • Rule of 7 is the hardest—save it for last or skip for younger students
  • Rule of 6 shows how composite numbers work: 6 = 2 × 3
