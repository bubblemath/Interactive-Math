⚠️ The Percent Addition Trap

You can only add percentages when they refer to the SAME base!

❌ "Leo got a 10% raise, then another 10% raise. His total raise was 20%." — FALSE! (Different bases)

✅ "Emanual spent 25% on food and 40% on housing. He spent 65% of his salary." — TRUE! (Same base)

📋 Key Concepts

Percent Definition

n% = n/100 ("per cent" = "per hundred")

Percent ↔ Decimal

Move decimal 2 places (75% = 0.75)

Percent Proportion

is/of = %/100

Simple Interest

I = Prt (linear growth)

Compound Interest

A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt) (exponential growth)

Same Base Rule

Only add percents with the same base!
