🔑 Terminating Decimal Rule

A fraction a/b (in lowest terms) terminates ⟺ b has only 2s and 5s as prime factors
Because 10 = 2 × 5, only denominators with factors of 2 and 5 can become powers of 10

📖 Key Concepts

Decimal Notation

Place value system extending to the right of the decimal point using powers of 10

Terminating Decimal

A decimal with a finite number of digits (e.g., 0.75, 0.125)

Repeating Decimal

A non-terminating decimal with a repeating pattern (e.g., 0.333..., 0.142857...)

Repetend

The block of digits that repeats in a repeating decimal

Period

The length of the repetend (number of repeating digits)

Bar Notation

A horizontal line (vinculum) over the repetend: 0.3̄ = 0.333...

Fundamental Law of Fractions

a/b = (a × n)/(b × n) for any integer n ≠ 0

0.999... = 1

A mathematical fact, not an approximation - they are the same number
