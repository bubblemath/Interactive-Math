📚 Understanding Integer Addition

🔑 Key Concept: Zero Pairs

When a positive chip (+1) and a negative chip (-1) are combined, they form a zero pair:

+ + − = 0

This is because a number plus its opposite equals zero: 1 + (-1) = 0

📋 Rules for Adding Integers

  • Same Signs: Add the absolute values, keep the common sign
    Example: 5 + 3 = 8 and (-5) + (-3) = -8
  • Different Signs: Subtract the absolute values, take the sign of the larger absolute value
    Example: 5 + (-3) = 2 and (-5) + 3 = -2
  • Opposites: A number plus its opposite equals zero
    Example: 5 + (-5) = 0

🔴🟡 Chip/Counter Model

How It Works

Use colored chips to represent integers:

+ = +1 (Yellow/Positive) − = -1 (Red/Negative)

When a yellow chip meets a red chip, they form a zero pair and cancel out!

📚 Teaching Tip

  • Use physical two-color counters for hands-on learning
  • Have students physically pair up positive and negative chips
  • Emphasize that zero pairs "cancel out" or "make zero"
  • This model builds understanding before introducing rules

📏 Number Line Model

How It Works

  • Start at the first number on the number line
  • Adding a positive: Jump right (→)
  • Adding a negative: Jump left (←)
  • End point is your answer

Step-by-Step Examples

📚 Teaching Tip

  • Use a floor number line for kinesthetic learners
  • Have students physically walk the number line
  • Connect to thermometers (vertical number line)
  • Emphasize: positive = right, negative = left

📝 Word Problem Model

📝 From Your Course Materials

"A useful approach to the addition of integers is by means of stories."

📬 Example from Your Materials

"You receive two letters in the mail. One letter is a bill for $2 and the other is a bill for $4. Are you richer or poorer and by how much?"

Translation: (-2) + (-4) = ?

Answer: -6 (You are $6 poorer)
