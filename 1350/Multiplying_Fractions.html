📚 Multiplying Fractions Overview

📝 Definition from Your Course Materials

Let a/b and c/d be two fractions. Then the product of the two fractions is defined as:

(a/b) × (c/d) = (a·c) / (b·d)

Multiply the numerators together and multiply the denominators together.

Key Concepts
Type Example Process
Fraction × Fraction 2/7 × 3/5 (2×3)/(7×5) = 6/35
Whole × Fraction 3 × 2/5 (3×2)/5 = 6/5 = 1 1/5
Mixed Numbers 2 1/2 × 2 1/2 5/2 × 5/2 = 25/4 = 6 1/4

📐 The Multiplication Algorithm

📝 Step-by-Step Process

  1. Multiply the numerators together
  2. Multiply the denominators together
  3. Simplify the result if possible

🔲 Area/Grid Model

📝 From Your Course Materials

The area model shows fraction multiplication visually. The grid represents a unit square (1 whole), and we shade:

  • ■ First fraction horizontally (rows)
  • ■ Second fraction vertically (columns)
  • ■ The overlap is the product!
Interactive Grid Model
×
First fraction (rows)
Second fraction (columns)
Overlap = Product

📚 Teaching Tip

The grid model is powerful because:

  • Students can COUNT the overlapping squares
  • It shows WHY we multiply numerators and denominators
  • The total grid squares = product of denominators
  • The overlapping squares = product of numerators

🔢 Mixed Numbers

📝 From Your Course Materials

Two methods for multiplying mixed numbers:

  • Method 1: Convert all mixed numbers to improper fractions, then multiply
  • Method 2: Use the Distributive Property (FOIL-like)

✂️ Cross-Canceling

📝 Simplifying Before Multiplying

Cross-canceling (or cross-reducing) simplifies the multiplication by dividing common factors BEFORE multiplying. This makes the numbers smaller and easier to work with.

Interactive Cross-Cancel
×
Example from Your Materials: 5/12 × 9/10
1

Look for common factors diagonally

5 and 10 share factor 5 (5÷5=1, 10÷5=2)

9 and 12 share factor 3 (9÷3=3, 12÷3=4)

2

Cancel and simplify

51/12 × 9/102

5/124 × 93/10

3

Multiply the simplified fractions

1/4 × 3/2 = 3/8

5/12 × 9/10 = 3/8
