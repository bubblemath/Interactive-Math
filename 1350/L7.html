⚖️ The Fundamental Relationship

GCF(a, b) × LCM(a, b) = a × b

This powerful relationship allows you to find LCM if you know GCF: LCM = (a × b) ÷ GCF

🔑 Key Concepts

Prime Number

A positive integer with exactly two distinct divisors: 1 and itself. By definition, 1 is NOT prime.

Composite Number

An integer greater than 1 that has positive factors other than 1 and itself.

Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic

Every composite number can be written as a product of primes in one and only one way (except for order).

GCF Method

When using prime factorization, take the MINIMUM exponent of each common prime.

LCM Method

When using prime factorization, take the MAXIMUM exponent of each prime (all primes).

Relatively Prime

Two numbers are relatively prime if GCF = 1. In this case, LCM = a × b.

"When using prime factorization for GCF, we take the smallest exponent. But when we use prime factorization for LCM, we are taking the largest exponent."
