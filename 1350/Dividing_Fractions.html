📚 Dividing Fractions Overview

📝 Definition from Your Course Materials

Let a/b and c/d be two fractions with c ≠ 0. Then:

(a/b) ÷ (c/d) = (a/b) × (d/c) = (ad)/(bc)

To divide by a fraction, multiply by its reciprocal.

Key Concepts
Concept Explanation Example
Reciprocal Flip the fraction (swap numerator and denominator) Reciprocal of 3/4 is 4/3
Invert & Multiply Change ÷ to × and flip the second fraction 2/3 ÷ 4/5 = 2/3 × 5/4
Common Denominator Alternative method: same denominator, divide numerators 8/12 ÷ 9/12 = 8/9
Two Interpretations of Division
Type Question Example: 3/4 ÷ 1/4
Measurement "How many groups of c/d are in a/b?" How many 1/4s are in 3/4? Answer: 3
Partitive "If a/b is divided into c/d equal parts, how big is each part?" If 3/4 is 1/4 of something, what's the whole?

🔄 Invert and Multiply

📝 The Algorithm

To divide fractions:

  1. Keep the first fraction the same
  2. Change division (÷) to multiplication (×)
  3. Flip the second fraction (use its reciprocal)
  4. Multiply and simplify

Memory aid: "Keep, Change, Flip" or "KCF"

🤔 Why Does "Invert and Multiply" Work?

📝 The Mathematical Reasoning

Division is the inverse of multiplication. When we divide by a fraction, we're asking: "What do I multiply by to get this result?"

Proof: Using the Definition of Division
1

Start with the division

(a/b) ÷ (c/d) = ?

2

Write as a complex fraction

a/b
c/d

3

Multiply top and bottom by the reciprocal of the denominator (d/c)

This equals 1, so we're not changing the value!

= (a/b) × (d/c) / (c/d) × (d/c)

4

The denominator becomes 1

(c/d) × (d/c) = cd/dc = 1

= (a/b) × (d/c) / 1 = (a/b) × (d/c)

Visual Understanding: "How Many Groups?"

Example: 3/4 ÷ 1/4 = ?

Question: "How many 1/4s are in 3/4?"

3/4 (the dividend)
Each 1/4 (the divisor)

Answer: There are 3 groups of 1/4 in 3/4, so 3/4 ÷ 1/4 = 3

📚 Teaching Tip

Students often memorize "invert and multiply" without understanding WHY. Use these approaches:

  • Measurement model: "How many 1/2s are in 3?" (Answer: 6)
  • Pattern discovery: Start with whole number division, then fractions
  • Complex fraction proof: For advanced students

📊 Common Denominator Method

📝 Alternative Algorithm

When fractions have the same denominator, you can simply divide the numerators:

(a/c) ÷ (b/c) = a/b

This method can be easier for some students to understand conceptually.

Interactive: Compare Both Methods
÷
Example: 2/3 ÷ 3/4

Method 1: Invert & Multiply

2/3 ÷ 3/4

= 2/3 × 4/3

= 8/9

Method 2: Common Denominator

2/3 ÷ 3/4

= 8/12 ÷ 9/12

= 8/9

Both methods give the same answer: 8/9

🔢 Dividing Mixed Numbers

📝 Process for Mixed Numbers

  1. Convert all mixed numbers to improper fractions
  2. Apply "invert and multiply"
  3. Simplify and convert back to mixed number if needed
