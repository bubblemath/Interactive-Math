📝 From Your Course Materials

"Laura wants to buy a blouse originally priced at $26.50 but now on sale at 40% off. She has $17 in her wallet and wonders if she has enough cash. How can she mentally find out? (Ignore the sales tax.)"

The Four Shopping Calculations
Type Formula Example
Discount Sale Price = Original × (1 - Discount%) $50 at 20% off = $50 × 0.80 = $40
Sales Tax Total = Price × (1 + Tax%) $40 + 8% tax = $40 × 1.08 = $43.20
Tip Total = Bill × (1 + Tip%) $30 + 20% tip = $30 × 1.20 = $36
Markup Selling Price = Cost × (1 + Markup%) $25 cost + 40% markup = $25 × 1.40 = $35

🔑 The Universal Formula

Final = Original × (1 ± Rate)

Use minus for discounts, plus for tax/tip/markup

Order of Operations

⚠️ Important: Discount FIRST, then Tax!

1
Apply discount to get the sale price
2
Calculate tax on the sale price (not original!)
3
Add tax to get the final total

Example: $100 item, 20% off, 8% tax
Step 1: $100 × 0.80 = $80 (sale price)
Step 2: $80 × 0.08 = $6.40 (tax)
Step 3: $80 + $6.40 = $86.40 (final)

🧮 The Math Behind It

Finding the Break-Even Point

1
Set the savings equal: Price × Percent = Dollar Amount
2
Solve for Price: Price = Dollar ÷ Percent
3
Example: 20% vs $10 → $10 ÷ 0.20 = $50 break-even

Rule of Thumb:
Below break-even → Dollar coupon is better
Above break-even → Percent coupon is better

🧠 Mental Math Strategies

📝 Laura's Problem (From Your Materials)

"Laura wants to buy a blouse originally priced at $26.50 but now on sale at 40% off. She has $17 in her wallet and wonders if she has enough cash. How can she mentally find out?"

Strategy 1: Find the Discount First
1
Round up: $26.50 ≈ $27 (easier to work with)
2
Find 40%: 40% = 4 × 10%, and 10% of $27 = $2.70
3
Calculate: 4 × $2.70 = $10.80 discount
4
Subtract: $27 - $10.80 ≈ $16.20
✓
Answer: Yes! $16.20 < $17, so Laura has enough!
Strategy 2: Find What You Pay (Complement)
1
Think: 40% off means you pay 60% (100% - 40% = 60%)
2
Break it down: 60% = 50% + 10%
3
Calculate 50%: Half of $26.50 = $13.25
4
Calculate 10%: $26.50 ÷ 10 = $2.65
5
Add: $13.25 + $2.65 = $15.90
✓
Answer: Yes! $15.90 < $17, so Laura has enough!
Quick Mental Math Reference
To Find... Mental Strategy
10% Move decimal one place left ($45 → $4.50)
5% Find 10%, then halve it
15% Find 10% + 5% (great for tips!)
20% Find 10%, then double it
25% Divide by 4 (quarter)
50% Divide by 2 (half)
