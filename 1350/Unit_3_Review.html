🍎 Educator's Pedagogy Lab

⚠️ Common Student Misconceptions

❌ "−5 is greater than −2 because 5 is bigger than 2"

Teaching Strategy: Use a number line! Show that −5 is to the LEFT of −2, so −5 < −2. "Further left = smaller"

❌ "−3² = 9"

Teaching Strategy: Emphasize that −3² means −(3²) = −9, while (−3)² = (−3)(−3) = 9. The exponent only applies to what it's directly attached to!

❌ "1/2 + 1/3 = 2/5"

Teaching Strategy: Use the Area Model! Show that 1/2 and 1/3 are different-sized pieces. You can't add pieces of different sizes without finding a common denominator first.

❌ "GCF uses MAX exponents"

Teaching Strategy: Memory trick: "GCF = Greatest Common Factor, but use the LEAST (MIN) exponents." Or: "GCF is smaller, LCM is larger."

📚 Word Problem Models (From Your Materials)

💰 The Mail Carrier Model (Integers)

"The mail carrier brings you a check for $5 and a bill for $2. Are you richer or poorer?"

Answer: +5 + (−2) = +3. You're $3 richer!

🍪 Bob, Dean, and Charlie (Partition Model)

"Suppose we have 18 cookies and want to give an equal number to each of three friends. How many should each receive?"

This is the Set (Partition) Model of division: 18 ÷ 3 = 6 cookies each.

🌭 Hot Dogs & Buns (LCM)

"Hot dogs come in packs of 10, buns come in packs of 8. What's the smallest number of each to buy so none are left over?"

LCM(10, 8) = 40. Buy 4 packs of hot dogs and 5 packs of buns!
