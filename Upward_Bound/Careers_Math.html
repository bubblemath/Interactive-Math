What calculus actually does for a career: It isn't just a class to get through — calculus is the language of change, optimization, and modeling. Every time an engineer calculates stress on a bridge, a data scientist trains a neural network, a pharmacist models drug absorption, or an economist forecasts market trends — they're using ideas that come directly from calculus. Even if you never solve an integral at your job, taking calculus proves you can handle rigorous quantitative reasoning, which is what hiring managers in these fields are screening for.
The stats path also matters: If calculus isn't your goal, statistics is still a powerful on-ramp to data science, public health, actuarial science, and research. A student who takes statistics seriously can reach many of the same high-paying roles — especially in data and healthcare — through a slightly different route.
