02 / Skills

12 Core Skills

Algebra 1 1
Solving Linear Equations
Single and multi-step equations — the engine behind every angle calculation and segment proof.
Algebra 1 2
The Coordinate Plane
Plotting, distance formula, midpoint formula, and slope — essential for all coordinate geometry.
Algebra 1 3
Linear Equations & Lines
Writing and interpreting equations in slope-intercept form; identifying parallel and perpendicular lines.
Algebra 1 4
Substitution & Systems
Solving systems algebraically and graphically; used constantly in coordinate and analytic geometry.
Spatial 5
Classifying Geometric Figures
Points, lines, angles, polygons, circles — precise vocabulary is the foundation of all geometric reasoning.
Spatial 6
Angle Relationships
Complementary, supplementary, vertical angles, and linear pairs — identifying and solving with each type.
Spatial 7
Perimeter, Area & Measurement
Triangles, quadrilaterals, and circles; decomposing irregular shapes; applying to real-world contexts.
Spatial 8
3D Visualization
Nets, cross-sections, and recognizing 3D solids from 2D representations.
Proof 9
Conditional Statements
If-then thinking, identifying hypothesis vs. conclusion, writing converses — the grammar of geometric proof.
Proof 10
Definitions, Postulates & Theorems
Understanding the hierarchy of geometric truth — what we define, what we assume, what we must prove.
Proof 11
Two-Column Proof
Using given information and known facts to reach a logical conclusion, one justified step at a time.
Proof 12
Inductive vs. Deductive Reasoning
Distinguishing patterns (inductive) from guaranteed logical conclusions (deductive) — a lifelong thinking skill.
03 / Activities

In-Class Activities

Day 1 / Icebreaker
"Geometry Everywhere" Scavenger Hunt
Students have 10 minutes to find and photograph or sketch 10 geometric figures in the school: a right angle, parallel lines, a polygon, a circle, an acute angle, and more. Anchors abstract vocabulary in the physical world immediately and gets everyone moving.
Day 110 minWhole class
Algebra Review / Visual
Coordinate Art
Students follow a sequence of ordered pairs to produce a geometric picture on graph paper, then answer embedded questions: slope between points, distance, midpoint. Algebra skills wrapped in something visual and satisfying — and they keep the drawing.
Week 145 minIndividual
Stations Lab
Angle Relationship Stations
Six stations around the room, each with a protractor task, a diagram to analyze, or an equation to solve using angle relationships. Students rotate in pairs every 12 minutes. Covers all six major angle relationships in one high-engagement session.
Week 275 minPairs
Applied Math
Real-World Measurement Challenge
Teams receive a "client brief" — a fictional room, garden, or sports field — and must calculate perimeter, area, and cost of materials using given unit prices. Requires decomposing irregular shapes. Feels like real work because it is real work.
Week 260 minTeams of 3
Logic & Reasoning
Argument Chain Activity
Students work through logic puzzles, then translate their reasoning into an explicit chain: "I know __ → therefore __ → because __." Builds the habit of citing reasons for every claim — exactly what proof requires. Low stakes, high payoff.
Week 240 minPairs
Tactile Lab
Nets & Cross-Section Lab
Students predict, then fold, printed nets to verify which 3D solid each produces. Then they slice clay or playdough solids and draw the cross-sections they find. Tactile, visual, and deeply memorable — spatial reasoning in its most literal form.
Week 255 minSmall groups
Critical Thinking
"True, False, or It Depends?"
Students receive 20 geometric statement cards and sort them: always true, never true, or depends. Rich discussion erupts naturally. ("All squares are rectangles" — True. "All rectangles are squares" — False.) Reveals exactly which definitions need reinforcement.
Week 335 minSmall groups
Proof Intro
Scrambled Proof Puzzle
A complete two-column proof is cut into strips — all statements and reasons mixed up — and students reassemble it in logical order. Far less intimidating than writing from scratch, but teaches the exact same structure. Perfect first proof experience.
Week 345 minPairs
Daily Routine
"Notice & Wonder"
Each day opens with a geometric image displayed for 2 minutes. Students write what they notice and what they wonder. No wrong answers. Builds the careful observation habits that proof writing demands, and gives shy students a low-stakes daily voice.
Daily5–7 minIndividual
04 / Capstone

Capstone Project

The Assignment

"Geometry in
the Built World"

Students choose a real structure or designed object and analyze it geometrically and algebraically. The goal is to demonstrate that geometry isn't just in the classroom — it's in every building, pattern, and designed space around us.

  • Identify and name at least 6 geometric figures in their structure
  • Calculate 3 measurements (perimeter, area, or volume)
  • Identify 2 angle relationships with if-then reasoning
  • Write a 4–6 sentence "proof paragraph" about one property
  • Present as a poster, slide deck, or annotated diagram

05 / Careers

Where Spatial Reasoning Takes You

🏗️
Engineering
Civil Engineer
Uses geometric proofs, angle calculations, and coordinate geometry to design roads, bridges, and structures that must be precisely right.
📐
Design
Architect
Translates spatial imagination into precise geometric drawings — every blueprint is a proof that a structure will stand and function.
🎮
Technology
Game Designer
Programs 3D worlds using coordinate geometry, transformations, and angle calculations to create spaces players can move through.
🏙️
Planning
Urban Planner
Uses area, measurement, and coordinate geometry to design cities — deciding where streets, parks, and buildings should go and why.
🔬
Medicine
Surgeon
Relies on 3D spatial reasoning to navigate complex anatomy. Laparoscopic and robotic surgery demand extraordinary visual-spatial thinking.
🔍
Investigation
Forensic Scientist
Uses angle measurements, coordinate geometry, and geometric reasoning to reconstruct crime scenes from physical evidence.
🎨
Creative
Animator
Uses transformations, coordinate planes, and 3D geometry to move characters and objects convincingly through space.
🛋️
Design
Interior Designer
Uses area, perimeter, angles, and spatial visualization to plan rooms that are functional, proportional, and beautiful.
