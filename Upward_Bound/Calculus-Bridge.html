03

In-Class Activities

Daily Warm-Up
Algebra Bootcamp Sprints
10-minute daily warm-ups covering one targeted algebra skill. Gamified with a class leaderboard. Students track their own improvement across the three weeks.
10 min Daily Algebra fluency
Lab Activity
Marble Ramp Lab
Students roll a marble down a ramp and record distance vs. time. They compute average rates over shrinking intervals, intuitively discovering the concept of an instantaneous rate — and the derivative.
60 min Week 2 Hands-on Rate of change
Tech Lab
Desmos Exploration Labs
Visual, low-stakes graphing tasks on Desmos. "What breaks this function?" explores domain and discontinuities. Limit labs use slider-animated tables to watch values approach a point.
45 min Weeks 1–2 Technology
Collaborative
Card Sort: Functions
Small groups match function equations, their graphs, their tables of values, and a real-world context card. Surfaces misconceptions and builds multiple representations thinking.
40 min Week 1 Groupwork
Collaborative Review
Speed Dating Review
Each student becomes the "expert" on one problem type. Partners rotate every 4 minutes. Students both teach and learn — covering 8–10 topics in a single energetic session.
50 min Week 3 Peer teaching
Problem Solving
Broken Calculator Problems
Students must solve problems without using a specific "broken" calculator key (e.g., no division key, no square root). Forces algebraic reasoning and creative mathematical thinking.
20 min Any day Critical thinking
04

Capstone Project

"Real World Rates"

Students choose a real-world phenomenon, model it mathematically, and explore how its rate of change tells a story — a hands-on first encounter with the power of calculus thinking.

1
Choose Your Phenomenon
Select a real-world topic from the list — or pitch your own with approval.
2
Collect & Plot Data
Find real data, enter it in Desmos, and fit a function model to it.
3
Compute Rates of Change
Calculate average rates over multiple intervals. Interpret each one in context.
4
Predict the Instantaneous Rate
Use shrinking intervals to estimate the rate "right now." Describe what it means.
5
Present at Gallery Walk
Poster or slides — explain your math to peers and receive written feedback.

Where Calculus Takes You

🏗️
Engineering
Civil & Structural Engineer
Uses calculus to model stress and strain on bridges, buildings, and infrastructure — ensuring they stand under load.
💊
Medicine
Pharmacokineticist
Models how drugs move through the body over time — calculating dosage rates using differential equations.
🌍
Environmental Science
Climate Scientist
Uses calculus-based models to simulate atmospheric change, ocean currents, and climate feedback loops.
📈
Finance
Quantitative Analyst
Applies calculus to price financial instruments, manage risk, and build algorithmic trading strategies.
🎮
Game Development
Physics Programmer
Programs realistic motion, gravity, and collisions in video games using calculus-based physics engines.
🤖
AI & Machine Learning
ML Engineer
Uses gradient descent — a calculus optimization technique — to train every modern AI model, including the ones you interact with daily.
