03 / Activities

In-Class Activities

Icebreaker / Mindset
Math Biography
Students write 5 sentences about their math history: their best topic, their hardest moment, their favorite math teacher, and one thing they want to change. Sets a growth mindset tone on day one and gives you immediate insight into each student's relationship with math.
Day 1 20 min Whole class
High-Energy Review
Factoring Relay Race
Teams of 4 work on one long factoring problem, each person solving one step and passing the paper. Speed and accuracy both count. The room gets loud, competitive, and memorable — exactly what factoring deserves.
Week 1 30 min Teams of 4
Metacognition
"Which Method Wins?"
Given 6 different quadratic equations, students individually decide which solving method is most efficient for each, then defend their choices to a partner. Builds strategic thinking, not just procedural fluency — a skill Precalculus constantly demands.
Week 1 40 min Pairs
Creative Tech Lab
Desmos Function Art
Students use 6–8 parent function equations with domain restrictions to draw a simple image in Desmos. They must use at least 4 different parent function types. Connects visual creativity to graph knowledge in a way students remember for years.
Week 2 50 min Individual
Game-Based Review
Transformation Bingo
Teacher displays a transformed graph; students identify the equation and check their bingo card. Covers shifts, reflections, and stretches for all parent function types. High engagement, immediate feedback, and naturally reveals who still has gaps.
Week 2 35 min Whole class
Conceptual Lab
Mirror Graph Activity
Students graph a function on paper, then physically fold the paper across the line y = x to reveal the inverse. The moment the inverse appears on the fold is reliably a "whoa" moment. Builds an intuition for inverses that no algebraic procedure can match.
Week 2 45 min Individual
Error Analysis
"Find the Mistake"
Students receive worked problems with embedded algebraic errors and must identify, explain in writing, and correct each mistake. Develops precision, mathematical communication, and the habit of checking work — all critical for Precalculus success.
Any day 20 min Pairs
04 / Capstone

Capstone Project

The Assignment

"Functions in
the Real World"

Students choose a real-world phenomenon, identify the function type that best models it, and analyze it through all four representations: a table of values, a graph, an equation, and a written explanation of what the function behavior means in context.

// Deliverable
A one-page "Function Profile" poster + a 3-minute verbal explanation at the gallery walk. Each poster must include all four representations and answer: "What does the rate of change tell us here?"
