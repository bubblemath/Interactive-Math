⚠ The Biggest Log Mistake — Write This Down and Don't Forget It:
\(\log(a + b) \;\neq\; \log(a) + \log(b)\)    There is no sum rule for logarithms. The product rule applies to multiplication inside the log, not addition.
Solving Exponential Equations
Guided: Solve \(e^{2x} = 15\)
Step 1:Take \(\ln\) of both sides:
\(\ln(e^{2x}) = \ln(15)\)
Step 2:\(2x = \ln(15)\)
Step 3:\(x = \dfrac{\ln(15)}{2} \approx 1.354\)

Solving Logarithmic Equations
Guided: Solve \(\log(x) + \log(x-3) = 1\)
Step 1:Combine: \(\log(x(x-3)) = 1\)
Step 2:Convert: \(x(x-3) = 10\)
Step 3:\(x^2-3x-10=0\) → \(x=5\) or \(x=-2\)
Check:\(x=-2\): \(\log(-2)\) undefined ✗
Answer:\(x = 5\) only
