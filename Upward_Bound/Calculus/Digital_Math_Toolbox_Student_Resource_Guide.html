📚 The Resource Map

Know Your Tools!

Each resource has a "superpower" — learn when to use each one!

🏆

Art of Problem Solving

Superpower: Deep problem-solving & competition prep
✅ Best For:
  • Challenging problems beyond textbooks
  • Math competition preparation
  • Building problem-solving intuition
  • Community forums for discussion
⚠️ These problems are HARD — don't get discouraged! Start with introductory forums.
🔗 Visit Art of Problem Solving
🎬

3Blue1Brown

Superpower: Visual intuition & the "why" behind math
✅ Best For:
  • Understanding concepts geometrically
  • Seeing the beauty in mathematics
  • Building deep conceptual understanding
  • Linear algebra & calculus visualization
⚠️ Great for insight, but won't help you practice procedures.
🔗 Visit 3Blue1Brown
📝

Paul's Online Math Notes

Superpower: Complete calculus/algebra tutorials
✅ Best For:
  • Step-by-step worked examples
  • Comprehensive topic coverage
  • Cheat sheets and formula references
  • Practice problems with solutions
⚠️ Excellent reference, but passive reading ≠ active learning.
🔗 Visit Paul's Math Notes
🔮

WolframAlpha

Superpower: Computational power & step-by-step solutions
✅ Best For:
  • Checking your work
  • Exploring mathematical patterns
  • Graphing complex functions
  • Step-by-step solution breakdowns
🚨 Using this to get answers without understanding is academic dishonesty AND hurts YOUR learning!
🔗 Visit WolframAlpha
🎓

Khan Academy

Superpower: Mastery-based practice with feedback
✅ Best For:
  • Building foundational skills
  • Self-paced learning
  • Immediate feedback on practice
  • Filling knowledge gaps
⚠️ The videos are great, but the PRACTICE is where learning happens.
🔗 Visit Khan Academy

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • WolframAlpha: Use to CHECK work, not to DO work. Understanding matters more than answers!
  • 3Blue1Brown: Great for "aha!" moments, but you still need to practice procedures.
  • Khan Academy: The videos help, but the PRACTICE problems are where learning happens.
  • Paul's Notes: Don't just read — work through the examples yourself!
  • Art of Problem Solving: Struggle is part of learning. Don't give up too quickly!
