🔗 Advanced Derivative Rules: Chain, Product & Quotient

Page 1: The Product Rule & Quotient Rule

⚠️ Why We Need New Rules

The basic rules DON'T work for products, quotients, or compositions!

❌ (fg)' ≠ f'g' ❌ (f/g)' ≠ f'/g' ❌ (f∘g)' ≠ f'∘g'

✖️ The Product Rule

d/dx [u · v] = u · v' + v · u'

Visual: As the rectangle grows, the change in area = two strips!

"First × d(Second) + Second × d(First)"

➗ The Quotient Rule

d/dx [u/v] = (v·u' − u·v') / v²
Lo · dHi − Hi · dLo
Lo · Lo

Hi = numerator (u), Lo = denominator (v)

"Low d-High minus High d-Low, over Low squared"
🔢 Step-by-Step Procedures
✖️ Product Rule Steps:
  1. Identify: Label u and v (the two factors)
  2. Differentiate: Find u' and v' separately
  3. Apply: u·v' + v·u'
  4. Simplify: Factor out common terms
➗ Quotient Rule Steps:
  1. Identify: Hi = numerator, Lo = denominator
  2. Differentiate: Find d(Hi) and d(Lo)
  3. Apply: (Lo·dHi − Hi·dLo) / Lo²
  4. Simplify: Expand and combine like terms
⚠️ Common Mistakes & Corrections
🚨 The "Distributive Trap"
❌ d/dx [x² · sin x] = 2x · cos x
✓ = x² · cos x + sin x · 2x = x²cos x + 2x sin x
🚨 The Subtraction Swap
❌ (Hi·dLo − Lo·dHi) / Lo²
✓ (Lo·dHi − Hi·dLo) / Lo² — Lo comes FIRST!
🚨 Forgetting Lo²
❌ d/dx [x/sin x] = (sin x · 1 − x · cos x)
✓ = (sin x − x cos x) / sin²x
🚨 Not Simplifying
❌ Leaving x·cos x + x·cos x unsimplified
✓ Combine: 2x cos x, or factor: x(cos x + cos x)
📝 Worked Example: Product Rule
Problem: Find d/dx [x³ · cos x]
Step 1: u = x³, v = cos x
Step 2: u' = 3x², v' = −sin x
Step 3: u·v' + v·u' = x³(−sin x) + cos x(3x²)
✓ = −x³ sin x + 3x² cos x or x²(3cos x − x sin x)
Rule Formula When to Use
Product Rule (uv)' = uv' + vu' Two functions MULTIPLIED together
Quotient Rule (u/v)' = (vu' − uv') / v² One function DIVIDED by another

Page 2: The Chain Rule & Master Synthesis
🔗 The Chain Rule — "Peel the Onion"
d/dx [f(g(x))] = f'(g(x)) · g'(x)

Outer derivative (leave inner alone) × Inner derivative

Leibniz Notation:

dy/dx = (dy/du) · (du/dx)

"The du's cancel" (helpful mnemonic!)

x

Peel outward:

1. Outer: sin³

2. Middle: sin

3. Inner: 2x

Example: sin³(2x) has 3 layers!
🔢 Chain Rule — Step by Step
  1. Identify Layers: Find the OUTER function and INNER function. Ask: "What's inside what?"
  2. Differentiate Outer: Take the derivative of the outer function, leaving the inner function UNTOUCHED.
  3. Multiply by Inner Derivative: Multiply by the derivative of the inner function.
  4. Repeat if Nested: If the inner function has its own inner function, apply Chain Rule again!
💡 The #1 Chain Rule Error: Forgetting to multiply by the inner derivative! Always ask: "Did I multiply by the inside's derivative?"
📝 Worked Example: Product + Chain Rule Combined
Problem: Find d/dx [x² · sin(5x)]
Identify: Product of u = x² and v = sin(5x)
Find u': u' = 2x
Find v' (Chain!): v = sin(5x) → outer: sin, inner: 5x
v' = cos(5x) · 5 = 5cos(5x)
Product Rule: u·v' + v·u' = x²·5cos(5x) + sin(5x)·2x
✓ = 5x²cos(5x) + 2x sin(5x) or x[5x cos(5x) + 2sin(5x)]
✅ Quick Reference Checklist
🎯 Rule Selection
  • Is it a PRODUCT? → Product Rule
  • Is it a FRACTION? → Quotient Rule
  • Is there a function INSIDE another? → Chain Rule
  • Multiple situations? → Combine rules!
🔗 Chain Rule Check
  • Did I identify ALL layers (outer/inner)?
  • Did I leave the inner alone when differentiating outer?
  • Did I MULTIPLY by the inner derivative?
  • Are there nested chains requiring multiple applications?
✖️ Product Rule Check
  • Did I identify both factors (u and v)?
  • Did I get TWO terms in my answer?
  • Did I apply Chain Rule to each factor if needed?
  • Did I simplify/factor the final answer?
➗ Quotient Rule Check
  • Is it Lo·dHi MINUS Hi·dLo? (order matters!)
  • Did I square the denominator (Lo²)?
  • Did I apply Chain Rule to Hi or Lo if needed?
  • Can I simplify the final fraction?
🚀 Calculus Preview — Where These Rules Lead
📐 Implicit Differentiation
d/dx [y²] = 2y · (dy/dx)

Chain Rule with y as the inner function — essential for curves like circles!

🔄 Related Rates
dV/dt = (dV/dr) · (dr/dt)

Chain Rule connects rates of change — how fast is the balloon inflating?

∫ Integration by Parts
∫u dv = uv − ∫v du

The Product Rule "in reverse" — a key integration technique!

🔀 u-Substitution
∫f(g(x))g'(x)dx = ∫f(u)du

The Chain Rule "in reverse" — the most common integration method!
