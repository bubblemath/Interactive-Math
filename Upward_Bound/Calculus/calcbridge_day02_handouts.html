\(\displaystyle f(x) = \frac{x + 1}{x^2 - 4}\)

② Verify

③ Why?

Part C — Extension: The Calculus Connection
Challenge level. Don't worry if you don't finish — this is a preview of what we'll fully explore in Week 2. Work as far as you can.
C1 Building toward the difference quotient
Start with \(f(x) = x^2\).   Now consider \(f(x + h)\).
① By hand
Expand \(f(x+h) = (x+h)^2\) fully. Simplify \(f(x+h) - f(x)\).
② In Desmos
Type \(f(x) = x^2\). Add a slider for \(h\). Graph \(f(x+h)\). What happens as you move \(h\)?
③ The big question
Simplify \(\dfrac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}\). What do you get? What happens as \(h \to 0\)?

Skill 2 — Evaluating \(f(x+h)\): The Calculus Expression
Replace every \(x\) with \((x+h)\) — then expand carefully using \((x+h)^2 = x^2 + 2xh + h^2\).
⚠ Critical mistake to avoid:  \((x+h)^2 \neq x^2 + h^2\)  — the middle term \(2xh\) is never zero and must not be dropped.
