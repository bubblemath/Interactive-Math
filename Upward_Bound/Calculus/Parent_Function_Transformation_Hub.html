📐 Group 1: Polynomials

🧠 Memory Hint

"Degree Determines Direction" — Even degrees make a U-turn (both ends same direction). Odd degrees make an S-curve (opposite directions).

Linear

\( f(x) = x \)
⚓ Anchor Points

\( (0,0), (1,1), (-1,-1) \)

Domain: All reals
Range: All reals
Symmetry: Odd
Asymptotes: None

Quadratic

\( f(x) = x^2 \)
⚓ Anchor Points

\( (0,0), (1,1), (-1,1), (2,4) \)

Domain: All reals
Range: \( y \geq 0 \)
Symmetry: Even
Asymptotes: None

Cubic

\( f(x) = x^3 \)
⚓ Anchor Points

\( (0,0), (1,1), (-1,-1), (2,8) \)

Domain: All reals
Range: All reals
Symmetry: Odd
Asymptotes: None

√ Group 2: Radicals

🧠 Memory Hint

"Even Roots Have Starting Points, Odd Roots Go Through"

Square Root

\( f(x) = \sqrt{x} \)
⚓ Anchor Points

\( (0,0), (1,1), (4,2), (9,3) \)

Domain: \( x \geq 0 \)
Range: \( y \geq 0 \)
Symmetry: Neither
Asymptotes: None

Cube Root

\( f(x) = \sqrt[3]{x} \)
⚓ Anchor Points

\( (0,0), (1,1), (-1,-1), (8,2) \)

Domain: All reals
Range: All reals
Symmetry: Odd
Asymptotes: None

➗ Group 3: Rationals

🧠 Memory Hint

"Forbidden Zones and Mirror Behavior" — \(1/x\) is odd (opposite corners), \(1/x^2\) is even (same side).

Reciprocal

\( f(x) = \frac{1}{x} \)
⚓ Anchor Points

\( (1,1), (-1,-1), (2,\frac{1}{2}) \)

Domain: \( x \neq 0 \)
Range: \( y \neq 0 \)
Symmetry: Odd
Asymptotes: VA: x=0, HA: y=0

Reciprocal Squared

\( f(x) = \frac{1}{x^2} \)
⚓ Anchor Points

\( (1,1), (-1,1), (2,\frac{1}{4}) \)

Domain: \( x \neq 0 \)
Range: \( y > 0 \)
Symmetry: Even
Asymptotes: VA: x=0, HA: y=0

📈 Group 4: Exponential & Logarithmic

🧠 Memory Hint

"Mirror Twins Across y = x" — \(e^x\) passes through (0,1), \(\ln(x)\) passes through (1,0).

Exponential

\( f(x) = e^x \)
⚓ Anchor Points

\( (0,1), (1,e), (-1,\frac{1}{e}) \)

Domain: All reals
Range: \( y > 0 \)
Symmetry: Neither
Asymptotes: HA: y=0

Natural Logarithm

\( f(x) = \ln(x) \)
⚓ Anchor Points

\( (1,0), (e,1), (\frac{1}{e},-1) \)

Domain: \( x > 0 \)
Range: All reals
Symmetry: Neither
Asymptotes: VA: x=0

🌊 Group 5: Trigonometric

🧠 Memory Hint

"Sine Starts at Zero, Cosine Starts at the Top"

Sine

\( f(x) = \sin(x) \)
⚓ Anchor Points

\( (0,0), (\frac{\pi}{2},1), (\pi,0), (\frac{3\pi}{2},-1) \)

Domain: All reals
Range: [-1, 1]
Symmetry: Odd
Period: \( 2\pi \)

Cosine

\( f(x) = \cos(x) \)
⚓ Anchor Points

\( (0,1), (\frac{\pi}{2},0), (\pi,-1), (\frac{3\pi}{2},0) \)

Domain: All reals
Range: [-1, 1]
Symmetry: Even
Period: \( 2\pi \)

Tangent

\( f(x) = \tan(x) \)
⚓ Anchor Points

\( (0,0), (\frac{\pi}{4},1), (-\frac{\pi}{4},-1) \)

Domain: \( x \neq \frac{\pi}{2}+n\pi \)
Range: All reals
Symmetry: Odd
Period: \( \pi \)

The H-S-V Transformation Method

Master transformations in the correct order: Horizontal → Stretch → Vertical

The Master Formula

\( y = a \cdot f(b(x - h)) + k \)
H

Horizontal

\( (x - h) \) and \( b \)

Shift: \( h \) units (opposite!)

🧠 "Opposite Land"
S

Stretch/Reflect

\( a \)

Stretch: Factor of \( |a| \)

🧠 "Amplitude Adjuster"
V

Vertical

\( + k \)

Shift: \( k \) units (as expected!)

🧠 "The Elevator"
