Section 2 — The "Giant One" Discovery Lab
The Giant One — Why Cancellation Works
\(\dfrac{x-a}{x-a} = 1 \qquad (x \neq a)\)
Cancelling a factor from numerator and denominator is dividing by 1 — it doesn't change the value of the expression. You can only do this with factors (things multiplied), never with terms (things added).
✗ ILLEGAL — Cancelling Terms
\(\dfrac{x+3}{x+5} \neq \dfrac{3}{5}\)
The \(x\) is added — you cannot cancel it. There is no Giant One here.
✓ LEGAL — Cancelling Factors
\(\dfrac{(x+3)(x-2)}{(x+5)(x-2)} = \dfrac{x+3}{x+5}\)
The \((x-2)\) is multiplied. \(\frac{x-2}{x-2}=1\) — that's the Giant One. ✓
Guided: Simplify \(\dfrac{x^2-4}{x^2+4x+4}\)
Factor:\(\dfrac{(x-2)(x+2)}{(x+2)^2}\)
Cancel:Giant One: \(\dfrac{x+2}{x+2}\)
Result:\(\dfrac{x-2}{x+2}\),  \(x \neq -2\)
Try it: Simplify \(\dfrac{x^2+x-6}{x^2-9}\)
State restrictions first, then simplify.
Result: _____________  \(x \neq\) _______

Section 3 — Operations Workshop
Multiplication
Rule: Factor first, then cancel, then multiply. Never multiply out first.
\(\dfrac{a}{b} \cdot \dfrac{c}{d} = \dfrac{ac}{bd}\)
Division
Rule: Flip the second fraction, then treat as multiplication.
\(\dfrac{a}{b} \div \dfrac{c}{d} = \dfrac{a}{b} \cdot \dfrac{d}{c}\)
Complex Fractions
Rule: Find the LCD of all inner fractions. Multiply every term top and bottom by the LCD.
Guided: Multiply
\(\dfrac{x^2-9}{x^2+5x+6} \cdot \dfrac{x+2}{x-3}\)
Factor:\(\dfrac{(x-3)(x+3)}{(x+2)(x+3)} \cdot \dfrac{x+2}{x-3}\)
Cancel:All four factors cancel
Result:\(1\),  \(x \neq -3, -2, 3\)
Try it: Multiply
\(\dfrac{x^2-1}{x^2+3x+2} \cdot \dfrac{x+2}{x-1}\)
Result: _____________  \(x \neq\) _______
⚠ The Most Common Operations Error — Never Do This:
\(\dfrac{a}{b} + \dfrac{c}{d} \neq \dfrac{a+c}{b+d}\)   You must build a common denominator first. Denominators are never added.
Addition & Subtraction — The LCD Builder
Guided: \(\dfrac{3}{x-1} + \dfrac{2}{x+1}\)
1
Factor each denominator: \(x-1\) and \(x+1\) (already factored)
2
Build LCD: \((x-1)(x+1) = x^2-1\)
3
Rewrite: \(\dfrac{3(x+1)}{(x-1)(x+1)} + \dfrac{2(x-1)}{(x-1)(x+1)}\)
4
Combine: \(\dfrac{3x+3+2x-2}{x^2-1} = \dfrac{5x+1}{x^2-1}\)
Try it: \(\dfrac{x}{x+2} - \dfrac{3}{x-1}\)
Result: _____________________
Calculus: The Quotient Rule \(\frac{d}{dx}[\frac{u}{v}] = \frac{u'v-uv'}{v^2}\) produces expressions requiring LCD skills to simplify. Partial Fraction Decomposition reverses this process for integration.
Section 4 — Rational Equation Solver
Strategy: Clear the Denominators by Multiplying by the LCD
Guided: Solve \(\dfrac{2}{x-3} + \dfrac{1}{x} = \dfrac{5}{x(x-3)}\)
LCD:\(x(x-3)\)
Multiply every term:\(2x + (x-3) = 5\)
Solve:\(3x-3=5 \Rightarrow x=\frac{8}{3}\)
Check:\(x=\frac{8}{3}\) makes no denom \(=0\) ✓

Answer: _____________ (check: ______)
★ Extraneous Solution Detector — Always Check!
Multiplying by the LCD can introduce solutions that make the original denominator \(= 0\). These must be rejected.
Example: Solve \(\dfrac{x}{x-2} = \dfrac{2}{x-2} + 1\)
Multiply by \((x-2)\): \(x = 2 + (x-2) = x\)
Solving gives \(x = 2\)
Check: \(x=2\) → denominator \(= 0\) ✗
Extraneous! No solution.
✅ Verification Checklist
  • Found the LCD correctly?
  • Multiplied every term by LCD?
  • Solved the resulting equation?
  • Checked each solution in the original?
  • Rejected any extraneous solutions?
Calculus: Extraneous values in rational equations are exactly the vertical asymptotes of the function. When finding critical points of \(f'(x)=0\), rejecting extraneous solutions means rejecting values outside the domain of \(f\).
