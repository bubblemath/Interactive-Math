📚 Why Does This Matter?

The Big Question

How do you measure speed at a single instant if speed requires distance and time?

This is the question that puzzled mathematicians for centuries — and led to the invention of calculus!

🚗 Average Speed vs. Instantaneous Speed

When you drive 60 miles in 1 hour, your average speed is 60 mph. But were you going exactly 60 mph the entire time? Probably not!

Average Rate of Change

\( \frac{\Delta d}{\Delta t} = \frac{d_2 - d_1}{t_2 - t_1} \)

Speed over an interval of time

Like the slope of a secant line

Instantaneous Rate of Change

\( \lim_{\Delta t \to 0} \frac{\Delta d}{\Delta t} \)

Speed at a single moment

Like the slope of a tangent line

📐 Slope Review: The Foundation

Before we discover the derivative, let's make sure we're solid on slope!

\( \text{Slope} = \frac{\text{Rise}}{\text{Run}} = \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x} = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1} \)

📱 Slow-Motion Video Analysis Guide

Why Slow-Motion?

A marble rolling down a ramp moves fast! Recording at 240 frames per second lets you capture precise timing data that would be impossible with a regular stopwatch.

📋 Step-by-Step Instructions

1

📐 Setup Your Ramp

  • Use a grooved track (foam pipe insulation cut in half works great!)
  • Prop one end 6-10 inches high — keep the angle consistent!
  • Mark distances every 10 cm with masking tape
  • Label marks clearly: 0cm, 10cm, 20cm... up to 100cm
Pro Tip: Place a meter stick in the background as a reference scale!
2

📱 Position Your Phone

  • Set up your phone perpendicular to the ramp (side view)
  • Use a tripod or prop it against books — stability is key!
  • Make sure all distance markers are visible in frame
  • Position at the same height as the ramp to avoid parallax error
Parallax Error: If you film from above or below, distances will appear distorted!
3

⚙️ Camera Settings

  • iPhone: Settings → Camera → Record Slo-mo → 240 fps
  • Android: Camera → More → Slow Motion → Select highest fps
  • Make sure you have good lighting (slow-mo needs more light!)
  • Clear storage space — slow-mo files are large!
Frame Rate Options: 240 fps is ideal, but 120 fps also works. The higher the better!
4

🎬 Recording

  • Start recording before releasing the marble
  • Release the marble from the same starting point each trial
  • Let it roll past the final marker before stopping
  • Record 3 trials and average your results
5

🔍 Data Extraction

  • Open the video and scrub frame-by-frame
  • Find the exact frame when the marble crosses each marker
  • Record the frame number for each distance
  • Use the converter below to calculate time in seconds

🎉 The Instantaneous Rate (The Derivative!)

As Δt → 0, the average rate approaches:

— cm/s
\( v(t) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{d(t+h) - d(t)}{h} = \frac{d}{dt}[d(t)] \)

🧠 The Big Reveal: This IS the Derivative!

What you just discovered is the limit definition of the derivative:

\( f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h} \)

For our marble with \( d(t) = \frac{1}{2}at^2 \), the derivative is:

\( v(t) = d'(t) = at \) (velocity increases linearly with time!)
