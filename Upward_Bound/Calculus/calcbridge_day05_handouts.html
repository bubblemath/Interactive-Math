Group members:
                                        
The question driving this lab: You can measure the marble's average speed over a time interval. But can you find its instantaneous speed — the speed at exactly one moment — from measurements alone? Today you're going to find out.
⬤   Roller
Releases the marble consistently from the top of the ramp — same hand, same position, every time.
⬤   Timer
Calls "go" and records the time when the marble reaches each tape checkpoint.
⬤   Recorder
Writes down all data, computes averages, and leads the written analysis sections.

Part E — Extension: The Algebra Connection (for early finishers)
Suppose the marble's distance from the ramp base follows the model \(d(t) = 60t^2\) cm (where \(t\) is in seconds). Using this model:

(c) How does your algebraic answer compare to your experimental estimate from Part D?
