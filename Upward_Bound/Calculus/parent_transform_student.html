Polynomials: Even degree = U-turn (both ends same). Odd degree = S-curve (opposite ends).
Exp & Log are mirror twins across \(y=x\) — \(e^x\) through \((0,1)\); \(\ln x\) through \((1,0)\).
Trig: Sine starts at 0. Cosine starts at the top. Both range \([-1,1]\), period \(2\pi\). Tangent period \(\pi\).
Rationals: \(\frac{1}{x}\) is odd (opposite corners). \(\frac{1}{x^2}\) is even (both sides positive). Both have VA at \(x=0\) and HA at \(y=0\).

The H-S-V Method & Inside-Outside Decoder
Name:                         
The Master Transformation Formula
\(y = a \cdot f\!\left(b(x - h)\right) + k\)
H
Horizontal
\(h\): shift right \(h\) (left if negative)
\(b\): compress by \(b\) (stretch by \(\frac{1}{b}\))
⚠ "Opposite Land"
S
Stretch/Reflect
\(|a|>1\): vertical stretch
\(0<|a|<1\): vertical compress
\(a<0\): reflect over x-axis
🧠 "Amplitude Adjuster"
V
Vertical
\(k>0\): shift up
\(k<0\): shift down
✓ "The Elevator"

The Inside-Outside Decoder — Why "Opposite Land" Works
⚠ INSIDE: \(f(b(x-h))\) — Works OPPOSITE
\((x-h)\) shifts RIGHT by \(h\).
Why? The input equals zero when \(x = \) ______.
That zero-point moved from \(x=0\) to \(x=h\) — rightward.
\(f(x+3)\) shifts _______ because \(x+3=0\) when \(x=\) _____
✓ OUTSIDE: \(a \cdot f(x) + k\) — Works AS EXPECTED
\(+k\) shifts up. \(-k\) shifts down. No surprises.
\(a>1\) stretches vertically. \(a<0\) reflects over x-axis.
Outside operations directly change the output value, so they work intuitively.
