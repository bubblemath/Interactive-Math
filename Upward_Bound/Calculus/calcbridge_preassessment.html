B
Function Thinking
Notation & evaluation  ·  Domain & range  ·  Composition & inverses  ·  Transformations
B1
If \(f(x) = x^2 - 3x + 1\), find \(f(-2)\) and \(f(a+1)\). Simplify fully.
B2
Find the domain of:  \(\displaystyle g(x) = \frac{\sqrt{x-4}}{x^2-9}\)
B3
If \(f(x) = 2x+1\) and \(g(x) = x^2\), find \((f \circ g)(x)\) and \((g \circ f)(x)\).
B4
Describe in words all transformations from \(y = x^2\) to \(y = -2(x+3)^2 - 1\).
B5
Find the inverse of \(\displaystyle f(x) = \frac{x-2}{3}\). Then verify your answer by showing \(f(f^{-1}(x)) = x\).

D
Calculus Preview
Average rate of change  ·  Difference quotient  ·  Limits  ·  "Notice & Wonder"
D1
For \(f(x) = x^2 + 1\), find the average rate of change from \(x = 1\) to \(x = 3\). Show all work.
D2
For the same \(f(x) = x^2 + 1\), find the average rate of change from \(x=1\) to \(x=1.5\), then from \(x=1\) to \(x=1.1\). What do you notice?
D3
A table shows the height (feet) of a ball at various times:

\(t\) (sec) 0 1 2 3
\(h\) (ft) 64 80 64 16
Find the average rate of change from \(t = 0\) to \(t = 2\). What does the sign of your answer tell you?
