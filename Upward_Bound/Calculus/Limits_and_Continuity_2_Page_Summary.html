∞ Limits & Continuity

Page 1: The Foundation of Limits — Approaching, Not Arriving

🎯 The Big Idea

limx→a f(x) = L

"As x gets closer and closer to a, f(x) gets closer and closer to L"

📖 Key Definitions with Visual Examples

The Limit Concept

limx→a f(x) = L

What is y approaching as x gets close to a? The limit describes the destination, not the arrival.

When Does a Limit Exist?

limx→a⁻ f(x) = limx→a⁺ f(x) = L

Both "roads" (left and right) must lead to the same destination!

Left-Hand Limit (x → a⁻)

limx→a⁻ f(x)

Approaching from values less than a
(x = 1.9, 1.99, 1.999...)

Right-Hand Limit (x → a⁺)

limx→a⁺ f(x)

Approaching from values greater than a
(x = 2.1, 2.01, 2.001...)

Visual: Even though f(a) is undefined (the hole), both sides approach the same y-value (L). The limit exists because both roads meet at the same height!
⚠️ Critical Insight

The limit can exist even if the function is undefined at that point! Example: f(x) = (x²−4)/(x−2) is undefined at x=2, but limx→2 f(x) = 4

🔢 Evaluating Limits Algebraically — Step by Step
  1. Direct Substitution: Plug in x = a. If you get a real number, you're done! That's the limit.
  2. Check for Indeterminate Form: If you get 0/0, this is the "Keep Going" signal — the limit may still exist!
  3. Algebraic Manipulation: Try one of these techniques:
    • Factor & Cancel — Factor numerator/denominator, cancel common factors
    • Rationalize — Multiply by conjugate for expressions with radicals
    • Simplify — Combine complex fractions or expand expressions
  4. Re-evaluate: After simplifying, substitute x = a again. Now you should get a number!
💡 The 0/0 Rule: Getting 0/0 means "there's more work to do" — it's NOT the answer! Factor, simplify, then try again.
⚠️ Common Mistakes & Corrections
🚨 Limit = Function Value?
❌ "limx→a f(x) must equal f(a)"
✓ The limit describes the APPROACH, not the arrival. They can be different (or f(a) may not exist)!
🚨 Dropping "lim" Too Early
❌ Writing "= 4" before actually substituting
✓ Keep "limx→a" in EVERY step until you do the final substitution!
🚨 Stopping at 0/0
❌ "I got 0/0, so the limit doesn't exist"
✓ 0/0 is INDETERMINATE — factor, simplify, and try again!
🚨 Ignoring One Side
❌ Only checking the left-hand limit
✓ Always verify BOTH sides approach the same value for the limit to exist!

Page 2: Continuity, Worked Example & Calculus Connection
✅ The 3-Condition Continuity Test — Is f(x) continuous at x = a?
1
f(a) EXISTS
f(a) = some number

"The point exists" — you can plug in a and get a real value

2
LIMIT EXISTS
limx→a f(x) = L

"The roads meet" — left and right limits are equal

3
THEY MATCH
limx→a f(x) = f(a)

"The hole is plugged" — limit equals function value

💡 All THREE must be YES! If ANY condition fails, the function is discontinuous at x = a.
Feature 🕳️ Hole (Removable) 📊 Vertical Asymptote
Algebraically Factor CANCELS (0/0) Factor REMAINS (k/0)
Limit exists? YES — finite value NO — goes to ±∞
Which condition fails? Condition 1 or 3 Condition 2
Can it be "fixed"? YES — redefine f(a) NO — unfixable
📝 Worked Example: Full Process
Problem: Evaluate limx→2 (x² − 4)/(x − 2)
Step 1: Direct substitution: (2² − 4)/(2 − 2) = 0/0 ← Indeterminate!
Step 2: Factor numerator: (x² − 4) = (x − 2)(x + 2)
Step 3: Rewrite: limx→2 [(x−2)(x+2)]/(x−2)
Step 4: Cancel (x−2): limx→2 (x + 2) for x ≠ 2
Step 5: Substitute: 2 + 2 = 4
✓ Answer: limx→2 (x² − 4)/(x − 2) = 4
🕳️ What does this mean graphically?

The function has a hole at (2, 4). The limit exists and equals 4, but f(2) is undefined!

✅ Quick Reference Checklist
📊 Evaluating Limits
  • Did I try direct substitution first?
  • If I got 0/0, did I factor or rationalize?
  • Did I keep "lim" notation until the final step?
  • Did I check both left and right sides?
✅ Testing Continuity
  • Is f(a) defined? (Condition 1)
  • Does the limit exist? (Condition 2)
  • Does limit = f(a)? (Condition 3)
  • All three YES → Continuous!
🔍 Identifying Discontinuities
  • Hole: Factor cancels, limit exists
  • VA: Factor remains, limit = ±∞
  • Jump: Left ≠ Right limit
  • Oscillating: Limit doesn't settle
📈 Graph Verification
  • Does my algebraic answer match the graph?
  • Can I trace the curve without lifting my pencil?
  • Are there any holes or asymptotes?
  • Do both sides approach the same height?
🚀 Calculus Preview — Why Limits Matter!
📐 The Derivative Definition
f'(x) = limh→0 [f(x+h) − f(x)] / h

The derivative IS a limit! It's the instantaneous rate of change — exactly what we explored in the Marble Lab.

📈 Horizontal Asymptotes
limx→∞ f(x) = L

Limits at infinity describe end behavior — what happens as x gets infinitely large or small.

⚡ L'Hôpital's Rule
lim [f/g] = lim [f'/g']

A powerful shortcut for 0/0 forms using derivatives — you'll learn this in Calc I!

∫ The Integral Connection
∫ab f(x)dx = lim Σf(xᵢ)Δx

Integrals are also defined using limits — the limit of Riemann sums as rectangles get infinitely thin.

💡 The Big Picture: Limits are the foundation of ALL of calculus. Every derivative, every integral, every infinite series — they all rely on the limit concept you're learning now!
