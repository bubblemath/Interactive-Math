Section 3 — One-Sided Limits & the Existence Rule
Left-Hand Limit
\(\lim_{x\to a^-}f(x)\)
x approaches from below \(a\)
↔
Right-Hand Limit
\(\lim_{x\to a^+}f(x)\)
x approaches from above \(a\)
The two-sided limit exists if and only if:  \(\lim_{x\to a^-}f(x) = \lim_{x\to a^+}f(x) = L\)
For \(\frac{|x|}{x}\) at \(x=0\):
Left-hand limit = _____
Right-hand limit = _____
Two-sided limit: ________
Three functions and their limit status:
\(\frac{x^2-4}{x-2}\) at \(x=2\): limit ___________
\(\frac{1}{x-2}\) at \(x=2\): limit ___________
\(\frac{|x|}{x}\) at \(x=0\): limit ___________
Calculus: Continuity requires the two-sided limit to exist. Differentiability requires continuity. Every major theorem (IVT, MVT) requires continuity on an interval. The sided limit is the gatekeeper for all of them.

Section 4 — The Limit Definition of the Derivative
The derivative IS a limit — the shrinking interval table, written algebraically
\(f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \dfrac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h}\)
Numerator = change in _______  ·  Denominator = _______  ·  Limit = _______ rate
Guided: Find \(f'(x)\) for \(f(x)=x^2\)
1
\(f'(x)=\lim_{h\to0}\dfrac{(x+h)^2-x^2}{h}\)
2
Expand: \(= \lim_{h\to0}\dfrac{x^2+2xh+h^2-x^2}{h}\)
3
Simplify: \(= \lim_{h\to0}\dfrac{2xh+h^2}{h} = \lim_{h\to0}\dfrac{h(2x+h)}{h}\)
4
Cancel \(h\): \(= \lim_{h\to0}(2x+h)\)
5
Substitute \(h=0\): \(f'(x) = \) _______
