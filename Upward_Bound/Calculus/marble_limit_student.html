Average Rate vs. Instantaneous Rate
Average Rate of Change
Speed over an interval — requires TWO points.

\[\frac{\Delta d}{\Delta t} = \frac{d_2 - d_1}{t_2 - t_1}\]
Geometric meaning: slope of the __________ line

Example: 120 miles in 2 hours → _____ mph average
Instantaneous Rate of Change
Speed at a single moment — what your speedometer reads.

\[\lim_{\Delta t \to 0} \frac{\Delta d}{\Delta t}\]
Geometric meaning: slope of the __________ line

Found by: making the interval _____________________

Step 3 — The Limit Definition of the Derivative
★ The Derivative — The Formula You Just Discovered
\[f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h}\]
Each row of your shrinking interval table was \(\dfrac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h}\) for one value of \(h\). The limit is the value that column converges to.
