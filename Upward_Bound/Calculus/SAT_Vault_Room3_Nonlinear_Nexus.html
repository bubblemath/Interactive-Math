🔢 Factoring Quadratics

\(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\)

When a = 1: Find two numbers that multiply to \(c\) and add to \(b\).

When a ≠ 1: Use the AC method — multiply \(a \cdot c\), find factors that add to \(b\), then split the middle term.

💡 Special Patterns

• Difference of Squares: \(a^2 - b^2 = (a+b)(a-b)\)
• Perfect Square: \(a^2 + 2ab + b^2 = (a+b)^2\)

📐 The Quadratic Formula

\(x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}\)

Works for ANY quadratic — even when factoring is hard.

⚠️ Common SAT Traps

• Forgetting the negative on \(-b\)
• Dividing only the radical by \(2a\) instead of the ENTIRE numerator
• Forgetting \(\pm\) gives TWO solutions

🔍 The Discriminant

\(\Delta = b^2 - 4ac\)
  • \(\Delta > 0\) → 2 real solutions (parabola crosses x-axis twice)
  • \(\Delta = 0\) → 1 real solution (parabola touches x-axis)
  • \(\Delta < 0\) → 0 real solutions (parabola misses x-axis)
💡 SAT Shortcut

If the SAT asks "how many solutions," you DON'T need to solve — just compute the discriminant!

📊 Vertex Form

\(y = a(x - h)^2 + k\)
Vertex: \((h, k)\)
  • \(a > 0\) → opens UP (vertex is minimum)
  • \(a < 0\) → opens DOWN (vertex is maximum)
⚠️ The Sign Trap

In \(y = (x - 3)^2\), the vertex is at \(x = 3\), NOT \(x = -3\)! The formula has a minus sign built in.

💡 Standard → Vertex Shortcut

For \(ax^2 + bx + c\): \(h = \frac{-b}{2a}\), then \(k = f(h)\)

📈 Exponential Growth & Decay

\(y = a \cdot b^x\)
  • \(a\) = initial value (when \(x = 0\))
  • \(b > 1\) → growth (increasing)
  • \(0 < b < 1\) → decay (decreasing)
  • \(b = 1 + r\) for growth rate \(r\)
  • \(b = 1 - r\) for decay rate \(r\)
💡 SAT Key Phrases

"Doubles every..." → \(b = 2\)
"Loses 15% per year" → \(b = 0.85\)
"Increases by 8% annually" → \(b = 1.08\)

🧮 Nonlinear Systems

When a line meets a parabola:

Method: Set the equations equal, then solve the resulting quadratic.

Example: \(y = x^2\) and \(y = 2x + 3\)
→ \(x^2 = 2x + 3\)
→ \(x^2 - 2x - 3 = 0\)
→ \((x-3)(x+1) = 0\)
💡 Intersection Count

Use the discriminant of the resulting quadratic to determine how many intersection points exist!
