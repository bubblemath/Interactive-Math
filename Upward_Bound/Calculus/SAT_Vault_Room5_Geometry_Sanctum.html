📋 Architect's Tools — Geometry & Trig Review

Master these concepts before activating the star gears.

📐 Pythagorean Theorem

\(a^2 + b^2 = c^2\)

Common Triples: 3-4-5, 5-12-13, 8-15-17, 7-24-25

And their multiples: 6-8-10, 9-12-15, 10-24-26...

💡 SAT Shortcut

Recognize triples instantly! If two sides are 9 and 12, the hypotenuse is 15 (3× the 3-4-5 triple). No calculation needed.

📏 Special Right Triangles

45-45-90: \(x : x : x\sqrt{2}\)
30-60-90: \(x : x\sqrt{3} : 2x\)
⚠️ The Side Trap

In 30-60-90: the SHORT leg is opposite 30°, the LONG leg (\(x\sqrt{3}\)) is opposite 60°, and \(2x\) is the hypotenuse. Don't mix them up!

🔺 SOH-CAH-TOA

\(\sin\theta = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}\)

\(\cos\theta = \frac{\text{Adjacent}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}\)

\(\tan\theta = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Adjacent}}\)
💡 Complementary Angles

\(\sin(x°) = \cos(90° - x°)\)
Example: \(\sin(30°) = \cos(60°) = 0.5\)

🔺 Similar Triangles

If triangles are similar:
\(\frac{a_1}{a_2} = \frac{b_1}{b_2} = \frac{c_1}{c_2}\)

AA Similarity: Two matching angles → triangles are similar.

⚠️ Matching Sides

Make sure you match corresponding sides! The shortest side of one triangle corresponds to the shortest side of the other.

🧊 Volume Formulas

Cylinder: \(V = \pi r^2 h\)
Cone: \(V = \frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h\)
Sphere: \(V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3\)
Rectangular Prism: \(V = lwh\)
💡 Memory Trick

A cone is ⅓ of a cylinder. A sphere is ⁴⁄₃ π r³. These are on the SAT reference sheet!

⭕ Arc Length & Sector Area

Arc Length: \(s = \frac{\theta}{360} \cdot 2\pi r\)

Sector Area: \(A = \frac{\theta}{360} \cdot \pi r^2\)
⚠️ Fraction of the Circle

Both formulas use the same fraction: \(\frac{\theta}{360}\). This is the fraction of the full circle represented by the central angle.
