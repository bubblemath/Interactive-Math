📚 Prerequisite Bridge: Factoring Foundation

The Golden Rule

Rational expressions are only as simple as your ability to factor!

Every simplification, operation, and equation solve starts with factoring.

🔧 Essential Factoring Patterns

GCF (Greatest Common Factor)

\(ab + ac = a(b + c)\)

Example: \(6x^2 + 9x = 3x(2x + 3)\)

Difference of Squares

\(a^2 - b^2 = (a-b)(a+b)\)

Example: \(x^2 - 9 = (x-3)(x+3)\)

Trinomial (a = 1)

\(x^2 + bx + c = (x+m)(x+n)\)

Example: \(x^2 + 5x + 6 = (x+2)(x+3)\)

Trinomial (a ≠ 1)

\(ax^2 + bx + c\) → AC Method

Example: \(2x^2 + 7x + 3 = (2x+1)(x+3)\)

Sum of Cubes

\(a^3 + b^3 = (a+b)(a^2-ab+b^2)\)

Example: \(x^3 + 8 = (x+2)(x^2-2x+4)\)

Difference of Cubes

\(a^3 - b^3 = (a-b)(a^2+ab+b^2)\)

Example: \(x^3 - 27 = (x-3)(x^2+3x+9)\)

🚨 Domain Restrictions: The "Forbidden Values"

Before simplifying ANY rational expression, identify values that make the denominator = 0.

Example: \(\frac{x+2}{x^2-9}\)

Factor denominator: \(x^2 - 9 = (x-3)(x+3)\)

Set each factor = 0: \(x - 3 = 0\) or \(x + 3 = 0\)

Domain Restrictions: \(x \neq 3\) and \(x \neq -3\)

⚠️ These restrictions persist even AFTER simplifying!

The Big Idea

Canceling is just dividing by a "Giant One" — a fraction equal to 1!

\(\frac{x-a}{x-a} = 1\) (when \(x \neq a\))

🚫 Illegal Canceling Alert!

❌ WRONG

\(\frac{x+3}{x+5} \neq \frac{3}{5}\)

Can't cancel terms that are ADDED!

✅ RIGHT

\(\frac{(x+3)(x-2)}{(x+5)(x-2)} = \frac{x+3}{x+5}\)

CAN cancel factors that are MULTIPLIED!

🔗 Calculus Connection

🕳️ Holes = Removable Discontinuities

In calculus, a "hole" means the limit exists even though the function is undefined there.

\(\lim_{x \to 2} \frac{x^2-4}{x-2} = 4\)

📊 Asymptotes = Infinite Limits

Vertical asymptotes produce infinite limits — the function "blows up."

\(\lim_{x \to 0^+} \frac{1}{x} = +\infty\)

🎯 The 0/0 Form

When a limit gives \(\frac{0}{0}\), factor and cancel to find the true limit!

🧮 Operations Workshop

✖️ ➗ Multiplication & Division

The easier operations! Factor everything, then cancel.

Multiplication:

\(\frac{a}{b} \cdot \frac{c}{d} = \frac{ac}{bd}\)

Factor first, THEN multiply (easier to cancel!)

Division:

\(\frac{a}{b} \div \frac{c}{d} = \frac{a}{b} \cdot \frac{d}{c}\)

Multiply by the reciprocal (flip the second fraction)

✅ Worked Example: Multiplication

\(\frac{x^2-9}{x^2+5x+6} \cdot \frac{x+2}{x-3}\)

Step 1: Factor: \(\frac{(x-3)(x+3)}{(x+2)(x+3)} \cdot \frac{x+2}{x-3}\)

Step 2: Cancel: \(\frac{\cancel{(x-3)}(x+3)}{\cancel{(x+2)}(x+3)} \cdot \frac{\cancel{x+2}}{\cancel{x-3}}\)

Step 3: Result: \(\frac{x+3}{x+3} = 1\) (when \(x \neq -3, -2, 3\))

➕ ➖ Addition & Subtraction: The LCD Challenge

You MUST have a common denominator!

🚨 Common Mistake Alert!

\(\frac{a}{b} + \frac{c}{d} \neq \frac{a+c}{b+d}\) ← NEVER add denominators!

🔧 LCD Builder: Step-by-Step

Problem: \(\frac{3}{x-1} + \frac{2}{x+1}\)

1
Factor each denominator

\(x-1\) and \(x+1\) are already factored

2
Build the LCD

LCD = \((x-1)(x+1) = x^2 - 1\)

3
Rewrite each fraction

\(\frac{3(x+1)}{(x-1)(x+1)} + \frac{2(x-1)}{(x-1)(x+1)}\)

4
Combine and simplify

\(\frac{3x+3+2x-2}{x^2-1} = \frac{5x+1}{x^2-1}\)

🎭 Complex Fractions

A fraction within a fraction! Two methods to simplify:

Method 1: Multiply by LCD

Find the LCD of ALL mini-fractions, multiply top and bottom.

Best for: Complex expressions

Method 2: Simplify & Divide

Simplify numerator and denominator separately, then divide.

Best for: Simpler expressions

🔗 Calculus Connection

📐 Quotient Rule

The derivative of a quotient produces expressions that need LCD skills!

\(\frac{d}{dx}\left[\frac{u}{v}\right] = \frac{u'v - uv'}{v^2}\)

🔄 Partial Fractions

Integration technique that reverses the LCD process!

\(\frac{1}{x^2-1} = \frac{A}{x-1} + \frac{B}{x+1}\)

⚡ Rational Equation Solver

The Strategy: Clear the Denominators!

Multiply EVERY term by the LCD to eliminate all fractions.

🎬 "Clear the Denominator" Simulator

Solve:

\(\frac{2}{x-3} + \frac{1}{x} = \frac{5}{x(x-3)}\)

1
Identify the LCD

Denominators: \(x-3\), \(x\), \(x(x-3)\)

LCD = \(x(x-3)\)

2
Multiply EVERY term by LCD

\(x(x-3) \cdot \frac{2}{x-3} + x(x-3) \cdot \frac{1}{x} = x(x-3) \cdot \frac{5}{x(x-3)}\)

3
Simplify (denominators cancel!)

\(2x + (x-3) = 5\)

4
Solve the resulting equation

\(3x - 3 = 5\) → \(3x = 8\) → \(x = \frac{8}{3}\)

5
CHECK for extraneous solutions!

Does \(x = \frac{8}{3}\) make any denominator = 0? NO! ✓

🔥 CRITICAL: Extraneous Solution Detector

Solutions that make the ORIGINAL denominator = 0 must be REJECTED!

Example: Solve \(\frac{x}{x-2} = \frac{2}{x-2} + 1\)

Solving gives: \(x = 2\)

x = 2

Check: denominator = \(2 - 2 = 0\)

Division by zero! UNDEFINED!

🔥

EXTRANEOUS! No solution exists.

✅ Solution Verification Checklist

  • ☐ Did you find the LCD correctly?
  • ☐ Did you multiply EVERY term by the LCD?
  • ☐ Did you solve the resulting equation correctly?
  • ☐ Did you check EACH solution in the ORIGINAL equation?
  • ☐ Did you reject any extraneous solutions?
