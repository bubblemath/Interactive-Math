The Final Case

Five locked rooms. One way out. Everything you've learned, on the line.

All summer, you trained. You learned to read a diagram the way a detective reads a crime scene — identify, translate, solve and verify. You measured what others only guessed at. You proved what others only assumed.

Now the training is over, and one last case remains. Five rooms lie ahead, each sealed by a lock that only geometry can open: a fog-bound forest, an abandoned town at dusk, a haunted manor, a hidden chamber buried in stone, and a laboratory out of some impossible future. Somewhere beyond them all is the way out — and the answer you've earned the right to find.

The lanterns are lit. The fog is waiting. Only one question is left, the one every detective must face sooner or later: do you dare?

Before you step through that door, look at how far you've come. Reaching this point in the course is not a small thing. It took showing up on the hard days, wrestling with proofs that made no sense at first, and pushing through problems most people would have quietly walked away from. You did that — again, and again, and again.

I have watched your dedication and your drive the whole way here, and I could not be prouder of the detective you've become. Every theorem, every transformation, every stubborn radical you refused to give up on built the skills this case will ask of you. You don't just hope you're ready — you've earned it, one problem at a time.

So walk in with your head high. You belong here, at the end of the bridge, on the edge of something you built yourself. Now go solve it.
