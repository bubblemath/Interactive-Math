📍 Case 1: Coordinate Evidence Days 7 & 8  ·  Distance · Midpoint · Slope
Three corners of a monument: A(2, 1), B(8, 1), C(8, 5). Your mission: find the fourth corner and verify it's a rectangle.
Task 1 — Find Vertex DPlot A, B, C first — D follows from the shape!
If ABCD is a rectangle, D = (2, 5)
Why? In a rectangle, opposite sides are parallel and equal. AD must be vertical (x = 2) and have the same height as BC (y = 5).
Because…
Opposite sides of a rectangle are parallel and equal in length — D shares x with A and y with C.
Task 2 — Perimeter of ABCDd = √[(x₂−x₁)² + (y₂−y₁)²]
AB = 6   BC = 4
Perimeter = 2(AB + BC) = 2(6 + 4) = 20 units
Because…
Distance Formula (Day 7): AB = √[(8−2)²+(1−1)²]=√36=6; BC = √[(8−8)²+(5−1)²]=√16=4
Task 3 — Center of MonumentM = ((x₁+x₂)/2, (y₁+y₂)/2)
Midpoint of diagonal AC:
M = ((2+8)/2, (1+5)/2) = (5, 3)
Because…
Midpoint Formula (Day 7): average the x-coords and average the y-coords of the diagonal endpoints A(2,1) and C(8,5).
Task 4 — Verify It's a RectanglePerp. → slopes are neg. reciprocals (or 0 ⊥ undefined)
Slope of AB = 0 (horizontal)
Slope of BC = undefined (vertical)
Are they perpendicular? YES ✓
Because (cite Day 8 rule)…
A horizontal line (slope = 0) is perpendicular to a vertical line (undefined slope) — Day 8 special case rule.
★ Plot first — then compute
D = (2, 5) — plotted ✓
📐 Case 2: The Angle Investigation Days 2, 3 & 4  ·  Angle Relationships · Triangles
Parallel lines cut by a transversal protect the monument. Decode the angles to unlock the grid.
Task 1 — Find x∠1 = (5x+10)°, ∠5 = (3x+40)° [corresponding]
Relationship: Corresponding Angles
Equation: 5x + 10 = 3x + 40
x = 15
Because (Day 3)…
Corresponding Angles Postulate: parallel lines cut by a transversal → corresponding ∠s equal
Task 2 — Find m∠1
m∠1 = 5(15) + 10 = 85°
Verify: m∠5 = 3(15) + 40 = 85° ✓
Task 3 — Find y∠3=(2y+20)°, ∠5=(4y−10)° [co-interior]
Relationship: Same-Side Interior
Equation: (2y+20)+(4y−10)=180
y = 28.33 (= 85/3)
Because (Day 3)…
Same-Side Interior Angles Theorem: co-interior angles are supplementary (sum = 180°)
Task 4 — Exterior AngleExt. angle = 140°, one remote interior = 65°
Other interior angle = 140° − 65° = 75°
Because (Day 4)…
Exterior Angle Theorem: ext. angle = sum of the two remote interior angles → other = 140° − 65° = 75°

🗺️ Case 3: The Scale Model Day 10  ·  Scale Factor · Proportions · Unit Conversions
Ancient map scale: 1 unit = 15 meters. Use proportional reasoning to find actual monument dimensions.
Task 1 — Actual PerimeterPerimeter on map = 20 units (from Case 1!)
20 units × (15 m/unit) = 300 meters
🔗 Connected to Case 1! (Perimeter = 20 units from Task 2)
Because (Day 10)…
Unit conversion / scale factor: multiply map distance by the scale (15 m per unit) — same as multiplying by 1.
Task 2 — Find Scale Factor kMap side = 3 units → actual = 45 m
k = actual / map = 45 / 3 = 15
Because…
Scale factor k = new (actual) ÷ original (map) — Day 10 definition of scale factor.
Task 3 — Missing SidesUse k from Task 2
2 unit side → 15 × 2 = 30 m
4 unit side → 15 × 4 = 60 m
Because…
New side = k × original side (Day 10 scale factor rule). k = 15.
Task 4 — Proportion (hardest!)Doorway width = x/12 units. Actual = 10 m. Scale: 1 unit = 15 m.
Set up: (x/12)/1 = 10/15
Simplify: x/12 = 2/3
Solve: x = 8
Because…
Cross-multiply: 3x = 12 × 2 = 24 → x = 8. (Day 10 proportion / cross-multiplication rule)
🔄 Case 4: The Final Shift Day 11  ·  Translation · Rotation · Reflection · Composition
Geological activity has moved the treasure chamber. Apply transformation rules to find its current location.
Translation
(x+a, y+b)
90° CCW

Task 1 — Translation of E(3, 2)
Rule: (x − 5, y + 3)
E(3, 2) → (−2, 5)
Rule used…
Translation (x+a, y+b): a = −5 (left 5), b = +3 (up 3). New: 3+(−5)=−2, 2+3=5.
Task 2 — Rotation of F(4, −1)90° CCW: (−y, x)
F(4, −1) → (−(−1), 4) = (1, 4)
⚠ −(−1) = +1, not −1!
Rule used…
90° CCW rotation rule: (x, y) → (−y, x). New x = −y = −(−1) = 1; new y = x = 4.
Task 3 — Reflection of G(6, 2)Over y = x: (y, x)
G(6, 2) → (2, 6)
Treasure coordinates: (2, 6)
Rule used…
Reflection over y = x: (x, y) → (y, x). Swap the coordinates: (6, 2) → (2, 6).
Task 4 — Composition at H(2, −4)Step 1: reflect x-axis → Step 2: translate (x+3, y−1)
Step 1 (reflect x-axis): (2, 4)
Step 2 (translate): (5, 3)
Final: (5, 3)
Order matters because…
Each step uses the output of the previous step as its input — reversing the order produces a different final point.

My Three Missions for 9th-Grade Geometry
📐 Notation Mission
Example: "I will use ≅ for shapes and = for measurements."
I will write ≅ when comparing shapes/angles as objects, and = when comparing their measurements.
I will include the overline for segments: AB̄, not just AB.
📜 Proof Mission
Example: "I will write a reason for every step."
I will write a named theorem, definition, or postulate as the reason for every proof step — never "it looks like it."
I will apply the "Because Test" before submitting any proof.
⭐ Personal Mission
Example: "I will ask for help on Day 1."

When the teacher says "transformations," I'll say: "I already know this!"
