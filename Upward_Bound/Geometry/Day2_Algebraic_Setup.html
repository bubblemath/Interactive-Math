📝 Algebraic Example

Problem: Point B is between A and C. If AB = 2x + 1, BC = 3x, and AC = 21, find x.

Step 1: IDENTIFY
B is between A and C → Segment Addition Postulate: AB + BC = AC
Step 2: TRANSLATE
(2x + 1) + (3x) = 21
Step 3: COMBINE
5x + 1 = 21
Step 4: SOLVE
5x = 20 → x = 4
Step 5: VERIFY
AB = 2(4) + 1 = 9, BC = 3(4) = 12, AC = 9 + 12 = 21 ✓

🎯 Key Insight

The Segment Addition Postulate is just common sense written as math: the whole equals the sum of its parts. When you see algebraic expressions for segment lengths, set up the equation and solve!

⏱️ Why Time Yourself?

Shape Detectives, timing yourself serves two purposes:

  1. Fluency Building: In geometry, you'll encounter these setups dozens of times per unit. Timing helps you move from "thinking through each step" to "automatic recognition."
  2. Exam Readiness: Standardized tests and classroom exams have time limits. Practicing under mild time pressure now builds confidence for when it counts.

Tip: Start with the timer OFF while learning. Turn it ON once you feel comfortable to build speed.
