📚 Triangle Vocabulary

Master these terms to identify triangles quickly and communicate precisely in proofs.

🔺 Classification by Angles

🔺
Acute Triangle
A triangle where ALL three angles are less than 90°.
Key: All angles < 90°
📐
Right Triangle
A triangle with exactly one 90° angle (a right angle).
Key: One angle = 90°
📊
Obtuse Triangle
A triangle with exactly one angle greater than 90°.
Key: One angle > 90°

📏 Classification by Sides

📐
Scalene Triangle
A triangle with NO equal sides (and no equal angles).
Key: All sides different lengths
⚖️
Isosceles Triangle
A triangle with at least two equal sides (called legs).
Key: Two sides equal (legs)
🔺
Equilateral Triangle
A triangle with ALL three sides equal (and all angles = 60°).
Key: All sides equal, all angles = 60°

⚖️ Anatomy of an Isosceles Triangle

🔷 Vertex Angle
The angle formed by the two equal sides (legs). It's at the "top" where the legs meet.
📏 Legs
The two equal sides of an isosceles triangle. They connect the vertex to the base.
➖ Base
The third side (not a leg). The base angles are at each end of the base.
📐 Base Angles
The two angles at the ends of the base. These are always equal!

🚪 Exterior Angle Investigator

An exterior angle of a triangle equals the sum of its two "remote" interior angles — the ones it doesn't touch!

🚪 Exterior Angle Theorem

An exterior angle of a triangle equals the sum of the two remote interior angles.

"Remote" means the two angles that don't share a vertex with the exterior angle.

🔍 Finding the Exterior Angle

Example: In triangle ABC, ∠A = 45° and ∠B = 65°. Find the exterior angle at C.

Step 1: Identify the remote interior angles: ∠A and ∠B

Step 2: Add them: 45° + 65° = 110°

✓ The exterior angle at C = 110°

🔄 Using Exterior Angle to Find Unknowns

Example: The exterior angle at C is 130°. If ∠A = (2x + 10)° and ∠B = (3x)°, find x.

Step 1: Exterior = Remote 1 + Remote 2

Step 2: 130 = (2x + 10) + (3x)

Step 3: 130 = 5x + 10

Step 4: 120 = 5x → x = 24

✓ Check: ∠A = 58°, ∠B = 72°, Sum = 130° ✓

⚖️ Isosceles Triangle Theorem

If two sides of a triangle are congruent (equal), then the angles opposite those sides are congruent.

Equal Legs → Equal Base Angles

🔄 The Converse

If two angles of a triangle are congruent, then the sides opposite those angles are congruent.

Equal Base Angles → Equal Legs

Example: In triangle XYZ, ∠X = 50° and ∠Y = 50°. What can you conclude?

✓ Since ∠X = ∠Y, the sides opposite them are equal: XZ = YZ

🔺 Special Case: Equilateral Triangles

If all three angles of a triangle are equal, each angle must be 60°.

Why? If ∠A = ∠B = ∠C and ∠A + ∠B + ∠C = 180°

Then 3 × (angle) = 180° → Each angle = 60°

✓ All angles equal → All angles = 60° → All sides equal (equilateral!)

In triangle ABC, ∠A = (2x + 10)°, ∠B = (3x - 5)°, and ∠C = 45°. Find x.
Step 1: IDENTIFY
Three interior angles → Triangle Sum Theorem (= 180°)
Step 2: TRANSLATE
(2x + 10) + (3x - 5) + 45 = 180
Step 3: SOLVE
5x + 50 = 180 → 5x = 130 → x = 26
Step 4: VERIFY
∠A = 62°, ∠B = 73°, ∠C = 45° → Sum = 180° ✓
