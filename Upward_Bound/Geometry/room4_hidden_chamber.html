Day 8 — Slope & Parallel / Perpendicular

  • Slope: m = (y₂−y₁) ÷ (x₂−x₁) = rise ÷ run — always reduce
  • Positive ↗ rises · Negative ↘ falls · Zero = horizontal (y = k) · Undefined = vertical (x = k)
  • Parallel: same slope → m₁ = m₂
  • Perpendicular: negative reciprocal — flip the fraction AND change the sign
  • Product check: m₁ × m₂ = −1 means perpendicular
  • Point-slope: y − y₁ = m(x − x₁)

Day 9 — Perimeter, Area & Pythagorean

  • Rectangle: A = ℓw · Triangle: A = ½bh
  • Parallelogram: A = bh · Trapezoid: A = ½(b₁+b₂)h · Circle: A = πr²
  • Composite — additive: split into pieces, add the areas. Subtractive: big shape − cutout
  • Pythagorean: a² + b² = c² (c = hypotenuse, the longest side)
  • Algebra sides: expand carefully — (a+b)² = a² + 2ab + b² — then reject negative lengths
  • Triples: 3-4-5 · 5-12-13 · 8-15-17 (and their multiples)

Tablet I   The Ascent · Slope
Sealed

The tablet shows two carved points, A(−2, 5) and B(4, −1). Find the slope of the line through them. Reduce your answer.

Strategy: Use m = (y₂ − y₁) ÷ (x₂ − x₁) — vertical change over horizontal change. Subtract in the same order on top and bottom, then reduce the fraction to lowest terms.
Numeral revealed → 2
Tablet II   The Right Angle · Pythagorean
Sealed

A right triangle carved in stone has legs 9 and 12. Find the length of the hypotenuse.

Strategy: The hypotenuse c is the longest side, opposite the right angle. Use a² + b² = c² — square both legs, add them, then take the square root. (This one is a 3-4-5 family triple.)
Numeral revealed → 8
Tablet III   The Mirror Path · Perpendicular
Sealed

A path has slope 3/4. Find the slope of a path that runs perpendicular to it. Give your answer as a reduced fraction.

Strategy: Perpendicular slope = the negative reciprocal: flip the fraction and change the sign. Verify with the product check — m₁ × m₂ should equal −1.
Numeral revealed → 6
Tablet IV   The Joined Stones · Composite Area
Sealed

A relief shows a rectangle 12 × 8 with a triangle sitting on top of it (triangle base 12, height 5). Find the total area of the whole figure.

Strategy: This is an additive composite: find each piece separately, then add. Rectangle area is ℓ × w; triangle area is ½ × b × h. Don't forget the ½ on the triangle.
Numeral revealed → 0
Tablet V   The Sealed Glyph · Pythagorean Algebra
Sealed

The final tablet: a right triangle has legs x and (x + 7) with hypotenuse 13. Solve for x. (Lengths must be positive — reject any negative solution.)

Strategy: Set up x² + (x+7)² = 13². Expand (x+7)² in full — remember the middle term 2ab. Collect into standard form, simplify, factor (AC method), then reject the negative root. Keep the positive length.
Numeral revealed → 4
