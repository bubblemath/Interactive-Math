Section 2 — Area Formula Library: Why Formulas Work
Use the GeoGebra applets to discover each formula BEFORE reading the explanation. Fill in the blanks as you explore.
▭ Rectangle
A = ℓw    P = 2ℓ + 2w
Base formula — all others derive from here. Area = rows × columns of unit squares.
▱ Parallelogram
A = bh
Cut-and-paste triangle to form a rectangle. h = perpendicular height, NOT the slant side!
△ Triangle
A = ½bh
Two congruent triangles form a parallelogram. Triangle = ½ parallelogram = ½bh.
⏢ Trapezoid
A = ½(b₁+b₂)h
Average of two bases × height. Like a rectangle whose width changes from b₁ to b₂.
● Circle
A = πr²   C = 2πr
Unroll into triangles: height = r, bases sum to 2πr. A = ½(2πr)(r) = πr².
◗ Semicircle
A = ½πr²
⚠ Perimeter = arc + diameter = πr + 2r. Don't forget the flat edge!

Section 3 — Composite Area Lab: Choose Your Method First
➕ Additive Method
🧱 + 🧱
Split the shape into simpler pieces.
Add their areas together.

Best for: L-shapes, T-shapes, shapes made from pieces put together.
A = A₁ + A₂ + A₃…
➖ Subtractive Method
🟦 − 🔲
Start with a bigger shape.
Subtract the missing piece.

Best for: shapes with holes, cutouts, or shaded regions.
A = A(big) − A(cutout)
