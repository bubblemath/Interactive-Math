Section 1 — Prerequisite Bridge: Day 2 Quick Check
Write the equation for each situation. You do not need to solve — just set it up correctly.
QB 1 — Supplementary
Two supplementary angles:
one is (3x + 20)°, other is (x + 40)°
Equation:
(3x + 20) + (x + 40) = 180
QB 2 — Vertical Angles
Vertical angles:
one is (5x − 10)°, other is (2x + 35)°
Equation:
5x − 10 = 2x + 35
QB 3 — Complementary
Complementary angles:
one is (4x)°, other is (x + 5)°
Equation:
4x + (x + 5) = 90
The Big Idea for today: The algebra is the same as Day 2. The new skill is reading a parallel-lines diagram and knowing which relationship to use. Once you can name the angle pair, you automatically know the equation setup.
Section 2 — Discovery Lab
Explore both applets before your teacher introduces the vocabulary. Your goal is to notice patterns on your own first.
Exploration 1 — Desmos: When Are Lines Parallel? desmos.com/geometry/yml570t9qt
Your task: Open the applet on your device. Drag the points to change the angles of the two lines. Watch the angle measurements update. Try to make the two lines look parallel — like railroad tracks that will never cross.
  • What do you have to do to the angles to make the lines look parallel?
  • When the lines ARE parallel, which angle pairs look equal? Which add to 180°?
  • Can you find two angles that are always equal, no matter how you tilt the transversal?
  • What happens to ALL the angles if you change just one angle measurement?
My observations (at least 2 things you noticed):
When the lines are parallel, corresponding angles (same position at each intersection) are always equal.
When any one angle changes, all eight angles change — there are only two distinct angle values in the whole diagram.
Angles on the same side of the transversal (interior) always add to 180°.

Your task: This applet shows two parallel lines cut by a transversal with 8 angles labeled 1–8. Click different angle pairs — the applet will tell you their name and relationship. Fill in the discovery table below as you click.

Section 3 — The 3-Step Detective Method
1
IDENTIFY
Name the angle pair type.
Corresponding? Alt. Interior? Alt. Exterior? Same-Side Interior?
2
TRANSLATE
Write the equation.
Equal → set expressions equal.
Supplementary → add to 180.
3
SOLVE & VERIFY
Solve for x. Check by substituting back.
Both angles should be equal (or sum to 180°).
Guided Example 1 — Corresponding Angles Watch the website animation, then fill in the blanks
Lines m ∥ n.  ∠1 = (5x + 10)°  and  ∠6 = (3x + 40)°.  Find x.
Step 1: IDENTIFY∠1 and ∠6 are Corresponding angles → they are EQUAL (congruent)
Step 2: TRANSLATEEquation: 5x + 10 = 3x + 40
Step 3: SOLVE2x = 30 → x = 15
Verify∠1 = 5(15) + 10 = 85°   ∠6 = 3(15) + 40 = 85° ✓
Guided Example 2 — Same-Side Interior Angles ⚠ This type uses a different equation setup
Lines m ∥ n.  ∠4 = (2x + 15)°  and  ∠5 = (3x + 25)°.  Find x and both angle measures.
Step 1: IDENTIFY∠4 and ∠5 are Same-Side Interior angles → they add to 180° (supplementary)
Step 2: TRANSLATEEquation: (2x + 15) + (3x + 25) = 180
Step 3: SOLVE5x + 40 = 180 → 5x = 140 → x = 28
∠4 = 71°     ∠5 = 109°     Check: ∠4 + ∠5 = 180° ✓
Spot the Error Challenge
Problem: Lines p ∥ q. ∠3 = (2x + 30)° and ∠5 = (x + 50)° are same-side interior angles. Find x.
Student's Work:
2x + 30 = x + 50
x = 20
What is wrong? (circle one)
○   Wrong algebra
○   Should add to 180°, not set equal
○   Forgot to distribute
○   Nothing is wrong
Correct solution:
(2x + 30) + (x + 50) = 180 → 3x + 80 = 180 → 3x = 100 → x = 100/3 ≈ 33.3
∠3 = 2(100/3)+30 = 96.7° · ∠5 = (100/3)+50 = 83.3° · sum = 180° ✓
Practice Problems — Use the 3-Step Method for Each
P1 — Alternate Interior
Lines m ∥ n. ∠3 = (4x − 10)° and ∠6 = (2x + 30)°. Solve for x.
Step 1: pair type & relationship?
Alternate Interior Angles → EQUAL → 4x − 10 = 2x + 30
2x = 40 → x = 20
x = 20
P2 — Corresponding
Lines m ∥ n. ∠1 = (7x + 5)° and ∠5 = (4x + 35)°. Solve for x.
Step 1: pair type & relationship?
Corresponding Angles → EQUAL → 7x + 5 = 4x + 35
3x = 30 → x = 10
x = 10
P3 — Alternate Exterior
Lines m ∥ n. ∠2 = (3x + 12)° and ∠8 = (5x − 20)°. Solve for x, then find each angle.
Alternate Exterior → EQUAL → 3x + 12 = 5x − 20 → 32 = 2x → x = 16
∠2 = 3(16)+12 = 60° · ∠8 = 5(16)−20 = 60° ✓
x = 16   ∠2 = 60°   ∠8 = 60°
P4 — Same-Side Interior
Lines m ∥ n. ∠3 = (x + 65)° and ∠5 = (2x + 25)°. Solve for x, then find each angle.
Same-Side Interior → ADD to 180 → (x+65)+(2x+25)=180 → 3x+90=180 → x=30
∠3 = 30+65 = 95° · ∠5 = 2(30)+25 = 85° · sum = 180° ✓
x = 30   ∠3 = 95°   ∠5 = 85°
Exit Ticket
ET1: Lines m ∥ n. ∠2 = (4x + 5)° and ∠7 = (7x − 34)° are alternate exterior angles. Find x.
Alt. Exterior → EQUAL → 4x + 5 = 7x − 34 → 39 = 3x → x = 13
∠2 = 4(13)+5 = 57° · ∠7 = 7(13)−34 = 57° ✓
x = 13
ET2: Name the one angle pair type that requires you to ADD to 180° (not set equal). Explain why in one sentence.
Same-Side Interior (Co-interior) Angles
These angles are on the same side of the transversal and between the parallel lines, so they form a straight line together and must add to 180°.
Pair type: Same-Side Interior (Co-interior)
