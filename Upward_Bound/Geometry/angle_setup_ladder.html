Nearly every angle problem is won or lost in the first five seconds — the moment you decide set equal, add to 90, or add to 180. This ladder drills that one decision until it's automatic, climbing from naming a relationship up to a full mixed diagram. No calculator — the whole skill is the setup, not the arithmetic.

Why working by hand wins

The calculator skips the one decision that matters

Naming sets the equation

Once you can say "vertical → equal" or "same-side interior → add to 180," the equation is automatic. The naming is the skill; the algebra is just Day 1.

Equal vs. sum is a judgment

Corresponding angles are equal; same-side interior angles are supplementary. Same diagram, opposite setups — a calculator can't make that call, and on a test, neither can it warn you when you've made it wrong.

The start is the bottleneck

Students rarely miss 5x=60. They miss it because they wrote =90 when it should have been =180. Drilling the setup removes the real error.

Calling "set equal or add to 180" before you write anything is the same instinct that flags a wrong setup instantly on the digital SAT — where the reference sheet hands you formulas but never tells you which relationship you're looking at. That judgment is yours only if you build it by hand.
