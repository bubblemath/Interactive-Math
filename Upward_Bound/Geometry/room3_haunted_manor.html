Day 7 — Distance & Midpoint

  • Distance: d = √[(x₂−x₁)² + (y₂−y₁)²] — the Pythagorean Theorem in disguise
  • Watch negatives: 4 − (−2) = 6, not 2
  • Simplify radicals: √(a·b) = √a · √b — pull out the largest perfect square
  • Triples (whole-number distances): 3-4-5 · 5-12-13 · 8-15-17 and multiples (6-8-10…)
  • Midpoint: M = ((x₁+x₂)/2, (y₁+y₂)/2) — just average the coordinates
  • Missing endpoint: apply the same change from endpoint → midpoint again, or solve (known + B)/2 = M

Day 6 — Deductive Reasoning & Proofs

  • The Because Test: every step is true because of a theorem, definition, or postulate — never "because it looks like it"
  • Algebraic reasons name the operation: Addition / Subtraction / Multiplication / Division Property of Equality, Distributive, Substitution
  • Geometric reasons: Vertical Angles Theorem, Def. of Midpoint, Def. of Angle Bisector, Segment/Angle Addition Postulate…
  • ≅ vs =: objects are congruent (≅); their measures are equal (=)
  • Converse: flip IF and THEN — not automatically true; prove it separately
  • Error types: Appearance · Wrong theorem · Missing step

Clue I   The Long Hall · Distance
Sealed

A corridor runs from A(0, 0) to B(6, 8). Find its exact length — the distance AB.

Strategy: Use d = √[(x₂−x₁)² + (y₂−y₁)²]. The two differences are the legs of a right triangle — square them, add, take the root. Check whether the legs fit a Pythagorean triple so the answer comes out whole.

Clue II   The Balcony · Midpoint
Sealed

Two candle sconces hang at A(−4, 7) and B(8, −3). Find the midpoint M exactly between them.

Strategy: The midpoint is just the average of the coordinates: M = ((x₁+x₂)/2, (y₁+y₂)/2). Average the x's, then the y's — and watch the signs when a coordinate is negative.

Clue III   The Study · Name the Reason
Sealed

In a two-column proof, the line 3x + 7 = 22 becomes 3x = 15. Which reason justifies that step?

Strategy: Name the step by the operation performed on both sides, not by the result. Ask: what was done to both sides of the equation to get from the first line to the second?

Clue IV   The Cellar · Missing Endpoint
Sealed

A passage starts at A(1, 4), and its midpoint is M(5, 7). Find the far endpoint B.

Strategy: The midpoint sits exactly halfway. Find the change in x and y from the known endpoint to the midpoint, then apply that same change again to reach the far endpoint. (Or solve (1 + x)/2 = 5 and (4 + y)/2 = 7.)
Spirit light ignites → 7
Clue V   The Séance · The Because Test
Sealed

A witness in the manor claims: "∠1 ≅ ∠2 because they look the same size." Why does this fail the Because Test as a proof reason?

Strategy: Apply the Because Test: a valid reason names a theorem, definition, or postulate. Ask what is wrong with justifying a claim by how something appears rather than by a stated rule.
