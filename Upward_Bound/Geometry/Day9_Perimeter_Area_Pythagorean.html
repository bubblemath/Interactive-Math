🛠️ AC Method Refresher: Factoring Quadratics

The Pythagorean Theorem with algebraic expressions often leads to quadratic equations. Let's review the AC Method to solve them!

📐 Standard Form

\(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\)

📝 The 5-Step AC Method

1
Write in Standard Form

Get all terms on one side: \(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\)

2
Find the Product \(a \cdot c\)

Multiply the leading coefficient by the constant term

3
Find Factor Pairs

Find two numbers that multiply to \(a \cdot c\) AND add to \(b\)

4
Split the Middle Term

Rewrite \(bx\) using the two numbers from Step 3

5
Factor by Grouping

Group terms and factor out common factors

🔷 The Diamond/X-Box Method

A visual tool to find the factor pairs:

💡 How It Works

Top: Product \(a \cdot c\)

Bottom: Sum \(b\)

Sides: The two numbers that multiply to the top and add to the bottom!

📝 Worked Example

Factor: \(2x^2 + 7x + 3 = 0\)

Step 1: Identify a, b, c
\(a = 2, b = 7, c = 3\)
Step 2: Find a·c
\(2 \times 3 = 6\)
Step 3: Find factors of 6 that add to 7
\(6 = 1 \times 6\) and \(1 + 6 = 7\) ✓
Step 4: Split middle term
\(2x^2 + 1x + 6x + 3 = 0\)
Step 5: Factor by grouping
\(x(2x + 1) + 3(2x + 1) = 0\)
\((x + 3)(2x + 1) = 0\)
\(x = -3\) or \(x = -\frac{1}{2}\)

💡 Key Insight: Why Formulas Work

Parallelogram → Rectangle: "Cut and paste" a triangle to form a rectangle. Same base, same height, same area!

Triangle = ½ Parallelogram: Two congruent triangles form a parallelogram, so one triangle is half.

Circle: Think of "unrolling" the circle into thin triangles — base = circumference, height = radius!

🧩 Composite Area Lab

Complex shapes can be broken into simpler pieces. Choose your strategy!

➕ Additive Method

🧱 + 🧱

Split the shape into pieces and ADD their areas

Best for: L-shapes, T-shapes, combined figures

➖ Subtractive Method

🟦 − 🔲

Find a bigger shape and SUBTRACT the missing piece

Best for: Shapes with holes, cutouts, shaded regions

📝 Example: Rectangle with Semicircular Cutout

A rectangle is 10 cm × 6 cm with a semicircle (diameter 4 cm) cut from one side. Find the area.

Step 1: Identify the method
Subtractive — we have a rectangle with something removed
Step 2: Find rectangle area
\(A_{rect} = 10 \times 6 = 60 \text{ cm}^2\)
Step 3: Find semicircle area
Diameter = 4, so radius = 2
\(A_{semi} = \frac{1}{2}\pi r^2 = \frac{1}{2}\pi(2)^2 = 2\pi \text{ cm}^2\)
Step 4: Subtract
\(A_{total} = 60 - 2\pi \approx 60 - 6.28 = 53.72 \text{ cm}^2\)

📝 Example: "Silo" Shape (Rectangle + Semicircle)

A barn door is shaped like a rectangle (8 ft × 12 ft) with a semicircle on top. Find the total area.

Step 1: Identify the method
Additive — rectangle PLUS semicircle
Step 2: Find rectangle area
\(A_{rect} = 8 \times 12 = 96 \text{ ft}^2\)
Step 3: Find semicircle area
Width = 8, so diameter = 8, radius = 4
\(A_{semi} = \frac{1}{2}\pi(4)^2 = 8\pi \text{ ft}^2\)
Step 4: Add
\(A_{total} = 96 + 8\pi \approx 96 + 25.13 = 121.13 \text{ ft}^2\)

💡 Detective Strategy

"Look at the shape. Can you SEE smaller shapes inside it? That's additive. Can you SEE a bigger shape with something missing? That's subtractive. Choose the method that makes the math easier!"

🔺 Pythagorean Algebra Lab: Finding X

When side lengths are algebraic expressions, the Pythagorean Theorem creates equations we can solve!

📐 The Pythagorean Theorem

\(a^2 + b^2 = c^2\)

Where a and b are legs, c is the hypotenuse

📝 Worked Example 1: One Algebraic Leg

A right triangle has legs x and 12, and hypotenuse 13. Find x.

Solution using "Identify → Translate → Solve → Check"

IDENTIFY
Right triangle → Pythagorean Theorem applies
TRANSLATE
\(x^2 + 12^2 = 13^2\)
SOLVE
\(x^2 + 144 = 169\)
\(x^2 = 25\)
\(x = 5\) (reject -5, lengths are positive)
CHECK
\(5^2 + 12^2 = 25 + 144 = 169 = 13^2\) ✓

📝 Worked Example 2: Two Algebraic Legs (Factorable!)

A right triangle has legs x and (x + 7), and hypotenuse 13. Find x.

This creates a quadratic — use the AC Method!

IDENTIFY
Right triangle → Pythagorean Theorem
TRANSLATE
\(x^2 + (x + 7)^2 = 13^2\)
EXPAND
\(x^2 + x^2 + 14x + 49 = 169\)
\(2x^2 + 14x + 49 = 169\)
STANDARD FORM
\(2x^2 + 14x - 120 = 0\)
Divide by 2: \(x^2 + 7x - 60 = 0\)
FACTOR (AC Method)
Need factors of -60 that add to 7: (12) and (-5)
\((x + 12)(x - 5) = 0\)
\(x = -12\) or \(x = 5\)
CHECK
Reject x = -12 (negative length)
x = 5: legs are 5 and 12, hypotenuse is 13 ✓ (5-12-13 triple!)

📝 Worked Example 3: Pythagorean Triple Pattern

A right triangle has legs 3x and 4x, and hypotenuse 5x. If the perimeter is 60, find x.

IDENTIFY
This is a 3-4-5 triple scaled by x!
TRANSLATE (Perimeter)
\(3x + 4x + 5x = 60\)
SOLVE
\(12x = 60\)
\(x = 5\)
CHECK
Sides: 15, 20, 25
\(15^2 + 20^2 = 225 + 400 = 625 = 25^2\) ✓

⚠️ Important Reminder

When solving quadratics from Pythagorean problems, always reject negative solutions — side lengths must be positive!

Also check that your answer makes sense: the hypotenuse should be the longest side.
