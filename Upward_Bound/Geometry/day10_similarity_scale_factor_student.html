What is the "zoom factor"?
In geometry we call this the scale factor k.

Scale Factor & Similar Figures
k = New Side / Original Side
New Side = k × Original Side
All angles stay equal. All sides scale by k.

Section 4 — Triangle Similarity Reference (AA, SAS, SSS)
Reference only — know the three postulate names and what each requires.
AA — Angle-Angle
What you need: 2 pairs of congruent angles
If ∠A ≅ ∠D and ∠B ≅ ∠E → △ABC ~ △DEF
Why only 2 angles? Triangle Sum guarantees the 3rd!
SAS — Side-Angle-Side
What you need: 2 pairs of proportional sides + included angle ≅
AB/DE = AC/DF, and ∠A ≅ ∠D → △ABC ~ △DEF
The angle must be BETWEEN the two proportional sides.
SSS — Side-Side-Side
What you need: all 3 pairs of sides proportional
AB/DE = BC/EF = AC/DF → △ABC ~ △DEF
All ratios must be equal (same scale factor k).
SSS Similarity ≠ SSS Congruence!
Similarity needs proportional sides. Congruence needs equal sides.
