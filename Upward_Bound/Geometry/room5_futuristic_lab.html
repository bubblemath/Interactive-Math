Day 10 — Similarity & Scale Factor

  • Scale factor: k = New ÷ Original
  • Missing side: New = k × Original (match the vertices in the ~ statement)
  • Proportion method: a/b = c/d → ad = bc (cross-multiply)
  • Similar (~): same shape, sides proportional, all angles equal
  • Congruent (≅): same shape AND same size (k = 1)
  • Area scales by k², not k. Sides ×k → area ×k²
  • Triangle similarity: AA, SAS, SSS

Day 11 — Transformations

  • Translate: (x+a, y+b) — +a right, −a left, +b up, −b down
  • Reflect x-axis: (x, −y) · y-axis: (−x, y)
  • Reflect y = x: (y, x) · y = −x: (−y, −x)
  • Rotate 90° CCW: (−y, x) · 180°: (−x, −y) · 270° CCW: (y, −x)
  • Dilate: (kx, ky) — the only non-rigid one; makes a similar figure
  • Composition: do the first-listed move first; feed its output into the next
  • Rigid (translate/reflect/rotate) = congruent. Dilation = similar.

Strategy: Sides scale by k, but area scales by k². Don't just multiply by 3 — first work out what k² is, then multiply the area by that.
