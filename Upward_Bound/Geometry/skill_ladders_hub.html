The idea

Why these ladders exist

A calculator will happily square a number, average two coordinates, or multiply by a scale factor. What it won't do is tell you whether to add or subtract, which formula to reach for, or whether to scale by k or k². Those decisions are the real mathematics — and they're exactly what a timed, no-calculator test rewards.

Each ladder isolates a single family of skills and makes that decision the point of every rung. The arithmetic is kept deliberately clean — whole numbers, reduced fractions, exact answers like 16π — so the work stays mental and the reasoning stays in front. Practice a little every day, and the moves that once needed a calculator become things you simply see.

01

One decision per rung

Each ladder begins with a near-trivial first rung and adds exactly one new decision as you climb, ending in a mixed challenge where nothing is labeled.

02

Open practice, badges to earn

Every rung is unlocked from the start — practice whatever you need. Four correct in a row earns that rung's badge, and your progress is saved on your device.

03

A signature first move

Every ladder drills one habit above all — predict the sign, call the setup, name the reason, pick the power. That's the "how do I start?" instinct that carries to the test.

04

Built for no calculator

Problems are randomized and answers stay exact, so there's always more practice and never a reason to reach for the keypad. The point is the thinking.
