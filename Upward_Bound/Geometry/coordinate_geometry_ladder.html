Why working by hand wins

The calculator skips the sanity checks

Sign before size

Reading rise and fall tells you a slope's sign before any arithmetic. A wrong sign is the most common slope error — and a calculator will never flag it for you.

Reduce, don't decimalize

Slope is a reduced fraction: −6/8 becomes −3/4, not −0.75. The decimal throws away the rise-over-run meaning the next step needs.

The midpoint lands between

A midpoint must sit between its two points and a distance must be positive. Feeling that catches a sign slip the keypad would have rubber-stamped.

Coordinate geometry is where algebra and geometry meet — a slope is a fraction, a distance is the Pythagorean theorem, a midpoint is an average. Build these two-point moves by hand and the graphs on the digital SAT stop being a separate subject; they're arithmetic you can already see coming.
