🔧 THE TRANSFORMATION TOOLKIT

THE MASTER FORMULA

\( y = \underbrace{a}_{\text{vert. stretch}} \cdot f\left(\underbrace{b}_{\text{horiz. stretch}}(x - \underbrace{h}_{\text{horiz. shift}})\right) + \underbrace{k}_{\text{vert. shift}} \)

📐 VERTICAL (Outside)

a > 1 Stretch vertically
0 < a < 1 Compress vertically
a < 0 Reflect over x-axis
+k Shift UP k units
−k Shift DOWN k units

↔️ HORIZONTAL (Inside)

b > 1 Compress horizontally
0 < b < 1 Stretch horizontally
b < 0 Reflect over y-axis
−h Shift RIGHT h units
+h Shift LEFT h units

🔶 INSIDE the Function

Affects \(x\)-values (horizontal)

⚠️ OPPOSITE of what you'd expect!

COUNTER-INTUITIVE

🔷 OUTSIDE the Function

Affects \(y\)-values (vertical)

✓ Does exactly what it says!

FOLLOWS THE SIGN
⚠️ COMMON MISTAKES & CORRECTIONS
❌ The Mistake Why It's Wrong ✅ The Correction
"The Sign Trap"
\(f(x+3)\) shifts right 		Inside changes are OPPOSITE! The formula is \(f(x-h)\), so \(f(x+3) = f(x-(-3))\) means \(h = -3\). \(f(x+3)\) shifts LEFT 3 units
\(f(x-3)\) shifts RIGHT 3 units
"Order Chaos"
Applying shifts before stretches 		The order of transformations matters! Wrong order = wrong graph. Use H-S-R-V order:
Horizontal → Stretch → Reflect → Vertical
"Reflection Blur"
\(-f(x) = f(-x)\) 		These are completely different reflections! \(-f(x)\) = reflect over x-axis
\(f(-x)\) = reflect over y-axis

The H-S-R-V Method & Worked Example
Page 2 of 2
🔧 PROCEDURE: The H-S-R-V Method

Apply transformations in this order for accurate hand sketches:

H
Horizontal Shift
Move left/right by \(h\)
S
Stretch/Compress
Apply \(a\) and \(b\)
R
Reflections
Flip if \(a\) or \(b\) negative
V
Vertical Shift
Move up/down by \(k\)
  1. Identify the parent function and list its anchor points.
  2. Extract the transformation parameters: \(a\), \(b\), \(h\), and \(k\).
  3. Transform each anchor point using the formula: \((x, y) \to \left(\frac{x}{b} + h, \; a \cdot y + k\right)\)
  4. Plot the new anchor points and connect with the appropriate curve shape.
  5. Verify domain and range match the transformations applied.
✏️ WORKED EXAMPLE: Sketch \(g(x) = -2|x + 3| + 4\)
Step 1: Identify Parent & Anchors
Parent: \(f(x) = |x|\) (Absolute Value)
Anchor points: \((0,0), (1,1), (-1,1)\)
Step 2: Extract Parameters
Rewrite: \(g(x) = -2|x - (-3)| + 4\)

• \(a = -2\) (vert. stretch ×2, reflect x-axis)
• \(b = 1\) (no horiz. stretch)
• \(h = -3\) (shift LEFT 3)
• \(k = +4\) (shift UP 4)
Step 3: Apply H-S-R-V
H: Shift all x-values left 3
S: Multiply y-values by 2
R: Negate y-values (flip)
V: Add 4 to all y-values
Step 4: Transform Anchor Points
Original \((x, y)\) After H After S After R After V
\((0, 0)\) \((-3, 0)\) \((-3, 0)\) \((-3, 0)\) \((-3, 4)\)
\((1, 1)\) \((-2, 1)\) \((-2, 2)\) \((-2, -2)\) \((-2, 2)\)
\((-1, 1)\) \((-4, 1)\) \((-4, 2)\) \((-4, -2)\) \((-4, 2)\)
Final Anchors: \((-3, 4), (-2, 2), (-4, 2)\)
Vertex at \((-3, 4)\), opens DOWN

👻 THE GHOST PARENT OVERLAY

Ghost: \(f(x) = |x|\) centered at origin, opens up

Transformed: \(g(x) = -2|x+3|+4\) vertex at \((-3, 4)\), opens down, steeper

Domain: \((-\infty, \infty)\) | Range: \((-\infty, 4]\)

✅ QUICK REFERENCE CHECKLIST
  • Did I identify the correct parent function?

  • Did I extract \(a\), \(b\), \(h\), and \(k\) correctly?
  • Did I remember "inside = opposite"?
  • Did I apply transformations in H-S-R-V order?
  • Did I transform ALL anchor points?
  • Does my curve match the parent's shape?
  • Did I verify domain and range?
