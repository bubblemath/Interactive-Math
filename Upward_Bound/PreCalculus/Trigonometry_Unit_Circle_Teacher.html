🎡 Teacher Notes: Day 10

Trigonometry & the Unit Circle | Pre-Calculus Bridge Course | Week 2, Day 6
📖 Lesson Overview

The Big Picture: This lesson transitions students from right-triangle trigonometry to "circular" trigonometry. We move from SOH-CAH-TOA with triangles to coordinates on a circle.

The Conceptual Anchor: 🎡 The "Ferris Wheel" Metaphor — As we move around the circle, our horizontal position is \(\cos(\theta)\) and our vertical position is \(\sin(\theta)\). The radius is always 1 (the "Unit" in Unit Circle).

Key Insight: In a Unit Circle, \(r = 1\), so \(\sin(\theta) = y/r = y\) and \(\cos(\theta) = x/r = x\). We aren't changing the rules of trig—we're just making the denominator easy!

Calculus Connection: Radian fluency is REQUIRED for all derivatives and integrals of trig functions. This is non-negotiable preparation.

🎯 Learning Objectives
  • Define the six trigonometric functions using the Unit Circle \((x, y, r)\).
  • Identify and memorize coordinates for special angles \((30°, 45°, 60°)\).
  • Convert between Degrees and Radians fluently.
  • Use the ASTC ("All Students Take Calculus") grid to determine signs in quadrants.
  • Calculate reference angles for any given rotation.
  • Evaluate reciprocal functions \((\csc, \sec, \cot)\) without calculator dependency.

🔄 Degree ↔ Radian Conversion
THE CONVERSION FORMULAS
Degrees → Radians: Multiply by \(\frac{\pi}{180°}\)
Radians → Degrees: Multiply by \(\frac{180°}{\pi}\)

Key Fact: \(180° = \pi\) radians  |  \(360° = 2\pi\) radians

🎤 Teacher Script: The "Unit" Secret
"Remind students that in a 'Unit' circle, the radius is 1. This is why \(\sin(\theta) = y/r\) simply becomes \(y\). We aren't changing the rules of trig; we're just making the denominator easy! The coordinates \((x, y)\) on the circle ARE the cosine and sine values."
📐 Reference Angle Formulas

Q I: ref = θ   |   Q II: ref = 180° − θ   |   Q III: ref = θ − 180°   |   Q IV: ref = 360° − θ

Day 10: Pitfalls & Calculus Connections

⚠️ Common Pitfalls & Teaching Strategies
1. Calculator Mode Error

Students solve in degrees while the problem is in radians (or vice versa).

Strategy: Mandatory "MODE CHECK" at the start of every problem. Have students physically point to their calculator screen and say "I'm in [degree/radian] mode."
2. Reciprocal Confusion

Students think \(\cos\) goes with \(\csc\) because they both start with 'c'.

Strategy: Use the "One 's' per pair" rule: \(\sin \leftrightarrow \csc\), \(\cos \leftrightarrow \sec\). The pair with NO 's' is \(\tan \leftrightarrow \cot\).
3. Quadrant Blindness

Students forget to check the sign (+/−) after finding the reference angle value.

Strategy: Enforce the "Sign-Value-Check" workflow: (1) Find the quadrant, (2) Find the reference angle, (3) Get the value, (4) Apply the correct sign using ASTC.
4. Undefined vs. Zero Confusion

Students confuse when trig functions are 0 vs. undefined (e.g., \(\tan 90°\)).

Strategy: Emphasize: "Division by zero = UNDEFINED." If the denominator is 0, the function doesn't exist at that angle. Draw the vertical asymptote!
🔗 Calculus Connections
Pre-Calculus Skill Calculus Application
Unit Circle Coordinates Evaluating limits of trig functions at special angles.
Radian Fluency REQUIRED for all derivatives and integrals of trig functions. Degrees don't work!
Reciprocal Identities Simplifying expressions for the Quotient Rule and integration.
Reference Angles Solving trigonometric equations in optimization problems.
Special Angle Values Evaluating derivatives: \(\frac{d}{dx}[\sin x] = \cos x\), so \(\frac{d}{dx}[\sin x]\big|_{x=\pi/3} = \cos(\pi/3) = \frac{1}{2}\)
✅ Formative Assessment Checkpoints
  • Instant Recall: Can they identify the coordinates of \(3\pi/2\) instantly? (Answer: \((0, -1)\))
  • Conceptual Understanding: Do they know WHY \(\tan(90°)\) is undefined? (Division by zero: \(x = 0\))
  • Conversion Fluency: Can they convert \(120°\) to radians without a calculator? (Answer: \(2\pi/3\))
  • ASTC Application: Given \(\sin(\theta) < 0\) and \(\cos(\theta) > 0\), which quadrant? (Answer: Q IV)

Day 10: Exit Ticket & Differentiation

📝 Exit Ticket & Answers
Exit Ticket Problems
  1. Evaluate \(\sin(210°)\).
    Answer: Reference angle = \(210° - 180° = 30°\). Q III → sin is negative. \(\sin(210°) = -\frac{1}{2}\)
  2. Evaluate \(\cos(3\pi/4)\).
    Answer: \(3\pi/4 = 135°\). Reference angle = \(180° - 135° = 45°\). Q II → cos is negative. \(\cos(3\pi/4) = -\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\)
  3. Evaluate \(\tan(180°)\).
    Answer: At \(180°\), coordinates are \((-1, 0)\). \(\tan = y/x = 0/-1 = \) \(0\)
  4. Convert \(5\pi/3\) to degrees.
    Answer: \(\frac{5\pi}{3} \times \frac{180°}{\pi} = \frac{5 \times 180°}{3} = \) \(300°\)
  5. If \(\cos(\theta) = -\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\) and \(\theta\) is in Q II, find \(\theta\) in radians.
    Answer: Reference angle with \(\cos = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\) is \(30° = \pi/6\). In Q II: \(\theta = \pi - \pi/6 = \) \(\frac{5\pi}{6}\)
🎚️ Differentiation Strategies
🔴 For Struggling Students
  • Provide a "skeleton" unit circle with only the axes and 45° marks to reduce cognitive load
  • Use color-coded quadrant cards (green Q1, blue Q2, etc.)
  • Start with only the "friendly" angles: 0°, 90°, 180°, 270°
  • Provide a conversion chart for degrees ↔ radians
  • Use the "hand trick" for special angle values
🟢 For Advanced Students
  • Introduce the Pythagorean Identity: \(\sin^2\theta + \cos^2\theta = 1\) and ask them to prove it using Unit Circle coordinates
  • Explore negative angles and coterminal angles
  • Challenge: Find all angles where \(\sin(\theta) = \cos(\theta)\)
  • Preview: Graph \(y = \sin(x)\) using unit circle values
  • Derive the other Pythagorean identities
🎤 Calculus Preview Teaser (End of Class)
"Today you learned that \(\sin(\pi/6) = 1/2\). In Calculus, you'll learn that the DERIVATIVE of sine is cosine. So if you want to know how fast sine is changing at \(\pi/6\), you evaluate \(\cos(\pi/6) = \sqrt{3}/2\). The unit circle values you memorized today will be used CONSTANTLY in Calculus. This is your foundation!"

Day 10: Partner & Small Group Activities

👥 Partner & Small Group Activities
1. The Human Unit Circle 🎡
Whole Class
Use masking tape on the floor to create a large circle with x and y axes. Assign students specific angles (e.g., "You are 120°"). Students physically move to their position on the circle and shout out their \((x, y)\) coordinates. Rotate assignments so everyone practices multiple angles.
📦 Materials: Masking tape, angle assignment cards, open floor space
✨ Reinforces: Spatial understanding, coordinate memorization, kinesthetic learning
2. Unit Circle "Speed Dating" 💕
Rotating Pairs
One student holds a card with an angle (e.g., \(210°\)). Their partner must provide: (1) the radian equivalent, (2) the reference angle, (3) the quadrant, and (4) the \(\sin\) and \(\cos\) values—all before the timer buzzes (30 seconds). Then rotate partners!
📦 Materials: Angle cards, timer, rotation chart
✨ Reinforces: Quick recall, conversion fluency, reference angle calculation
3. "Who Am I?" Trig Riddles 🔍
Groups of 3-4
Students receive riddle cards with clues like: "I am negative in Q2 and Q3, and my value is \(-\sqrt{3}/2\) at \(210°\). Who am I?" (Answer: \(\cos\)). Groups race to solve riddles correctly. Create your own riddles as an extension activity!
📦 Materials: Riddle cards, answer key, scoreboard
✨ Reinforces: ASTC signs, function identification, logical reasoning
4. Radian-Degree "War" 🃏
Pairs
A card game where students flip cards showing angles in different units (some in degrees, some in radians). Both players must convert to the same unit to compare. The player with the LARGER angle wins the round and takes both cards. Most cards at the end wins!
📦 Materials: Mixed angle cards (degrees and radians), reference sheet optional
✨ Reinforces: Conversion fluency, comparing angle measures, mental math
5. Reference Angle Relay 🏃
Teams of 4
A relay race where each team member handles one step: Student 1 identifies the quadrant, Student 2 calculates the reference angle, Student 3 determines the sign using ASTC, and Student 4 writes the final trig value. First team with correct answer scores!
📦 Materials: Problem cards, whiteboards, markers, relay batons (optional)
✨ Reinforces: Step-by-step procedure, teamwork, accuracy under pressure
6. Symmetry Discovery Lab 🔬
Pairs
Using patty paper or transparencies, students draw the first quadrant of the unit circle with special angles labeled. They then FOLD the paper over the y-axis to "discover" Q2 coordinates, then fold over the x-axis for Q3 and Q4. Students record their discoveries.
📦 Materials: Patty paper/transparencies, unit circle template, colored pencils
✨ Reinforces: Symmetry understanding, visual learning, coordinate patterns
7. "Build the Circle" Puzzle Challenge 🧩
Groups of 3
Give each group a blank unit circle and a set of cards with: angle measures (degrees), angle measures (radians), and coordinate pairs. Groups must correctly match and place ALL cards on the circle. First group with a complete, correct circle wins!
📦 Materials: Blank unit circle poster, matching cards (3 types), tape/magnets
✨ Reinforces: Complete unit circle knowledge, matching, collaborative problem-solving
