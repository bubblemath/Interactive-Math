The Unit Circle
Q I pre-printed  ·  Complete Q II, Q III, and Q IV  ·  (cos θ, sin θ) at every standard angle
Name:                         
Definition
r = 1
Circle centered at origin with radius 1. Every point on it is exactly 1 unit from the center.
The Key Coordinate Fact
( cos θ, sin θ )
Every point on the unit circle has coordinates (x, y) = (cos θ, sin θ). The x-coordinate IS cosine; the y-coordinate IS sine.
Why it works
x = cos θ = A/H = A/1
y = sin θ = O/H = O/1
When r = 1, SOH-CAH-TOA gives x = cos θ and y = sin θ directly.

★ The Big Connection: The Unit Circle IS the Sine and Cosine Waves
As a point moves counterclockwise around the unit circle at a steady speed, its y-coordinate traces out the sine wave and its x-coordinate traces out the cosine wave.
→
The circle has circumference 2π. One full trip around = one full period of sin and cos. That is why both functions have period .
