Section 3 - The Discriminant: Your Strategic Compass
The Discriminant Formula

Section 4 - The AC Method (Diamond and X-Box)

Use this when \(a \neq 1\) or when you need a systematic way to factor.

The 5-Step Procedure:

  1. Multiply \(a \cdot c\). This is your "Target Product."
  2. Identify \(b\). This is your "Target Sum."
  3. Find the Factor Pair. Find two numbers that multiply to and add to .
  4. Split the Middle. Rewrite \(bx\) using the two numbers from Step 3.
  5. Factor by Grouping. Group the first two terms and the last two terms. Factor out the GCF from each group. The "matching parenthesis" is your first factor!
