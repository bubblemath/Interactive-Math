🎮 Warm-Up: Transform or Inverse?

Identify whether each statement describes a transformation or an inverse relationship.

Statement 1: The graph of \(g(x) = (x-3)^2 + 2\) compared to \(f(x) = x^2\)

✓ Transformation! The function is shifted right 3 and up 2 from the parent.

Statement 2: If \(f(x) = 2x + 1\), then \(g(x) = \frac{x-1}{2}\) is related by...

✓ Inverse! g(x) "undoes" f(x). Check: f(g(x)) = 2·(x-1)/2 + 1 = x ✓

Statement 3: The graph of \(h(x) = -\sqrt{x}\) compared to \(f(x) = \sqrt{x}\)

✓ Transformation! The negative reflects the graph over the x-axis.

Statement 4: The functions \(f(x) = e^x\) and \(g(x) = \ln(x)\) are related by...

✓ Inverse! The natural log is the inverse of the exponential function.

📚 Key Concepts

🎢 Transformation Form

\(y = a \cdot f(x - h) + k\)
a Vertical stretch (|a|>1) or compress (|a|<1)
If a < 0: reflect over x-axis
h Horizontal shift
(x - h): shift RIGHT h units
(x + h): shift LEFT h units
k Vertical shift
+k: shift UP
-k: shift DOWN

🔄 Finding Inverses Algebraically

Step 1: Replace f(x) with y

Step 2: Swap x and y

Step 3: Solve for y

Step 4: Replace y with f⁻¹(x)

\(f(f^{-1}(x)) = x\) and \(f^{-1}(f(x)) = x\)

📝 Worked Example: Finding an Inverse

Find the inverse of \(f(x) = 3x - 5\)

Step 1

\(y = 3x - 5\)

Step 2

\(x = 3y - 5\)

Step 3

\(x + 5 = 3y\)
\(y = \frac{x+5}{3}\)

Step 4

\(f^{-1}(x) = \frac{x+5}{3}\)

📏 Horizontal Line Test

A function has an inverse if and only if every horizontal line crosses the graph at most once.

Passes HLT: Linear, Cubic, Exponential, Logarithmic

Fails HLT: Quadratic, Absolute Value, Sine, Cosine

🪞 Reflection Property

The graphs of f and f⁻¹ are reflections over the line y = x.

If (a, b) is on f, then (b, a) is on f⁻¹

Domain of f = Range of f⁻¹
Range of f = Domain of f⁻¹
