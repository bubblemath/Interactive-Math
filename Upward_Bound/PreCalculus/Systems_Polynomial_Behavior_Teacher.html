📊 Teacher Notes: Day 9

Systems of Equations & Polynomial Behavior | Pre-Calculus Bridge Course | Week 2, Day 5
📖 Lesson Overview

The Big Picture: Day 9 bridges algebraic solving with graphical analysis. Students master three methods for solving systems (Substitution, Elimination, Graphing) while learning to predict polynomial behavior from the equation alone.

The Conceptual Anchor: "The Story of the Ends" — The degree and leading coefficient dictate the ultimate fate of the graph. Think of the Leading Coefficient as the "Boss" and the Degree as the "Rulebook."

Key Insight: Multiplicity determines local behavior at roots. Odd multiplicity = "Cross through." Even multiplicity = "Bounce back."

Calculus Connection: End behavior previews limits at infinity; multiplicity connects to local extrema and inflection points.

🎯 Learning Objectives
  • Identify the degree and leading coefficient of a polynomial.
  • Apply the Leading Coefficient Test to determine end behavior.
  • Determine the multiplicity of roots and describe "Cross vs. Bounce" behavior.
  • Solve 2×2 systems of linear equations using Substitution and Elimination.
  • Interpret system solutions graphically (One solution, No solution, Infinite solutions).
  • Sketch polynomial graphs using end behavior and root behavior.

🎤 Teacher Script: The Boss & Rulebook
"Think of the Leading Coefficient as the 'Boss' and the Degree as the 'Rulebook.' If the degree is even, the ends want to go the same way—like a touchdown signal. If the degree is odd, they go in opposite directions—like a disco dance move. A negative Boss? He flips the Rulebook upside down!"
🎯 Multiplicity: Cross vs. Bounce
✖️ CROSS (Odd Multiplicity)

Multiplicity 1, 3, 5, ...

Graph passes THROUGH the x-axis

"Screen door—walk right through"

🔄 BOUNCE (Even Multiplicity)

Multiplicity 2, 4, 6, ...

Graph TOUCHES and bounces back

"Trampoline—bounce back"

Tip: Multiplicity 3+ creates a "flattening" effect at the root—like pushing through a heavy door.

Day 9: Pitfalls & Calculus Connections

⚠️ Common Pitfalls & Teaching Strategies
1. Confusing Degree with Leading Coefficient

Students mix up "the highest power" with "the number in front."

Strategy: Color-code! Highlight the exponent in RED and the coefficient in BLUE. For \(f(x) = -3x^4 + 2x\), the degree is 4 and the LC is -3.
2. Forgetting to Distribute in Elimination

Students multiply only the first term when scaling an equation.

Strategy: Use the "Distributive Justice" metaphor—EVERYONE in the equation gets the multiplier. No term left behind!
3. Misinterpreting "No Solution" as \(x = 0\)

When elimination yields \(0 = 5\), students think \(x = 0\).

Strategy: Show parallel lines visually. Emphasize: "0 = 5 is a FALSE STATEMENT, not a value. It means the lines never meet!"
4. Incorrect End Behavior for Negative LC

Students forget that a negative leading coefficient "flips" the behavior.

Strategy: Remind them: "A negative Boss flips the Rulebook upside down." Practice with hand signals—flip hands when LC is negative.
🔗 Calculus Connections
Pre-Calculus Skill Calculus Application
End Behavior Introduction to Limits at Infinity: \(\lim_{x \to \infty} f(x)\) and horizontal asymptotes.
Multiplicity of Roots Identifying local extrema and points of inflection. Even multiplicity often indicates a local min/max.
Systems of Equations Finding points of intersection for Area Between Curves: \(\int_a^b [f(x) - g(x)] dx\).

✅ Formative Assessment Checkpoints
  • The 3-Second Rule: Flash a polynomial on the screen. Students have 3 seconds to show end behavior with their hands (both up, both down, or opposite).
  • Substitution vs. Elimination Vote: Present a system. Students vote (thumbs up for Substitution, thumbs down for Elimination) and justify their choice.
  • Cross or Bounce? Show a factored polynomial. Students hold up 1 finger for "Cross" or 2 fingers for "Bounce" at each root.
  • Quick Sketch: Give degree, LC sign, and roots. Students sketch the general shape on whiteboards.

Day 9: Exit Ticket & Differentiation

📝 Exit Ticket & Answers
Exit Ticket Problems
  1. Determine the end behavior of \(f(x) = -2x^3 + 5x\).
    Answer: Odd degree (3), Negative LC (-2). Up on left, Down on right. (↖ ↘)
  2. Describe the behavior at \(x = 2\) for \(g(x) = (x-2)^2(x+1)\).
    Answer: At \(x = 2\), multiplicity is 2 (even). The graph BOUNCES at this root.
  3. Solve the system: \(\begin{cases} x + y = 4 \\ x - y = 2 \end{cases}\)
    Answer: Add equations: \(2x = 6\) → \(x = 3\). Substitute: \(3 + y = 4\) → \(y = 1\). Solution: (3, 1)
  4. What type of solution does this system have? \(\begin{cases} 2x + 4y = 8 \\ x + 2y = 4 \end{cases}\)
    Answer: Second equation × 2 = first equation. Infinite solutions (same line).
🎚️ Differentiation Strategies
🔴 For Struggling Students
  • Provide a "Leading Coefficient Cheat Sheet" with all 4 visual cases
  • Color-code systems: \(x\) terms in blue, \(y\) terms in red
  • Use the "Wall" analogy cards for multiplicity
  • Start with systems where one variable is already isolated
  • Provide graph paper for visual system solving
🟢 For Advanced Students
  • Introduce 3×3 systems of equations
  • Challenge: Find a polynomial with specific roots AND a specific y-intercept
  • Explore what happens when multiplicity > 3
  • Connect to partial fractions (Calculus II preview)
  • Investigate systems with non-linear equations
🎤 Calculus Preview Teaser (End of Class)
"Today you learned to predict where a polynomial goes at the far ends and what it does at each root. In Calculus, you'll learn to find the EXACT turning points—where the graph switches from going up to going down. That's where derivatives come in. You're building the foundation right now!"

Day 9: Partner & Small Group Activities

👥 Partner & Small Group Activities
1. End Behavior "Human Graphs" 🙋
Groups of 3-4
Give each group a polynomial equation. Students must use their arms to represent the end behavior (both up, both down, or opposite directions) while one student stands in the middle to represent the "turning points" based on the degree. Groups present to the class.
📦 Materials: Polynomial cards, open space for movement
✨ Reinforces: Leading Coefficient Test, Degree identification, Kinesthetic learning
2. Systems Speed Dating 💕
Rotating Pairs
Half the class gets "System Cards" (pairs of equations), the other half gets "Solution Cards" (ordered pairs). Students rotate around the room, performing quick mental math or scratch work to find their matching partner. Once matched, they verify together and swap cards for the next round.
📦 Materials: System cards, Solution cards, timer
✨ Reinforces: Substitution, Elimination, Verification, Mental math
3. Multiplicity "Sort & Sketch" 🎨
Groups of 3
Provide cards with factored polynomials. Groups must sort them into three categories: "Crosses at all roots," "Bounces at one root," or "Flattens at a root (multiplicity 3+)." After sorting, each group sketches one polynomial from each category on a whiteboard.
📦 Materials: Factored polynomial cards, whiteboards, markers, sorting mat
✨ Reinforces: Multiplicity, Root behavior, Polynomial sketching
4. "Who Broke the System?" Error Analysis 🔍
Pairs
Provide 3-4 "solved" systems with intentional, common errors (e.g., failing to distribute a negative, arithmetic mistakes, forgetting to substitute back). Partners act as "Math Detectives" to find each error and write a "Corrective Action Report" explaining what went wrong and how to fix it.
📦 Materials: Error problem sheets, "Detective Report" templates
✨ Reinforces: Elimination method, Attention to detail, Error recognition
5. Polynomial Reverse Engineering 🔄
Groups of 3
Give groups a graph of a polynomial (showing end behavior and x-intercepts with their behavior). They must work backward to write a possible equation in factored form, accounting for the end behavior and the behavior at each x-intercept. Groups compare answers—multiple correct answers are possible!
📦 Materials: Polynomial graph cards, answer recording sheets
✨ Reinforces: Synthesis of ALL Day 9 concepts, Multiple representations
6. The "3-Second Challenge" Tournament 🏆
Teams of 4
Teams compete in rounds. A polynomial is displayed for 3 seconds. Teams must quickly identify: (1) the degree, (2) the sign of the LC, and (3) show the end behavior with hand signals. Points awarded for speed and accuracy. Championship bracket format!
📦 Materials: Polynomial slides, scoreboard, timer, small prizes
✨ Reinforces: Quick identification, Leading Coefficient Test, Team collaboration
7. "Build the System" Challenge 🧱
Pairs (Advanced)
Give pairs a solution point (e.g., (2, 5)) and constraints (e.g., "one equation must have a coefficient of 3"). Pairs must CREATE a system of equations that has that exact solution. They swap with another pair to verify. Award bonus points for creative or elegant systems.
📦 Materials: Solution point cards, constraint cards, verification sheets
✨ Reinforces: Deep understanding of systems, Reverse problem-solving
