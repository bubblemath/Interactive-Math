📚 Key Concepts

📊 Function Modeling Toolkit

📈 Linear

y = mx + b

Use when: Constant rate of change

Examples: Distance at constant speed, simple interest, depreciation

📐 Quadratic

y = ax² + bx + c

Use when: Rate of change is changing linearly

Examples: Projectile motion, area, braking distance

📊 Exponential

y = a · bˣ

Use when: Constant percentage change

Examples: Population, compound interest, radioactive decay

🌊 Sinusoidal

y = A·sin(Bx) + k

Use when: Periodic/cyclical patterns

Examples: Tides, temperature, sound waves

♾️ Limit Notation & Meaning

\(\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L\)

"As x approaches a, f(x) approaches L"

➡️ One-Sided Limits

\(\lim_{x \to a^-}\) = from the LEFT
\(\lim_{x \to a^+}\) = from the RIGHT

Two-sided limit exists only if both one-sided limits are equal!

🔄 Limits at Infinity

\(\lim_{x \to \infty} f(x)\)
\(\lim_{x \to -\infty} f(x)\)

Describes end behavior / horizontal asymptotes

📈 Infinite Limits

\(\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = \infty\)
\(\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = -\infty\)

Indicates vertical asymptote at x = a

🧮 Evaluating Limits Algebraically

  1. Try direct substitution first! If you get a number, you're done.
  2. If you get 0/0 (indeterminate form):
    • Factor and cancel
    • Rationalize (multiply by conjugate)
    • Simplify complex fractions
  3. If you get number/0: limit is ±∞ or DNE

🚀 Calculus Preview: The Derivative Definition

\(f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h}\)

This is THE fundamental concept of Calculus! It measures the instantaneous rate of change at a point.

— Secant line (average rate)  |  — Tangent line (instantaneous rate)

As h → 0, the secant line approaches the tangent line!
