📜 The Ancient Scrolls of Algebra

Study these sacred texts to master the arts of factoring and radical simplification.

🔷 Factoring when a = 1

For \(x^2 + bx + c\):

  1. Find two numbers that multiply to \(c\)
  2. AND add to \(b\)
  3. Write as \((x + \text{num}_1)(x + \text{num}_2)\)

Example: \(x^2 + 7x + 12 = (x+3)(x+4)\)
because \(3 \times 4 = 12\) and \(3 + 4 = 7\)

🔶 The AC Method (a > 1)

For \(ax^2 + bx + c\):

  1. Multiply \(a \times c\)
  2. Find factors of \(ac\) that add to \(b\)
  3. Rewrite middle term using those factors
  4. Factor by grouping

Example: \(2x^2 + 5x + 3\)
\(ac = 6\), factors: 2 and 3
\(= 2x^2 + 2x + 3x + 3\)
\(= 2x(x+1) + 3(x+1)\)
\(= (2x+3)(x+1)\)

⬛ Difference of Squares

The sacred pattern:

\(a^2 - b^2 = (a+b)(a-b)\)

Example: \(x^2 - 25 = (x+5)(x-5)\)
Example: \(4x^2 - 9 = (2x+3)(2x-3)\)

√ Simplifying Radicals

To simplify \(\sqrt{n}\):

  1. Find the largest perfect square factor of \(n\)
  2. Use \(\sqrt{ab} = \sqrt{a} \cdot \sqrt{b}\)
  3. Simplify the perfect square

Example: \(\sqrt{50} = \sqrt{25 \cdot 2} = 5\sqrt{2}\)
Perfect squares: 1, 4, 9, 16, 25, 36, 49, 64, 81, 100...
