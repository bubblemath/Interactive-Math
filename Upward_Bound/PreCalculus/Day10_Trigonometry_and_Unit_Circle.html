🎮 Warm-Up: Degree ↔ Radian Conversion

Quick conversions to get your brain in trig mode!

Convert: 90° to radians

✓ π/2! 90° × (π/180) = π/2. A quarter turn around the circle.

Convert: π/3 to degrees

✓ 60°! (π/3) × (180/π) = 60°. One of our special angles!

Convert: 180° to radians

✓ π! 180° is a half turn = π radians. This is the key conversion to remember!

Convert: 5π/6 to degrees

✓ 150°! (5π/6) × (180/π) = 5 × 30 = 150°. In Quadrant II!

📚 Key Concepts

📐 SOH-CAH-TOA Review

Sine (SOH)

\(\sin(\theta) = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}\)

On the unit circle: y-coordinate

Cosine (CAH)

\(\cos(\theta) = \frac{\text{Adjacent}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}\)

On the unit circle: x-coordinate

Tangent (TOA)

\(\tan(\theta) = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Adjacent}} = \frac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}\)

Slope of the radius line!

📐 Special Right Triangles

45-45-90 Triangle

sin(45°) = cos(45°) = \(\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\)

30-60-90 Triangle

sin(30°) = 1/2, cos(30°) = \(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\)

💡 Key Insight: Because sine is positive in Quadrants I and II, the equation sin(θ) = c (where 0 < c ≤ 1) always has two solutions in [0, 2π):

  • One in Quadrant I: θ₁ = arcsin(c)
  • One in Quadrant II: θ₂ = π - arcsin(c)
