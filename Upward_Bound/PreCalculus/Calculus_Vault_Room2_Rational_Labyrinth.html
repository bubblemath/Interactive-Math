🚪 Gate 3: The Extraneous Trap

Solve this equation — but beware! One solution may be a trap!

\(\frac{x}{x-3} - \frac{2}{x} = \frac{6}{x(x-3)}\)
📝 Step-by-Step Guide
1
Find the LCD: What is the LCD of all three fractions?
2
Multiply both sides by LCD: This clears all denominators.

\(\frac{x}{x-3} \cdot x(x-3) - \frac{2}{x} \cdot x(x-3) = \frac{6}{x(x-3)} \cdot x(x-3)\)

3
Simplify and solve:

\(x^2 - 2x + 6 = 6\) → \(x^2 - 2x = 0\) → \(x(x-2) = 0\)

🚫 The Golden Rule: Factors vs. Terms

You can ONLY cancel FACTORS, never TERMS!

✅ Legal: \(\frac{(x+3)(x-2)}{(x+3)} = (x-2)\)
The (x+3) is a FACTOR of both.

✅ Also Legal: \(\frac{x^2+4}{4} = \frac{x^2}{4} + 1\)
Splitting a fraction by dividing each term is OK!

🚫 ILLEGAL:

\(\frac{x+5}{x} \neq 5\)

The x is a TERM in the numerator, not a factor!

⚠️ Domain Restrictions

A rational expression is undefined when the denominator equals zero.

To find restrictions:

  1. Set denominator = 0
  2. Solve for x
  3. These values are EXCLUDED

Example: \(\frac{x+1}{x^2-4}\)
\(x^2 - 4 = 0\) → \(x = \pm 2\)
Domain: \(x \neq 2, x \neq -2\)

🔧 The LCD Recipe

To find the Least Common Denominator:

  1. Factor all denominators completely
  2. List all unique factors
  3. Use the highest power of each factor

Example:
\(\frac{1}{x-3}\) and \(\frac{1}{x+2}\)
LCD = \((x-3)(x+2)\)

🔥 Extraneous Solutions

When solving rational equations, always check your answers!

An extraneous solution is a value that:

  • Appears when solving algebraically
  • BUT makes a denominator = 0
  • Must be REJECTED!
⚠️ The Trap:

If your solution equals a domain restriction, it's EXTRANEOUS — throw it out!
