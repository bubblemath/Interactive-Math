Product Rule
\(x^m \cdot x^n = x^{m+n}\)
Quotient Rule
\(\frac{x^m}{x^n} = x^{m-n}\)
Power Rule
\((x^m)^n = x^{mn}\)
Zero Exponent
\(x^0 = 1\) (where \(x \neq 0\))
Negative Exponent
\(x^{-n} = \frac{1}{x^n}\)
Power of Product
\((xy)^n = x^n y^n\)
Power of Quotient
\(\left(\frac{x}{y}\right)^n = \frac{x^n}{y^n}\)

Quotient Rule
\(\sqrt[n]{\frac{a}{b}} = \frac{\sqrt[n]{a}}{\sqrt[n]{b}}\)
Rational Exponent
\(x^{m/n} = \sqrt[n]{x^m} = (\sqrt[n]{x})^m\)

🎮 Warm-Up: Power Play - Spot the Illegal Move

Each equation shows a "simplification." Is it LEGAL or ILLEGAL?

Problem 1: The Freshman's Dream

\((x + y)^2 = x^2 + y^2\)
🚫 ILLEGAL! Exponents do NOT distribute over addition!

Correct: \((x+y)^2 = x^2 + 2xy + y^2\)
You must FOIL or use the perfect square pattern.

Problem 2: Product Rule

\(x^3 \cdot x^4 = x^7\)
✓ LEGAL! When multiplying same bases, ADD the exponents.

\(x^3 \cdot x^4 = x^{3+4} = x^7\) ✓

Problem 3: Radical Split

\(\sqrt{x^2 + 9} = x + 3\)
🚫 ILLEGAL! You cannot "split" a radical over addition!

\(\sqrt{a + b} \neq \sqrt{a} + \sqrt{b}\)
The expression \(\sqrt{x^2 + 9}\) cannot be simplified further.

Problem 4: Negative Exponent

\(x^{-3} = \frac{1}{x^3}\)
✓ LEGAL! A negative exponent means "reciprocal."

\(x^{-n} = \frac{1}{x^n}\) is the definition of negative exponents.

Problem 5: Power Rule Confusion

\((x^2)^3 = x^5\)
🚫 ILLEGAL! When raising a power to a power, MULTIPLY exponents!

Correct: \((x^2)^3 = x^{2 \cdot 3} = x^6\)
Don't confuse with the product rule (which adds).

🔄 Rational Exponents

\(x^{1/n} = \sqrt[n]{x}\)

The denominator is the index

\(x^{m/n} = \sqrt[n]{x^m} = (\sqrt[n]{x})^m\)

Power over root (either order works!)

⚠️ Common Errors to Avoid

❌ The Freshman's Dream:

\((x + y)^n \neq x^n + y^n\)

❌ Radical Split Error:

\(\sqrt{x + y} \neq \sqrt{x} + \sqrt{y}\)

❌ Rule Confusion:

\(x^m \cdot x^n \neq x^{mn}\)

ADD exponents when multiplying!

✓ Remember:

\(\sqrt{xy} = \sqrt{x} \cdot \sqrt{y}\)

CAN split over multiplication!
