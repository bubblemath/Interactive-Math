Section 6 - Reference Angles
📐 What is a Reference Angle?

The reference angle is the acute angle formed between the terminal side and the -axis.

It's always between 0° and 90° (or 0 and \(\frac{\pi}{2}\)).

Section 9 - Common Pitfalls
❌ The Trap ✅ The Fix
Calculator Mode Error
Solving in degrees when problem is in radians		 "MODE CHECK" — Before every problem, verify your calculator is in the correct mode. Point to the screen and say it out loud!
Reciprocal Confusion
Thinking cos goes with csc		 "One 's' per pair" — sin↔csc, cos↔sec. The pair with NO 's' is tan↔cot.
Quadrant Blindness
Forgetting to check the sign		 "Sign-Value-Check" — Always end with: "In Q___, this function is ___."
Undefined vs. Zero
Confusing \(\tan(90°) = 0\)		 \(\tan(90°)\) is UNDEFINED (division by zero). Zero means the numerator is 0, not the denominator!

🚀 Calculus Connection: Why This Matters

Derivatives of Trig Functions

\(\frac{d}{dx}[\sin x] = \cos x\)

\(\frac{d}{dx}[\cos x] = -\sin x\)

To evaluate these derivatives at specific points, you NEED unit circle values!

Example: Rate of Change

Critical: Radians are REQUIRED for all calculus with trig. Degrees don't work in derivatives!
