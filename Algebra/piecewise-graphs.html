Algebra · Reading piecewise graphs

Read the graph; write the interval.

A graph holds its domain, range, intercepts, and every value of \(f\) — you just have to read them off. The domain is the shadow it casts on the x-axis; the range is the shadow on the y-axis. The only fine print is the dots: a filled dot is included (a square bracket), a hollow one is excluded (a parenthesis), and \(\infty\) always takes a parenthesis.

Dots become brackets

Filled · included
closed dot → [ ] square bracket
Hollow · excluded

Skill 01 · Read it off the graph

Drag along the graph; project the shadows to get domain and range.

Drag left and right to move the reader and see \((x, f(x))\) — it respects the dots, so a hollow endpoint reads as undefined. Hit Domain to drop the graph's shadow onto the x-axis with the correct brackets, or Range for the y-axis. Intercepts marks where it crosses the axes. Generate a new graph anytime.

filled = included hollow = excluded → arrow = continues to ∞
Don't cross the axes up. Domain is the horizontal shadow (x-axis); range is the vertical shadow (y-axis). Reading one off the wrong axis is the most common slip.

Skill 02 · Practice on fresh graphs

Read each feature; check your answer.

A new graph each time, with a rotating question: domain, range, a value \(f(a)\), or the intercepts. Type interval notation like [-3, 2) ∪ (2, 5]; equivalent forms are accepted, but a bracket where a parenthesis belongs is not.

Cheat sheet

Shadows for sets, dots for brackets.

Domain

The x-shadow: how far left-to-right the graph reaches. Read it in interval notation, low to high.

Range

The y-shadow: how far down-to-up the graph reaches. Watch for gaps — they make a union with ∪.

Brackets

Filled dot = [ or ]. Hollow dot = ( or ). Infinity is always ( or ).

f(a) & intercepts

f(a): go up from a to the curve, read the y — at a jump, use the filled dot. y-intercept is f(0); x-intercepts are where it crosses y = 0.
