🧮 Solving Linear Equations

Master the art of finding the unknown through multiple approaches

⭐ The Golden Rule of Equations ⭐

Whatever you do to one side, you MUST do to the other side!

This keeps the equation balanced and the equality true.

🔄 Inverse Operations

To isolate the variable, use the opposite operation to "undo" what's been done.

Operation Inverse Operation Example
Addition (+) Subtraction (−) x + 5 = 12 → x = 12 − 5
Subtraction (−) Addition (+) x − 3 = 7 → x = 7 + 3
Multiplication (×) Division (÷) 4x = 20 → x = 20 ÷ 4
Division (÷) Multiplication (×) x/2 = 6 → x = 6 × 2

📐 Literal Equations

A literal equation contains multiple variables. You solve for one variable in terms of the others using the same inverse operation rules.

Example: Solve A = lw for w

Divide both sides by l: w = A/l

Example: Solve P = 2l + 2w for l

Subtract 2w: P − 2w = 2l

Divide by 2: l = (P − 2w)/2

↔️ Variables on Both Sides

Move all variable terms to one side and all constants to the other.

Example: 5x + 3 = 2x + 15

Subtract 2x from both sides: 3x + 3 = 15

Subtract 3 from both sides: 3x = 12

Divide both sides by 3: x = 4

📋 Order of Operations (Solving)

  1. Simplify each side (distribute, combine like terms)
  2. Clear fractions/decimals if present
  3. Move variables to one side
  4. Move constants to the other side
  5. Divide by the coefficient of the variable
  6. Check your answer!

📐 Literal Equations: These equations have multiple variables. You solve for ONE variable in terms of the others using the same inverse operation rules. These are common on the TSIA2!
Formula Context: A = lw is the area formula for a rectangle, where A = area, l = length, w = width
A = lw, solve for w Literal
1
A ÷ l = lw ÷ l
Divide both sides by l to isolate w
2
w = A/l
Solution! Width equals Area divided by length
Formula Context: d = rt is the distance formula, where d = distance, r = rate (speed), t = time
d = rt, solve for t Literal
1
d ÷ r = rt ÷ r
Divide both sides by r to isolate t
2
t = d/r
Solution! Time equals distance divided by rate
Formula Context: P = 2l + 2w is the perimeter formula for a rectangle
P = 2l + 2w, solve for l Literal
1
P − 2w = 2l + 2w − 2w
Subtract 2w from both sides to isolate the l term
2
P − 2w = 2l
Simplify the right side
3
(P − 2w) ÷ 2 = 2l ÷ 2
Divide both sides by 2
4
l = (P − 2w)/2
Solution! Length equals (Perimeter minus 2 times width) divided by 2
Formula Context: F = (9/5)C + 32 converts Celsius to Fahrenheit
F = (9/5)C + 32, solve for C Literal
1
F − 32 = (9/5)C + 32 − 32
Subtract 32 from both sides
2
F − 32 = (9/5)C
Simplify
3
(F − 32) × (5/9) = (9/5)C × (5/9)
Multiply both sides by 5/9 (the reciprocal of 9/5)
4
C = (5/9)(F − 32)
Solution! Celsius equals 5/9 times (Fahrenheit minus 32)
Formula Context: I = Prt is the simple interest formula, where I = interest, P = principal, r = rate, t = time
I = Prt, solve for r Literal
1
I ÷ (Pt) = Prt ÷ (Pt)
Divide both sides by Pt to isolate r
2
r = I/(Pt)
Solution! Rate equals Interest divided by (Principal times time)
Formula Context: y = mx + b is the slope-intercept form of a linear equation
y = mx + b, solve for x Literal
1
y − b = mx + b − b
Subtract b from both sides
2
y − b = mx
Simplify
3
(y − b) ÷ m = mx ÷ m
Divide both sides by m
4
x = (y − b)/m
Solution! x equals (y minus b) divided by m
Formula Context: Ax + By = C is the standard form of a linear equation
Ax + By = C, solve for y Literal
1
Ax − Ax + By = C − Ax
Subtract Ax from both sides
2
By = C − Ax
Simplify
3
By ÷ B = (C − Ax) ÷ B
Divide both sides by B
4
y = (C − Ax)/B
Solution! This can also be written as y = −(A/B)x + C/B

📖 How to Use the Balance Scale

  • The scale represents an equation - both sides must stay balanced
  • Enter a number in the input box, then click an operation button
  • The operation will be applied to BOTH sides simultaneously
  • Watch the Solution Steps panel to see your work recorded

⚠️ When Variables Appear on BOTH Sides:

  1. First: Look at which side has the smaller coefficient of x
  2. Then: Subtract that smaller x-term from BOTH sides
  3. Example: For 5x + 3 = 2x + 15, subtract 2x from both sides → 3x + 3 = 15
