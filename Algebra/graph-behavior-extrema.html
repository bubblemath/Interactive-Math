Precalculus · Reading graphs

Where does it climb, where does it fall, and where are the peaks?

Read a graph left to right. Where it rises it's increasing, where it drops it's decreasing, where it's flat it's constant — and you report those as intervals of \(x\). The turning points are the relative max and min; the very highest and lowest points over the whole graph are the absolute max and min.

Peaks, valleys, and the global high and low.

Enter the y-values. Relative maxima/minima are the turning points; the absolute max/min are the highest/lowest over the whole graph. Use none where there are none (for example, when an arrow runs to infinity).

Cheat sheet

Left to right, x-intervals, open at the turns.

Increasing / decreasing / constant

Rising → increasing, falling → decreasing, flat → constant. Report as open x-intervals, unioned. Never read them off the y-axis.

Relative extrema

Turning points: up-then-down is a relative max, down-then-up a relative min. The value is the y. Endpoints don't count.

Absolute extrema

The single highest and lowest y over the whole graph. Can occur at a turning point or an endpoint.

Arrows

An arrow to \(+\infty\) → no absolute maximum. An arrow to \(-\infty\) → no absolute minimum.
