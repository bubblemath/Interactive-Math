Algebra · Piecewise functions

Pick the right piece to evaluate; draw each one only where it lives.

A piecewise function is several rules sharing one name, each ruling its own stretch of the x-axis. To evaluate, find the piece whose condition your x satisfies — the boundary belongs to whichever piece has \(\le\) or \(\ge\). To graph, draw each piece only on its interval, with a closed dot where it's included, an open dot where it isn't, and an arrow where it runs forever.

Skill 02 · Evaluate before, at, and after

The boundary is the whole test.

Just before and just after the transition, the piece is obvious. Exactly at the transition, you must use the piece whose condition includes that value — the one with \(\le\) or \(\ge\). Plugging the transition into the wrong piece (or into both) is the classic slip.

At the boundary, read the inequality. If the condition is \(x \ge a\), the value \(a\) belongs to that piece; if it's \(x > a\), it does not.

Cheat sheet

Match the condition; mind the dot.

Evaluate

Find the condition your x makes true, then use that piece. At the boundary, the ≤/≥ piece wins.

Dots

≤ or ≥ → closed dot. < or > → open dot. At a jump, one of each.

Arrows

An end running to ±∞ gets an arrow. A line segment between two boundaries gets a dot on each end.

Square root

√ starts at its endpoint and goes one direction only — never both. Draw each piece only on its own interval.
