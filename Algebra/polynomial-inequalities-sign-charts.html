01

The plan

One side, factor, critical numbers, test, brackets.

Move every term to one side so you’re comparing a single polynomial to 0, then factor it. Each factor’s zero is a critical number — the only place the polynomial can switch between positive and negative. Plot them on a number line and they cut it into intervals; inside each interval the sign is constant, so picking one test point and checking its sign tells you the sign everywhere in that interval.

Keep the intervals whose sign matches: > 0 wants the positive ones, < 0 the negative. The brackets come from the relation: ≤ or ≥ includes the critical numbers (closed, [ ]), while < or > excludes them (open, ( )); infinity is always open. Watch the repeated factors — a squared factor doesn’t change the sign as you cross it, which is what creates the surprises below.
