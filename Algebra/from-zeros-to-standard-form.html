01

Every zero is a factor

And the sign flips on the way in.

If r is a zero of a polynomial, then (x − r) is a factor — this is the Factor Theorem read backwards. So a list of zeros hands you a list of factors, and a monic polynomial with exactly those zeros is just their product: .

The one thing to watch is the sign flip. A zero of 3 gives the factor (x − 3), but a zero of −2 gives (x + 2), because x − (−2) = x + 2. After that, multiplying the factors two at a time and collecting like terms gives the standard form.
