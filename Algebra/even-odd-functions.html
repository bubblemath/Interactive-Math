Algebra · Even & odd functions

Even mirrors across the y-axis; odd spins 180° about the origin.

Two symmetries, two tests. Even means \(f(-x)=f(x)\): fold the graph over the y-axis and it lands on itself. Odd means \(f(-x)=-f(x)\): rotate it half a turn about the origin and it lands on itself. To check an equation, drop \(-x\) into every \(x\) and see which one you get — and remember most functions are neither.

Skill 01 · See the symmetry

Reflect or rotate the graph and watch whether it lands on itself.

Choose a function, then run a test. Reflect draws the mirror image across the y-axis; if it overlaps the original, the function is even. Rotate spins the graph 180° about the origin; if that overlaps, it's odd. Drag the point to see its partner — at \((-x, f(x))\) for the mirror, at \((-x,-f(x))\) for the rotation.

Keep the two apart. Even = fold over the y-axis (left/right mirror). Odd = half-turn about the origin (which flips both left/right and up/down). Mixing them up is the most common error.
Skill 02 · Substitute −x

Drop −x into every x, simplify, then compare.

Replace each \(x\) with \(-x\) and simplify. The sign rule does the work: \((-x)^2=x^2\) (even power, sign stays) but \((-x)^3=-x^3\) (odd power, sign flips). Then compare the result with \(f(x)\) and \(-f(x)\).

Polynomial shortcut. All even-power terms (a constant counts as even) → even. All odd-power terms, no constant → odd. A mix of even and odd terms → neither.
Worked examples

Both ways: read the graph, run the algebra.

From a graph

Even
A parabola like \(y=x^2\) is a mirror image across the y-axis → even.
Odd
A cubic like \(y=x^3\) maps onto itself under a 180° turn about the origin → odd.
Neither
\(y=x^2+x\) has no y-axis mirror and no origin spin that fits → neither.

From an equation

f(x) = x² − 4
\(f(-x)=(-x)^2-4=x^2-4=f(x)\) → even.
f(x) = 2x³ − x
\(f(-x)=2(-x)^3-(-x)=-2x^3+x=-(2x^3-x)=-f(x)\) → odd.
f(x) = x³ + 1
\(f(-x)=-x^3+1\). That's not \(f(x)\) and not \(-f(x)=-x^3-1\) → neither.

Cheat sheet

Two symmetries, one substitution.

Even

f(−x) = f(x). Mirror across the y-axis. Examples: x², |x|, x⁴, constants.

Odd

f(−x) = −f(x). 180° about the origin. Examples: x, x³, x⁵.

The sign rule

(−x)²=x² (sign stays), (−x)³=−x³ (sign flips). Substitute into every term.

Neither

A mix of even and odd terms — like x² + x — is neither. This is the most common case.
