📚 What is Completing the Square?

Completing the square is a method that transforms a quadratic expression into a perfect square trinomial, allowing us to solve using square roots. It's not a trick—it's based on the geometry of squares!

🎯 Learning Goals

  • Recognize a perfect square trinomial
  • Explain why adding \(\left(\frac{b}{2}\right)^2\) completes the square
  • Solve quadratics by completing the square when \(a = 1\)
  • Solve quadratics by completing the square when \(a \neq 1\)
🌟 The "Magic Number" Formula 🌟

\(\displaystyle x^2 + bx + \left(\frac{b}{2}\right)^2 = \left(x + \frac{b}{2}\right)^2\)

💡 Key Ideas to Remember

Balance: An equation is like a balance scale. If you add a number to one side, you must add the same number to the other side to keep the solutions unchanged.

Undoing a Square: The equation \((x + h)^2 = k\) can be solved by taking square roots: \(x + h = \pm\sqrt{k}\). Completing the square transforms any quadratic into this form!

📝 Pattern Recognition

In any perfect square trinomial \(x^2 + bx + c\):

  • The middle coefficient \(b\) is twice the number inside the parentheses
  • The constant \(c\) is the square of that number
\((x + 3)^2 = x^2 + 6x + 9\) → Middle: \(6 = 2(3)\), Last: \(9 = 3^2\)
