01

What the factors tell you

Two ideas do most of the work: cancel for holes, compare degrees for the ends.

Write f(x) = N(x)/D(x) with the top and bottom factored. A zero of the bottom is trouble — but what kind depends on whether the top shares that factor. If a factor is common to both, it cancels and leaves a hole; a factor left in the bottom is a vertical asymptote.

Hole

A factor in both top and bottom cancels. The graph is the simplified curve with one point punched out — an open circle at x = c.

Vertical asymptote

A leftover factor in the bottom sends the curve to ±∞ at x = a — a dashed vertical line it never crosses.

The ends come from comparing the degree of the top to the bottom: bottom bigger → the x‑axis y = 0; equal → the ratio of leading coefficients; top one bigger → a slant line (from dividing); top two or more bigger → a curved (polynomial) asymptote. The x‑intercepts are zeros of the top that survive; the y‑intercept is f(0); the domain drops every zero of the original bottom.
