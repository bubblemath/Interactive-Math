Precalculus · Solving absolute value inequalities

The symbol decides the shape: between or beyond.

Isolate the absolute value, then read the inequality. "Less than" traps the inside between two bounds — an AND, one interval. "Greater than" pushes it outside — an OR, two rays. And when the right side comes out negative, you get the two extremes: no solution, or every real number.

The one fork that runs the whole topic

\(|X| < a \Rightarrow -a < X < a\)
Between. One interval, like \((-3, 3)\).
GreatOR
\(|X| > a \Rightarrow X<-a \;\text{or}\; X>a\)
Beyond. Two rays, like \((-\infty,-3)\cup(3,\infty)\).

Skill 01 · Greater than vs less than — AND or OR

Read the symbol first; it tells you the shape of the answer.

After isolating \(|X|\), two questions settle everything. Is the right side negative? Then it's a special case. Is it positive? Then the symbol decides: \(<\) (or \(\le\)) means the inside is squeezed between \(-a\) and \(a\) (an AND), while \(>\) (or \(\ge\)) means it's pushed beyond them in either direction (an OR). Skill 02 · Less than → between (AND)

\(|X| < a\): the inside is trapped between \(-a\) and \(a\).

Isolate the absolute value, confirm the right side is positive, then rewrite as a single compound inequality \(-a < X < a\) and solve all three parts. The answer is one bounded interval — strict \(<\) gives parentheses, inclusive \(\le\) gives brackets.

The rewrite

\[ |X| < a \;\Longrightarrow\; -a < X < a \qquad |X| \le a \;\Longrightarrow\; -a \le X \le a \]

One interval, no union. This is the AND case.

Worked example

Solve \(\;3|x+2| - 4 < 11\)
Step 1 · Isolate
\[3|x+2| < 15 \;\Rightarrow\; |x+2| < 5\]
Step 2 · Right side positive, < → between (AND)
\[-5 < x+2 < 5\]
Step 3 · Subtract 2 from all three parts
\[-7 < x < 3\]
Interval notation
\[(-7,\ 3)\]

Skill 03 · Greater than → beyond (OR)

\(|X| > a\): the inside is pushed beyond \(-a\) and \(a\) — two rays.

Isolate, confirm the right side is positive, then split into two separate inequalities joined by OR: \(X < -a\) or \(X > a\). The answer is two rays, written with a union \(\cup\). The single most common error here is forgetting that there are two pieces.

The split

\[ |X| > a \;\Longrightarrow\; X < -a \;\text{ or }\; X > a \]

Two rays joined by a union: \((-\infty,-a)\cup(a,\infty)\). The \(\le/\ge\) versions close the endpoints.

Worked example

Solve \(\;2|x-1| + 1 \ge 7\)
Step 1 · Isolate
\[2|x-1| \ge 6 \;\Rightarrow\; |x-1| \ge 3\]
Step 2 · Right side positive, ≥ → beyond (OR)
\[x-1 \le -3 \;\text{ or }\; x-1 \ge 3\]
Step 3 · Solve each
\[x \le -2 \;\text{ or }\; x \ge 4\]
Interval notation (union)
\[(-\infty,\ -2\,] \cup [\,4,\ \infty)\]

Skill 04 · No solution & all real numbers

When the right side is negative, the distance idea settles it instantly.

An absolute value is a distance, so it's always \(\ge 0\). Once you isolate and find the right side is negative, you don't split into cases — you read the meaning. \(|X| < \text{negative}\) asks for a distance smaller than a negative: impossible, no solution. \(|X| > \text{negative}\) asks for a distance bigger than a negative: always true, all real numbers.

Worked example · no solution

Solve \(\;|x+4| + 5 < 2\)
Step 1 · Isolate
\[|x+4| < -3\]
Step 2 · Read it
A distance can't be less than \(-3\).
Conclusion
No solution \((\varnothing)\).

Worked example · all real numbers

Solve \(\;|3x-2| + 6 > 1\)
Step 1 · Isolate
\[|3x-2| > -5\]
Step 2 · Read it
A distance is always greater than \(-5\).
Conclusion
All real numbers: \((-\infty,\ \infty)\).

Cheat sheet

Isolate, check the right side, then AND or OR.

Less thAND

\(|X| < a\,(a>0)\) → \(-a < X < a\). One interval, no union. Inclusive \(\le\) closes both ends.

GreatOR

\(|X|>a\,(a>0)\) → \(X<-a\) or \(X>a\). Two rays joined by \(\cup\). Don't forget the second piece.

Negative right side

\(<\) negative → no solution \((\varnothing)\). \(>\) negative → all reals \((-\infty,\infty)\).

Always

Isolate the absolute value first. \(\infty\) takes a parenthesis. Strict → ( ); inclusive → [ ].
