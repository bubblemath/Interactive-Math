Master four powerful methods for solving any quadratic equation

🎯 Choose Your Method

Every quadratic equation can be written in standard form: \(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\). There are four main methods to solve them—the key is choosing the most efficient one for each problem!

The Quadratic Formula: \(\displaystyle x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}\)

🔮 The Discriminant: \(D = b^2 - 4ac\)

\(D > 0\)
Two distinct real solutions
\(D = 0\)
One repeated real solution
\(D < 0\)
Two complex solutions

💡 Pro Tip: If \(D\) is a perfect square (0, 1, 4, 9, 16...), the quadratic is factorable!

🧩

Factoring (AC Method)

Break down quadratics into binomials using the AC method and the Zero Product Property. Find two numbers that multiply to \(ac\) and add to \(b\), then factor by grouping.

Best when: The discriminant is a perfect square (factorable over integers)

√

Square Root Method

Isolate the squared term and take the square root of both sides. Remember to use \(\pm\) for both positive and negative roots!

Best when: No middle term (\(b = 0\)) or already in \((x + h)^2 = k\) form
Open Square Root Lab
⬜

Completing the Square

Transform any quadratic into a perfect square trinomial by adding \(\left(\frac{b}{2}\right)^2\) to both sides. This reveals the vertex form and allows solving with square roots.

Best when: Need vertex form, or when other methods are messy
Open Completing Square Lab
🧪

Quadratic Formula

The universal "fail-safe" method that works for ANY quadratic equation. Derived from completing the square on the general form. Use the discriminant to predict solution types.

Best when: Doesn't factor nicely, or you want a guaranteed method
