01

What each factor tells you

The exponent on a factor is the whole story at that intercept.

A factor (x − r) puts an x‑intercept at x = r. Its exponent — the multiplicity — decides the shape there. Near the root, only that factor changes sign:

An even multiplicity (like (x−r)^2) keeps the same sign on both sides, so the curve runs into the axis and bounces back — it touches. An odd multiplicity flips the sign, so the curve passes through — it crosses (and for multiplicity 3 or more it flattens as it goes through). The y‑intercept is just f(0), and the end behavior comes from the degree (sum of the multiplicities) and the sign of the leading coefficient.
