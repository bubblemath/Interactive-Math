Precalculus · Transformations

Move every key point; the graph follows.

A transformed graph is just the parent with each key point relocated. Build a table by sending every parent point \((x_0,y_0)\) to its image, plot the images, and the new curve appears. Master one rule and all nine parents transform the same way.

The whole idea on one line

\(g(x)=a\,f\big(b(x-h)\big)+k \quad\Longrightarrow\quad (x_0,y_0)\to\left(\dfrac{x_0}{b}+h,\ \ a\,y_0+k\right)\)
\(a\) — vertical stretch; \(a<0\) flips over the x-axis.
\(b\) — horizontal stretch by \(1/b\); \(b<0\) flips over the y-axis.
\(h\) — shift right (the \(x-h\) sign flips).
\(k\) — shift up.

Worked examples

Map the points, plot, connect.

Reflect & shift · \(g(x)=-2\sqrt{x-1}+3\)

Read the parameters
\(a=-2,\ b=1,\ h=1,\ k=3\). So \((x_0,y_0)\to(x_0+1,\ -2y_0+3)\).
Map the key points
\((0,0)\to(1,3)\); \((1,1)\to(2,1)\); \((4,2)\to(5,-1)\); \((9,3)\to(10,-3)\).
Plot & connect
Start at \((1,3)\) and fall to the right — the root is reflected down (\(a<0\)), stretched by 2, shifted right 1 and up 3.

Compress & shift · \(g(x)=\tfrac12|x+2|-1\)

Read the parameters
\(a=\tfrac12,\ b=1,\ h=-2,\ k=-1\). So \((x_0,y_0)\to(x_0-2,\ \tfrac12 y_0-1)\).
Map the key points
\((0,0)\to(-2,-1)\); \((1,1)\to(-1,-\tfrac12)\); \((-1,1)\to(-3,-\tfrac12)\); \((2,2)\to(0,0)\).
Plot & connect
A wider, flatter V (vertical compression by \(\tfrac12\)) with vertex moved to \((-2,-1)\).

Reciprocal · the asymptotes move too · \(g(x)=\dfrac{1}{x-3}+2\)

Parameters
\(a=1,\ b=1,\ h=3,\ k=2\). Points \((x_0,y_0)\to(x_0+3,\ y_0+2)\).
Asymptotes shift with the center
The center \((0,0)\) moves to \((3,2)\): vertical asymptote \(x=3\), horizontal asymptote \(y=2\).
Map & plot
\((1,1)\to(4,3)\); \((2,\tfrac12)\to(5,\tfrac52)\); \((-1,-1)\to(2,1)\). Two branches around \((3,2)\).
Cheat sheet

One rule, applied to every point.

The mapping

\((x_0,y_0)\to\left(\tfrac{x_0}{b}+h,\ a y_0+k\right)\). Horizontal changes act on \(x\), vertical on \(y\) — independently.

Horizontal is backwards

\(x-h\) shifts right by \(h\); the factor \(b\) stretches by \(1/b\). Inside the function, everything is inverted.

Vertical is direct

\(a\) multiplies \(y\) (stretch/compress, flip if negative); \(k\) adds to \(y\) (up/down). Outside the function, as written.

Reciprocals & steps

The center/asymptotes move to \(x=h,\ y=k\); the step's jumps shift and, under a reflection, swap closed and open ends.
