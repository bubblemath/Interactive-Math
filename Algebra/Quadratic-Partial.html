📚 Welcome to Quadratic Equations

A quadratic equation is any equation that can be written in the form:

\(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\) where \(a \neq 0\)

The solutions (also called roots or zeros) are the x-values where the parabola crosses the x-axis.

📐 The Standard Form

💡 Pro Tip: Always rewrite the equation in standard form (\(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\)) before identifying a, b, and c!

Original Equation Standard Form a b c
\(x^2 - 5x + 6 = 0\) Already in standard form 1 −5 6
\(x^2 = 3x - 1\) \(x^2 - 3x + 1 = 0\) 1 −3 1
\(2x^2 + 8 = 0\) Already in standard form 2 0 8
\(3x^2 = 12\) \(3x^2 - 12 = 0\) 3 0 −12
\((x+2)(x-3) = 0\) \(x^2 - x - 6 = 0\) 1 −1 −6

🔮 The Discriminant: Predicting Your Solutions

Discriminant: \(D = b^2 - 4ac\)

This value tells you how many solutions to expect and what type they are!

Discriminant Value Number of Solutions Type of Solutions Graph Behavior
\(D > 0\) 2 solutions Two distinct real numbers Parabola crosses x-axis twice
\(D = 0\) 1 solution One repeated real number Parabola touches x-axis at vertex
\(D < 0\) 2 solutions Two complex conjugates Parabola never crosses x-axis

🛠️ Four Methods for Solving Quadratics

🧩 Factoring (AC Method)

Write the quadratic as a product of binomials, then use the Zero Product Property.

  • Find factors of \(a \cdot c\) that add to \(b\)
  • Factor by grouping if \(a \neq 1\)
  • Set each factor equal to zero
Best when: The discriminant is a perfect square (factorable over integers)

√ Square Root Method

Isolate the squared term and take the square root of both sides.

  • Get \(x^2\) or \((x+h)^2\) alone
  • Take \(\pm\) square root
  • Solve for x
Best when: No middle term (\(b = 0\)) or already in \((x+h)^2 = k\) form

⬜ Completing the Square

Transform the equation into a perfect square trinomial.

  • Move constant, divide by \(a\)
  • Add \(\left(\frac{b}{2}\right)^2\) to both sides
  • Factor and use square roots
Best when: Need vertex form, or when other methods are messy

🧪 Quadratic Formula

The universal solver that works for ANY quadratic equation.

  • Identify \(a\), \(b\), \(c\)
  • Substitute into formula
  • Simplify carefully
Best when: Doesn't factor nicely, or you want a guaranteed method
The Quadratic Formula: \(\displaystyle x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}\)

🎯 Method Selection Guide

  1. Is \(b = 0\)? → Use Square Root Method
  2. Is it already in \((x+h)^2 = k\) form? → Use Square Root Method
  3. Is the discriminant a perfect square? → Try Factoring
  4. Need vertex form? → Use Completing the Square
  5. Otherwise → Use the Quadratic Formula

🎓 Remember: All four methods give the same solutions! The "best" method is simply the fastest for that particular equation.
