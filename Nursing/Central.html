Mean (Average)

📌 Definition

The mean is the arithmetic average of a data set. You find it by adding all the numbers in the data set and dividing by how many numbers there are.

📐 Formula

Mean = Sum of all values ÷ Number of values

Quick Example

Data set: 4, 8, 6, 5, 7

Mean = (4 + 8 + 6 + 5 + 7) ÷ 5 = 30 ÷ 5 = 6

M

Median (Middle Value)

📌 Definition

The median is the middle number in a data set when the numbers are listed in ascending or descending order. If there's an even number of values, the median is the average of the two middle numbers.

Odd Number of Values

Data: 3, 5, 7, 9, 11

Middle number = 7

Even Number of Values

Data: 2, 4, 6, 8

Average of 4 and 6 = (4+6)÷2 = 5

⚠️ Important Step

Always arrange data in ORDER (smallest to largest) before finding the median!
Mo

Mode (Most Frequent)

📌 Definition

The mode is the value that occurs most often in a data set. The mode tells you what numbers you are most likely to encounter. A data set can have no mode, one mode, or multiple modes.

One Mode

Data: 2, 3, 3, 3, 5, 7

Mode = 3 (appears 3 times)

Two Modes (Bimodal)

Data: 1, 2, 2, 4, 5, 5

Modes = 2 and 5

No Mode

Data: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5

No mode (all appear once)

R

Range (Spread)

📌 Definition

The range is the difference between the highest and lowest values in a data set. The range tells you how much the data set fluctuates or spreads out.

📐 Formula

Range = Maximum Value − Minimum Value

Quick Example

Data set: 12, 5, 22, 8, 15

Range = 22 − 5 = 17

📋 Quick Reference Summary

Measure What It Tells You How to Find It
Mean The "average" or center of the data Add all values, divide by count
Median The middle value (not affected by outliers) Order data, find middle number
Mode Most common value you'll encounter Find the most frequent number
Range How spread out the data is Subtract: Max − Min

TEAS Exam Tip

On the TEAS, you'll need to analyze data from tables, charts, and graphs using these statistics. Practice identifying which measure is being asked for — the question might say "average" (mean), "middle value" (median), "most frequent" (mode), or "difference between highest and lowest" (range). Read carefully!

Example 1: Finding the Mean

Mean

Problem:

A nursing student received the following scores on five quizzes:

85, 92, 78, 88, 92

What is the mean (average) quiz score?

✅ Solution:

1
Add all the values together:
85 + 92 + 78 + 88 + 92 = 435
2
Count how many values there are:
5 quiz scores
3
Divide the sum by the count:
435 ÷ 5 = 87
Answer: Mean = 87

Example 2: Finding the Median (Odd Count)

Median

Problem:

Find the median of the following patient wait times (in minutes):

23, 15, 42, 8, 31, 19, 27

✅ Solution:

1
Arrange the data in ascending order:
8, 15, 19, 23, 27, 31, 42
2
Count the values: 7 values (odd number)
3
Find the middle position: (7 + 1) ÷ 2 = 4th position
8, 15, 19, 23, 27, 31, 42
Answer: Median = 23 minutes

Example 3: Finding the Median (Even Count)

Median

Problem:

Find the median of these test scores:

72, 85, 91, 68, 77, 83

✅ Solution:

1
Arrange in ascending order:
68, 72, 77, 83, 85, 91
2
Count: 6 values (even number) — find the two middle values
68, 72, 77, 83, 85, 91
3
Average the two middle values:
(77 + 83) ÷ 2 = 160 ÷ 2 = 80
Answer: Median = 80

Example 4: Finding the Mode

Mode

Problem:

A hospital recorded the following number of patient admissions over 10 days:

12, 15, 12, 18, 15, 12, 20, 15, 12, 17

What is the mode?

✅ Solution:

1
Count how many times each value appears:
12 appears 4 times
15 appears 3 times
17 appears 1 time
18 appears 1 time
20 appears 1 time
2
Identify the value that appears most often: 12 (4 times)
Answer: Mode = 12

Example 5: Finding the Range

Range

Problem:

The temperatures (°F) recorded in a patient's room over a week were:

68, 72, 70, 75, 69, 71, 74

What is the range of temperatures?

✅ Solution:

1
Identify the maximum (highest) value:
Maximum = 75
2
Identify the minimum (lowest) value:
Minimum = 68
3
Subtract: Maximum − Minimum
75 − 68 = 7
Answer: Range = 7°F

Example 6: Finding All Four Measures

Complete Analysis

Problem:

Find the mean, median, mode, and range for this data set:

5, 8, 12, 8, 15, 8, 10

✅ Solution:

1
Mean: Add all values and divide by count
(5+8+12+8+15+8+10) ÷ 7 = 66 ÷ 7 ≈ 9.43
2
Median: Order the data, find middle value
5, 8, 8, 8, 10, 12, 15 → Median = 8
3
Mode: Find most frequent value
8 appears 3 times → Mode = 8
4
Range: Maximum − Minimum
15 − 5 = 10 → Range = 10
Mean ≈ 9.43 | Median = 8 | Mode = 8 | Range = 10
