📖 Following Directions

On the TEAS — and every day in nursing — you’ll read directions and have to carry them out exactly: in the right order, under the right conditions, and within the limits the instructions set. The secret is to slow down and notice the signal words. Click the diagram below to meet the four kinds that matter most.

📋 The Core Ideas

🔢 Order & Time Words

WordWhat it tells you
First / next / then / finallyThe sequence — which step comes when
BeforeThis step happens earlier than another
AfterThis step happens later, following another
While / once / untilTiming relative to another step

🔀 Condition Words (If / Then)

  • If… then… means a step happens only in a certain case. “If the reading is below 70, give juice.”
  • Otherwise covers every other case — do this when the “if” doesn’t apply.
  • Unless marks an exception: do it except when the condition is true.

⚠️ Warning & Limit Words

  • Do not / never: a hard stop — this action is forbidden.
  • Only / just: a limit — do exactly this and no more.
  • Except: everything applies but this one case.
  • These small words change the whole meaning — miss one and you do the wrong thing.

🌟 How to Follow Multi-Step Directions

  • Read all the steps first before you start.
  • Note the order — number the steps if it helps.
  • Watch for conditions (if / unless) and warnings (do not / only).
  • Do exactly what’s asked — no more, no less, in the stated order.

✏️ Example 1: Get the order right

To give the medication: First, take it with a full glass of water. Next, wait 30 minutes before eating. Then stay upright — do not lie down. Finally, record the time.
  1. Find the order words: first → next → then → finally.
  2. First = take with water; finally = record the time.
  3. So “record the time” is the last step, not the first.

Takeaway: order words tell you exactly where each step belongs.

🔀 Example 2: Follow the condition

Check the patient’s temperature. If it is above 100.4°F, notify the nurse. Otherwise, record it and continue.
  1. The reading is 99.1°F.
  2. Is 99.1 above 100.4? No.
  3. So the “if” doesn’t apply — follow “otherwise”: record it and continue.

Takeaway: check whether the condition is actually true before you act on the “if” branch.

⚠️ Example 3: Don’t miss the warning

Take one tablet twice a day with food. Do NOT crush the tablet, and do not take it with dairy.
  1. Quantity: one tablet, twice a day (not two once a day).
  2. Warnings: don’t crush it; no dairy.
  3. So: swallow it whole, with a non-dairy meal, twice daily.

Takeaway: the words “do not” and “with food” completely change the correct action.
