📖 What Are Context Clues?

When you meet an unfamiliar word, you don’t always need a dictionary — the surrounding words often reveal its meaning. These hints are called context clues. There are four common kinds, and each has its own signal words. Click each clue type around the mystery word below to learn how it works.

📋 The Core Ideas

🔑 The Four Clue Types

Clue typeHow it helpsSignal words
DefinitionStates the meaning directlymeans, is, refers to, — … —
SynonymRestates it with a familiar wordor, in other words, that is
AntonymGives the opposite meaningunlike, but, however, instead
ExampleLists examples that reveal itsuch as, for example, including

🧭 How to Use Context Clues

  • Read the whole sentence (and the one before or after) — don’t stop at the hard word.
  • Look for signal words like or, unlike, such as, means.
  • Make your best guess at the meaning.
  • Substitute it back in — if the sentence still makes sense, your guess is likely right.

🌟 Detective Tips

  • The clue is usually right next to the unfamiliar word.
  • A comma or a pair of dashes often sets off a definition.
  • “Unlike” or “but” means look for the opposite.
  • Don’t panic at a big word — the sentence is quietly explaining it.

📘 Example 1: Definition clue

The patient was diagnosed with hypertension, meaning her blood pressure was far too high.
  1. See the signal word “meaning” — a definition is coming.
  2. It says the blood pressure was too high.
  3. So hypertension = high blood pressure.

Clue type: definition — the meaning is stated right there.

🔁 Example 2: Antonym / contrast clue

Unlike her usually gregarious roommate, Mia was shy and preferred to stay quiet.
  1. The signal word “Unlike” tells you to look for the opposite.
  2. Mia is shy and quiet — so gregarious means the opposite.
  3. So gregarious = outgoing and social.

Clue type: antonym / contrast — “unlike” flips the meaning.

📝 Example 3: Example clue

The clinic treats many chronic conditions, such as diabetes and arthritis, that last for years.
  1. The signal “such as” introduces examples.
  2. Diabetes and arthritis last for years.
  3. So chronic = long-lasting.

Clue type: example — the examples reveal the meaning.
