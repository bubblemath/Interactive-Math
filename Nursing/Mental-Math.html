🎯 Why Mental Math Matters on the TEAS

The TEAS Math section gives you approximately 1.5 minutes per question. Mental math strategies help you:

  • Save precious time on calculations
  • Quickly eliminate wrong answer choices
  • Check your work efficiently
  • Build confidence during the exam

🔢 Strategy 1: Rounding & Estimation

📍 Rounding Rules

The 5-Rule

If the digit to the right is 5 or greater, round UP. If it's less than 5, round DOWN.

2,346 → nearest 100 → 2,300 (4 < 5, round down)
19.796 → nearest hundredth → 19.80 (6 ≥ 5, round up)

Estimation Strategy

Round numbers to make calculations easier, then adjust.

48 × 52 ≈ 50 × 50 = 2,500 (actual: 2,496)

⚡ Time Saver

When answer choices are spread apart, estimation can immediately eliminate 2-3 wrong answers!

💯 Strategy 2: Percentage Shortcuts

📊 Key Percentage Conversions (MEMORIZE!)

50% = ½ Divide by 2
25% = ¼ Divide by 4
10% = 1/10 Move decimal left 1 place
1% = 1/100 Move decimal left 2 places
20% = ⅕ Divide by 5
33⅓% = ⅓ Divide by 3

Building Percentages

Combine known percentages to find others:

15% = 10% + 5% (where 5% = half of 10%)
23% of $450: 20% = $90, 3% = $13.50 → $103.50

Percentage Increase/Decrease Formula

% Change = (New - Original) / Original × 100
128 babies → 160 babies
Change = (160-128)/128 × 100 = 32/128 × 100 = 25% increase

Finding Original Price (Markup/Markdown)

Original = Sale Price / (1 - discount rate)
Item marked down 23% to $346.50
Original = $346.50 / 0.77 = $450

✖️ Strategy 3: Multiplication Tricks

🚀 Quick Multiplication Methods

Multiply by 5

Divide by 2, then multiply by 10

48 × 5 = 48 ÷ 2 × 10 = 24 × 10 = 240

Multiply by 9

Multiply by 10, then subtract the original number

23 × 9 = 23 × 10 - 23 = 230 - 23 = 207

Multiply by 11

Multiply by 10, then add the original number

45 × 11 = 45 × 10 + 45 = 450 + 45 = 495

Multiply by 25

Divide by 4, then multiply by 100

36 × 25 = 36 ÷ 4 × 100 = 9 × 100 = 900

Squaring Numbers Ending in 5

Take the tens digit, multiply by (itself + 1), append 25

35² = 3 × 4 = 12, append 25 → 1225
65² = 6 × 7 = 42, append 25 → 4225

➗ Strategy 4: Division Shortcuts

⚡ Quick Division Methods

Divide by 5

Multiply by 2, then divide by 10

135 ÷ 5 = 135 × 2 ÷ 10 = 270 ÷ 10 = 27

Divide by 25

Multiply by 4, then divide by 100

450 ÷ 25 = 450 × 4 ÷ 100 = 1800 ÷ 100 = 18

Divisibility Rules

  • By 2: Last digit is even
  • By 3: Sum of digits divisible by 3
  • By 4: Last two digits divisible by 4
  • By 5: Ends in 0 or 5
  • By 6: Divisible by both 2 and 3
  • By 9: Sum of digits divisible by 9
  • By 10: Ends in 0

🔄 Strategy 5: Fraction Shortcuts

📐 Quick Fraction Methods

Cross-Multiplication for Comparing

To compare a/b and c/d: compare a×d with b×c

Compare 3/7 and 5/11:
3 × 11 = 33 vs 7 × 5 = 35
33 < 35, so 3/7 < 5/11

Butterfly Method for Adding/Subtracting

2/3 + 3/4 = (2×4 + 3×3)/(3×4) = (8+9)/12 = 17/12

Common Fraction-Decimal Equivalents

1/2 = 0.5
1/4 = 0.25
3/4 = 0.75
1/5 = 0.2
1/8 = 0.125
1/3 ≈ 0.333

🔄 Common TEAS Conversions

1 quart = 32 fluid ounces
3.2 quarts = 3.2 × 32 = 102.4 fl oz

Weight/Mass

📝 Strategy 7: Word Problem Translation

🔤 Keywords to Operations

Addition (+) sum, total, plus, more than, increased by, combined
Subtraction (−) difference, minus, less than, decreased by, fewer
Multiplication (×) product, times, of, twice, double, triple
Division (÷) quotient, per, ratio, divided by, split equally
Equals (=) is, are, was, were, gives, yields, results in

Setting Up Equations

"Greg had $105. After depositing two identical paychecks, he had $563."
Equation: 105 + 2x = 563
Solve: 2x = 458, x = $229 per paycheck

🌟 TEAS Mental Math Success Tips

  • Estimate first – Eliminate obviously wrong answers before calculating
  • Use friendly numbers – Round to numbers that are easy to work with
  • Break complex problems into steps – Solve one piece at a time
  • Check your work with estimation – Does your answer make sense?
  • Memorize common conversions – Especially for nursing-related problems
  • Practice daily – Mental math improves with regular practice
  • Know your multiplication tables – Up to 12 × 12 minimum
  • Use the answer choices – Sometimes working backwards is faster

✏️ Worked Examples by Strategy Type

📍 Type 1: Rounding

Problem:

What is 2,346 rounded to the nearest 100?

📝 Mental Math Solution:

Step 1: Identify the hundreds place: 2,346
Step 2: Look at the digit to the right (tens place): 4
Step 3: Since 4 < 5, round DOWN
Step 4: Replace digits to the right with zeros
Answer: 2,300

📊 Type 2: Estimation

Problem:

A 4,324-lb truck needs to carry a load across a bridge with a legal limit of 6,400 lbs. What would be the largest load that the truck could legally carry?

📝 Mental Math Solution:

Step 1: Round for estimation: 6,400 - 4,300 ≈ 2,100
Step 2: Calculate exactly: 6,400 - 4,324
Step 3: Break it down: 6,400 - 4,000 = 2,400; then 2,400 - 324 = 2,076
Answer: 2,076 lbs (≈ 2,050 lbs rounded)

💯 Type 3: Percentage Calculations

Problem:

Last year, 128 babies were born on the fifth floor. This year, 160 babies were born. What was the percentage increase?

📝 Mental Math Solution:

Step 1: Find the change: 160 - 128 = 32
Step 2: Divide by original: 32 ÷ 128
Step 3: Simplify: 32/128 = 1/4 = 0.25
Step 4: Convert to percent: 0.25 × 100 = 25%
Answer: 25% increase

🏷️ Type 4: Finding Original Price

Problem:

Jasmine purchased a tablet marked down 23% to $346.50. What was the original price?

📝 Mental Math Solution:

Step 1: If marked down 23%, she paid 77% (100% - 23%)
Step 2: $346.50 = 77% of original
Step 3: Original = $346.50 ÷ 0.77
Step 4: Estimate: $350 ÷ 0.75 ≈ $467 (close to $450)
Step 5: Calculate: $346.50 ÷ 0.77 = $450
Answer: $450

⚖️ Type 5: Proportions & Ratios

Problem:

A nurse adds 4 g of salt to 20 ml of water. How much salt should be added to 75 ml of water for the same concentration?

📝 Mental Math Solution:

Step 1: Set up proportion: 4/20 = x/75
Step 2: Simplify ratio: 4/20 = 1/5 (1 g per 5 ml)
Step 3: Calculate: 75 ÷ 5 = 15
Answer: 15 g of salt

🔄 Type 6: Unit Conversions

Problem:

A nurse poured 3.2 quarts of liquid into a container. How many fluid ounces were in the container?

📝 Mental Math Solution:

Step 1: Know that 1 quart = 32 fluid ounces
Step 2: Calculate: 3.2 × 32
Step 3: Break it down: 3 × 32 = 96; 0.2 × 32 = 6.4
Step 4: Add: 96 + 6.4 = 102.4
Answer: 102.4 fluid ounces

📝 Type 7: Word Problems

Problem:

Greg had $105 in his savings account. After depositing two identical weekly paychecks, he had $563. How much does Greg earn each week?

📝 Mental Math Solution:

Step 1: Translate to equation: 105 + 2x = 563
Step 2: Find total deposited: 563 - 105 = 458
Step 3: Divide by 2: 458 ÷ 2 = 229
Answer: $229 per week

💊 Type 8: Medication Dosage (Nursing Application)

Problem:

Each day, a 125-lb patient should receive 0.8 mg of medication per kilogram of body weight. What dosage should the patient receive?

📝 Mental Math Solution:

Step 1: Convert lbs to kg: 125 ÷ 2.2 ≈ 56.8 kg
Step 2: Calculate dosage: 56.8 × 0.8
Step 3: Estimate: 57 × 0.8 ≈ 45.6 mg
Step 4: Exact: 56.8 × 0.8 = 45.44 mg
Answer: 45.44 mg (or approximately 45.5 mg)
