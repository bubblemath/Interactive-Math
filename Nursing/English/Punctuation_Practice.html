Step-by-step: one of each mark

1. Commas in a series

Sentence: "The nurse gathered gauze tape and scissors."
There are three items in a list: gauze, tape, and scissors.
Separate each item with a comma — including the one before "and" (the serial comma).
Don't add a comma between the last item and the rest of the sentence.

✓ "The nurse gathered gauze, tape, and scissors."

2. Semicolon between two complete sentences

Sentence: "The lab was backed up the results came late."
"The lab was backed up" is a full sentence. "The results came late" is also a full sentence.
There's no joining word (and, but, so…), so a comma alone would be a splice. Use a semicolon.

✓ "The lab was backed up; the results came late."

3. Apostrophe for possession

Sentence: "Please update the patients medication list."
The list belongs to one patient — that's ownership, so an apostrophe is needed.
One owner → add apostrophe + s: patient's. (Two or more owners would be patients'.)

✓ "Please update the patient's medication list."
