Vocabulary in Context

You don't always need a dictionary. The sentence around an unfamiliar word — its context — usually leaves a clue: a definition, a synonym, an opposite, or an example. Find the clue, and the meaning follows.

Step-by-step

1. A definition clue

The tumor was benign, meaning it was not harmful or cancerous.
The word "meaning" signals that a definition is coming.
Right after it: "not harmful or cancerous." That's the definition.
So benign means not harmful.

2. A contrast (antonym) clue

Unlike her talkative brother, Maria was reticent.
"Unlike" signals a contrast — the two people are opposites.
Her brother is "talkative," so Maria must be the reverse.
So reticent means quiet and reserved.

3. An example clue

Analgesics like ibuprofen and acetaminophen reduce pain.
"like" signals that examples are coming.
Ibuprofen and acetaminophen are familiar pain medicines that reduce pain.
So analgesics are pain relievers.
