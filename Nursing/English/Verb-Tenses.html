Verb Tenses

Keep your verbs in the same time frame. When a passage is in the past, every verb should stay in the past — unless the meaning truly changes. Learn to spot the verb that slips, and fix it.

Key Concepts

Tense tells us when something happens. The rule is simple: stay in one tense across a passage unless the timing really changes. Tap any card to open it.

💡 Big idea: Keep your verbs in the same tense across a sentence or passage. To fix a tense error, find the verb that doesn't match the others (or the time word), and change it to the tense everyone else is using.

Try it

Step-by-step

1. A past-tense passage

✗ "Mr. Diaz woke up early, ate breakfast, and takes his medication."
Scan the verbs: woke and ate are past tense.
takes is present — it's the one that doesn't match.
Change it to the past to match the rest → took (an irregular past).

✓ "…woke up early, ate breakfast, and took his medication."

2. A present-tense passage

✗ "The heart pumps blood, and the lungs supplied oxygen."
This states a general fact, so the tense is present: pumps.
supplied is past — it slipped out of the present.
Match the subject "lungs" (plural) in the present → supply.

✓ "The heart pumps blood, and the lungs supply oxygen."

3. Let the time word decide

✗ "Last night the aide restocked the carts and labels the bins."
The time word Last night sets the past tense.
restocked agrees; labels is present — it doesn't fit.
Change it to the past → labeled.

✓ "Last night the aide restocked the carts and labeled the bins."
