Pronoun Agreement

A pronoun stands in for a noun — its antecedent. The pronoun has to match that noun: one for one, more for more, and person for person. Find the antecedent first, and the right pronoun follows.

Key Concepts

Step-by-step

1. Match in number

Sentence: "The nurses charted ___ notes."
Find the antecedent — it's nurses, which is plural (more than one).
A plural antecedent takes a plural pronoun → their.

✓ "The nurses charted their notes."

2. A single thing takes "it"

Sentence: "Each of the rooms has ___ own monitor."
The antecedent is Each — always singular — and a room is a thing.
Singular thing → its. (Not "their" — that's plural.)

✓ "Each of the rooms has its own monitor."

3. Ignore the phrase in the middle

Sentence: "The box of gloves was moved, so ___ is now by the sink."
The antecedent is box, not "gloves." "of gloves" is just a describing phrase.
One box (a thing) → it.

✓ "…the box of gloves was moved, so it is now by the sink."
