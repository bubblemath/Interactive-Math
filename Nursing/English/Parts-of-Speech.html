Parts of Speech

Every word in a sentence has a job. A word's part of speech is that job — and the same word can do different jobs in different sentences. Learn the eight roles, then spot them in context.

Key Concepts

There are eight parts of speech. Four name and do; four describe and connect. Tap any card to open it.

💡 Big idea: A word's part of speech is its job in the sentence — not the word itself. The same word can be a noun in one sentence and a verb in another. Always ask, “What is this word doing here?”

Step-by-step

1. Label every word

Each word has one job. Here's a whole sentence, labelled:

Thearticle kindadjective nursenoun quicklyadverb calmedverb thearticle frightenedadjective childnoun.

nurse and child name people → nouns.
kind and frightened describe those nouns → adjectives.
calmed is the action → verb. quickly tells how she calmed → adverb.

2. The same word, different jobs

"Please record the intake." — here record is the action → verb.
"Update the record." — here record is a thing → noun.
"Her pulse dropped fast." — fast tells how it dropped → adverb. But "a fast heartbeat" → adjective (it describes the noun).

✓ Don't memorize a word's part of speech — read its job in this sentence.

3. Adjective or adverb?

"She gave a calm response." — calm describes the noun "response" → adjective.
"She responded calmly." — calmly describes the verb "responded" → adverb.
Ask what the word is describing: a noun → adjective; a verb → adverb (and it often ends in -ly).
