🧬 Making Gametes, Hormones, and New Life

The reproductive system produces gametes (sex cells) and the hormones that drive development. The female system makes eggs (ova) and the hormones estrogen and progesterone; the male system makes sperm and testosterone. When a sperm fertilizes an egg, a new individual can develop. 🥚 Gametes & Hormones

  • Gametes (sex cells): the female gamete is the egg (ovum); the male gamete is the sperm. Each carries half the genetic information.
  • Female hormones: the ovaries make estrogen and progesterone, which run the menstrual cycle and pregnancy.
  • Male hormone: the testes make testosterone, which drives sperm production and male characteristics.
  • The boss above the gonads: the pituitary releases FSH and LH, which tell the ovaries and testes when to make gametes and hormones.

🔁 The Menstrual Cycle in Brief

  1. Each cycle (about 28 days), the endometrium thickens to prepare for a possible pregnancy.
  2. Around the middle of the cycle (~day 14), an ovary releases an egg — this is ovulation.
  3. If the egg is fertilized, it implants in the endometrium and pregnancy begins.
  4. If it is not fertilized, the thickened endometrium breaks down and sheds — that flow is menstruation, and the cycle starts again.

🌟 High-Yield TEAS Facts

  • Gonads = ovaries (female) and testes (male); they make the gametes and sex hormones.
  • Gametes: egg/ovum (female), sperm (male).
  • Fertilization usually happens in the fallopian tube; the embryo implants in the uterus.
  • Path of sperm: testis → epididymis → vas deferens → urethra → out.
  • The endometrium thickens and sheds each cycle (menstruation) when there is no pregnancy.

🥚 Example 1: The Path of an Egg (and Where Fertilization Happens)

📝 From ovary to uterus

  • Step 1: An ovary releases a mature egg (ovulation).
  • Step 2: Finger-like fimbriae sweep the egg into the fallopian tube.
  • Step 3: If sperm are present, fertilization usually happens here, in the fallopian tube.
  • Step 4: The fertilized egg travels to the uterus and implants in the endometrium.

Answer: the path is ovary → fallopian tube → uterus. Remember the two key spots: fertilization in the tube, implantation in the uterus.

🚹 Example 2: The Path of Sperm

📝 From testis to outside the body

  • Step 1: Sperm are made in the testes.
  • Step 2: They move into the epididymis, where they mature and are stored.
  • Step 3: They travel up the vas deferens.
  • Step 4: The seminal vesicle and prostate add fluid to make semen.
  • Step 5: Semen exits through the urethra, inside the penis.

Answer: the order is testis → epididymis → vas deferens → urethra → out. A common memory hook is "SEVEN UP": Seminiferous tubules, Epididymis, Vas deferens, Ejaculatory duct, (Nothing), Urethra, Penis.

🔁 Example 3: Why the Endometrium Builds Up and Sheds

📝 Reading the menstrual cycle

  • Step 1: After a period, hormones from the ovaries cause the endometrium to thicken, building a cushion of blood-rich tissue.
  • Step 2: Around day 14, ovulation releases an egg from the ovary.
  • Step 3a: If the egg is fertilized and implants, the lining stays to support the pregnancy.
  • Step 3b: If it is not fertilized, hormone levels drop, the lining can't be maintained, and it sheds as menstruation.

Answer: the endometrium thickens to prepare for a fertilized egg; if none arrives, it sheds and the ~28-day cycle restarts. The thickening and shedding are controlled by the ovarian hormones estrogen and progesterone.
