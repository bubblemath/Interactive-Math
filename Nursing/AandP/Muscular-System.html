💪 The Muscles That Move You

The muscular system creates movement, holds your posture, and even makes heat. There are three kinds of muscle, but the ones you can name and control are the skeletal muscles attached to your bones. 🔄 How Muscles Create Movement

  • Muscles attach to bones with tendons.
  • Muscles can only pull (contract), never push. They shorten to move a bone.
  • Because they only pull, muscles work in antagonistic pairs — one contracts while its partner relaxes.
  • Example: to bend your elbow, the biceps contracts and the triceps relaxes. To straighten it, they swap roles.
  • Contraction needs calcium and energy (ATP), and is triggered by a signal from a motor neuron.

🤸 Common Movement Terms

  • Flexion — bending a joint (decreasing its angle). Extension — straightening it (increasing the angle).
  • Abduction — moving a limb away from the midline. Adduction — moving it toward the midline.
  • Rotation — turning around an axis (shaking your head "no").
  • Circumduction — moving a limb in a circle (a cone shape).

Memory aids: "abduct" = take away; "ADDuction" = add back to the body.

🌟 High-Yield TEAS Facts

  • Three muscle types: skeletal (voluntary), cardiac (heart), smooth (organs).
  • Only skeletal muscle is voluntary; cardiac and smooth are involuntary.
  • Muscles attach to bone by tendons and can only pull.
  • Muscles work in antagonistic pairs (biceps/triceps, quadriceps/hamstrings).
  • Flexion bends, extension straightens; abduction moves away, adduction moves toward the midline.

💪 Example 1: Why Muscles Come in Pairs

📝 The biceps and triceps at the elbow

  • Step 1: A muscle can only pull (contract) — it can't push.
  • Step 2: So to move a bone both ways, you need two muscles on opposite sides: an antagonistic pair.
  • Step 3: To bend (flex) the elbow, the biceps contracts while the triceps relaxes.
  • Step 4: To straighten (extend) it, the triceps contracts while the biceps relaxes.

Answer: the biceps is the flexor and the triceps is the extensor at the elbow — a classic antagonistic pair. The quadriceps and hamstrings do the same job at the knee.

🧬 Example 2: Which Muscle Type Is It?

📝 Sort by control and location

  • Can you move it on purpose, and is it attached to a bone? → Skeletal (voluntary, striated).
  • Is it in the heart? → Cardiac (involuntary, striated).
  • Is it in the wall of an organ or blood vessel, working automatically? → Smooth (involuntary, not striated).

Example: the muscle that pushes food through your intestines is smooth muscle — you can't control it, and it's not attached to bone.

🤸 Example 3: Abduction vs. Adduction

📝 Away or toward the midline?

  • Picture the body's midline — an imaginary line straight down the center.
  • Abduction moves a limb away from that line (raising your arm out to the side). Think "abduct = take away."
  • Adduction moves a limb toward that line (bringing your arm back down). Think "ADD = add back to the body."

Tip: the two words look almost identical — the only difference is "ab" (away) vs. "ad" (toward). Lock in the mnemonics and you won't mix them up.
