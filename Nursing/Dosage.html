Problem 1: Basic Tablet Calculation

Basic
A physician orders 500 mg of amoxicillin. The pharmacy supplies 250 mg tablets. How many tablets should the nurse administer?

✅ Solution:

1
Identify what you have:
Desired: 500 mg | Available: 250 mg/tablet
2
Apply the formula:
Tablets = Desired ÷ Available = 500 mg ÷ 250 mg
3
Calculate:
500 ÷ 250 = 2
Answer: 2 tablets

Problem 2: Liquid Medication

Basic
A patient is prescribed 400 mg of ibuprofen suspension. The medication is available as 200 mg per 5 mL. How many mL should be administered?

✅ Solution:

1
Set up the proportion:
200 mg / 5 mL = 400 mg / x mL
2
Cross multiply:
200 × x = 400 × 5
200x = 2000
3
Solve for x:
x = 2000 ÷ 200 = 10
Answer: 10 mL

Problem 3: Weight-Based Dosing

Intermediate
A child weighs 44 pounds. The physician orders a medication at 5 mg/kg. What is the correct dose in mg?

✅ Solution:

1
Convert pounds to kilograms:
44 lb ÷ 2.2 = 20 kg
2
Calculate the dose:
Dose = Weight × mg/kg
Dose = 20 kg × 5 mg/kg = 100 mg
Answer: 100 mg

Problem 4: IV Flow Rate (mL/hr)

Basic
A patient is to receive 1,000 mL of normal saline over 8 hours. What is the flow rate in mL/hr?

✅ Solution:

1
Apply the formula:
Flow rate = Total Volume ÷ Time
2
Calculate:
Flow rate = 1000 mL ÷ 8 hr = 125 mL/hr
Answer: 125 mL/hr

Problem 5: IV Drip Rate (gtts/min)

Advanced
An IV of 500 mL is to infuse over 4 hours. The drop factor is 15 gtts/mL. What is the drip rate in gtts/min?

✅ Solution:

1
Convert hours to minutes:
4 hours × 60 = 240 minutes
2
Apply the formula:
gtts/min = (Volume × Drop Factor) ÷ Time
gtts/min = (500 mL × 15 gtts/mL) ÷ 240 min
3
Calculate:
gtts/min = 7500 ÷ 240 = 31.25 ≈ 31
Answer: 31 gtts/min
