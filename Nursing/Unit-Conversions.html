The Metric System

The metric system is based on powers of 10, making conversions straightforward. It's the standard in healthcare and science worldwide.

🧠 Memory Trick for Metric Prefixes

"King Henry Died By Drinking Chocolate Milk"

Kilo → Hecto → Deka → Base unit → Deci → Centi → Milli
Prefix Symbol Meaning Example
Kilo- k 1,000 (10³) 1 km = 1,000 m
Hecto- h 100 (10²) 1 hm = 100 m
Deka- da 10 (10¹) 1 dam = 10 m
BASE — 1 (10⁰) meter, liter, gram
Deci- d 0.1 (10⁻¹) 1 dm = 0.1 m
Centi- c 0.01 (10⁻²) 1 cm = 0.01 m
Milli- m 0.001 (10⁻³) 1 mm = 0.001 m

Essential Metric Conversions for TEAS:

Type Conversion Factor
Length Kilometers ↔ Meters 1 km = 1,000 m
Meters ↔ Centimeters 1 m = 100 cm
Centimeters ↔ Millimeters 1 cm = 10 mm
Mass Kilograms ↔ Grams 1 kg = 1,000 g
Grams ↔ Milligrams 1 g = 1,000 mg
Milligrams ↔ Micrograms 1 mg = 1,000 mcg
Volume Liters ↔ Milliliters 1 L = 1,000 mL

The Standard (US Customary) System

The standard system uses units like inches, feet, pounds, and gallons. Conversions are not based on 10, so you must memorize the factors.

Type Conversion Factor
Length Feet ↔ Inches 1 ft = 12 in
Yards ↔ Feet 1 yd = 3 ft
Miles ↔ Feet 1 mi = 5,280 ft
Weight Pounds ↔ Ounces 1 lb = 16 oz
Tons ↔ Pounds 1 ton = 2,000 lb
Volume Gallons ↔ Quarts 1 gal = 4 qt
Quarts ↔ Pints 1 qt = 2 pt
Pints ↔ Cups 1 pt = 2 cups
Cups ↔ Fluid Ounces 1 cup = 8 fl oz
Time Hours ↔ Minutes 1 hr = 60 min
Minutes ↔ Seconds 1 min = 60 sec

Dimensional Analysis Method

📋 Steps for Unit Conversion

  1. Write what you're starting with — Include the number and unit
  2. Set up conversion factor(s) — Write as a fraction with units that cancel
  3. Arrange so units cancel — The unit you want to eliminate goes in the denominator
  4. Multiply across — Multiply all numerators, divide by all denominators
  5. Check your answer — Does the unit make sense? Is the number reasonable?

Example: Convert 5 kilometers to meters

5 km × 1,000 m 1 km = 5,000 m

The "km" units cancel, leaving only "m" — exactly what we want!

💊

Critical for Nursing!

Unit conversions are essential for medication safety. Common nursing conversions include: mg to g, mL to L, kg to lb (for weight-based dosing), and mcg to mg. A conversion error can result in a patient receiving 10x or 1/10th the intended dose — potentially fatal! Always double-check your work.

Example 1: Metric Length Conversion

Metric

Problem:

Convert 3.5 meters to centimeters.

✅ Solution:

1
Identify the conversion factor: 1 m = 100 cm
2
Set up the conversion (cm on top to keep cm in answer):
3.5 m × 100 cm 1 m
3
Cancel units and multiply:
3.5 × 100 = 350 cm
Answer: 3.5 m = 350 cm

Example 2: Metric Mass Conversion

Metric

Problem:

Convert 2,500 milligrams to grams.

✅ Solution:

1
Identify the conversion factor: 1 g = 1,000 mg
2
Set up the conversion (g on top, mg on bottom to cancel):
2,500 mg × 1 g 1,000 mg
3
Calculate:
2,500 ÷ 1,000 = 2.5 g
Answer: 2,500 mg = 2.5 g

Example 3: Standard Volume Conversion

Standard

Problem:

Convert 3 gallons to cups.

✅ Solution:

1
Chain of conversions: gal → qt → pt → cups
1 gal = 4 qt, 1 qt = 2 pt, 1 pt = 2 cups
2
Set up the chain:
3 gal × 4 qt 1 gal × 2 pt 1 qt × 2 cups 1 pt
3
Multiply: 3 × 4 × 2 × 2 = 48 cups
Answer: 3 gallons = 48 cups
