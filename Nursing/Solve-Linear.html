What is a Linear Equation?

Definition

A linear equation is an equation where the variable (usually x) has an exponent of 1. The graph of a linear equation is always a straight line.

3x + 5 = 14

The Goal

Solving a linear equation means finding the value of the variable that makes the equation true. We want to isolate the variable on one side of the equation.

x = ?

The Balance Principle

An equation is like a balance scale. Whatever you do to one side, you must do to the other side to keep it balanced.

If 3x + 5 = 14, then 3x + 5 - 5 = 14 - 5

Checking Your Answer

Always substitute your answer back into the original equation to verify it's correct. Both sides should be equal.

If x = 3: Does 3(3) + 5 = 14? → 9 + 5 = 14 ✓

⚖️ The Golden Rule of Equations

Whatever operation you perform on one side of the equation, you MUST perform the same operation on the other side. This keeps the equation balanced and true.

🔄

Inverse Operations

To isolate the variable, we use inverse operations — operations that "undo" each other. Think of them as opposites.

Operation Inverse Operation Example
Addition (+) Subtraction (−) x + 5 = 12 → subtract 5 → x = 7
Subtraction (−) Addition (+) x − 3 = 10 → add 3 → x = 13
Multiplication (×) Division (÷) 4x = 20 → divide by 4 → x = 5
Division (÷) Multiplication (×) x/3 = 6 → multiply by 3 → x = 18
📝

Steps to Solve Linear Equations

General Strategy (for multi-step equations):

  1. Simplify each side — Distribute and combine like terms if needed
  2. Move variable terms — Get all variable terms on one side using addition/subtraction
  3. Move constant terms — Get all numbers (constants) on the other side
  4. Isolate the variable — Divide or multiply to get the variable alone
  5. Check your answer — Substitute back into the original equation

One-Step Equations

Only one operation needed to solve. Use the inverse operation once.

x + 7 = 15 Subtract 7: x = 8

Two-Step Equations

Two operations needed. Usually: undo addition/subtraction first, then multiplication/division.

2x + 3 = 11 Subtract 3: 2x = 8 Divide by 2: x = 4

Variables on Both Sides

Move all variable terms to one side first, then solve as usual.

5x + 2 = 3x + 10 Subtract 3x: 2x + 2 = 10 Subtract 2: 2x = 8 → x = 4

With Parentheses

Distribute first (multiply through parentheses), then combine like terms and solve.

3(x + 2) = 15 Distribute: 3x + 6 = 15 Subtract 6: 3x = 9 → x = 3
⚠️

Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Forgetting to Do Both Sides

If you subtract 5 from the left side, you MUST subtract 5 from the right side too.

❌ Wrong Order of Operations

Undo addition/subtraction BEFORE multiplication/division (reverse of PEMDAS).

❌ Sign Errors

Be careful with negative numbers! Subtracting a negative is the same as adding.

❌ Incomplete Distribution

When distributing, multiply EVERY term inside the parentheses: 3(x + 2) = 3x + 6, not 3x + 2.

💊

Why This Matters for Nursing

Linear equations are essential for dosage calculations. For example: "If a patient needs 500 mg of medication and each tablet contains 125 mg, how many tablets are needed?" This becomes the equation: 125x = 500, so x = 4 tablets. Accurate equation solving ensures patient safety!

Example 1: One-Step (Addition)

One-Step

Problem: Solve for x

x + 9 = 17

✅ Solution:

1
Identify The variable has 9 added to it. Use the inverse: subtract 9.
2
Subtract 9 from both sides:
x + 9 − 9 = 17 − 9
3
Simplify:
x = 8
✓ Check:

8 + 9 = 17 ✓

Answer: x = 8

Example 2: One-Step (Multiplication)

One-Step

Problem: Solve for x

5x = 35

✅ Solution:

1
Identify The variable is multiplied by 5. Use the inverse: divide by 5.
2
Divide both sides by 5:
5x ÷ 5 = 35 ÷ 5
3
Simplify:
x = 7
✓ Check:

5(7) = 35 ✓

Answer: x = 7

Example 3: Two-Step Equation

Two-Step

Problem: Solve for x

3x + 7 = 22

✅ Solution:

1
First Undo addition — subtract 7 from both sides:
3x + 7 − 7 = 22 − 7
3x = 15
2
Second Undo multiplication — divide both sides by 3:
3x ÷ 3 = 15 ÷ 3
x = 5
✓ Check:

3(5) + 7 = 15 + 7 = 22 ✓

Answer: x = 5

Example 4: Two-Step with Subtraction

Two-Step

Problem: Solve for x

4x − 5 = 19

✅ Solution:

1
First Undo subtraction — add 5 to both sides:
4x − 5 + 5 = 19 + 5
4x = 24
2
Second Undo multiplication — divide both sides by 4:
4x ÷ 4 = 24 ÷ 4
x = 6
✓ Check:

4(6) − 5 = 24 − 5 = 19 ✓

Answer: x = 6

Example 5: Variables on Both Sides

Variables Both Sides

Problem: Solve for x

6x + 4 = 2x + 20

✅ Solution:

1
Move variables Subtract 2x from both sides:
6x − 2x + 4 = 2x − 2x + 20
4x + 4 = 20
2
Move constants Subtract 4 from both sides:
4x + 4 − 4 = 20 − 4
4x = 16
3
Isolate Divide both sides by 4:
x = 4
✓ Check:

6(4) + 4 = 24 + 4 = 28 and 2(4) + 20 = 8 + 20 = 28 ✓

Answer: x = 4

Example 6: With Parentheses (Distributive Property)

Multi-Step

Problem: Solve for x

2(x + 5) = 18

✅ Solution:

1
Distribute Multiply 2 by each term inside parentheses:
2 · x + 2 · 5 = 18
2x + 10 = 18
2
Subtract Subtract 10 from both sides:
2x + 10 − 10 = 18 − 10
2x = 8
3
Divide Divide both sides by 2:
x = 4
✓ Check:

2(4 + 5) = 2(9) = 18 ✓

Answer: x = 4
