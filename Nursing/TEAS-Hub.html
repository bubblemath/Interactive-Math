First, how the exam works

📘 The ATI TEAS 7 at a glance

The TEAS is the entrance exam most nursing programs use. It has 170 questions and a total time limit of 209 minutes (just under 3½ hours). Only 150 questions are scored — the other 20 are unscored questions the test-makers are trying out, and you can't tell which is which. That's actually freeing: if a question looks bizarre, it may not even count. Answer it and move on.

⏱️ How to spend your prep time well

  • Diagnose before you study. Take the pre-tests first. Two hours spent finding your weak spots will save you twenty hours of re-reading things you already know.
  • Study by weight, not by comfort. It's tempting to practice what you're already good at. Science is 50 questions and English is 37 — spend your hours where the questions are.
  • Short and often beats long and rare. Four 45-minute sessions across a week will stick far better than one 3-hour cram. The scheduler below is built around this.
  • Practice with a clock. Most people who struggle on the TEAS know the material but run out of time. Once you're comfortable, do a practice set while timing yourself at the pace above.
  • Rework every miss. A wrong answer you understand is worth more than a right answer you guessed. Our practice tests explain why each wrong choice is wrong — read those.
  • Rest the day before. Cramming the night before reliably lowers scores. Sleep is a study strategy.

🔒 Where your plan is saved (and who can see it)

Your exam date, your settings, and every box you tick are saved only on this device, in this browser, using a built-in browser feature called local storage. Think of it like a sticky note your browser keeps for this one website.

  • Nothing is uploaded. Your plan never leaves your device. There is no account, no login, and no server storing it — not even the college.
  • No one else can see it. Not your instructor, not other students. Only someone using this browser on this device.
  • It's per-browser and per-device. A plan built on your phone won't appear on your laptop, and a plan built in Chrome won't appear in Safari.
  • You're in control. The Delete my saved data button erases it instantly and completely.
⚠️ Because it lives in this browser only: clearing your browsing data, using private/incognito mode, or switching devices will lose your checklist. If your plan matters to you, use the Print plan button to keep a paper copy.
