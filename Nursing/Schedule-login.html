📋 TEAS Exam Overview

What is the TEAS?

The Test of Essential Academic Skills (TEAS) is a standardized admissions exam used by nursing schools to assess a student's readiness for health science programs. The exam measures basic academic skills in reading, mathematics, science, and English language usage.

Exam Structure

📖 Reading

45 Questions 55 Minutes

  • Key Ideas & Details
  • Craft & Structure
  • Integration of Knowledge & Ideas
  • Pre-test Questions (6 unscored)

🔢 Mathematics

38 Questions 57 Minutes

  • Numbers & Algebra
  • Measurement & Data
  • Pre-test Questions (4 unscored)
  • Calculator allowed for some sections

🔬 Science

50 Questions 60 Minutes

  • Human Anatomy & Physiology
  • Biology
  • Chemistry
  • Scientific Reasoning
  • Pre-test Questions (6 unscored)

✏️ English & Language Usage

37 Questions 37 Minutes

  • Conventions of Standard English
  • Knowledge of Language
  • Using Language & Vocabulary
  • Pre-test Questions (4 unscored)

Total Exam Summary

170 Total Questions 209 Minutes (3 hrs 29 min) 150 Scored Questions 20 Unscored Pre-test Questions

Scoring

  • Composite Score: Overall score ranging from 0% to 100%
  • Content Area Scores: Individual percentages for each of the four sections
  • Passing Score: Varies by program (typically 60-80%)
  • Competitive Score: 70% or higher is generally competitive
  • Academic Preparedness Levels: Developmental, Basic, Proficient, Advanced, Exemplary

💡 Tips & Tricks for Success

  • Time Management: Practice pacing yourself. You have roughly 1-1.5 minutes per question depending on the section.
  • Process of Elimination: Narrow down answers by eliminating obviously wrong choices first.
  • Read Carefully: Pay attention to words like "EXCEPT," "NOT," and "MOST" in questions.
  • Science Focus: Anatomy & Physiology makes up the largest portion of the science section—prioritize it!
  • Math Basics: Review fractions, percentages, ratios, and basic algebra. Know metric conversions.
  • Reading Strategy: Skim passages first, then read questions to know what to look for.
  • Grammar Rules: Review subject-verb agreement, punctuation, and sentence structure.
  • Practice Tests: Take full-length practice exams under timed conditions.
  • Rest Before Exam: Get a good night's sleep and eat a healthy breakfast on test day.
  • Don't Leave Blanks: There's no penalty for guessing—answer every question!
