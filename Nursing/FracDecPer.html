Fractions

Understanding Fractions

A fraction represents a part of a whole. The numerator (top) shows how many parts you have, and the denominator (bottom) shows how many equal parts make up the whole.

3 4 means 3 parts out of 4 equal parts

Simplifying Fractions

Divide both numerator and denominator by their Greatest Common Factor (GCF) to reduce a fraction to lowest terms.

12 16 ÷ 4 4 = 3 4

Adding & Subtracting Fractions

Find a common denominator, convert each fraction, then add or subtract the numerators. Keep the denominator the same.

1 4 + 2 3 = 3 12 + 8 12 = 11 12

Multiplying Fractions

Multiply numerators together and denominators together. Simplify the result if possible.

2 3 × 3 4 = 6 12 = 1 2

Dividing Fractions

Keep, Change, Flip: Keep the first fraction, change ÷ to ×, flip the second fraction (reciprocal).

2 3 ÷ 4 5 = 2 3 × 5 4 = 5 6

Mixed Numbers & Improper Fractions

Convert mixed numbers to improper fractions before operations: multiply whole number by denominator, add numerator.

2 1 3 = (2×3)+1 3 = 7 3

Decimals

Place Value

Each position after the decimal point represents a power of 10: tenths, hundredths, thousandths, etc.

0.375 = 3 tenths + 7 hundredths + 5 thousandths

Converting Fractions to Decimals

Divide the numerator by the denominator. Use long division or recognize common conversions.

3 4 = 3 ÷ 4 = 0.75

Converting Decimals to Fractions

Write the decimal over its place value (10, 100, 1000), then simplify.

0.625 = 625 1000 = 5 8

Adding & Subtracting Decimals

Line up the decimal points, then add or subtract as with whole numbers.

12.45 + 3.70 = 16.15

Multiplying Decimals

Multiply as whole numbers, then count total decimal places in both factors and place the decimal in the product.

2.5 × 0.4 = 1.00 = 1 (2 decimal places)

Dividing Decimals

Move the decimal in the divisor to make it a whole number. Move the decimal in the dividend the same number of places.

4.5 ÷ 0.5 = 45 ÷ 5 = 9
%

Percentages

Understanding Percentages

Percent means "per hundred." A percentage represents a fraction with a denominator of 100.

75% = 75 100 = 0.75

Converting Percentages to Decimals

Divide by 100 (move decimal point 2 places left).

45% = 45 ÷ 100 = 0.45

Converting Decimals to Percentages

Multiply by 100 (move decimal point 2 places right).

0.125 = 0.125 × 100 = 12.5%

Finding a Percentage of a Number

Convert the percentage to a decimal, then multiply.

What is 20% of 150? 0.20 × 150 = 30

Finding What Percent One Number is of Another

Divide the part by the whole, then multiply by 100.

What percent is 24 of 80? (24 ÷ 80) × 100 = 30%

Percent Increase/Decrease

Calculate the change, divide by the original, multiply by 100.

% Change = New − Original Original × 100

TEAS Exam Tip

On the TEAS, you'll often see these concepts in healthcare contexts—medication dosages, patient statistics, and lab values. Practice converting between fractions, decimals, and percentages quickly. Memorize common conversions:

1 2 = 0.5 = 50% | 1 4 = 0.25 = 25% | 3 4 = 0.75 = 75%

Example 1: Adding Fractions

Fractions

Problem:

A nurse administers 23 of a medication dose in the morning and 14 of the dose in the evening. What fraction of the total dose was administered?

✅ Solution:

1
Find the Least Common Denominator (LCD) of 3 and 4:
LCD = 12
2
Convert 23 to twelfths:
2 3 × 4 4 = 8 12
3
Convert 14 to twelfths:
1 4 × 3 3 = 3 12
4
Add the fractions:
8 12 + 3 12 = 11 12
Answer: 11 12 of the total dose

Example 2: Multiplying Decimals

Decimals

Problem:

A patient needs 2.5 mg of medication per kg of body weight. If the patient weighs 68.4 kg, how many mg of medication are needed?

✅ Solution:

1
Set up the multiplication:
2.5 × 68.4
2
Multiply as whole numbers:
25 × 684 = 17,100
3
Count decimal places: 2.5 has 1 place, 68.4 has 1 place
Total: 2 decimal places
4
Place the decimal point:
17100 → 171.00 = 171
Answer: 171 mg

Example 3: Finding a Percentage of a Number

Percentages

Problem:

A hospital has 240 beds. If 85% of the beds are occupied, how many beds are occupied?

✅ Solution:

1
Convert 85% to a decimal:
85% = 0.85
2
Multiply by the total:
0.85 × 240
3
Calculate:
0.85 × 240 = 204
Answer: 204 beds are occupied

Example 4: Converting Between Forms

Conversions

Problem:

Express 58 as a decimal and as a percentage.

✅ Solution:

1
Convert to decimal by dividing:
5 ÷ 8 = 0.625
2
Convert decimal to percentage by multiplying by 100:
0.625 × 100 = 62.5%
Answer: 5 8 = 0.625 = 62.5%

Example 5: Percent Decrease

Percentages

Problem:

A patient's blood pressure dropped from 150 mmHg to 120 mmHg. What is the percent decrease?

✅ Solution:

1
Find the change:
150 − 120 = 30
2
Divide by the original value:
30 150 = 0.2
3
Convert to percentage:
0.2 × 100 = 20%
Answer: 20% decrease

Example 6: Dividing Fractions

Fractions

Problem:

A medication bottle contains 34 liter of solution. If each dose is 18 liter, how many doses are in the bottle?

✅ Solution:

1
Set up division:
3 4 ÷ 1 8
2
Keep, Change, Flip:
3 4 × 8 1
3
Multiply:
3 × 8 4 × 1 = 24 4
4
Simplify:
24 4 = 6
Answer: 6 doses
