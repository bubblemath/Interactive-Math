📐 TEAS Exam: Area & Perimeter

Measure shapes with confidence — including combined shapes

📖 Area & Perimeter: The Big Picture

These two measurements answer two different questions. Perimeter is the distance around the outside of a shape — think of walking the edge of a room. Area is the amount of space inside a shape — think of the carpet that covers the floor. On the TEAS you'll see these in healthcare settings: the floor space for equipment, the surface a dressing covers, the edging around a wound, or the area of a treatment room.

🎯 Your 3-Step Shape Playbook

  1. Name the shape(s). Is it a single shape, or a combined shape you can break into a rectangle, triangle, and/or circle?
  2. Pick the formula and plug in. Watch two traps: use the radius (not diameter) for circles, and keep your units straight.
  3. Combine carefully. For area, add the pieces (or subtract a cut-out). For perimeter, trace only the outside edge — don't count edges hidden inside the shape.

📏 Units Matter

  • Perimeter is measured in linear units (cm, in, ft, m).
  • Area is measured in square units (cm², in², ft², m²).
  • If your answer choices show "square" units, they're asking for area. Plain units mean perimeter.

🧮 Formulas to Know Cold

The TEAS does not give you a formula sheet, so memorize these. Use π ≈ 3.14.

🔗 Combined (Composite) Shapes

  • Break it apart into shapes you recognize — for example, a rectangle with a half-circle on top (like a window).
  • Area: find each piece, then add them. If a piece is cut out, subtract it instead.
  • Perimeter: only count the edges on the outside. When a half-circle sits on top of a rectangle, the rectangle's top edge disappears — replace it with the curved edge, don't count both.

⚠️ The Radius vs. Diameter Trap

Circle formulas use the radius (center to edge). If a problem gives you the diameter (all the way across), cut it in half first. A diameter of 10 means a radius of 5. Using 10 where the formula wants 5 is the most common circle mistake on the test.

🌟 TEAS Success Tips

  1. Read the question twice — does it want area (inside) or perimeter (around)?
  2. Sketch combined shapes and label every dimension before you calculate.
  3. For triangles, never forget the ½.
  4. For circles, halve the diameter to get the radius before plugging in.
  5. Check your units: square units = area, plain units = perimeter.
  6. Estimate first to eliminate answers that are obviously too big or too small.

▭ Example 1: Rectangle (Area & Perimeter)

A rectangular treatment room is 8 feet long and 5 feet wide. What is its area, and how much baseboard trim is needed to go around the floor?

📝 Step-by-Step Solution

  • Area = length × width = 8 × 5 = 40
  • Perimeter = 2 × (length + width) = 2 × (8 + 5) = 2 × 13 = 26

Answer: Area = 40 square feet (the floor space). Perimeter = 26 feet of baseboard.

🔺 Example 2: Triangle (Area)

A triangular sail-shaped privacy screen has a base of 10 inches and a height of 6 inches. What is its area?

📝 Step-by-Step Solution

  • Triangle area = ½ × base × height
  • = ½ × 10 × 6 = ½ × 60 = 30

Answer: 30 square inches. (Forgetting the ½ would give 60 — a common slip.)

⭕ Example 3: Circle (the Diameter Trap)

A circular bandage has a diameter of 10 cm. What is its area? (Use π ≈ 3.14.)

📝 Step-by-Step Solution

  • The formula needs the radius, but we're given the diameter. Cut it in half: r = 10 ÷ 2 = 5.
  • Area = π × r² = 3.14 × 5² = 3.14 × 25 = 78.5

Answer: 78.5 square cm. (Using 10 instead of 5 would give a wrong answer four times too big.)

🪟 Example 4: Combined Shape — Area

A window-shaped panel is a rectangle 10 in wide and 6 in tall, with a half-circle on top. Find the total area. (Use π ≈ 3.14.)

📝 Step-by-Step Solution

  • Break it apart: a rectangle + a half-circle.
  • The half-circle sits on the 10-inch top, so its diameter is 10 → radius = 5.
  • Rectangle area = 10 × 6 = 60
  • Half-circle area = ½ × π × r² = ½ × 3.14 × 25 = 39.25
  • Add them: 60 + 39.25 = 99.25

Answer: 99.25 square inches.

📐 Example 5: Combined Shape — Perimeter

Using the same window shape (rectangle 10 wide × 6 tall, half-circle on top), find the perimeter — the distance all the way around the outside.

📝 Step-by-Step Solution

  • Trace the outside: bottom + two sides + curved top.
  • Bottom = 10, left side = 6, right side = 6.
  • Curved top = half-circle edge = π × r = 3.14 × 5 = 15.7
  • Do NOT count the rectangle's top edge — the half-circle replaced it.
  • Add the outside: 10 + 6 + 6 + 15.7 = 37.7

Answer: 37.7 inches around.
