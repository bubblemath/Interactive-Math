Understanding Ratios

What is a Ratio?

A ratio compares two quantities by division. It shows the relative size of two values. Ratios can be written in three ways:

3 to 4 or 3 : 4 or 3 4

Simplifying Ratios

Simplify ratios by dividing both terms by their Greatest Common Factor (GCF), just like simplifying fractions.

12 : 18 = 2 : 3 (÷ 6)

Part-to-Part vs Part-to-Whole

Part-to-Part: Compares one part to another part (e.g., boys to girls = 3:2)
Part-to-Whole: Compares one part to the total (e.g., boys to total students = 3:5)

If boys:girls = 3:2, then boys:total = 3:5

Unit Rates

A unit rate is a ratio with a denominator of 1. It tells you the amount per single unit (per hour, per mile, per item).

$45 for 3 hours = $15 per hour

Equivalent Ratios

Equivalent ratios have the same value when simplified. Multiply or divide both terms by the same number to find equivalent ratios.

2 : 3 = 4 : 6 = 6 : 9 = 8 : 12

Comparing Ratios

To compare ratios, convert them to the same form (fractions with common denominators or decimals).

3:4 = 0.75 vs 2:3 ≈ 0.67

Understanding Proportions

What is a Proportion?

A proportion is an equation stating that two ratios are equal. If two ratios form a proportion, their cross products are equal.

a b = c d means a × d = b × c

Cross Multiplication

To solve a proportion with an unknown, cross multiply and then solve for the variable.

3 4 = x 20 4 × x = 3 × 20 4x = 60, so x = 15

Setting Up Proportions

When setting up a proportion, make sure like units are in the same position (both numerators or both denominators).

miles hours = miles hours

Direct Proportion

In a direct proportion, as one quantity increases, the other increases at the same rate. The ratio stays constant.

If 2 items cost $6, then 4 items cost $12

Scale Factor

A scale factor is the ratio between corresponding measurements. Used in maps, models, and similar figures.

1 inch : 50 miles → 3 inches = 150 miles

Checking Proportions

To verify if two ratios form a proportion, check if their cross products are equal.

Does 6:8 = 9:12? 6 × 12 = 72 and 8 × 9 = 72 ✓
💊

Healthcare Applications

Medication Dosage

Dosages are often calculated using proportions based on patient weight or prescribed amounts.

If dosage is 5 mg per kg: 5 mg 1 kg = x mg 70 kg

IV Drip Rates

IV flow rates use ratios to determine drops per minute or mL per hour.

500 mL 4 hours = 125 mL/hour

Solution Concentrations

Mixing solutions requires understanding ratios of solute to solvent or solution strength.

A 1:4 solution means 1 part medication to 4 parts total

Staffing Ratios

Healthcare facilities use ratios to determine appropriate nurse-to-patient ratios.

1 nurse : 4 patients → 20 patients need 5 nurses
💡

TEAS Exam Strategy

On the TEAS, ratio and proportion problems often appear in healthcare contexts. Key strategies:

  • Label your units — this helps you set up proportions correctly
  • Keep like units aligned — numerator with numerator, denominator with denominator
  • Cross multiply carefully — this is your main solving technique
  • Check your answer — substitute back to verify the proportion is true
  • Watch for unit conversions — you may need to convert before setting up the proportion

Example 1: Simplifying Ratios

Ratios

Problem:

In a hospital unit, there are 24 nurses and 36 patients. What is the simplified ratio of nurses to patients?

✅ Solution:

1
Write the ratio:
24 : 36
2
Find the GCF of 24 and 36:
GCF = 12
3
Divide both terms by the GCF:
24 ÷ 12 = 2 and 36 ÷ 12 = 3
Answer: 2 : 3 (or 2 nurses for every 3 patients)

Example 2: Solving a Proportion

Proportions

Problem:

If 3 tablets contain 750 mg of medication, how many mg are in 5 tablets?

✅ Solution:

1
Set up the proportion (tablets over mg):
3 tablets 750 mg = 5 tablets x mg
2
Cross multiply:
3 × x = 750 × 5
3
Simplify:
3x = 3750
4
Solve for x:
x = 3750 ÷ 3 = 1250
Answer: 1250 mg

Example 3: Finding Unit Rate

Unit Rates

Problem:

A patient receives 240 mL of IV fluid over 3 hours. What is the flow rate in mL per hour?

✅ Solution:

1
Write the ratio:
240 mL 3 hours
2
Divide to find the unit rate:
240 ÷ 3 = 80
Answer: 80 mL per hour

Example 4: Medication Dosage

Healthcare

Problem:

A medication is prescribed at 8 mg per kg of body weight. How much medication should be given to a patient weighing 65 kg?

✅ Solution:

1
Set up the proportion:
8 mg 1 kg = x mg 65 kg
2
Cross multiply:
1 × x = 8 × 65
3
Calculate:
x = 520
Answer: 520 mg

Example 5: Scale Problems

Proportions

Problem:

On a map, 2 inches represents 50 miles. If two cities are 7 inches apart on the map, what is the actual distance between them?

✅ Solution:

1
Set up the proportion:
2 inches 50 miles = 7 inches x miles
2
Cross multiply:
2 × x = 50 × 7
3
Solve:
2x = 350 → x = 175
Answer: 175 miles

Example 6: Part-to-Whole Ratios

Ratios

Problem:

In a nursing class, the ratio of female to male students is 5:2. If there are 35 students total, how many are female?

✅ Solution:

1
Find total parts:
5 + 2 = 7 parts
2
Set up proportion (female to total):
5 7 = x 35
3
Cross multiply and solve:
7x = 5 × 35 = 175 → x = 25
Answer: 25 female students
