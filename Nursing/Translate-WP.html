What is Word Problem Translation?

Word problem translation is the skill of converting everyday language into mathematical expressions or equations. This is essential for solving real-world problems on the TEAS exam, especially in healthcare contexts.

Expression vs. Equation

Expression: A mathematical phrase without an equals sign (e.g., 3x + 5)

Equation: A mathematical statement with an equals sign showing two things are equal (e.g., 3x + 5 = 20)

The Unknown (Variable)

Words like a number, what, how many, or how much represent the unknown value — use a variable like x or n.

🔑

Key Words for Each Operation

Operation Key Words & Phrases Example
Addition (+) sum, plus, added to, increased by, more than, total, combined, together, and "5 more than x" → x + 5
Subtraction (−) difference, minus, subtracted from, decreased by, less than, fewer, reduced by, take away "7 less than x" → x − 7
Multiplication (×) product, times, multiplied by, of, twice, double, triple, each "twice a number" → 2x
Division (÷) quotient, divided by, per, ratio, split, shared equally, out of "x divided by 4" → x/4
Equals (=) is, are, was, were, equals, gives, yields, results in, the same as "x plus 3 is 10" → x + 3 = 10
⚠️

Tricky Phrases to Watch For

"Less Than" — Order Matters!

5 less than x means x − 5 (NOT 5 − x)

Think: Start with x, then take away 5.

"7 less than a number" → x − 7

"Subtracted From" — Reversed!

5 subtracted from x means x − 5

The number after "from" comes first.

"3 subtracted from 10" → 10 − 3

"Of" Usually Means Multiply

half of x means x/2 or 0.5x
25% of x means 0.25x

"one-third of a number" → x/3

"More Than" vs "Is More Than"

5 more than x = x + 5 (addition)

x is more than 5 = x > 5 (inequality)

📋 Step-by-Step Translation Strategy

  1. Read carefully — Read the entire problem first to understand the context
  2. Identify the unknown — What are you solving for? Assign it a variable (x)
  3. Find key words — Circle or underline operation words (sum, times, less than, etc.)
  4. Translate piece by piece — Convert each phrase to math, one at a time
  5. Write the expression/equation — Put the pieces together
  6. Check your work — Read it back: does it match the original problem?
💡

Common TEAS Patterns

Consecutive Numbers

Consecutive integers: x, x+1, x+2
Consecutive even/odd: x, x+2, x+4

Age Problems

"In 5 years" → add 5 to current age
"5 years ago" → subtract 5 from current age

Percent Problems

"x% of a number" → (x/100) × n
"increased by 20%" → n + 0.20n = 1.20n

Rate Problems

"per" usually means division
"miles per hour" → miles ÷ hours

💊

Healthcare Application

In nursing, you'll constantly translate word problems: "A patient needs 500 mg of medication. Each tablet contains 125 mg. How many tablets are needed?" This becomes: 125x = 500, where x is the number of tablets. Mastering translation is essential for safe medication administration!

Example 1: Basic Expression

Expression

Translate into a mathematical expression:

"Eight more than three times a number"

✅ Solution:

1
Identify the unknown: "a number" → Let this be x
2
Find key words: "three times" means multiply by 3
3 × x = 3x
3
"Eight more than" means add 8 to the result
3x + 8
Answer: 3x + 8

Example 2: "Less Than" (Tricky!)

Expression

Translate into a mathematical expression:

"Seven less than twice a number"

✅ Solution:

1
Identify the unknown: "a number" → x
2
"twice a number" means 2 times x
2x
3
"Seven less than" means subtract 7 FROM the previous result
2x − 7

⚠️ Note: "less than" reverses the order — 7 comes AFTER the subtraction sign!
Answer: 2x − 7

Example 3: Writing an Equation

Equation

Translate into an equation:

"The sum of a number and twelve is twenty-five"

✅ Solution:

1
Identify the unknown: "a number" → x
2
"The sum of a number and twelve" means add x and 12
x + 12
3
"is" means equals (=)
4
Put it together:
x + 12 = 25
Answer: x + 12 = 25

Example 4: Two-Step Equation

Equation

Translate into an equation:

"Four times a number, decreased by nine, equals thirty-one"

✅ Solution:

1
"a number" → x
2
"Four times a number"
4x
3
"decreased by nine" means subtract 9
4x − 9
4
"equals thirty-one"
4x − 9 = 31
Answer: 4x − 9 = 31

Example 5: Healthcare Word Problem

Healthcare

Translate into an equation:

"A nurse needs to administer 750 mg of medication. If each tablet contains 250 mg, how many tablets are needed?"

✅ Solution:

1
Identify the unknown: "how many tablets" → Let x = number of tablets
2
Each tablet has 250 mg, so x tablets have:
250x mg
3
This must equal the total needed (750 mg):
250x = 750
Answer: 250x = 750 (x = 3 tablets)

Example 6: Multi-Part Problem

Multi-Step

Translate into an equation:

"The quotient of a number and five, increased by three, is equal to twice the number"

✅ Solution:

1
"a number" → x
2
"The quotient of a number and five" means divide x by 5
x/5 or x ÷ 5
3
"increased by three" means add 3
x/5 + 3
4
"is equal to" means =
5
"twice the number" means 2x
x/5 + 3 = 2x
Answer: x/5 + 3 = 2x
