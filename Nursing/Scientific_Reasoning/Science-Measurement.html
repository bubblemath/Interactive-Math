🔢 Metric Prefixes

  • kilo- means 1,000×. So 1 kilogram (kg) = 1,000 grams (g); 1 kilometer = 1,000 m.
  • centi- means 1/100. So 1 centimeter (cm) = 0.01 m (100 cm in a meter).
  • milli- means 1/1,000. So 1 milligram (mg) = 0.001 g; 1 milliliter (mL) = 0.001 L.
  • Same prefixes, every unit: to go from a bigger unit to a smaller one you multiply; smaller to bigger you divide.

🩺 Units in Healthcare

  • Medication doses: liquids in milliliters (mL); drug amounts in milligrams (mg).
  • Body weight: in kilograms (kg). Body temperature: in °C (normal ≈ 37 °C).
  • Reading a liquid’s volume: at eye level, read the bottom of the meniscus (the curved surface).

🌟 High-Yield TEAS Facts

  • Length → ruler → meters; Mass → balance → grams; Volume → graduated cylinder → liters.
  • Temperature → thermometer → °C; Time → stopwatch → seconds.
  • kilo = 1,000; centi = 1/100; milli = 1/1,000.
  • 1 kg = 1,000 g · 1 g = 1,000 mg · 1 L = 1,000 mL · 1 m = 100 cm.
  • Read a liquid’s volume at the bottom of the meniscus.

✏️ Example 1: Pick the right tool and unit

📝 A nurse needs to measure 10 mL of a liquid medication. What tool and unit fit?

  1. “10 mL” is a volume of liquid (mL = milliliters).
  2. Volume of a liquid is measured with a graduated cylinder (or a marked syringe/medicine cup).
  3. A ruler measures length, a balance measures mass — neither fits a liquid volume.

Answer: a graduated cylinder (or marked syringe), reading in milliliters (mL).

🔄 Example 2: A metric conversion

📝 A drug label reads 2 grams, but the order is in milligrams. How many milligrams is 2 g?

  1. milli- means one-thousandth, so 1 g = 1,000 mg.
  2. Going from a bigger unit (g) to a smaller one (mg), you multiply.
  3. 2 g × 1,000 = 2,000 mg.

Answer: 2 g = 2,000 mg. (And in reverse, 500 mg = 500 ÷ 1,000 = 0.5 g.)

📏 Example 3: Choose a sensible unit

📝 Which unit best fits a newborn’s length: kilometers, centimeters, or milligrams?

  1. Length rules out milligrams (that’s mass).
  2. Kilometers measure long distances between towns — far too big for a baby.
  3. Centimeters are the right scale for something about 50 cm long.

Answer: centimeters (cm). Matching the unit to the size of the thing is a key measurement skill.
