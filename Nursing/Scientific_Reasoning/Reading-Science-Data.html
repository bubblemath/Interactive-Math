📋 The Core Ideas

🧭 A Four-Step Way to Read Any Graph

  • 1. Read the title — what is this graph about?
  • 2. Read both axes — what does each one measure, and in what units?
  • 3. Find the value — go up from the x-axis and across from the y-axis to meet the data.
  • 4. Spot the trend — is the data going up, going down, or staying flat overall?

📉 Describing a Trend

  • Increasing: the line rises from left to right (values going up).
  • Decreasing: the line falls from left to right (values going down).
  • Constant / plateau: the line stays flat (values not changing).
  • A steeper line means a faster change.

🌟 High-Yield TEAS Facts

  • Always read the title and axis labels first — including the units.
  • The x-axis is horizontal (often time); the y-axis is vertical (what’s measured).
  • To read a point: go up from the x-value, then across to the y-value.
  • Up = increasing, down = decreasing, flat = no change. Steeper = faster.
  • A legend (key) tells you what each line or color means.

✏️ Example 1: Read a value from a line graph

📝 Using the heart-rate graph above, what was the heart rate at 6 minutes?

  1. Find 6 on the x-axis (Time).
  2. Go straight up until you hit the line.
  3. Go straight across to the y-axis (Heart rate): about 138 bpm.

Answer: ≈138 bpm. Up from the x-value, across to the y-value — that’s how you read any point.

📊 Example 2: Compare bars in a bar chart

📝 On which day were the most glasses of water recorded, and how many more than Wednesday?

  1. The tallest bar is Thursday (9 glasses).
  2. Wednesday is the shortest at 5 glasses.
  3. 9 − 5 = 4.

Answer: Thursday had the most, 4 more than Wednesday. Bar charts are all about comparing heights.

🌡️ Example 3: Read a trend from a table

📝 What is happening to this patient’s temperature, and how much did it change from Day 1 to Day 4?

  1. Reading down the column, the temperature drops each day — a decreasing trend.
  2. Day 1 is 38.5 °C; Day 4 is 37.0 °C.
  3. 38.5 − 37.0 = 1.5 °C.

Answer: the fever is going down — a drop of 1.5 °C over three days. A falling trend here is good news.
