🌡️ The Three States of Matter

The same substance can exist as a solid, a liquid, or a gas — think ice, water, and steam. What changes isn’t the molecules themselves but how much energy they have and how they’re arranged. Add or remove heat and a substance moves between states through a phase change. Click around the diagram to see each state and each change.

🌡️ Temperature Stays Put During a Change

  • During a phase change, the temperature does not change. All the added (or removed) heat goes into breaking or forming the bonds between particles, not changing the temperature.
  • That’s why a heating curve has flat plateaus at the melting point and the boiling point.
  • The melting point and freezing point are the same temperature (0°C for water) — one going up, one coming down.

🌟 High-Yield TEAS Facts

  • Solid → Liquid → Gas = adding energy (particles move more, spread out).
  • Melting, vaporization, sublimation absorb heat (endothermic); freezing, condensation, deposition release heat (exothermic).
  • Temperature stays constant during a phase change — the flat parts of a heating curve.
  • Sublimation = solid straight to gas (dry ice); deposition = gas straight to solid (frost).
  • Evaporation absorbs heat — that’s how sweating cools the body.

✏️ Example 1: Read the flat part of the curve

📝 On a heating curve, the temperature sits flat at 0°C for a while. What’s happening?

  1. A flat part (plateau) means the temperature is not changing even though heat is being added.
  2. At 0°C, that means the ice is melting into water.
  3. The heat isn’t raising the temperature — it’s breaking the bonds that held the solid together.

Answer: the ice is melting. Temperature stays constant during any phase change.

💧 Example 2: Name the change

📝 Steam touches a cold mirror and turns into water droplets. What phase change is this?

  1. Identify the states: it goes from gas (steam) to liquid (droplets).
  2. Gas → liquid is condensation.
  3. Going from gas to liquid releases heat (the particles slow down and come together).

Answer: condensation. It’s the same reason droplets form on a cold drink on a humid day.

💦 Example 3: Endothermic or exothermic?

📝 Sweat evaporating from your skin — does it absorb or release heat, and why does that matter?

  1. Evaporation is vaporization (liquid → gas).
  2. Vaporization absorbs heat — it’s endothermic.
  3. The heat it absorbs comes from your skin, so your body cools down.

Answer: it absorbs heat, which cools the body. This is exactly how sweating helps regulate body temperature.
