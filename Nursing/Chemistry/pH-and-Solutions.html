🧪 Solutions, Acids & Bases

When you dissolve something in water you get a solution. Some solutions are acids (like stomach acid or lemon juice) and some are bases (like baking soda or bleach). The pH scale tells you which is which, running from 0 to 14. Slide along the scale below and tap a few substances to see where they land.

📋 The Core Ideas

💧 Parts of a Solution

  • A solution is a mixture where one substance is fully dissolved in another.
  • The solute is what gets dissolved (like salt or sugar). The solvent is what does the dissolving (usually water).
  • Water dissolves so many things it’s called the universal solvent. A solution with lots of solute is concentrated; with little, it’s dilute.

🔢 Reading the pH Scale

  • The scale runs 0 to 14. 7 is neutral (pure water).
  • Below 7 = acidic (the lower, the stronger the acid). Above 7 = basic (the higher, the stronger the base).
  • Each whole step is a 10× change. A pH of 3 is 10 times more acidic than 4, and 100 times more than 5.

🩺 pH in the Body

  • Blood pH is about 7.4 — slightly basic — and the body holds it in a tight 7.35–7.45 range. Drifting outside is dangerous (acidosis or alkalosis).
  • Stomach acid is very acidic (pH ~1.5–2) to digest food and kill germs.
  • An acid + a base react in a neutralization reaction → a salt + water. That’s how an antacid calms stomach acid.

🌟 High-Yield TEAS Facts

  • pH 0–14; 7 is neutral. Below 7 = acid, above 7 = base.
  • Lower pH = more acidic. Each step is a 10× change.
  • Acids are sour and release H⁺; bases are bitter/slippery and release OH⁻.
  • Blood ≈ 7.4 (slightly basic, tightly controlled); stomach acid ≈ 1.5–2.
  • Acid + base → salt + water (neutralization).
  • Solute dissolves in a solvent to make a solution; water is the universal solvent.

✏️ Example 1: Compare two acids

📝 Lemon juice has a pH of 2 and coffee has a pH of 5. How much more acidic is lemon juice?

  1. Both are below 7, so both are acidic — lemon juice (2) is the stronger one.
  2. Each step on the scale is a 10× change. From pH 5 to pH 2 is 3 steps down.
  3. 10 × 10 × 10 = 1,000.

Answer: lemon juice is about 1,000 times more acidic than coffee. Small pH differences mean big changes.

🩺 Example 2: Is blood acidic or basic?

📝 Normal blood pH is about 7.4. Acidic, neutral, or basic — and why does the range matter?

  1. 7.4 is just above 7, so blood is slightly basic.
  2. The body keeps it in a very tight band: 7.35–7.45.
  3. Drifting below is acidosis; above is alkalosis — both are medical emergencies.

Answer: slightly basic. Because the safe range is so narrow, blood pH is one of the most tightly controlled values in the body.

⚗️ Example 3: Neutralizing an acid

📝 What happens when an antacid (a base) meets stomach acid?

  1. An acid and a base react together in a neutralization reaction.
  2. The products are a salt and water (for example, HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O).
  3. The pH moves toward 7, so the acid is no longer harsh.

Answer: the base neutralizes the acid, forming salt and water and easing the burn — exactly how antacids relieve heartburn.
