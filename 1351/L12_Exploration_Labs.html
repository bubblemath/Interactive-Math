Nets & Surface Area Reference

See how 2D nets unfold from 3D solids — and how face areas sum to total surface area

📐 What is a Net?

A net is a 2D pattern that can be folded to form a 3D solid. Every face of the solid appears as a flat shape in the net. To find surface area, calculate the area of each face in the net and add them together.

3D Solid Net (Unfolded) Name Surface Area Formula
Cube6 identical square faces
\(SA = 6s^2\)
where \(s\) = side length
Rectangular
Prism3 pairs of rectangular faces
\(SA = 2LW + 2LH + 2WH\)
where \(L\) = length, \(W\) = width, \(H\) = height
Cylinder2 circles + 1 rectangle
(width = \(2\pi r\))
\(SA = 2\pi r^2 + 2\pi rh\)
where \(r\) = radius, \(h\) = height
Square
Pyramid1 square base + 4 triangles
\(SA = B + \frac{1}{2}P\ell\)
where \(B\) = base area, \(P\) = perimeter, \(\ell\) = slant height
Cone1 circle + 1 sector
\(SA = \pi r^2 + \pi r\ell\)
where \(r\) = radius, \(\ell\) = slant height
No net possible
(curved surface cannot
be flattened)		 SphereAll curved surface
\(SA = 4\pi r^2\)
where \(r\) = radius

Teaching Tip

Have students trace each face on the 3D solid and find it in the net. Ask: "Which face in the net corresponds to the bottom of the solid? The sides?" This builds spatial reasoning. For the cylinder, emphasize that the rectangle's width equals the circumference \(2\pi r\) — this is the key insight students often miss.

🧠 Key Concepts to Remember

Lateral Surface AreaSum of side faces only (no bases)
Total Surface AreaLateral area + area of all bases
UnitsAlways in square units (cm², m², in², ft²)
Slant Height (\(\ell\))≠ vertical height — measured along the face
SphereCannot be unfolded into a flat net

Have students find where \(°F = °C\) by dragging the slider. When they discover \(-40°\), connect it to the algebraic proof. Kelvin can never be negative — absolute zero means no molecular motion.
