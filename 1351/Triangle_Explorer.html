Classifying Triangles by Sides AND Angles

📐

Every triangle has TWO classifications—one by its sides and one by its angles! A triangle can be both "Isosceles" AND "Right" at the same time.

📚 Triangle Classifications

📏 By Sides

Scalene

No equal sides

Isosceles

At least 2 equal sides

Equilateral

All 3 sides equal

📐 By Angles

Acute

All angles < 90°

Right

Exactly one 90° angle

Obtuse

Exactly one angle > 90°

🔑 Important Notes

✅ Isosceles Includes Equilateral

An equilateral triangle has "at least 2 equal sides" (it has 3!), so every equilateral triangle is also isosceles.

📐 Angle Sum = 180°

The sum of all interior angles in ANY triangle is always exactly 180°. This limits which combinations are possible!

🔄 Dual Classification

Every triangle gets TWO labels: one for sides (scalene/isosceles/equilateral) and one for angles (acute/right/obtuse).

🎓 Educator's Lab

Teaching strategies and common misconceptions for future teachers.

🧠 Van Hiele Connection

Level 1 → Level 2 Transition:

Students move from analyzing properties (Level 1: "This triangle has two equal sides") to understanding class inclusion (Level 2: "All equilateral triangles are also isosceles").

The dual classification system helps students see that shapes can belong to multiple categories simultaneously.

⚠️ Common Misconception

"Isosceles means EXACTLY two equal sides"

Many students (and some textbooks!) incorrectly define isosceles as having exactly two equal sides.

The Correct Definition:

Isosceles means at least two equal sides. This means every equilateral triangle is also isosceles!
