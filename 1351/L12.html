Surface Area, Volume, Mass & Temperature

Math 1351 · Fundamentals of Mathematics II

🎯 Lesson Overview

This lesson bridges 2D measurement (Lesson 11) into the third dimension. Students explore the "skin" of 3D solids (surface area), the "filling" inside them (volume), and extend measurement to mass and temperature. Future educators will develop an arsenal of techniques — from physical net-folding to water-pouring experiments — to help their students visualize why formulas work, not just memorize them.

📝 Key Formulas at a Glance

Cube
SA = 6s² | V = s³
Rectangular Prism
SA = 2(LW+LH+WH) | V = LWH
Cylinder
SA = 2πr²+2πrh | V = πr²h
Pyramid
SA = B+½Pℓ | V = ⅓Bh
Cone
SA = πr²+πrℓ | V = ⅓πr²h
Sphere
SA = 4πr² | V = ⁴⁄₃πr³
°F ↔ °C
°F = ⁹⁄₅C+32 | °C = ⁵⁄₉(F−32)
°C ↔ K
K = C + 273.15

🧠 Van Hiele Levels in This Lesson

Level 0 — Visualization

Identifying solids by sight, estimating mass by feel, sensing temperature

Level 1 — Analysis

Applying SA/Volume formulas, performing conversions, calculating composite solids

Level 2 — Informal Deduction

Deriving formulas from nets, discovering the ⅓ relationship, connecting 2D to 3D

Level 3 — Formal Deduction

Proving the k²/k³ scaling relationship, optimization problems, Cavalieri's Principle
