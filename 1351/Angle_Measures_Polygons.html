🔶 Lesson 6

Angle Measures in Polygons

Interior and Exterior Angle Relationships

📐

The sum of interior angles grows as we add sides: (n-2) × 180°. But the sum of exterior angles—one at each vertex—is ALWAYS 360° regardless of the polygon!

📚 Essential Angle Formulas

Interior Angle Sum

(n - 2) × 180°

The sum of ALL interior angles in any n-sided polygon. Works for regular AND irregular polygons!

Each Interior Angle (Regular)

\(\frac{(n-2) \times 180°}{n}\)

The measure of ONE interior angle in a REGULAR polygon (all angles equal).

Exterior Angle Sum

360°

ALWAYS 360° for any convex polygon—no matter how many sides! This is the "total turn" around the shape.

Each Exterior Angle (Regular)

\(\frac{360°}{n}\)

The measure of ONE exterior angle in a REGULAR polygon.

🔗 The Supplementary Relationship

At any vertex:

Interior Angle + Exterior Angle = 180°

This is because they form a linear pair (straight line)!

💡 Why is it always 360°?

Imagine walking around the polygon. At each vertex, you turn by the exterior angle. After completing one full lap, you've turned in a complete circle—exactly 360°! This is true no matter how many sides the polygon has.

🎓 Educator's Lab

Teaching strategies for angle measures in polygons.

🧠 Van Hiele Connection

Level 2 (Informal Deduction): Students use properties and formulas to DEDUCE angle measures without measuring every time.

The triangulation method helps students understand WHY the formula works, not just memorize it.

Key Insight:

Students who understand triangulation can derive the formula themselves—they don't need to memorize it!

⚠️ Common Misconception

"The exterior angle sum increases as the polygon gets larger."

Students often think more sides = more exterior angles = larger sum.

Fix: Use the "Shrink" animation to show that all exterior angles always combine to form exactly one complete rotation (360°).

💡 Teaching Tip

Build from familiar shapes:

  • Start with triangle (180°)—students know this!
  • Then quadrilateral (360°)—two triangles
  • Then pentagon (540°)—three triangles
  • NOW introduce the (n-2) × 180° formula

This builds conceptual understanding before the formula.

🗣️ "Explain to a 3rd Grader"

Exterior Angles:

"If you walk all the way around a park and end up facing the same way you started, you've turned in one big circle—that's 360°!"

Interior Angles:

"We can cut any shape into triangles. Each triangle has 180° inside. Count the triangles and multiply!"
