Transformations Lab

Translations, Rotations, Reflections & Dilations

🔄

Transformations are operations that move or change figures in the plane. Rigid motions (translations, rotations, reflections) preserve size and shape, while dilations change size but preserve shape. Understanding what properties each transformation preserves is key to Van Hiele Level 2 thinking!

📚 Key Concepts

Master the four fundamental transformations and understand what each preserves!

➡️ Translation (Slide)

Moves every point of a figure the same distance in the same direction. Think of it as "sliding" without turning.

\((x, y) \rightarrow (x + a, y + b)\)
Preserves: Size, shape, orientation, angle measures, side lengths

🔄 Rotation (Turn)

Turns a figure around a fixed point (center of rotation) by a specified angle. Positive angles rotate counterclockwise.

90° CCW: \((x, y) \rightarrow (-y, x)\)
Preserves: Size, shape, angle measures, side lengths

🪞 Reflection (Flip)

Flips a figure over a line (line of reflection), creating a mirror image. Each point is the same distance from the line on opposite sides.

Over y-axis: \((x, y) \rightarrow (-x, y)\)
Preserves: Size, shape, angle measures, side lengths
Reverses orientation!

🔍 Dilation (Scale)

Enlarges or shrinks a figure by a scale factor from a center point. k > 1 enlarges, 0 < k < 1 shrinks.

\((x, y) \rightarrow (kx, ky)\) from origin
Preserves: Shape, angle measures
Changes size!

🔑 Key Vocabulary

Rigid Motion (Isometry): A transformation that preserves distance and angle measures. Translations, rotations, and reflections are rigid motions.
Pre-image: The original figure before transformation.
Image: The resulting figure after transformation (often labeled with prime notation: A → A').
Orientation: The "handedness" of a figure—whether vertices go clockwise or counterclockwise.

🎓 Educator's Lab

Teaching strategies, common misconceptions, and Van Hiele connections for transformations.

📊 Van Hiele Connections

Level 1 (Analysis): Students recognize and name transformations—"That's a rotation!"

Level 2 (Informal Deduction): Students understand properties preserved—"Reflections reverse orientation, but rotations don't."

💡 Progression Activity

Have students predict which properties will be preserved BEFORE applying a transformation, then verify. This builds Level 2 reasoning!

⚠️ Common Misconceptions

"Rotation changes the shape"

Students may think a rotated shape is different. Use physical manipulatives to show it's the same shape, just turned.

"Reflection = Rotation by 180°"

These are different! Reflection reverses orientation; 180° rotation does not. Use the L-shape to demonstrate.

"Dilation changes the shape"

Dilation changes SIZE but preserves SHAPE. The angles stay the same!

🛠️ Teaching Strategies

  • Patty Paper: Trace shapes and physically slide, flip, or turn them
  • Miras: Use transparent mirrors for reflections
  • Coordinate Grid: Plot points and apply transformation rules
  • Real-World Examples: Sliding doors (translation), clock hands (rotation), mirror images (reflection), photo zoom (dilation)
💡 Orientation Check

Use an asymmetric shape (like the L-shape or letter F) to make orientation changes visible!

💬 "Explain to a 5th Grader"

Translation: "Imagine sliding a book across your desk without turning it—that's a translation!"

Rotation: "Think of spinning a fidget spinner around your finger—the center stays still while everything else turns."

Reflection: "Look in a mirror—your reflection is flipped! If you raise your right hand, your reflection raises its left."

Dilation: "When you zoom in on a photo, everything gets bigger but keeps the same shape—that's dilation!"

🔗 Composition of Transformations

Key Insight: The order of transformations matters! Rotating then translating gives a different result than translating then rotating.

Glide Reflection: A translation followed by a reflection over a line parallel to the translation vector. This is the fourth type of isometry!

Activity Idea: Have students predict the result of a sequence, then use the Sequence Builder to verify. Discuss why order matters.
