🔑 Key Formulas at a Glance

Rectangle Perimeter
P = 2L + 2W
Circumference
C = 2πr = πd
Triangle Area
A = ½bh
Parallelogram Area
A = bh
Trapezoid Area
A = ½(b₁+b₂)h
Circle Area
A = πr²
Regular Polygon
A = ½ap
Sector Area
A = (θ/360)πr²
Arc Length
L = (θ/360)2πr
Unit Fraction
12 in / 1 ft = 1

🧠 Van Hiele Levels in This Lesson

Level 0 — Visualization

Physically measuring objects, comparing sizes, counting unit squares for area, wrapping string for perimeter.

Level 1 — Analysis

Applying formulas correctly, performing dimensional analysis chains, calculating composite figure areas.

Level 2 — Informal Deduction

Deriving area formulas from simpler ones, explaining why area units are squared, justifying why unit fractions equal 1.

Level 3 — Formal Deduction

Proving circle area via polygon limits, proving scaling laws algebraically, dimensional consistency proofs.
