🔶 Lesson 6

Curves & Polygon Foundations

Building Vocabulary from Simple Curves to Polygons

💡

A polygon is a simple, closed curve with sides that are line segments. Understanding these foundational terms is the Van Hiele Level 0 → Level 1 transition!

📚 Key Vocabulary

Term Definition
Simple Curve A curve that does not cross itself; starting and stopping points may be the same.
Closed Curve A curve that starts and stops at the same point.
Polygon A simple, closed curve with sides that are line segments.
Convex A simple, closed curve with no indentations; any segment connecting two interior points stays inside.
Concave A simple, closed curve that has an indentation; at least one diagonal falls outside the shape.
Vertex A point where two sides of a polygon meet (plural: vertices).
Side A line segment that forms part of the boundary of a polygon.
Diagonal A line segment connecting two non-adjacent vertices of a polygon.

✅ What Makes a Polygon?

A shape is a polygon if and only if it meets ALL THREE criteria:

1️⃣

SIMPLE

Does not cross itself

2️⃣

CLOSED

Starts and ends at same point

3️⃣

STRAIGHT SIDES

All sides are line segments

📖 Understanding the Tests

🔵 The Rubber Band Test

Imagine stretching a rubber band around the shape:

  • Convex: The rubber band touches ALL sides
  • Concave: The rubber band has "gaps" where it doesn't touch

📐 The Diagonal Test

Draw all possible diagonals:

  • Convex: ALL diagonals stay INSIDE the shape
  • Concave: At least ONE diagonal goes OUTSIDE

🎓 Educator's Lab

Teaching strategies and common misconceptions for future teachers.

🧠 Van Hiele Connection

Level 0 → Level 1 Transition:

Students move from "it looks like a box" to "it has four straight sides and is closed."

This lesson builds the vocabulary needed for Level 1 thinking: simple, closed, polygon, convex, concave.

⚠️ Common Misconceptions

Students often reject non-prototypical polygons:

"Too skinny to be a triangle"

"That's a diamond, not a square"

"That's not a real shape"

💬 "Explain to a 3rd Grader"

Practice Prompts:

Simple curve: "A path you can draw without your pencil crossing over a line you already drew."

Closed curve: "A path that ends exactly where it started—like walking around the block and coming home."

Polygon: "A closed shape made only of straight lines that don't cross each other."

🛠️ Manipulative Suggestions

  • Geoboards: Create polygons with rubber bands; impossible to make curved sides!
  • String: Lay string on desk to show simple vs. not simple curves
  • Pattern Blocks: All pieces are convex polygons—discuss why
  • Paper Folding: Fold paper to create concave shapes from convex ones
