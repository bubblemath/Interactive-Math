📚 Unit II: Logic & Symbolic Reasoning

Master the foundations of mathematical logic and argumentation

Liberal Arts Mathematics

📋 Unit Overview

In this unit, you will explore the fundamental principles of symbolic logic. You'll learn to translate everyday language into logical symbols, construct truth tables, identify equivalent statements using De Morgan's Laws, and evaluate the validity of arguments. These skills form the foundation of mathematical reasoning and critical thinking.

Logic

Introduction to Logical Statements & Quantifiers

Begin your journey into symbolic logic by learning to identify statements, understand quantifiers (all, some, none), and translate between English and symbolic notation.

  • Distinguish statements from non-statements
  • Work with universal and existential quantifiers
  • Negate quantified statements correctly
  • Use Venn diagrams to visualize logical relationships

Compound Statements

Conjunctions, Disjunctions & Negations

Learn to combine simple statements using logical connectives to form compound statements. Master the symbols and meanings of AND (∧), OR (∨), NOT (~), IF-THEN (→), and IF AND ONLY IF (↔).

  • Translate English sentences into symbolic form
  • Understand inclusive vs. exclusive OR
  • Write negations of compound statements
  • Identify the components of conditional statements

Truth Tables

Constructing & Analyzing Logical Truth Values

Master the construction of truth tables to systematically evaluate compound statements. Learn to determine when statements are tautologies, self-contradictions, or contingencies.

  • Build truth tables for 2, 3, and 4 variables
  • Evaluate compound statements step by step
  • Identify tautologies (always true) and contradictions (always false)
  • Understand the truth values of conditionals and biconditionals

9

Equivalent Statements

De Morgan's Laws & Conditional Variations

Discover how to identify logically equivalent statements using truth tables and logical laws. Master De Morgan's Laws and understand the relationships between conditionals and their variations.

  • Apply De Morgan's Laws: ~(p ∧ q) ≡ ~p ∨ ~q
  • Find the converse, inverse, and contrapositive
  • Prove equivalence using truth tables
  • Rewrite conditionals as disjunctions: p → q ≡ ~p ∨ q

Arguments

Validity, Fallacies & Logical Reasoning

Learn to analyze arguments for validity using symbolic logic and truth tables. Recognize common valid argument forms and identify logical fallacies that lead to invalid conclusions.

  • Distinguish valid from invalid arguments
  • Apply Modus Ponens, Modus Tollens, and Disjunctive Syllogism
  • Identify the Fallacy of the Converse and Fallacy of the Inverse
  • Test argument validity using truth tables
