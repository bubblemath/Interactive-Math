🔄 Rounding Rules

Steps to Round:

  1. Mark the place you are rounding to. This is called the rounding digit.
  2. Locate the digit to the right of the rounding digit. This is called the test digit.
  3. Apply the rule:
    • If the test digit is ≥ 5, add 1 to the rounding digit and replace all digits to the right with zeros.
    • If the test digit is < 5, keep the rounding digit the same and replace all digits to the right with zeros.

💡 Memory Tip: "5 or more, raise the score. 4 or less, let it rest!"

📊 Types of Graphs

Circle Graph (Pie Chart): Shows parts of a whole as percentages. Great for comparing proportions.

Bar Graph: Uses bars to compare quantities across categories. Easy to identify highest/lowest values.

Line Graph: Shows change over time. Perfect for identifying trends and calculating rates of change.

📈 Linear Model: When data shows a constant rate of change, we can write an equation: \(y = mx + b\) where \(m\) is the rate of change (slope) and \(b\) is the starting value.
