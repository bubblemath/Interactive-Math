📚 Key Concepts

📝 What is a Statement?

A statement is a sentence that is either TRUE or FALSE, but not both simultaneously.

We use lowercase letters p, q, r, s to represent statements symbolically.

✓ Statements✗ NOT Statements
The Astros won the World Series in 2017.Michael Jordan was the best basketball player. (Opinion)
Houston is located in Oklahoma.Is it raining? (Question)
2 + 2 = 5Think before you speak. (Command)
The light bulb needs replacing.Fire! (Exclamation)

🔄 Negations

Negating a statement changes its truth value.

  • Negating a TRUE statement makes it FALSE
  • Negating a FALSE statement makes it TRUE

The notation for negating statement p is ~p (read "not p" or "It is not true that p")

Statement (p)Negation (~p)
The Astros won the World Series in 2017.The Astros did NOT win the World Series in 2017.
Houston is not located in Texas.Houston IS located in Texas.
The light bulb needs replacing.The light bulb does NOT need replacing.

🎯 Quantifiers

The words all, some, and no (or none) are called quantifiers.

PropositionMeaningEquivalent
All A are BEvery single A is a B"There are no A that are not B"
No A are BNot a single A is a B"All A are not B"
Some A are BAt least one A is a B"There exists at least one A that is a B"
Some A are not BAt least one A is not a B"Not all A are B"

⚠️ Common Mistake: Negating "All" Statements

The negation of "All A are B" is NOT "No A are B"!

The correct negation is: "Some A are not B"

Example: The negation of "All birds can fly" is "Some birds cannot fly" (NOT "No birds can fly")

⚠️ Watch Out for Double Negatives!

When a statement already contains "not," negating it removes the "not."

Example:

  • p: "Houston is not located in Texas."
  • ~p: "Houston is located in Texas." (The "not" is removed!)

💡 Memory Tip

Notice the pattern:

  • "All" pairs with "Some...not"
  • "No" pairs with "Some"

Universal statements (All/No) are negated by particular statements (Some/Some...not), and vice versa!
