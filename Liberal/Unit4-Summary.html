Multiplication Rule & Permutations

Counting When Order Matters

Learn the Fundamental Counting Principle and how to count arrangements where the order of selection matters. Permutations are used when arranging people in a line, assigning positions, or creating ordered sequences.

Permutation Formula:
P(n, r) = n! / (n - r)!
n = total items, r = items being arranged
  • Apply the Fundamental Counting Principle
  • Calculate factorials (n!)
  • Use permutations for ordered arrangements
  • Solve problems involving positions and rankings
💡 Remember: Use permutations when the ORDER matters (1st, 2nd, 3rd place are different!)

Combinations

Counting When Order Doesn't Matter

Master counting techniques for selecting groups where the order of selection does not matter. Combinations are used when forming committees, choosing teams, or selecting items from a set.

Combination Formula:
C(n, r) = n! / [r!(n - r)!]
n = total items, r = items being chosen
  • Distinguish between permutations and combinations
  • Calculate combinations for group selection
  • Solve committee and team formation problems
  • Apply combinations to card and lottery problems
💡 Remember: Use combinations when ORDER doesn't matter (a committee of A, B, C is the same as C, B, A)

Probability with Combinations & Permutations

Applying Counting to Likelihood

Combine your counting skills with probability concepts to calculate the likelihood of specific outcomes. Learn to determine probabilities in complex scenarios involving cards, dice, committees, and more.

Probability Formula:
P(Event) = (Number of favorable outcomes) / (Total number of outcomes)
Use permutations or combinations to count outcomes
  • Calculate probability using counting techniques
  • Determine favorable vs. total outcomes
  • Solve card probability problems (poker hands, etc.)
  • Apply to real-world selection scenarios

Probability Operations

Union, Intersection & Complement Rules

Learn the fundamental rules for combining probabilities. Master the Addition Rule for "or" scenarios, the Multiplication Rule for "and" scenarios, and the Complement Rule for "not" scenarios.

Key Probability Rules:
Addition: P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A and B)
Multiplication: P(A and B) = P(A) × P(B|A)
Complement: P(not A) = 1 - P(A)
  • Apply the Addition Rule (union of events)
  • Use the Multiplication Rule (intersection of events)
  • Calculate complements efficiently
  • Distinguish between independent and dependent events

Mean & Standard Deviation

Measures of Center & Spread

Calculate the two most important descriptive statistics: the mean (average) tells you the center of your data, while the standard deviation tells you how spread out the data is around that center.

Key Formulas:
Mean: x̄ = Σx / n
Standard Deviation: s = √[Σ(x - x̄)² / (n - 1)]
  • Calculate the mean (arithmetic average)
  • Find deviations from the mean
  • Compute variance and standard deviation
  • Interpret what standard deviation tells us about data
  • Apply the Empirical Rule (68-95-99.7)
💡 Interpretation: A small standard deviation means data points are close to the mean; a large standard deviation means data is more spread out.
