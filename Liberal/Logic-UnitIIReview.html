Conditional Variations

Original: p → q

Converse: q → p

Inverse: ~p → ~q

Contrapositive: ~q → ~p

⚠️ Common Mistake

Only the contrapositive is logically equivalent to the original!

De Morgan's Laws

~(p ∧ q) ≡ ~p ∨ ~q

~(p ∨ q) ≡ ~p ∧ ~q

"Break the bar, change the sign"

Valid Argument Forms

Modus Ponens (Law of Detachment):

p → q, p ∴ q

Modus Tollens:

p → q, ~q ∴ ~p

Disjunctive Syllogism:

p ∨ q, ~p ∴ q

Common Fallacies

Fallacy of the Converse:

p → q, q ∴ p ✗

Fallacy of the Inverse:

p → q, ~p ∴ ~q ✗

⚠️ Remember

Affirming the consequent or denying the antecedent = INVALID!

Statement Types

Tautology: Always TRUE

Self-Contradiction: Always FALSE

Contingency: Sometimes T, sometimes F

Check the final column of the truth table!

Equivalence Test

Two statements are logically equivalent if they have identical truth tables.

Symbol: ≡

Key Equivalences:

p → q ≡ ~p ∨ q

p ↔ q ≡ (p→q) ∧ (q→p)
