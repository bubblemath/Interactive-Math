Question 1: Calculate Total Cost with Sales Tax

A laptop costs $850. The sales tax rate is 7%. What is the total cost including tax?

Hint: Tax Amount = Tax Rate × Original Price
Total Cost = Original Price + Tax Amount

Question 2: Calculate Discount and Sale Price

A jacket originally costs $120. It is on sale for 25% off. What is the discount amount and the sale price?

Hint: Discount Amount = Discount Rate × Original Price
Sale Price = Original Price - Discount Amount

Question 3: Discount AND Sales Tax

A TV costs $600. It has a 15% discount. After the discount, you must pay 6% sales tax. What is the final total cost?

Hint: Apply discount first, then calculate tax on the discounted price!
1. Find discount amount and sale price
2. Calculate tax on the sale price
3. Add tax to sale price for final total

Question 4: Work Backwards from Total Cost

You paid a total of $428 for a phone, which includes 7% sales tax. What was the original price of the phone before tax?

Hint: If the original price is P, then:
Total = P + (0.07 × P) = P × 1.07
So: P = Total ÷ 1.07

Instructions: Calculate percent increase/decrease or find missing values. Remember: Percent Change = (Amount of Change ÷ Original Amount) × 100

Question 5: Calculate Percent Increase

A stock was worth $45 in January. In June, it is worth $63. What is the percent increase?

Hint: Amount of Change = New Value - Original Value
Percent Change = (Amount of Change ÷ Original Value) × 100

Question 6: Calculate Percent Decrease

A sofa regularly sells for $840. The sale price is $714. Find the percent decrease.

Hint: For decrease: Amount of Change = Original - New
Percent Decrease = (Amount of Change ÷ Original Value) × 100

Question 7: Find New Value After Percent Increase

A population of 12,000 people increased by 15%. What is the new population?

Hint: Amount of Increase = Original × Percent (as decimal)
New Value = Original + Amount of Increase

Question 8: Find Original Value Before Percent Decrease

After a 20% decrease, a car is now worth $16,000. What was the original value of the car?

Hint: If original is P, after 20% decrease:
New Value = P - (0.20 × P) = P × 0.80
So: P = New Value ÷ 0.80
