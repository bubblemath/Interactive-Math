📚 Quick Reference Guide

🎯 The Master Framework for Percent Problems

Percent × Whole = Part

Use this formula for ALL percent problems!

"OF" → Multiply (×)
"IS" → Equals (=)
"WHAT" → Variable (x)

📐 Ratios

Comparison of two quantities
a : b or a/b or "a to b"
  • Must have the same units
  • Values must be positive numbers
  • Order matters! 3:2 ≠ 2:3
Example: 6 cups flour to 24 cookies
Ratio: 6:24 = 1:4 (simplified)

⚡ Rates & Unit Rates

Rate: Comparison with different units
Unit Rate: Denominator = 1
  • 30 mph = 30 miles per 1 hour
  • $2.50/lb = $2.50 per 1 pound
Example: $2160 for 12 weeks
Unit rate: $2160 ÷ 12 = $180/week

⚖️ Proportions

Two equal ratios: \(\frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d}\)

Cross-multiply: a × d = b × c
Example: If 5 oranges cost $1.15, what do 15 cost?
\(\frac{5}{1.15} = \frac{15}{x}\)
5x = 17.25 → x = $3.45

💯 Percents

"Per 100" or "Out of 100"

25% = 0.25 = 1/4
  • % → Decimal: Move decimal 2 left
  • Decimal → %: Move decimal 2 right
Conversions:
45% = 0.45 = 45/100 = 9/20

🧮 Three Types of Percent Problems

1. Find the Part:
"What is 25% of 80?" → 0.25 × 80 = 20

2. Find the Percent:
"5 is what % of 20?" → 5 ÷ 20 = 0.25 = 25%

3. Find the Whole:
"12 is 30% of what?" → 12 ÷ 0.30 = 40

🗺️ Map Scale Problems

Set up a proportion with matching units
Example: Scale: 1.2 cm = 2 miles
If map shows 3.6 cm, actual distance?
\(\frac{1.2}{2} = \frac{3.6}{x}\)
1.2x = 7.2 → x = 6 miles

⚠️ Common Mistakes: (1) Forgetting to convert percent to decimal before multiplying. (2) Setting up proportions with mismatched units. (3) Confusing "of" (multiply) with "is" (equals).
