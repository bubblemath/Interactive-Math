Hard Level Problems - Solve for Different Variables

🔥 Challenge: These problems require you to solve for Principal (P), Rate (r), or Time (t) instead of Interest and Total Amount!
HARD
Question 1 - Solve for Principal (P)
An investment earns $945 in simple interest at a rate of 3.5% per year over 6 years. What was the original principal amount invested?

Hint: Rearrange I = P × r × t to solve for P
HARD
Question 2 - Solve for Interest Rate (r)
A principal of $8,000 grows to $10,400 after 5 years with simple interest. What was the annual interest rate?

Hint: First find the interest earned (A - P), then rearrange I = P × r × t to solve for r
HARD
Question 3 - Solve for Time (t)
An investment of $12,000 at 4.25% simple interest per year earns $3,060 in interest. How many years was the money invested?

Hint: Rearrange I = P × r × t to solve for t
HARD
Question 4 - Solve for Principal (P)
After 8 years, an account with 5.5% simple interest per year has a total value of $18,840. What was the original principal deposited?

Hint: Use A = P + I and I = P × r × t. Substitute to get A = P + (P × r × t), then solve for P
