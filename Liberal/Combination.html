🎯 Combinations vs Permutations

Learn when order matters and when it doesn't!

The Big Question: Does Order Matter?

🔑 The Key Difference

PERMUTATION = Order MATTERS

COMBINATION = Order DOESN'T Matter

P Permutations

Order MATTERS

  • Items selected from the same group
  • No item is used more than once
  • The order of arrangement makes a difference
ₙPᵣ = n! / (n - r)!

Think: "Picking AND Placing in order"

C Combinations

Order DOESN'T Matter

  • Items selected from the same group
  • No item is used more than once
  • The order of items makes NO difference
ₙCᵣ = n! / (r!(n - r)!)

Think: "Just picking a group"

📖 Side-by-Side Examples

PERMUTATION

Ice Cream Cone (Stacked)

An ice cream shop offers 12 flavors. You want a 3-scoop cone where the order matters (bottom, middle, top).

Why order matters: Chocolate-Vanilla-Strawberry is DIFFERENT from Strawberry-Vanilla-Chocolate

₁₂P₃ = 12!/(12-3)! = 12 × 11 × 10 = 1,320 cones

COMBINATION

Ice Cream Bowl (Mixed)

Same shop, 12 flavors. You want a bowl with 3 different flavors mixed together.

Why order doesn't matter: A bowl with Chocolate, Vanilla, Strawberry is THE SAME as Strawberry, Vanilla, Chocolate

₁₂C₃ = 12!/(3!(12-3)!) = 12!/(3! × 9!) = 220 bowls

PERMUTATION

Race Medals

30 runners in a race. How many ways can gold, silver, and bronze be awarded?

Why order matters: 1st place ≠ 2nd place ≠ 3rd place

₃₀P₃ = 30 × 29 × 28 = 24,360 ways

COMBINATION

Committee Selection

400 students, need to form a committee of 20. How many different committees?

Why order doesn't matter: A committee is just a group—no ranks or positions

₄₀₀C₂₀ = 400!/(20! × 380!) = very large number!

🧠 Quick Decision Guide

Ask yourself: "If I rearrange the selected items, do I get something different?"

  • YES → PERMUTATION (medals, officers, rankings, codes, schedules)
  • NO → COMBINATION (committees, teams, card hands, pizza toppings)

📝 More Examples to Identify

Electing President, VP, Secretary from 12 club members

→ PERMUTATION (positions are different)

Choosing 6 DVDs from 200 to take home

→ COMBINATION (just selecting, no order)

Dealing 5 cards for a poker hand

→ COMBINATION (hand is a set of cards)

Arranging 5 books on a shelf

→ PERMUTATION (left-to-right order matters)
