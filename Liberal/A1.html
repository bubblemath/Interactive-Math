Understanding Number Systems

Natural Numbers

Counting numbers: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5...

Symbol: ℕ

Whole Numbers

Natural numbers plus zero: 0, 1, 2, 3, 4...

Symbol: W

Integers

Whole numbers and their negatives: ...-2, -1, 0, 1, 2...

Symbol: ℤ

Rational Numbers

Numbers that can be written as fractions: 1/2, -3/4, 0.5, 2

Symbol: ℚ

Irrational Numbers

Non-repeating, non-terminating decimals: π, √2, e

Note: Cannot be written as simple fractions

Real Numbers

All rational and irrational numbers combined

Symbol: ℝ

Key Concept: Number Hierarchy

Each set builds on the previous one:

Natural ⊂ Whole ⊂ Integers ⊂ Rational ⊂ Real

Every natural number is also a whole number, integer, rational number, and real number!

Integer Operations Rules

Adding & Subtracting Integers

Situation Rule Example
Same Signs Add absolute values, keep the sign -3 + (-5) = -8
Different Signs Subtract absolute values (bigger - smaller), use larger value's sign -3 + 5 = 2
Double Negative Two negatives next to each other become addition 3 - (-5) = 3 + 5 = 8

Multiplying & Dividing Integers

Signs Result Examples
Same Signs Positive (+) × (+) = (+)
(-) × (-) = (+)
Different Signs Negative (+) × (-) = (-)
(-) × (+) = (-)

💡 Quick Tips

  • Subtracting a negative is the same as adding a positive
  • Two negatives multiplied or divided always give a positive
  • One negative and one positive always give a negative
  • Use parentheses to keep track of negative signs

Understanding the Counter Model

🟢 Green Counters = Positive (+1 each)

🔴 Red Counters = Negative (-1 each)

⚖️ Zero Pairs: One positive and one negative counter together equal zero and can be removed!

Total Value: 0

📚 Guided Scenarios - Try These!

Scenario 1: Adding Positive Numbers (3 + 2)

Model this by adding 3 positive counters, then adding 2 more positive counters.

What to discover: When you add positives to positives, you get more positives! The total is 5 positive counters = +5.

Scenario 2: Adding Negative Numbers (-3 + (-2))

Model this by adding 3 negative counters, then adding 2 more negative counters.

What to discover: When you add negatives to negatives, you get more negatives! The total is 5 negative counters = -5.

Scenario 3: Adding Different Signs (5 + (-3))

Model this by adding 5 positive counters, then adding 3 negative counters. Remove zero pairs!

What to discover: After removing zero pairs, you have 2 positive counters left = +2. This shows why we subtract when signs are different!

Scenario 4: Adding Different Signs (-5 + 3)

Model this by adding 5 negative counters, then adding 3 positive counters. Remove zero pairs!

What to discover: After removing zero pairs, you have 2 negative counters left = -2. The larger value's sign stays!

Scenario 5: Subtracting a Negative (4 - (-3))

Start with 4 positive counters. To subtract -3, we need to REMOVE 3 negative counters. But we don't have any! So add 3 zero pairs first (3 positive + 3 negative), then remove the 3 negatives.

What to discover: After removing the 3 negative counters, you're left with 7 positive counters = +7. This is the same as 4 + 3! That's why subtracting a negative equals adding a positive!
