📐 Product Rule

\(\sqrt[n]{ab} = \sqrt[n]{a} \cdot \sqrt[n]{b}\)
Example: \(\sqrt{50} = \sqrt{25 \cdot 2} = \sqrt{25} \cdot \sqrt{2} = 5\sqrt{2}\)

📐 Quotient Rule

\(\sqrt[n]{\frac{a}{b}} = \frac{\sqrt[n]{a}}{\sqrt[n]{b}}\)
Example: \(\sqrt{\frac{49}{4}} = \frac{\sqrt{49}}{\sqrt{4}} = \frac{7}{2}\)

⚠️ Even vs. Odd Index

Even root of negative = No real solution
Odd root of negative = Negative result
Even: \(\sqrt{-4}\) = No real solution
Odd: \(\sqrt[3]{-8} = -2\)

❌ Common Error

WRONG: \(\sqrt{9} + \sqrt{16} = \sqrt{25}\)

RIGHT: \(\sqrt{9} + \sqrt{16} = 3 + 4 = 7\)
(You cannot add under the radical!)

🔬 Anatomy of a Radical

Understanding the parts of a radical expression:

\(\sqrt[n]{a}\)
Index (n)
The small number indicating which root
Square root: n=2 (usually not written)
Cube root: n=3
Radicand (a)
The number under the radical symbol
This is what we're taking the root of
Radical Symbol (√)
The root symbol itself
Indicates we want a root, not a power

🔢 What Does a Radical Mean?

\(\sqrt[n]{a}\) means: "What number, when raised to the nth power, equals a?"

\(\sqrt[3]{8} = 2\) because \(2^3 = 8\)
\(\sqrt{25} = 5\) because \(5^2 = 25\)

📝 Reading Radicals

\(\sqrt{16}\) = "the square root of 16"
\(\sqrt[3]{27}\) = "the cube root of 27"
\(\sqrt[4]{81}\) = "the fourth root of 81"
\(\sqrt[5]{32}\) = "the fifth root of 32"

✅ Even Index (2, 4, 6...)

Can only take even roots of non-negative numbers.

\(\sqrt{16} = 4\) ✓
\(\sqrt{-16}\) = No real solution ✗
Why? No real number times itself an even number of times gives a negative result.

✅ Odd Index (3, 5, 7...)

Can take odd roots of any real number, including negatives.

\(\sqrt[3]{8} = 2\) ✓
\(\sqrt[3]{-8} = -2\) ✓
Why? A negative times itself an odd number of times stays negative!
\((-2)^3 = (-2)(-2)(-2) = -8\)

🏗️ Simplification Lab

📝 The 3-Step Process: 1) Factor the radicand into prime factors. 2) Find groups equal to the index. 3) Groups "go out" of the radical!

Simplify Step-by-Step

📖 Example Walkthrough: \(\sqrt{72}\)

1
Factor: \(72 = 36 \times 2\) (find the largest perfect square factor)
2
Split: \(\sqrt{72} = \sqrt{36 \times 2} = \sqrt{36} \cdot \sqrt{2}\)
3
Simplify: \(\sqrt{36} \cdot \sqrt{2} = 6\sqrt{2}\)
