Simple Interest

The Foundation of Interest Calculations

Understand the basics of interest calculated only on the principal amount. Simple interest is commonly used for short-term loans and some savings instruments.

Simple Interest Formula: I = Prt
I = Interest, P = Principal, r = rate (decimal), t = time (years)
  • Calculate interest earned or owed
  • Find the total amount (Principal + Interest)
  • Solve for any variable in the formula
  • Convert between time units appropriately

Compound Interest

The Power of Interest on Interest

Discover how money grows exponentially when interest is earned on both the principal and previously accumulated interest. This is the key to understanding long-term investments and savings.

Compound Interest Formula: A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)
A = Amount, P = Principal, r = rate, n = compounds per year, t = time
  • Compare simple vs. compound interest growth
  • Calculate future value of investments
  • Understand different compounding frequencies
  • Apply continuous compounding (A = Pe^rt)

Annuities

Regular Payments & Future Value

Calculate the future value of a series of equal payments made at regular intervals. Annuities are the foundation of retirement planning, college savings, and systematic investing.

Future Value of Annuity: FV = PMT × [(1 + r/n)^(nt) - 1] / (r/n)
FV = Future Value, PMT = Payment amount, r = rate, n = periods per year
  • Calculate future value of regular deposits
  • Plan for retirement savings goals
  • Understand ordinary annuities vs. annuities due
  • Determine required payment amounts

Fixed Installments

Loan Payments & Amortization

Learn how loans are repaid through equal periodic payments that cover both principal and interest. This applies to mortgages, auto loans, student loans, and other installment debt.

Payment Formula: PMT = P × [r/n] / [1 - (1 + r/n)^(-nt)]
PMT = Payment, P = Principal (loan amount), r = rate, n = payments per year
  • Calculate monthly loan payments
  • Determine total interest paid over loan life
  • Understand amortization schedules
  • Compare loan options and terms
