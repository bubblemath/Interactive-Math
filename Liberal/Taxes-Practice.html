Problem 4
Medium

Scenario: David is self-employed and needs to calculate his FICA taxes:

Self-employment income$150,000
Social Security tax rate12.4%
Social Security wage base limit (2016)$118,500
Medicare tax rate2.9%

Note: Self-employed individuals pay both employer and employee portions of FICA.

Calculate the following:
💡 Hint:

• Social Security tax only applies up to the wage base limit ($118,500)

• Social Security Tax = $118,500 × 0.124

• Medicare tax applies to ALL income (no limit)

• Medicare Tax = $150,000 × 0.029

• Total FICA = Social Security Tax + Medicare Tax
